> [!warning] DM-only entry. The party has not visited the Isle of Solenne, and almost nothing of its true nature is publicly known. Promote material into a public Codex entry as it is discovered.

The **Isle of Solenne** is the single large island at the centre of the [[Gulf of Miriel]], sitting in the open water between the western cities and the gulf's eastern mouth. It is no seat of power — only a scatter of small fishing villages along its shores — yet it is dominated by a single immense and ancient temple. The faithful now flocking to claim that temple believe it a long-lost house of [[Maris Wavecaller]]; in truth it is older and stranger than any of them understand.

## Location & Geography

The isle sits in the dead centre of the [[Gulf of Miriel]], the one piece of land in the open basin, splitting the water between the gulf's western cities — [[Val Miriel]], [[Camaar]], [[Farhaven]], and [[Sabine]] — and its only sea-gate at the eastern mouth. It is a genuinely large island — a couple of hours' walk from one shore to the other — yet it holds no city; its rocky, weather-worn coast and rolling interior carry only small coastal towns and fishing villages, whose people live off the same rich inner-shore fishing banks that have fed [[Camaar]] and [[Sabine]] for centuries. Warm and wet like the rest of the gulf, the isle is wreathed in the same autumn fogs that sit on the water until midday, out of which the temple rises like a second, stranger shore.

It is, by the gulf's standards, of no consequence: small and poor enough that the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]] has never bothered to garrison it, and outside the Empire-and-[[Mawbreakers|Mawbreaker]] contest for the gulf entirely. It is neutral ground — left, until now, to settle its own quiet affairs.

## The Ancient Temple

A single colossal temple crowns a rise near the island's heart, a landmark visible from every shore. It is old beyond reckoning — older than the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]], older than [[Val Miriel]] and the other Cities of Old, raised in an age the present world has all but forgotten. Long derelict, it stands only partly intact: a cavernous warren of weathered stone, collapsed halls, and dark and dangerous depths the island's folk have always been careful to leave well alone. For generations it was simply *there* — too vast to use, too old to explain — a brooding monument the fisherfolk lived beneath without ever truly entering.

## The Truth — Istishia's House

The temple is not, and never was, Maris Wavecaller's. It is a temple of [[Istishia]], the Water Lord — one of the **Gods of Old** — raised before the [[The Shattering|Shattering]], in the age of the [[Chaos Wars]] or earlier. That makes it one of the oldest structures still standing anywhere in the world, predating every kingdom of the present age. After Istishia's relic *Umberlee's Grasp*, it is the most significant surviving trace of the forgotten god to be found.

The worshippers now claiming it have no idea. Because Istishia has been forgotten and [[Maris Wavecaller]] carries his sphere — water — they mistake his temple for an ancestral seat of the Wavecaller's faith, a water-god's house owed to the goddess of the sea. The truth is written across the stone for anyone able to read it: **cresting-wave iconography** (Istishia's own symbol) and **pre-Shattering inscriptions in an old tongue** the movement cannot decipher. They are restoring a god's house to a saint who never owned it — and to a power they have never heard named.

## The Riftshard

A [[Riftshard]] lies within, or beneath, the temple — a plane-leak left by the Shattering's imperfect sealing, almost certainly a breach toward the **Elemental Plane of Water** (Istishia is King of the Water Elementals). Near it the air runs charged and magic turns wild, warping toward water; this is the true source of the "dangerous lingering powers" the islanders sense in the deep halls. It mirrors the Faewild Riftshard the party already encountered beneath the goblin temple during the Goblin Contract.

The movement's push to dig out and re-consecrate the temple's depths risks **disturbing the Riftshard** — and whatever the temple's builders bound, sealed, or simply left behind in the god's own house.

## The Wavecaller's Movement

The long quiet is breaking. A new and fervent movement of Maris Wavecaller worshippers, gathered behind a single zealous leader, has come to the isle determined to claim the temple, cleanse it, and re-consecrate it to the goddess — pressing into its long-sealed depths to do so. The island's wary, insular folk do not welcome them; they have always left the temple alone, by a custom they may no longer fully understand, and they sense these outsiders are waking something better left asleep. With no greater power minded to intervene on neutral ground, the quarrel is the islanders' and the zealots' alone — for now.

**Open thread:** whether the leader genuinely *senses* the power in the temple and seeks it, or is an earnest zealot serving as the unwitting instrument of something far older.

## Campaign Significance

The isle ties straight into the **Gods-of-Old / Second Shattering** spine of the campaign: the [[Riftshard]]s as a running thread, [[Istishia]] as a withdrawn elder power whose stirring remains an open question, and *Umberlee's Grasp* as his most persistent material link to the present world. A surviving temple of his — with a Riftshard inside it, sitting in water the party already sails through — is a slow-burn site a future session could turn on.
