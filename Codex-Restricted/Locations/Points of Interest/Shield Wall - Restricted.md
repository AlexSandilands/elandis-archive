> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Shield Wall]]*

## True Scale & Construction

The Shield Wall does not merely guard the harbour approach. It encircles the base of the island itself — extending a mile or more into the open sea from both headlands, an unbroken arc of stone that puts the entire island of [[Val Solis]] inside its perimeter. From the sea it appears first, before the island does.

Its foundations are Chaos Wars era or older — almost certainly pre-dating the Kingdoms of Old, and built using methods that are not well understood today and could not be reproduced. The stone is massive, the foundations sunk into the seabed, and the Wall's outer face rises sixty feet or more above the waterline. The construction is unlike anything elsewhere on [[Valtorra]].

## The Triple Gate

There is one entrance: a single gate in the Wall's western face, permanently closed, approachable only along a narrow channel that passes beneath the towers on both sides. Behind it, the channel continues inward between two further walls — three barriers in succession, each completely independent. A ship that forces the first gate finds itself inside the killing ground between the first and second walls, with every battery above still in range and no advantage gained. A ship that somehow forces the second faces the third in the same position.

The Empire's expeditions broke against the first gate. None reached the second.

## Armaments

The Wall's full arsenal:
- Standard ballista emplacements along the entire length of the outer face
- Catapult platforms at regular intervals, covering the sea approaches
- Specialised fireball towers at intervals — a design unique to the Shield Wall, built to discharge fire at approaching ships by some combination of engineering and craft that is no longer easily reproduced. These towers are the primary reason the Empire's wooden-hulled fleets suffered such catastrophic losses.

## Crewing & Land Access

The Shield Wall is permanently crewed. Its garrison rotates in postings of weeks or months at a time — it functions as a second settlement, with its own quarters, stores, and maintenance infrastructure built into the Wall's interior. Accessing the Wall from the sea is impossible except through the gate; from the city, a network of passages runs through the mountain rock connecting the inner city to the fortifications.

The island's outer coast offers no alternative approach. The cliffs on every shore except the harbour face are unclimbable and offer no landing site for a ship. There is no beach. A force attempting to land on the island's exterior and approach the city overland through the mountains would find the terrain impassable. The Wall is the only way in.

## The Signal

There is a way through. A specific sequence of flag signals — known only to select parties — identifies a ship as non-hostile and prompts the Wall's crew to open the first gate partially, enough for contact. Ships that use the signal are not admitted to the city; trade and communication happen at the entrance under the watch of the Wall's towers.

Those who hold the signal: certain captains among the [[Mawbreakers]] of [[Camaar]] (heavily vetted, the arrangement passed down across generations), and the [[Order of Ravens]] (who are the only outsiders admitted past the first gate and into the city itself).

The signal is not written down. It is passed in person, to individuals, and is not common knowledge even within the Mawbreakers as a whole.

## Tactical Notes

Any party attempting to reach Val Solis needs either the signal or a plan to acquire it. Without it, approaching the Wall by ship triggers the Wall's defences — the fireball towers have the range to engage ships well before they reach the gate. A direct assault is not survivable. The signal (a Mawbreaker or Ravens contact) is the only realistic route of approach.
