> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Emberspire]]*

## True Origin

- The Emberspire is a structure of the [[Chaos Wars]], raised by powerful arcanists as a **defensive bastion against the hordes of monsters pouring through the planar Rifts** of that age — the breaches torn open by [[The Shattering]].
- It is the **residual arcane power** still bound in the tower, all these centuries later, that protects [[Lighthaven]] today. The wards never fully faded; the town simply inherited a working war-engine it no longer understands and learned to wake its light.
- The [[Darkwood]] is *not* part of the Spire's origin — the forest overgrew the region long after, as the ancient world fell to ruin. The Spire predates it and merely holds it back now, doing incidentally what it was built to do deliberately.
- The reason the wood is so monstrous: it is riddled with [[Riftshard]]s, and riftshards attract and breed monsters. The Spire was raised to repel exactly that. So holding back the Darkwood's horrors is not a coincidence of geography — it is the tower still doing its original job against the same kind of threat, with a great Plane-of-Water shard sitting in the Sunken Temple at the forest's heart (see [[Darkwood]] restricted).

## Place in the Cosmology

- This ties the Spire directly into established history: the same era of dragons and rift-spawn that the [[Riftshard]]s, [[Praetor'Varanous]], and the four planar-rift sites the party has already encountered all trace back to.
- The Emberspire is one of the rare defensive works of that war still standing **and still functioning** — a survivor of the age that broke the world, quietly doing its job in a town that has forgotten there ever was a war.

## The Buried Irony

- The presiding speaker's ceremonial line at the Vigil — *"at the conjunction of the planes"* — is treated by the town as hollow, poetic tradition. It is, unknowingly, **literally** what the Spire was built for.
- The tower's anti-magic / dispelling aura fits a fortress engineered to **suppress and repel planar and arcane incursion** — a defensive property, not a quirk.

## What's Unknown

- None of this is known to the town, the Emberwardens, or (presumably) the party. The original builders, their war, and the Spire's true function are all lost to memory.
- Open thread: whether the annual solstice surge is the tower's defences cycling, a stored reserve venting, or something tied to the date itself — left undetailed for now.
