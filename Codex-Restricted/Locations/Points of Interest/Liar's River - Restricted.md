> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Liar's River]]*

## The Water's Unexpected Effects
- The water does more than drown intruders and quench steel — and what, exactly, is deliberately left open. Treat it as a reservoir of hooks rather than a fixed catalogue: reach for a new effect when a scene wants one (a strange reflection, a draught that does something unforeseen, a property that surfaces only under the right conditions) rather than committing to a fixed list now.
- Players know only the public-facing facts: it kills those who try to cross, and its water under moonlight makes moon-quenched steel.

## The Name
- The three "lies" the river is named for — it lies about distance (the receding mountains), its surface throws back reflections that can't be trusted, and old tales hold it answers questions falsely — are all genuinely true of it. No single one is the canonical reason for the name. Keep it unexplained on the page and at the table; the mystery is the point.

## Crossing — By Design
- There is no unsanctioned crossing for players to discover. The Guardian Bridge and the Ancient Rites are the intended path; the river is meant to funnel travellers to [[Grumble, The Anniahilator|Grumble]], not to be solved or circumvented.
- The Long Hunt crossing is the proof of the rule, not an exception: even an army can ford it, but only by paying in drowned bodies. Anyone insisting on crossing the water directly should pay a comparable toll.
