The Xericana Sands are the vast, arid desert that fills the western third of [[Valtorra]] — a dune sea running from the foot of the [[Silver Mountains]] all the way to the western ocean. It is one of the continent's great ungoverned wilds, lawless and beyond the reach of Imperial order, and the homeland of the peoples the Empire names monsters. Beneath its sands lies a buried world: the ruins of a country that thrived before the [[The Shattering|Shattering]] turned it to desert, and the treasure-vaults that draw adventurers west to dig it back out.

## Geography & Landscape

The Xericana fills the whole western flank of [[Valtorra]], west of the [[Silver Mountains]] that form the continent's spine. From the bare stone at the mountains' foot the land falls away into open dune sea and runs unbroken to the western shore, where it meets the ocean; only at its southern edge does the desert soften, the sand giving way to greener scrubland before the coast. It is a classic sand desert — rolling dunes from horizon to horizon under a killing sun, where water is scarce and the crossing itself is the first thing that tries to kill a traveller.

It is not, however, empty stone. Scattered across the dunes and sunk beneath them lie the ruins of an older world — toppled walls and half-buried structures breaching the sand, and far more below: vaults, chambers, and tunnels the desert has swallowed whole. And where the dunes meet that buried stone the ground turns black with tar-pits and oil seeps, crusted at their edges with bright yellow sulphur — the source of the desert's plainer wealth.

## History

The Xericana was not always desert. Before the [[The Shattering|Shattering]], this was living, inhabited country, rich enough to raise cities and fill them — which is why the sands are so thick with ruins now. The peoples who built them belong to the ancient world of [[Valtorra]], the early human and elven cultures and others whose names have not survived; their history lies largely lost beneath the dunes, waiting to be read out of the scrolls and stone the desert has kept.

It was the changing climate that followed the Shattering that dried the land to desert. The rains failed, the country withered, and the sand came in and buried the old world where it stood — its cities, its roads, and its vaults sealed under the dunes for later ages to dig back out. What stands today is the aftermath: a dead land laid over a once-living one.

## Peoples & Powers

The Xericana belongs to the peoples the Empire calls monsters and would rather pretend away — goblinoids of every stripe, goblins, hobgoblins, and bugbears, alongside orcs, the giantkin ogres and trolls, and bestial folk such as minotaurs and centaurs. They claim the Sands as their own, and the desert is theirs in every way that matters.

There is no single ruler and no unifying power. The Xericana is a patchwork of warring tribes who fight one another as readily as any outsider, and no warlord or horde has ever bound them into one. The [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]] tried, at some point, to bring the desert to heel and failed badly: it is all but impossible to station and supply troops across open sand, and the campaign came to nothing. What Imperial presence remains is a small, mostly symbolic showing at [[Xenith]] on the coast — a token kept up for the sake of a face and a little diplomacy, with no real authority behind it.

## Settlements & Sites

The desert has no settled interior; its named places cling to the rim, where the dunes meet the mountains or the sea.

- [[Xenith]] — the rough, polyglot port on the far western coast, the great market where the spoils of the desert are bought and sold; it belongs to the monstrous peoples and runs on whatever law the strongest interests make.
- [[Brightwater]] — an Imperial dock city at the greener southern edge of the Sands, the Empire's one real foothold here, built to feed the supply line to the dwarven holds.

## Spoils of the Sands

Everything the desert is worth comes out of it the hard way — dug from buried vaults or drawn from the seeps — and finds its way to market at [[Xenith]]. The finds fall into two kinds: the buried wealth of the lost world, which draws adventurers, and the raw materials of the living one, which keep the caravans running.

| Find | What it is, and why it's sought |
|---|---|
| Ancient coin & gems | The buried treasuries of the pre-Shattering world — gold, cut stones, and worked valuables sealed in ruin-vaults under the dunes. The straightforward prize, and the reason most delvers come west. |
| Lost magic | Items, relics, and enchanted goods from a vanished age, turned up intact in undergrounds the sand kept sealed. Rare, dangerous to recover, and worth a fortune at [[Xenith]]. |
| Forgotten lore | Books, scrolls, maps, and inscriptions — the history the surface world has lost. Valued less for gold than for what it reveals; scholars and factions will pay for a single readable page. |
| Naphtha | Black oil drawn from the desert's tar-pits and seeps — a burnable trade-good and a weapon, the stuff of alchemist's fire and desert warfare. |
| Sulphur | Bright crusts gathered around the seeps. One of the three components of black powder, which quietly makes the lawless Sands a supplier to the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]] and the [[Mawbreakers]] alike. |

## Dangers

- The desert itself — open dune with little water and a killing sun, where the unprepared simply do not come back.
- The tribes — predators, every one. Delvers and caravans crossing the dunes are prey, and raiding bands come off the sand to hit the supply roads and the edges of settled country wherever they can reach.
- The ruins — the buried structures and sealed undergrounds that draw treasure-hunters are collapsing, lightless, and old; what guards or remains within them is anyone's guess until it is too late.

## Significance

The Xericana is one of the four great wilds of [[Valtorra]] and the homeland of every people the Empire drove to its margins — a standing reminder of the limits of Imperial order, a country it tried to take and could not hold. It is also the continent's great unopened reliquary: a buried civilisation's worth of gold, gems, magic, and lost lore lies under its dunes, which is why adventurers come west to dig despite everything the desert and its tribes can do to them. And quietly, beneath the grave-robbing, the Sands feed the wider world — the sulphur drawn from its seeps is one of the three components of black powder, so the lawless desert helps arm the very [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]] and the privateer [[Mawbreakers]] who would master it.

## Trivia

- Of the continent's four great peoples, the goblinoids and their kin are the only ones the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]] never managed to hold — the elves withdrew behind their wards, the dwarves were conquered and bound to the mines, the humans were left their thrones as governors, and the peoples of the Xericana were simply left to the sand the Empire could not cross.
- The desert is, in the most literal sense, built on its own past: nearly everything of value in it — the treasure, the lore, the very ruins the tribes shelter among — predates the Shattering that made it a desert in the first place.
