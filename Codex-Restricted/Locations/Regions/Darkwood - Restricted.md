> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Darkwood]]*

## Why the Wood Is Monstrous — The Riftshards

- The Darkwood is riddled with [[Riftshard]]s, the plane-leaks left by [[The Shattering]]. Riftshards attract and breed monsters, which is the true reason the wood is so deadly and why creatures perpetually spill toward [[Lighthaven]].
- This is exactly what the [[Emberspire]] was raised to hold back. The tower is a [[Chaos Wars]]-era defensive work built to repel monsters pouring through the rifts; the forest grew over the ruined world long after, and the Spire now does incidentally what it once did deliberately. (See the [[Emberspire]] restricted entry.)
- The ancient civilisation whose ruins [[Lighthaven]] stands on was a Chaos-Wars people; the Darkwood overgrew their fallen world. The Sunken Temple below was one of their great places of worship.

## The Sunken Temple

- At the centre of the forest, drowned beneath the great lake, lies the Sunken Temple — an ancient hall of worship to one of the gods of old, now submerged.
- It holds a particularly large Riftshard linked to the Plane of Water. This shard is the lake's true source: water gushes from it to fill the basin above. It is the inverse twin of [[The Maw]], whose own Plane-of-Water shard pulls water the other way, out of the Material. (See [[The Maw]] restricted.)
- Set into a great altar at the centre of the temple's massive central hall is a large emerald jewel — one of a matched pair, mined from the deepest dwarven workings in the [[Silver Mountains]] and gifted to the ancient civilisation here during the [[Chaos Wars]]. Its sister is the red jewel now in [[Glimmerforge]].

## The Merfolk

- A reclusive faction of merfolk live in the Sunken Temple and revere the Riftshard. Their daily ritual casts a spell that opens it to gush water and top up the lake — a spiritual ceremony that also keeps the water level high enough that the temple stays submerged and hidden.
- They are hostile to any intruder barring very smooth talking; the temple is their home and religious centre.
- They do not know what they are guarding. They worship the Riftshard, not the jewel, and have no idea the emerald set in their altar is a key to the apocalypse.
- Still just a concept — no name, leadership, or detail yet. Needs its own faction entry.

## The Jewel and the Second Shattering

- [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow]] needs both jewels to power his ritual for the Second Shattering. He already controls the red one — the [[Valtorran Empire]] holds the dwarven kingdom — so the Darkwood emerald is the last missing key.
- This is why [[Dread General Gerard Blackmarsh|Blackmarsh]] dispatched [[General Korvas]] to the Darkwood to retrieve "the jewel" (Session 39). Korvas is bound for the Sunken Temple now.

## Korvas Returns to Where He Was Unmade

- The Sunken Temple's great Riftshard is the same one that once shattered [[General Korvas]]'s Imperial conditioning and left the blank-slate man who became [[Vaeleran]] (see Korvas restricted).
- So Blackmarsh is unknowingly sending Korvas back to the very place that unmade him. Open thread: whether proximity to the Riftshard fractures his freshly-restored conditioning a second time — a loaded gun to fire when it serves the story, possibly with the party present at the altar.

## Ryo's Hidden Home

- [[Ryo]]'s childhood treehouse home stands somewhere in the wood — deliberately sealed against light leaking out, tied to his elven royal bloodline rather than to the temple plot. The Darkwood is simply where [[Jek]] of the [[Darkwood Vanguard]] hid and raised him; his presence here is incidental to the jewel.
