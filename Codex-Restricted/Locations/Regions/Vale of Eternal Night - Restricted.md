> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Vale of Eternal Night]]*

The Star-Veiled Queen's throne rests on a secret she has kept for two thousand years, and on a prisoner she keeps beneath it. This is the truth of the Vale, known to no one in the Court and to none in Sumara.

## The Queen Is Two

[[Mirileth]] was not always an archfae with a body. She began as a bodiless spirit: long ago a powerful and deeply evil entity severed her spirit from her flesh and destroyed that flesh entirely, leaving her discorporate and dispossessed.

The pact that ended the Long Hunt was the price of her return. The bodiless Mirileth and [[Ellesandra Silmara]] struck a bargain: Mirileth would seize the Vale, bring the Hunt to heel and end the war — and in return, Ellesandra would give up her body. Ellesandra honoured the first half at once, returning from the Vale bearing the head of the Hunt's leader, exactly as the legend records. The second half came due a few years later. Her recorded death was not what it seemed: she left Sumara, returned to the Vale, and surrendered her body to Mirileth — likely working some magic to make her passing appear natural, so that no one would trace the truth. Mirileth has worn that body ever since. The two now cohabitate a single form: two minds behind one face, the elven hero and the archfae both. This is why the Queen reads as elven-but-wrong, and why her own people do not know what she is. It is also why a Silmara walking into the Vale is no ordinary embassy — [[Ryo]] is, in the most literal sense, kin to the body on the throne.

## The Prisoner in the Soulstone Anchor

Once she had a body and a throne, Mirileth hunted down the entity that had unmade her in the first place. She did not destroy it — she bound it. That entity is what lies inside the [[Soulstone Anchor]] held in the Veiled Citadel of [[Caer Nystral, The Dawnless City|Caer Nystral]]: an ancient, extremely evil, patient and conniving thing, biding its time for any chance to slip its cage. The Anchor is Mirileth's secret prize and her secret terror at once. She keeps it because she will not let it loose; she keeps it hidden because she dares not let anyone know it can be loosed.

She will kill — quietly, and without hesitation — anyone who learns the Anchor exists, because anyone who knows of it is a door the prisoner might one day open. This is the danger the party is walking into. [[Praetor'Varanous|Praetor]] has sent them to take the very thing the Queen murders to protect; the moment they reveal they know what it is, they become people she must silence. The embassy that "solves a lot of problems" may instead carry them straight to the one secret in the Vale worth dying over.

## Asha Vayne

[[Asha Vayne]] did not die on the border sixty years ago. Mirileth does not permit loose ends, and she did not waste him: he is held within the Court, bound into its service — glamoured and charmed, his old self buried but not gone, and recoverable by someone who reaches him. [[Warden Sylvaris]]'s read is correct.

## Open Thread — The Barrow-Crone *(planned, not yet played)*

Prospective material for the party's arrival, not yet established at the table. A powerful old arch-hag — Mallt Sootmarrow, the Barrow-Crone — dwells in the barrow-mounds on the Vale's outer edge and is quietly plotting against the Queen: a power in the Vale older than the Court itself, and no friend to it. The first Hunt band the party meets are secretly her loyalists, not Mirileth's. If the party asks to be brought before the Queen, the band will play along until the city's edge and then veer off, marching the embassy instead to the Crone's barrow in the Barrow-Ring — seeing a living Silmara as the lever they have waited for to move against the Citadel. The Crone deals in lies: she will paint Mirileth as an oppressor and a tyrant, and may claim the Queen is plotting against Sumara, all to turn the party into her instrument. She is a third power in the Vale and a deceiver, not an ally. How much of this lands depends on the party's choices when they cross.
