**Val Aran** is the great walled city of [[Valtorra]]'s far north — a wide, stone-built settlement planted at the southern mouth of the [[Borealia Forest]], in the gap between the [[Silver Mountains]] to the west and the [[Borealia Range]] to the east. One of the six ancient Kingdoms of Old that took shape in the long centuries following the [[Chaos Wars]], it predates the [[Valtorran Empire]] by well over a thousand years and has remained beyond its effective reach throughout. Its walls are old and thick, built not for war between nations but against the constant, unhuman dangers that press down from the forest. For the scattered trapping communities of the far north, Val Aran is the last major city before the treeline, and the closest thing to a capital the cold north has.

## Geography & Layout

Val Aran sits on the northern edge of the [[Luminor Plains]], where the broad temperate grasslands that roll south toward [[Val Miriel]] come to an end at the foot of the northern ranges. The city occupies a natural corridor between the [[Silver Mountains]] to the west and the [[Borealia Range]] to the east — the continental divide pinching here before the boreal forest closes off the horizon. The [[Borealia Forest]] begins almost immediately north of the walls, and on clear days its dark canopy is visible from the city's highest keeps.

Val Aran is large by northern standards — not a frontier outpost but a proper city, expanded over centuries into its current scale. Its curtain walls are pale granite, worn smooth by generations of wind, and built for endurance rather than ceremony. Several gatehouses pierce the circuit; the northern gate, which faces the forest directly, is the most heavily manned. Within the walls, the city is built largely in stone, with thatch-roofed homes and merchant halls on the lower ground and stone keeps and garrison buildings rising on the higher ground. The streets are broad enough to move troops and goods but unpaved — hard-packed earth in summer, ice-rimed in winter.

## The People

The people of Val Aran are hardy and practical, shaped by the climate and the proximity of the forest. Heavy furs and layered wool are standard dress for most of the year, and the city's markets deal primarily in the products of the northern wilderness — pelts, dried game, timber, and the tools of the hunt. Trapping is the backbone of the local economy, and rangers who work the forest's safer edges are a respected profession, trusted with knowledge of the forest's hazards that most citizens would rather not think about.

Val Aran's culture bears comparison to the old seafaring peoples of distant coasts — communal, self-reliant, with a high tolerance for hardship and a low tolerance for those who cannot carry their own weight. Feasts and storytelling are important fixtures of civic life; strangers who prove useful are welcomed quickly, and those who do not are noticed just as fast. The dead wandering the deep [[Borealia Forest]] have been an unpleasant background fact for as long as anyone can remember, and the city has made a certain dark peace with its northern neighbour — treating the undead as a hazard to be managed rather than a horror to be feared.

Val Aran is also known, if distantly, for the quality of its steel. The city's smiths have long worked with iron from the northern ranges, and weapons forged here carry a distinctive character that those with knowledge of steelcraft can identify at a glance.

## History

Val Aran was one of the six human Kingdoms of Old — alongside [[Val Noren]], [[Val Miriel]], [[Val Luminor]], [[Val Solis]], and [[Val Aerie]] — the ancient realms that grew from the wreckage of the [[Chaos Wars]] as civilisation rebuilt itself. The *Val* prefix the cities share is a relic of those founding centuries, most commonly interpreted as "seat" or "hold."

Of the six, Val Aran grew in the most isolated position, far from the trade routes and rivers that shaped its southern siblings. What the city had in their place was the [[Borealia Forest]] — and the reputation that went with it. A powerful necromancer once rose to infamy in the forest's depths, performing a ritual of lasting consequence that left a mark on the land it has never shed. The undead that have wandered the wood ever since are that mark's ongoing legacy, and the city has lived alongside them for centuries.

## Government

Val Aran has been governed by the Aran family across generations — a hereditary arrangement that has given the city unusual continuity of leadership for a frontier hold. The current Governor, [[Bjorn Aran]], maintains formal correspondence with [[King Marius Noren]] of [[Val Noren]], an old alliance of the northern holds that has outlasted the rise of the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]] to the south.

## [[The Bloody Nails|Campaign: The Bloody Nails]]

Val Aran has featured repeatedly as a destination and a source of off-screen intelligence, though the party has not yet traveled there.

#### [[Session 20 - The Streets of Val Miriel]]

[[Vaeleran]]'s greatsword was identified by a smith in Val Miriel as northern steelcraft, likely forged in or near Val Aran — the first suggestion the party received that the far north might lie on their path.

#### [[Session 21 - The Governor's Daughter]]

Among intelligence gathered in Val Miriel, the party learned that the dead were walking in greater numbers in the [[Borealia Forest]] north of Val Aran, and that Governor [[Bjorn Aran]] may have been distracted by the problem.

#### [[Session 22 - Inferno at Serenity Plaza]]

[[Felix]] presented the party with a Ring of Protection recovered from ruins north of Val Aran — evidence that objects of value had been brought back from the forest's depths before.

#### [[Session 23 - Ruby Falls Goldmine]]

[[Thaddeus Karr]], Nest Father of the Val Miriel [[Order of Ravens]], sent word ahead to the Val Aran Nest to expect the [[The Bloody Nails|Bloody Nails]].

#### [[Session 26 - A Midnight Romp]]

A looted sword examined by guild contacts was identified as northern royalty make, potentially belonging to the Aran family itself.

#### [[Session 28 - The Underrun]]

[[Vaeleran]] warned a contact that the dead were walking near Val Aran, passing on what the party had learned of the forest's worsening state.

#### [[Session 43 - The Shield of the North]]

[[King Marius Noren]] provided [[Berberis]] with a letter of recommendation addressed to Governor [[Bjorn Aran]], pointing the party north toward Val Aran as their next destination.

## Trivia

- The name Val Aran follows the same *Val* prefix as the other ancient human kingdoms — most commonly interpreted as "seat" or "hold." Whether the city took the name from the Aran family or the family took it from the city is a question the locals tend to find either obvious or irrelevant, depending on whom you ask.
