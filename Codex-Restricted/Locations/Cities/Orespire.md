Orespire guards the southern mouth of the Silver Valley, built into the mountain face above the river that drains the valley southward and out onto the open continent. It is the forge city of the [[Silver Mountains]] — the place where ore from [[Gemhold]]'s deep shafts and the broader range is smelted down and worked into weapons, armour, and tools. Its furnaces have burned without pause for centuries. Under Imperial occupation, that output has been redirected toward military production for the [[Valtorran Empire]].

## Geography & Layout

Orespire is built within the mountain at the southern terminus of the Silver Valley, its lower gates opening onto the river that flows out from the valley floor. The city proper is underground — a complex of smelting halls, foundry chambers, and residential warrens carved deep into the mountain face — but its lower reaches connect to the river valley, and ore and finished goods move out through these lower gates onto the waterway below. The forges within vent their heat and smoke through shafts cut into the rock above, and the columns of smoke rising from those vents are visible at considerable distance in the valley. Northward, the underground road connects to [[Glimmerforge]] and [[Gemhold]].

## History

Orespire was built to do one thing: take what the mines produce and turn it into something made. Its foundry tradition is ancient — the smith clans here developed techniques over centuries that remain among the most respected on the continent, and dwarven weapons and armour carrying an Orespire mark have long commanded a premium in any market. The city has always had a harder edge than the other holds, shaped by the physical demands of continuous heavy industry and by the martial traditions that naturally developed alongside weapons manufacture.

During the Dwarven Rebellions, Orespire's population contributed heavily to the rebel forces. The city did not see the fighting that [[Gemhold]] did, but it supplied much of what the rebels carried into the tunnels. The rebellion's failure hit the forge clans hard; many of the city's best smiths left in the aftermath.

## Government & Imperial Presence

Orespire's output is now directed by Imperial military commission — the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]]'s demand for weapons and armour for its garrisons and campaigns takes precedence over civilian or commercial production. Dwarven smiths work to Imperial specifications under overseer supervision, producing what the Empire orders rather than what the craft tradition would otherwise produce. The commission controls pricing, output targets, and distribution. The gem conscription applies here as across the [[Silver Mountains]], though in Orespire young dwarves are as likely to end up at a forge as in a mine shaft.

## The People

Orespire's population is almost entirely dwarven, drawn from the forge clans whose identity is inseparable from the foundries. Smiths, engineers, and heavy labourers are the backbone of the city. The population trends younger than the other holds — the work is physically demanding and the post-rebellion diaspora thinned the older generations. Those who remain carry a pronounced bitterness about the redirection of their craft toward Imperial ends: they are making weapons that enforce the occupation on their own people, and they know it.
