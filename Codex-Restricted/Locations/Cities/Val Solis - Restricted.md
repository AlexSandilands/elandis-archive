> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Val Solis]]*

## The Affliction

Val Solis is a gothic horror kingdom operating behind the image of a sealed, proud city. The exterior reputation — unconquered, isolationist, mysterious — is accurate as far as it goes, but the closure no longer serves independence alone.

At some point in the last century a vampire lord gained a foothold in Val Solis and turned the city's ruling class. The noble houses and the Solis dynasty itself have been turned. The common folk — the bulk of the ~45,000 population — live under their control in a sustained state of terror: a Van Helsing gothic horror society sealed behind the Shield Wall and a hundred years of silence. The cursed rulers maintain the city's external posture of proud isolation because it serves them perfectly. No one comes in, and nothing leaks out.

This is why agents sent to investigate have not returned. They are turned, killed, or kept.

## The Necromancer Connection

The origin of the Val Solis vampires is tied to the historical Necromancer of [[Borealia Forest]] — the ancient figure whose ritual work centuries ago tainted the forest and left the dead walking the north.

The vampire lord was present in the early days of the Necromancer's rise and instrumental in empowering it — providing resources, knowledge, and protection during the period when the Necromancer was still consolidating power. The relationship was one of mutual use: the vampire wanted a servant and a distraction, something to occupy the north and draw attention away from the sea. The Necromancer wanted power and accepted the patronage.

The arrangement ended when the Necromancer grew too powerful — turned in a direction the vampire had not anticipated and became something uncontrollable. The vampire cut ties and departed, leaving the Necromancer to their work and eventual fall. Shortly after, the vampire arrived at Val Solis and began the patient work of turning the ruling class. Whether the island was always the intended destination, or simply a defensible and remote place to settle, is not established.

The two horrors share a root. A thorough investigation of Borealia's history may surface the connection; an encounter with the Val Solis vampires should eventually cast a shadow back toward the forest.

## The Signal

There is a way through the [[Shield Wall]]. A specific sequence of flag signals identifies a ship as non-hostile and triggers the Wall's gate to open partially — enough for contact at the entrance, no further. Ships that use the signal are not admitted to the city; trade, news, and limited communication are conducted at the entrance under the watch of the Wall's crew.

The following know the signal: select captains among the [[Mawbreakers]] of [[Camaar]], and the [[Order of Ravens]]. The Mawbreakers who hold it are heavily vetted and have maintained the arrangement for generations; it is not common knowledge even within the Mawbreakers as a whole. Ravens are the only outsiders routinely admitted past the gate and into the city itself.

## The Ravens Cannot Leave

The vampire rulers value intelligence about the mainland. Ravens who come to Val Solis — ostensibly to disappear, to use the refuge the Order's records describe — are admitted inside and not permitted to leave. They are kept. The rulers extract what the Ravens know about the Empire, the political landscape, and current events, and use it to stay informed without exposing themselves to the outside world.

Ravens who enter Val Solis go silent. The [[Order of Ravens]] leadership does not know why — they have heard rumours that something is wrong with the nest, but the full truth of what Val Solis has become has not reached them. The phrase "gone to Solis" carries a darker meaning than most users of it realise.

## Plot Hook: Recruiting Val Solis

The party's mission includes recruiting the Kingdoms of Old to the rebellion. Val Solis is one of them. Reaching it requires knowing the signal — a Mawbreaker or Ravens contact is the most likely route.

What they find when they arrive is a city whose rulers are undead monsters and whose people are desperate for rescue. Options for resolution:
- Destroy or drive out the vampire ruling class and deal with the governance vacuum that follows.
- Negotiate with the vampires — a hundred years of isolation gives them their own reasons to want the Empire gone, if the party can find the angle.
- Find and support a resistance among the common folk who might rise up given outside help.

The Shield Wall's military power and the city's strategic position make it worth the difficulty regardless of which path is taken.
