**Sabine** is a large, centuries-old working city on the northern shore of the [[Gulf of Miriel]] — one of the four settlements ringing the gulf alongside [[Val Miriel]], [[Camaar]], and [[Farhaven]]. Comparable in size to Camaar, it is a city of the water rather than a seat of power: it catches and cures fish like the rest of the gulf, but its defining trade is building and mending the boats the whole gulf sails on. It is the one gulf city where two facts are true at once — a deeply embedded smuggling culture and a visible [[Valtorran Empire|Imperial]] garrison — and the quiet, daily accommodation between those two forces is the texture of the place.

## Geography & Layout

Sabine sits on the northern shore of the [[Gulf of Miriel]], facing south across the sheltered inner water toward the [[Isle of Solenne]] and, far beyond it, the gulf's eastern mouth at [[The Maw]]. The rich fishing banks of the inner shore — the same shoals that have fed [[Camaar]] and Sabine for centuries — lie within easy reach of its harbour, and the coastal road runs west along the shore toward the capital region around [[Val Miriel]].

The waterfront is the city's engine and its signature. For much of its length the shore is given over to the trade that defines Sabine: slipways and timber-framed drydocks where the pale ribs of half-built hulls stand open to the weather, timber ponds and sawpits, ropewalks, sail-lofts, and the smoke of the curing-houses where the day's catch is salted and barrelled. Behind the working shore the city climbs the gentle northern slope in a dense sprawl of grey-slate and weathered-timber rooftops, packed without grand plan — a working city grown outward across centuries rather than laid down to a design. It has no great walls; the outer edge of the settlement is its border, and the Imperial garrison holds a keep among the rooftops above the harbour.

## Trade & Industry

Sabine's reason to exist beyond its fishing banks is its **boatyards**. The city builds and repairs the gulf's working craft — fishing hulls, coastal traders, and small shallow-draft vessels — and a steady stream of craft from the other gulf cities come to Sabine to be laid down, refitted, or careened. Its slipways, ropewalks, sail-lofts, and timber yards are the largest concentration of shipwright's work anywhere on the gulf, and timber, cordage, and sailcloth flow out of the city alongside the salt-fish from its curing-houses.

Fishing and curing remain the old foundation — the northern banks have fed the gulf for generations — but it is the yards that give Sabine its standing among the gulf cities and its draw for the rest of them.

## Government & Imperial Presence

Sabine is administered much as [[Farhaven]] is: a civilian **Mayor** handles trade permits, taxation, and ordinary civil disputes, sitting beneath a permanent [[Valtorran Empire|Imperial]] garrison with a resident commander, to whom the Mayor defers on all matters of law and security. The garrison is not large, but it is a standing fixture of the city rather than a periodic show of force — Imperial authority in Sabine is light in numbers but visible, and present every day.

## Factions

### Mawbreakers

The [[Mawbreakers]] maintain an active chapter in Sabine, and their grip on the city runs as deep as it does in [[Camaar]] — woven into the docks, the yards, and daily working life rather than confined to a single chapter house. The difference is that in Sabine the guild does this under the garrison's eye rather than in its absence. The result is a city that runs on a quiet, ongoing accommodation between the guild and the Imperial commander: the boatyards keep building and maintaining fast, shallow hulls that no one asks too many questions about, the garrison keeps the kind of order that does not disturb the trade, and both sides understand exactly how far the other can be pushed. Sabine is the home port of Lysandra Starling, captain of the *Nightingale* and one of the guild's notable captains.
