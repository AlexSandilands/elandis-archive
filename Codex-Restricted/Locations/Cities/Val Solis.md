Val Solis is the one exception in a century of Imperial conquest — the single human city on [[Valtorra]] that the [[Valtorran Empire]] did not take. An island city of dark stone and cold air off the north-western coast of the continent, it was built by one of the six ancient Kingdoms of Old on a site so naturally defensible that when the Empire's fleets arrived, they could not pass the outer fortifications. The [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]] tried three times over fifteen years and abandoned the campaign. Val Solis closed its gates and docks and has not reopened them since. What passes within its walls in the present day is known to almost no one on the mainland — agents sent to learn do not return — and the city exists in the common imagination of Valtorra as a kind of legend: the place that said no and lived.

## Geography & Layout

Val Solis occupies a cold, mountainous island off the north-western coast of [[Valtorra]], separated from the mainland by a stretch of open and frequently rough water. The island is not large, but its terrain is extreme — mountain ridges run along its spine from north to south, with the city built in the one significant lowland on the island's western face: a shallow natural harbour backed by steep slopes rising directly into peaks cloud-covered for most of the year.

The island's coastline is cliff and rock. There is no beach, no second anchorage, no sheltered bay on any other shore. Any ship approaching Val Solis must come from the west, through open water, and face the [[Shield Wall]] — there is no other way in, and the terrain of the outer coast makes landing and approaching the city overland through the mountains impossible.

The city itself climbs the harbour slope in compressed tiers of dark stone — low-ceilinged buildings built for cold and wind, pressing up against the mountain rock behind them. The lowest tier is the sealed dockside; above it rise the residential and civic quarters, and at the highest margin the ancient Solis Hall, seat of the dynasty that has ruled the city since it was founded.

## History

Val Solis is one of the six human Kingdoms of Old, raised in the aftermath of the Chaos Wars on a site chosen primarily for its defensibility. Its founders were seafarers and shipwrights, and for many centuries the city was a significant maritime power — the [[Shield Wall]] was built to protect a working harbour, and its merchant and fishing fleets ranged widely across the open sea.

When the [[Valtorran Empire]] rose under [[Empress Morganna Eventide]], Val Solis watched the mainland cities fall one by one and concluded that an island city with a fortified harbour had options no mainland kingdom shared. When the Imperial fleet arrived, the Wall's gate was closed. Three expeditions shattered against it over the span of fifteen years. The Empire bled for an island it could not take, and eventually cut its losses and turned its attention elsewhere.

Val Solis's response was to cut itself off from the world entirely. Closing the docks and the harbour gate removed any leverage the Empire might apply through trade interdiction or infiltration. One hundred years later, the closure holds.

## The Sealed City

The harbour gate has not stood open in a century. Observers who have studied the Wall from outside with long glasses report lights and movement within the enclosed harbour — ships at anchor, figures on the quayside — but nothing that crosses the gate. No vessel has been seen departing. Agents sent to learn what passes within have not returned to report it.

The city is believed to still be inhabited. Whether the Solis dynasty yet holds the throne, what the population has become after a hundred years without outside contact, what Val Solis has made of itself in isolation — these are not known on the mainland.

## Government

Val Solis is the only human city on [[Valtorra]] that never became an Imperial governorship. The Solis dynasty held the throne when the gates closed; whether they hold it still, or what a hundred years of sealed succession has produced, is unknown outside the island.

## Culture & Faith

Before the closure, Val Solis was a seafaring and mercantile city. Its faith centred on [[Istishia]] — deity of seas and tides — and its festivals were organised around weather, the fishing seasons, and the rhythms of ocean life. What the city's faith and culture look like after a century of isolation is not known.

## Landmarks

### The Shield Wall

The [[Shield Wall]] is the defining fortification of [[Val Solis]] — a vast arc of ancient stone sweeping across the western approach to the island, its towers rising from the waterline into low cloud. Its age is uncertain; most accounts place its construction before the Kingdoms of Old, and some claim it predates the Chaos Wars. Three Imperial fleets were sent to force it; three were broken. The Empire has not sent a fourth.

What the Wall is like at close range, what armaments it carries, and how it is crewed and maintained are not matters that have been reliably reported from the outside. Its gate has not been seen open in a century. Nothing passes through.

[[Governor Tarlis Evandron]] named Val Solis in his survey of the Kingdoms of Old's possible alignment with a growing resistance. His assessment was brief: the city had been isolated for nearly a century and no agents sent in had ever returned. He offered no recommendation about how to approach it, because he had none.

## Trivia

- Of the six Kingdoms of Old, Val Solis is the only one that never became part of the [[Valtorran Empire]]. The other five kings and queens made their individual calculations and chose governorship; Val Solis made a different calculation and chose the sea wall. It is alone in that choice.
- The phrase "gone to Solis" entered common usage across [[Valtorra]] within a generation of the closure. Used by criminals, debtors, and people desperate to disappear, it means to reach a place from which one does not return and does not send word. Whether it refers to actual flight to Val Solis or simply to vanishing entirely, no one who uses it is entirely certain.
- The [[Shield Wall]]'s gate was originally designed to protect a working harbour from storm as well as attack — shelter was its founding purpose, and the same design that guarded against weather proved militarily impossible to penetrate.
