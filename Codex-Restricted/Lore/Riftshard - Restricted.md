> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Riftshard]]*

## The Truth: Plane-Leaks

- A Riftshard is not merely a "charged crack." Every shard is a plane-leak — a point where one specific plane bleeds into the Material, and magic worked nearby warps toward whichever plane is linked.
- The distinct "flavour" a person senses is that plane showing through. The public page describes the sensation (heat, dread, damp); the truth is that the sensation identifies the plane.
- Known links the party has touched:
  - Faewild — the goblin temple shard ([[Session 07 - Grakthor Falls]]); why their spells surged wild there.
  - Necromantic / Shadowfell — the [[Tumbler's Crag]] shard ([[Session 12 - How to Start a Rebellion]]).
  - Plane of Fire — the [[Frostway]] cavern shard ([[Session 34 - Mourning Bells]]), with a remorhaz egg incubating in it.
  - Plane of Water — the [[Isle of Solenne]] temple shard, the [[Darkwood]] Sunken Temple shard, and [[The Maw]].

## Matched Pairs

- [[The Maw]] and the [[Darkwood]] Sunken Temple shard are an inverse pair, both Plane-of-Water leaks. The Maw drains water out of the Material into the Plane of Water (the inward-spiralling whirlpool); the Darkwood shard gushes water the other way to feed the lake that hides the temple.
- A clean demonstration of shard behaviour if the party ever wants to study one somewhere reachable. (See [[The Maw]] and [[Darkwood]] restricted.)

## Forcing a Shard

- Shards leak; they are not doorways. Under ordinary conditions nothing passes bodily through.
- A shard can be forced, though. Someone who reaches a limb into the rift and casts magic to activate it risks being pulled bodily through to the linked plane. This is the seed of weaponising a shard as a deliberate plane-door.
- The [[Fungrals]] of the [[Bioluminescent Forest]] are the standing precedent — a people who came through a Faewild shard into the Material and stayed.

## Riftshards and the Conditioning

- Riftshards disrupt and degrade the Empress's mind-control conditioning. Proximity erodes it; a great enough shard shatters it outright.
- This is what unmade [[General Korvas]] into the blank-slate man who became [[Vaeleran]] — in the great Plane-of-Water shard beneath the [[Darkwood]] Sunken Temple. It also explains the mental confusion Vaeleran showed near the goblin-temple and Tumbler's Crag shards in the early sessions.
- The party does not know this. If they piece it together it threatens every conditioned Hand and Voice on the continent. (See [[General Korvas]] restricted, and the Hand / [[Valtorran Empire]] restricted entries.)

## The Second Shattering Spine

- Riftshards are the connective tissue of the Second Shattering plot. Every gemstone hoard, every dragon thread, [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow]]'s ambitions, and the [[Valtorran Empire]] as his funding engine all trace back to the same breach the shards point toward. (See [[The Shattering]] and [[Chaos Wars]] restricted.)
