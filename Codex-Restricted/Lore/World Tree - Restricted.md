> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[World Tree]]*

> The public page presents the World Tree as the one true god, withdrawn since the Sundering — accurate as far as the elves' reverence goes. The reserve holds its *place in the order of things*, why the Material Plane was made magic-less, and the cosmic-war lesson that underlies the whole divine restraint of the age.

## The Order of Things
The Tree is older than the gods and **stands above them**. Beneath it the powers of the cosmos fall into a hidden order:
- **The World Tree** at the summit — the one genuinely transcendent divinity, the source of the planes themselves. The elves calling it Mother are nearest the actual truth.
- Beneath it, the **True Gods** — real powers, but lesser than the Tree. These are the gods who, during [[The Shattering]], dared not strike [[Tiamat]] directly, for fear that open war among powers of that order might wound reality as the cosmic war once wounded the Tree.
- Beneath *them*, the **[[The Pantheon|Seven Divines]]** — the ascended mortal champions of the Shattering, lingering demigods the world mistakes for eternal gods.

The whole towering structure of the world's religion rests three tiers below the only being in it that was ever truly a god — and the elves, in their reverence for a tree, are closer to the divine than every temple of the Seven combined.

## The Magic-less Material Plane
One root mattered above all. The **Material Plane** — the mortal world — was grown from the World Tree's roots to be a realm of *ordered peace and logic*, the one plane in all creation deliberately made without magic. Every other plane is suffused with it; the Material Plane alone was set apart, raised to be a place of iron and reason and quiet. This is the secret that makes [[The Shattering]] the violation it was: when [[Tiamat]] poured raw chaotic magic into the mortal world, she was corrupting, in small and local form, the very design the Sundering had been fought to preserve.

## The Cosmic-War Lesson
The severing was not quiet: elven armies fought at the roots of the Tree alongside vast **blue-skinned, many-winged celestial beings**, a war waged across the realms at the foundation of the cosmos. What they fought, and what the corruption truly was, is lost — no account names the enemy. It is the same memory of cosmic war the True Gods would later refuse to repeat against [[Tiamat]]: proof, written into the deepest past, of what it costs when powers of that order go to war at the root of the world.

## The Binding Runs Both Ways
In sundering the Tree, [[Ryordan Silmara]] bound his bloodline to it irrevocably — a tie that runs both ways, the line bound to the Tree and the Tree's residual magic bound to the line. What such a bond might yet demand of the bloodline — for a tie that runs both ways is owed from both ends — no Silmara has ever been asked to learn.

## The Deepest Truth Held By No Archive
Nowhere is it written that the Tree stands *above* the gods, that the Material Plane was grown magic-less from its roots, or that the war which wounded it is the same lesson that stayed the True Gods' hand in [[The Shattering]]. The elven reverence for Mother is, unknowing, the nearest the world comes to that truth — and even the elves do not grasp how nearly right they are.

## DM Notes
- **Unrevealed truth:** the Tree stands *above* the gods — beneath it the **True Gods**, beneath them the **[[The Pantheon|Seven Divines]]** (ascended mortal champions of [[The Shattering]]). The **Material Plane was grown magic-less from the Tree's roots**, which is why Tiamat's chaos was such a violation. The party has the *edges* — Praetor's vision, the archivist's tale, [[High Priest Oldir]]'s "false idols" — not the whole shape.
- **Connections:** the Sundering is the older catastrophe explaining the gods' restraint in [[The Shattering]] — the cosmic war they refused to repeat. The Silmara binding ties the Tree to [[Ryordan Silmara]], [[Ryo]], the [[World Gates]], and the [[Vault of Memories]].
- **Plot hooks:** the binding "runs both ways" — what might the Tree, or its residual will, yet demand of a living Silmara? The withdrawn Tree may still be reachable. The corruption that wounded it was never named — leave the door open for it to return, or to have spread to other planes.
- **Open threads:** could the corruption still linger or return? Could the Tree die after all? Is the withdrawn Tree reachable — and by what? Did a similar sundering happen on *other* planes? Where, exactly, is the Tree now? Leave its present state deliberately undefined — a lever for cosmic-scale plot, not a settled fact.
