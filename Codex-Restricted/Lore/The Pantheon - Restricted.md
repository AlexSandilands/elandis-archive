> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[The Pantheon]]*

> The public page allows that the Seven were *once mortal heroes raised up* — a deep-history footnote most of the faithful set aside. The reserve is the full order of the divine: that they are ascended mortals standing in for the true and ancient **Gods of Old**, whom the world does not know exist.

## The Full Order of Things
The Seven Divines are **not gods at all**, or not as the world believes. They are the seven mortal champions who ended the [[Chaos Wars]], raised in their sacrifice to a lingering demigodhood — real powers that answer real prayers, but neither eternal nor first. Behind them, unworshipped and all but forgotten, stand the true and ancient **Gods of Old**.

The structure runs:
- the **[[World Tree]]** at the summit — the one genuinely transcendent god;
- beneath it, the **Gods of Old** (the True Gods) — real and ancient and hidden;
- beneath *them*, the **Seven Divines** — the ascended champions the world actually prays to.

The whole religion rests two tiers below the powers that made it, and no temple of the Seven knows it.

## How the Seven Came To Be
In the age before the [[The Shattering|Shattering]], the Gods of Old held their spheres openly. When [[Tiamat|the Dragon Queen]] tore the world open and the [[Chaos Wars]] burned for five centuries, those gods could not strike at her directly, for fear of the cosmic war that had once wounded the World Tree itself. So they chose champions: seven mortals, each raised with a measure of divine power greater than any follower had been granted, who at the war's end joined their borrowed strength into the working that sealed the breach and cast the dragons down. The Seven were consumed in that working but not ended — their spirits linger, unable to hold material form for long yet no longer mortal: they have *genuinely become demigods*, able to pass a thread of power to those who pray. The faithful never know they were once mortal heroes, nor that the Gods of Old who made them are real. As [[High Priest Oldir]] put it: the Seven are "false idols masking the existence of the True Gods."

## The Champion Pairings
Each of the Seven was, in life, the chosen champion of one God of Old, empowered to carry that sphere into the war. When the Seven ascended and the old gods withdrew, the world kept the champion and forgot the god:

- **[[Seraphina Dawnbringer]]** (Sun) — champion of **[[Lathander]]**, the Morninglord.
- **[[Elaris Moonsong]]** (Moon) — champion of **[[Selûne]]**, the Moonmaiden — the clearest-documented pairing, and the one the party has brushed against.
- **[[Torgar Earthshaker]]** (Earth) — champion of **[[Grumbar]]**, the Earthlord.
- **[[Azara Flametongue]]** (Fire) — champion of **[[Kossuth]]**, the Lord of Flames.
- **[[Gale Winddancer]]** (Air) — champion of **[[Akadi - Restricted]]**, the Queen of Air.
- **[[Maris Wavecaller]]** (Water) — champion of **[[Istishia]]**, the Water Lord.
- **[[Eldrin Starweaver]]** (Arcane) — champion of **[[Mystra]]**, the Lady of Mysteries.

## Who Knows
The knowledge is held by almost no one. [[High Priest Oldir]] carries it as a guarded **hereditary heresy** passed down his line; a scattering of the deepest scholars and longest-lived elves hold fragments. To teach it openly would be, in Oldir's view, too dangerous for the world to bear. The party has only the edges — Oldir's "false idols" (S20), and what they've since learned of [[Selûne]] through [[Kai]].

Every named relic of the faith (Dawnstar, Moon's Tear, Grumbar's Fist, etc.) was a *mortal champion's* weapon before it was a holy artifact — the gear of heroes, sanctified by a worship that forgot they were ever wielded by hands. The "seven" the faith reveres is only the count of the war's champions, not of the gods.

## DM Notes
- **Unrevealed truth:** the Seven are ascended mortal champions of the Shattering/[[Chaos Wars]] — lingering demigods, not eternal gods — empowered by the Gods of Old and standing in for them. Keep consistent with restricted [[World Tree]], [[The Shattering]], [[Selûne]], [[Shar]] (World Tree ▸ Gods of Old ▸ Seven Divines).
- **Connections:** this is the spine the campaign's divine threads hang from — [[Selûne]] and [[Shar]] (Gods of Old now stirring), [[Kai]]'s arc as a Chosen, the question of whether the *other* Gods of Old wake as a second Shattering nears.
- **Plot hooks:** the six elder gods behind the other Divines are undeveloped and waiting — any could stir as Selûne has. The relics-were-heroes'-gear angle is a quiet thread: the Seven's artifacts are scattered, and recovering one means handling a dead champion's weapon. What the Seven know, want, or fear — and whether they can manifest more than a thread of power — is open.
- **Open threads:** how many Gods of Old are there? Do any still hold open worship somewhere remote? Are the Seven aware of what they truly are? Could the heresy, if it spread, break the faith — and who would want it to?
