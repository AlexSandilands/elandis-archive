Grumbar, the Earthlord, is the god of elemental earth — of stone, soil, permanence, and the binding weight of an oath sworn. He is one of the Gods of Old, the ancient powers who held the world before the [[The Shattering|Shattering]], and is known to the present age only through scattered scholarly fragments.

## Overview

Grumbar governs earth in all its forms: the mountain that does not move, the bedrock beneath the sea, the slow and certain endurance of stone. In the oldest theology he stands among the four elemental lords, paired with [[Akadi - Restricted|Akadi]] (air), [[Istishia]] (water), and [[Kossuth]] (fire) — though the names of the elemental powers mean little to common folk in Elandis today. He is invoked, where invoked at all, as the keeper of oaths, for an oath sworn by the earth is held to be as fixed as the earth itself.

Like the other Gods of Old, Grumbar withdrew from the mortal world after the [[The Shattering|Shattering]]. His one recorded act of that age was the choosing and empowering of his mortal champion, [[Torgar Earthshaker]], during the [[Chaos Wars]].

## In Memory & Belief

Grumbar is not worshipped. The steadfastness and strength of the earth that the world still honours it attaches to [[Torgar Earthshaker]], not to the god who stood behind him. Grumbar himself survives only in old miners' and masons' sayings and the marginalia of pre-Shattering texts — spoken, for the most part, by those who do not know they invoke a god at all.
