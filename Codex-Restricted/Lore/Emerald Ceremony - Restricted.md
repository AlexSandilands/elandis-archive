> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Emerald Ceremony]]*

## The True Mandate
- "Watch, wait, and help" is the version Sumara tells itself. The volunteers' deeper charge runs past observation: they are trying to uncover the *greater threat* behind the Empire — specifically, whether [[Elandis]] stands on the verge of a second [[The Shattering|Shattering]]. The Empire is the surface; the planar danger beneath it is what the network truly hunts.
- This is why the Council could be moved to break a century of silence for it. The fear in the room was never just an empire — it was the possibility that the wound the Seven died to seal is being torn open again.

## The Network — Tradecraft
- The hundred were seeded across Valtorra as deep-cover agents, disguised and living as ordinary folk. They know *of* one another's existence, but deliberately do **not** know where any of the others are stationed — compartmentalisation, so that a captured agent cannot betray the rest.
- Consequently no single agent — not even [[Ayana Syndrosa]] — knows the full roster or the current stations of those still embedded. An unknown number remain alive and in place across the Empire to this day, their identities hidden even from each other.
- Fates have diverged over the decades: some were caught and killed by the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]]; some have gone dark; others remain patiently in cover, gathering intelligence and aiding where they can.

## Felix
- [[Felix]] is one of the hundred. He came through the Ceremony alongside Ayana and anchored in [[Val Miriel]], establishing [[The Bridge of Stars]] as both a legitimate business and an agent's cover.
- Unrevealed to the party: that Felix is an elf, that he is magically disguised, or that he has any connection to Ayana or the Emerald Ceremony. He stays off the public Ceremony page for this reason.

## Ryo and the Overturned Premise
- The Ceremony's one-way nature rested entirely on the belief that the [[Silmara Family|Silmara]] line was extinct — no Silmara, no [[World Gates]], no safe road home.
- Ryo's existence quietly demolishes that premise. The Gates work again; the elves are no longer sealed away by necessity. The implications run well past Ayana's homecoming — every remaining agent now has, in principle, a way back, and Sumara has, in principle, a way *out* of its century of isolation. Whether the Council wants that is a separate and live question (see the Confluence debate).
