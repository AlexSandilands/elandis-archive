> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Shar]]*

> The public page presents Shar as the faintest of the forgotten Gods of Old — barely a memory, only a borrowed name on the lesser moon. The truth is that she is **alive, feeding, and lately moving** — the dark sister and eternal opposite of [[Selûne]].

## The Cosmic Truth
Shar is one of the **Gods of Old**: the true gods of the cosmos, older than the [[The Shattering|Shattering]], lesser only than the [[World Tree]] and greater by far than the [[The Pantheon|Seven Divines]] the world mistakes for gods (tier: World Tree ▸ Gods of Old ▸ Seven Divines). She is [[Selûne]]'s twin and her perfect inverse — where the Moonmaiden is light, mercy, and forgiveness, Shar is the dark, the wound that will not heal, and the cold pleasure taken in another's pain. Her portfolio is **suffering, vengeance, and chaos** — and beneath them the older, colder things: loss, secrets, and the primordial dark from which the light was a wound.

## How She Feeds
What makes her dangerous is not an army or a church but her nature: she draws power not from prayer or sacrifice but from **needless anguish**, at every scale. A massacre feeds her; so does a betrayal, a casual humiliation, a mean joke, a pointless little cruelty done for pleasure. Every spiteful act in the world is, in effect, an offering she never had to ask for. She needs no worshippers and never has — only a world that goes on hurting itself. If she has servants at all, most do not know they serve her. Her worship, if it was ever open at all, may never have been: a goddess who feeds on cruelty has no need to be loved, only indulged.

Her **ascendant nights** are the inverse of her sister's Unshadowed: when the lesser moon **Sha** rides full while **Sel** is dark and gone — four times a year, on the first of each elemental season — she is strongest and Selûne weakest. No rite marks them, but her reach is longest then.

## Allegiance: None
Shar serves **no one** — not the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]], not [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow|Kharazoth]], not the Dragon Queen. Where the other old gods withdrew out of restraint, Shar's withdrawal is patience, not principle. A second [[The Shattering|Shattering]] would glut her on suffering beyond measure, so their works please her — but the alignment is incidental, and she would as gladly see *them* suffer. Treat her as a wildcard who may aid or sabotage any side if it sows more suffering; her only loyalty is to chaos and her own appetite.

## The Two Sisters & The Contest Over Kai
Shar and [[Selûne]] are the oldest of opposites, bound to the moon and to each other since before the Shattering — the light and dark of the same sphere, mercy against malice. What is new is that she has stirred to *act*, and her chosen field is the same one her sister has moved onto: the young man [[Kai]], whom Selûne would make her champion of the light and whom Shar means to turn. Where the bright sister offers the path of light, Shar offers the slow seduction of the dark — to break him, or better, to *keep* him, and make of the moon's champion a thing of vengeance instead. The **black stars** in Kai's Starry Form are her work.

## DM Notes
- **Unrevealed truth:** Shar is a living God of Old / True God — [[Selûne]]'s dark twin, who feeds directly on cruelty and needs no worshippers. The world knows none of this; even the party has only Oldir's fragment ("the Lady of Vengeance"). Keep tier consistent with restricted [[World Tree]], [[The Shattering]], [[Selûne]].
- **Why now:** her stirring is the shadow of Selûne's — as the bright sister raises a Chosen against a feared second Shattering, Shar moves to corrupt that same Chosen ([[Kai]]) into a dark champion.
- **Plot hooks:** the tug-of-war over Kai; a moment of mass suffering (massacre, great betrayal) as a power-spike; the possibility she raises an unwitting agent or true dark cult as the Shattering nears; her ascendant nights as windows when her reach is longest.
- **Open threads:** does she want Kai *turned* into her champion, or simply *broken* to deny him to Selûne? Is anyone already serving her unknowingly through ordinary cruelty? What would a dark champion of hers become?
- **FR source to mine lightly:** Shar — darkness, loss, secrets, forgotten things, the offer of oblivion to the grieving, the eternal twin-struggle with Selûne.
