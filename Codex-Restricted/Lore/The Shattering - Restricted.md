> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[The Shattering]]*

> The public page tells the Shattering as remembered history and folk legend. The reserve holds what the world does not know: *why* the world was magic-less, *why* the gods held back, *what* the Seven really are, and that the Dragon Queen's work was never finished.

## The Material Plane Was Made Magic-less
The mortal world was not magic-less by accident — it was *grown* that way. Of all the planes, the Material Plane alone was formed from the roots of the [[World Tree]] to be a realm of ordered peace and logic, deliberately set apart from the magic that suffuses every other plane. The Shattering's true horror is that [[Tiamat]] poured raw chaos into a world engineered to be without it — corrupting, in small and local form, the very design the Sundering had been fought to preserve. (See restricted [[World Tree]].)

## Why The Gods Did Not Stop Tiamat Themselves
The True Gods could not move directly against the Dragon Queen: open conflict between powers of that order risks a cosmic war — the very kind that, in the unimaginable past, had wounded the World Tree itself and led to its Sundering. Having seen once what gods at war can cost the fabric of reality, they would not pay that price a second time. So they did not fight Tiamat. They chose champions, and made of them something the world had never seen.

## What The Seven Really Are
Forbidden to strike Tiamat themselves, the gods **imbued seven mortal champions with a measure of divine power greater than any follower had ever been granted** — raising them, for a time, to something near demigods (the "seven archmages" of folk account). At the breach they joined that borrowed power into a single ritual, cast the Dragon Queen back into her own realm, and sealed the tear; the release killed near nine in ten of every living thing for a vast span around the breach.

The Seven were consumed in their own working — but not, quite, ended. Their spirits linger: unable to hold material form for long, no longer truly mortal, *genuinely become demigods* able to pass a thin thread of power to those who pray. This is the secret root of the world's religion. The **[[The Pantheon|Seven Divines]]** are these ascended champions — real powers answering real prayers, but *not* the True Gods who made them, only standing in for them in the minds of the faithful. As [[High Priest Oldir|a priest]] put it: "false idols masking the existence of the True Gods" — though the falsehood is subtler: the idols are real, only mistaken for something greater than they are.

## The Seal Was Imperfect — Two Living Flaws
**The Riftshards are plane-leaks.** The wound did not close cleanly: scattered across the world remain the [[Riftshard]]s, fractures where the sealing failed. Each is a *point where another plane bleeds into the Material*, and magic worked nearby warps toward whichever plane is linked. (The Riftshard beneath a certain goblin temple was a Material-to-Faewild link — why the party's spells surged wild there.) Every Riftshard points, like a compass, back toward the original breach.

**The dragons were not all killed.** A red, a green, and a black dragon at least survived the sealing and fled into the broken world — and rather than abandon their Queen's design, took it up in secret, resuming the slow gathering of wealth and gemstones for the ritual that would tear the breach open again. Across two thousand years they have insinuated themselves into mortal society, ruling by proxy. Foremost is **[[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow]]**, the *Architect of the Second Shattering* — the true power behind the [[Valtorran Empire]], working through the lich-empress [[Empress Morganna Eventide]] to amass over a century the wealth a second breach demands. The Shattering is not finished history: it is a clock still running.

## What The World Does Not Know
The world venerates the Seven Divines as eternal gods and never suspects they were mortal heroes ascended in death; does not know the True Gods exist behind them, nor why those gods held back; does not know the Material Plane was *made* magic-less and that the Shattering was a violation of its very nature; and does not know that three of Tiamat's dragons still walk the world, finishing her work. The legend is not a lie so much as a mercy — the shape of a truth too large and too dangerous for the world to carry.

## Restricted Campaign Notes
- **S47 (Confluence of the Seven):** the party carried to the Confluence the evidence of a *second Shattering in the making* — [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow|Kharazoth]], the Red Priests, the dragon's century-long design — forcing the elves to weigh whether isolation could shield them.
- **S49 (The Silver Sage):** through a memory torn from [[Praetor'Varanous|Praetor]]'s mind, the party witnessed the Shattering's final battle — two titans above a battlefield of hundreds of thousands, a city-sized rift bleeding red light, and [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow|Kharazoth]] driving a claw of black energy into the silver dragon's chest. Proof the war was real and one of its monsters endures.

## DM Notes
- **Unrevealed truth:** the Material Plane was grown magic-less from the [[World Tree]]'s roots; the True Gods exist *behind* the worshipped Seven and stayed their hand for fear of another Sundering; the Seven Divines are the ascended champions, lingering demigods. The party has the *edges* (Oldir's "false idols," Praetor's vision) but not the whole.
- **Connections:** the spine tying the campaign together — [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow|Kharazoth]] and the Second Shattering, the [[Valtorran Empire]] as his funding engine, the [[Riftshard]]s, [[Praetor'Varanous]] as living eyewitness, the [[World Tree]] / Sundering as the older catastrophe explaining the gods' restraint.
- **Plot hooks:** the Second Shattering is the slow apocalypse the party is racing — every gemstone hoard, every Riftshard, every dragon thread leads back here. A Riftshard could be weaponised as a deliberate plane-door. The lingering Seven could manifest briefly to a chosen at need.
- **Open threads:** who or what are the True Gods, and will they ever act? How close is Kharazoth to the wealth a second breach needs? Do the green and black survivors have their own designs, or serve Kharazoth? Could the original breach site be found — and what remains there?
