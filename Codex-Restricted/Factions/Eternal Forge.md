The Eternal Forge is a dwarven resistance movement spread through the holds of the [[Silver Mountains]], named in deliberate defiance for the sacred furnace at the heart of [[Glimmerforge]] that the occupation has turned to Imperial ends. Where the old kingdom's rebellion was broken at the gates of the gem-city a generation ago, the Eternal Forge has risen to inherit its cause — to drive the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]] from the mountain and restore the dwarven crown.

## Purpose & Goals

The Eternal Forge exists to undo the conquest of the [[Silver Mountains]]. Its members reject the legitimacy of the Ummal Regency, hold the regents to be traitors who sold the mountain for riches, and work toward the day the dwarves govern themselves again. In the near term that means keeping resistance alive where the last uprising failed — sheltering the hunted, moving people and word between the holds, and chipping at the machinery of occupation: the gem conscription that empties dwarven households of their young, the shipments that carry the mountain's wealth east, and the enforcers who hold the districts down.

The name is a creed in itself. The furnace the Empire now feeds for its own weapons is the same fire at which dwarves have sworn binding oaths since before the city's records began — and the movement claims it back in spirit, casting the struggle as a sacred duty rather than mere rebellion.

## Structure & Leadership

The Eternal Forge operates as a decentralised web of cells rather than a single body, a structure forced on it by the [[Cindercloaks]]' relentless hunting. No cell knows the full shape of the others; word and people pass through trusted intermediaries so that a broken cell cannot unravel the whole. Leadership is held in deliberate secrecy — even sympathisers rarely know who truly directs the movement — and orders propagate through the network without the rank and file ever seeing the hand that gives them.

## Symbols & Iconography

The movement is marked by an anvil crowned with a single everburning flame, cradled by two looping arms that interlock into an eternal knot — the undying fire of the Silver Mountains rendered as a sign of the cause that will not go out. The device is worked in iron and tarnished bronze, passed quietly among those who carry it; to display it openly in [[Glimmerforge]] is to invite the [[Cindercloaks]].

## History

![[Eternal_Forge_2_small.webp|left|180]]

The Eternal Forge grew out of the wreckage of the dwarven rebellion that fell at Glimmerforge's gates roughly fifty years ago. That uprising had come close — the rebels had taken [[Gemhold]] and were marching through the mountain tunnels — before it was undone and the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]] installed the Ummal Regency over the broken kingdom. In the decades since, as the occupation hardened into gem conscription and Cindercloak patrols, a new resistance gathered in the city's underbelly and across the holds, taking up the old cause under a new name. It remains the inheritor of that lost rebellion, and the chief reason the Regency still fears its own city.

## Relations

### Cindercloaks

The [[Cindercloaks]] are the Eternal Forge's deadliest enemy — the Ummal Regency's enforcement corps and secret police, raised expressly to ensure the broken rebellion could never reform. Hunting the Forge's cells and breaking its sympathisers is the corps' central preoccupation, and the struggle between the two defines daily life in occupied [[Glimmerforge]].

### Valtorran Empire

The [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]] is the occupying power the movement exists to expel. It conquered the [[Silver Mountains]], sanctions the Regency that rules in its name, and draws the mountain's gem-wealth eastward — every grievance that feeds the resistance traces back to it.

## Trivia

- The movement takes its name from the Eternal Forge itself, the furnace at the heart of [[Glimmerforge]] that has burned without interruption since before the city's founding — a piece of defiance turned into a banner, claiming the occupation's most sacred prize as the symbol of its undoing.
