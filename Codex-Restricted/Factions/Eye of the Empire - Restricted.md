> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Eye of the Empire]]*

## Structure & Leadership

The Eye is organised into distributed cells, each isolated from the others by design. An operative in one city will rarely know the identity or location of another operative working nearby — a deliberate measure to contain the damage if any single agent is compromised or captured. Communication between operatives and their handlers passes through blood-paper notes: sealed parchments that activate when bloodied and connect to a designated recipient elsewhere in the network.

At the network's apex is a figure who operates from within the Citadel of [[Val Luminor]], in close proximity to [[Empress Morganna Eventide]]. This individual's identity is not known outside the innermost circle of Imperial power. The chain of communication runs upward through cell handlers to this central authority, and from there to the Empress alone.

## Methods

All operatives share a common foundation: stealth, disguise, and the ability to kill without leaving evidence. Beyond this, the Eyes are not a uniform instrument — different operatives are assigned to different kinds of work, selected according to their strengths.

Field agents handle surveillance and the gathering of intelligence, moving through the Empire and beyond its borders to observe and report. Assassins are deployed when a threat must be removed with neither trial nor confrontation. The most patient and most dangerous are the political infiltrators: operatives embedded for years in noble courts and civic institutions, living entirely invented lives, steering factions and engineering the replacement of leaders whose loyalty has begun to waver.

## Recruitment

The Eye draws from two sources. The primary pipeline begins in childhood — promising children are taken from loyalist Imperial families and trained over years for a life in the Empress's service, entering the network long before they could choose otherwise. The second path leads from the [[Hand of the Empire]]: soldiers who demonstrate more aptitude for subtlety and intrigue than for open combat may be identified and quietly transferred into the Eye's ranks.

## The Eye in Val Miriel

An unnamed operative is embedded close to [[Governor Tarlis Evandron]] in [[Val Miriel]]. Morganna does not fully trust him, and this Eye is almost certainly tasked with monitoring his loyalties and reporting any sign of defection. Identity and specifics TBD — to be developed when the party returns to Val Miriel or the plot requires it.

## Knowledge Hierarchy

Within the Empire's three-arm structure, the Eye's existence is deliberately tiered: the rank and file of both the [[Hand of the Empire]] and the [[Voices of the Empire]] are not told the Eyes exist. Only higher-ranking members of both arms are aware of them. This means most Hands and Voices encountered by the party will have no knowledge of the Eye at all — and cannot be pressured or coerced into revealing information they do not have.
