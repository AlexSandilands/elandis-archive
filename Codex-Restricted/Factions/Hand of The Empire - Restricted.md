## Selection & Conditioning

Hands are not born to the role and do not advance to it through seniority alone. Promising officers and soldiers from across the Imperial provinces are identified and brought to [[Val Luminor]] for a period of rigorous training at the [[Golden Downs Palaestra]], a dedicated facility within the city. Candidates are drilled, tested, and assessed over an extended period; many wash out and are returned to the regular legions. Those who pass are eligible for graduation.

Graduation involves a private audience with the Empress herself, conducted alone in the Luminor Citadel. The exact nature of what occurs in that chamber is not disclosed — not to candidates beforehand, not to the Empire at large, and apparently not to the Hands themselves in any meaningful way afterward. What emerges is a soldier whose loyalty to the Empress and the Empire is no longer a quality they choose and maintain but something structural, operating below the threshold of doubt or deliberation. Former peers describe the change as a closing: the officer they knew is still present in technical ability and military bearing, but something warmer — the hesitation, the personal stakes, the capacity for judgment that takes the human cost into account — is gone. [[General Korvas]] is the most thoroughly documented case; those who served under him before his selection describe a garrison commander who led without fear and governed without contempt. The man who emerged from graduation was capable and cold.

The process is presented to candidates and to the Empire at large as a profound mark of distinction. Soldiers seek selection; families consider it an honour. No one who has undergone the graduation has ever spoken against it.

## The Graduation Ceremony

What occurs in the private audience with the [[Empress Morganna Eventide|Empress]] is not disclosed and is not understood by the Hands themselves in any meaningful sense. In practice: the candidate kneels alone before her in the Luminor Citadel. The violet orb — her phylactery — performs two functions simultaneously. It bends the candidate's will to the Empress's, structurally and permanently: from this moment their loyalty to her and the Empire operates below the threshold of deliberate choice. It also draws a measure of life force from the candidate during the ceremony — energy that feeds into the same chain of stolen vitality that sustains the Empress's youth. The candidate leaves feeling tired, honoured, and wholly loyal. They understand neither the theft nor the mechanism.

The ceremony is framed as an investiture — a solemn honour confirming elevation to the order. The framing works because no one who has undergone it has any motivation to question what happened, and no one outside it has any way to know.

## Eyes of the Empire

Rank-and-file Hands are never told that the [[Eye of the Empire|Eyes of the Empire]] exist. Only those operating at the Dread General tier are made aware. This means Eyes can and do infiltrate Hand operations without detection: [[Seraphina]] passed among a Hand escort for the entire voyage from [[Val Noren]] to [[Skyreach]], undetected, because none of the Hands present knew to look for her.

## Riftshards and the Conditioning

The Empress's mind-control magic is not impervious. [[Riftshards]] disrupt and degrade it — a vulnerability the Empire does not know exists and the Empress has not fully mapped. Proximity to Riftshards was behind the mental confusion [[General Korvas|Vaeleran]] showed in the goblin temple in the early sessions and in the cave with [[Salla the Brave]]. The full fracture of Korvas's conditioning occurred in the Darkwood, where a very large Riftshard in a sunken lake temple shattered both his loyalty and his pre-fracture memories simultaneously. If the party discovers this, it has implications for every conditioned Hand and Voice on the continent. Full details in [[General Korvas - Restricted]].
