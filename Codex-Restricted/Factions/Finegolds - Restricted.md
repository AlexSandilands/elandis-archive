> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Finegolds]]*

## The Caravan's True Nature
- The Finegolds are not merely an allied merchant house that helps the resistance. The caravan *is* the [[Order of Ravens|Grand Raven]]'s own network — its private smuggling route and information line, hidden inside a family trade. (Full picture: [[Order of Ravens - Restricted]].)
- [[Gemma Finegold|Gemma]] is the Grand Raven of the [[Order of Ravens]] — and currently the only one alive in the role, elevated by her predecessor [[Ebon]] shortly before the campaign began. The public entry frames her only as the caravan-master and a resistance courier; her true office is the deepest secret on the page.
- She issues the Order's commands hidden inside her own trade. A Raven receives a trade letter or a passing word from a travelling merchant and never suspects the merchant *is* the Grand Raven, giving the order to their face. To the wider Order, the Finegolds look like a useful allied caravan; in truth it is the Grand Raven hiding in plain sight inside her own family business.

## Who Knows
- No one knows who Gemma truly is — not the crew, not [[Barak Stormrider|Barak]], not the Nest Fathers. No Raven has ever seen her face unobscured.
- The crew know they do rebellion work — you cannot smuggle the [[Skyreach]] package without your scout and your smith noticing — but none of them know Gemma is the Grand Raven. To [[Lilliana Featherfoot|Lilliana]], [[Doran Ironfist|Doran]] and the rest, she is simply the late master's clever daughter who took over the company.
- [[Harland Finegold|Harland]] never knew either. He thought he was grooming an heir into the family trade; he died without ever learning his daughter was running a war from his wagons.

## The Bloody Nails
- The party have worked for Gemma — through her, by her design — from their very first job. The Skyreach mission was her opening move as Grand Raven, and folding the escaped prisoners into the caravan as guards was her own initiative. They have never known whose orders they were following.
