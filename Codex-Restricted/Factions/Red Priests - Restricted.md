> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Red Priests]]*

## True Allegiance
- The Red Priests are acolytes of [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow|Kharazoth]], the ancient red dragon — that is what their name means and where it comes from. Their devotion is to him, not to the Empress. The Empire is merely the vehicle they serve through; the dragon is the object of their faith.
- The serpentine, fire-breathing dragon scarred onto every priest's back is Kharazoth's sigil — a mark of devotion worn in the flesh.
- Their home, the temple carved beneath [[Valfyria]], is Kharazoth's seat of power on [[Valtorra]] — the nerve centre through which he works the continent at a remove.

## Structure & Leadership
- They answer to Kharazoth directly and individually. There is no high priest, grand master, or head of the order standing between them and the dragon. (This is why the public entry lists the leader as concealed — there is no visible internal hierarchy to point to.)
- They move and work in small covens — five operated together at the fall of [[Val Noren]]. Both men and women serve.
- They operate wholly apart from the Imperial command structure: a recent, autonomous addition the army's own officers regard as outsiders — useful, dangerous, and not theirs to command.

## Recruitment
- Priests are drawn to Kharazoth one at a time, by different means. Some were seduced by him appearing as a powerful and attractive man; others were taken in when they were at their lowest and he provided for them.
- This grooming-into-instrument pattern deliberately mirrors how Kharazoth cultivated [[Empress Morganna Eventide|the Empress]] herself — the same patient predator, the same method, applied across the order.

## The Queen of Dragons
- A faction within the order are zealous true believers who have dreamed of the coming of the Queen of Dragons — an entity their doctrine names the Breaker of Realms and the Mother of Magic.
- Open thread: how this prophesied figure relates to Kharazoth's own endgame — the second Shattering — is undecided. Develop before it becomes plot-relevant. (No codex entry for the Queen of Dragons exists yet.)

## Magical Specialities
- Each priest is trained in one of two disciplines, which determines how they fight if the party faces them:
  - Death — necromancy: animating undead, scythe-work, the psychic Spirit Wail seen at the Stoneheart.
  - Elemental — elemental magic.
- This is a mechanical distinction more than an organisational one; both kinds are equally Kharazoth's.
