> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Dread's Hunger]]*

## True Nature
- The blade is genuinely cursed. The public entry deliberately stops at "evil and dangerous" — the players only suspect a curse; they have not confirmed it. True rarity is Artifact-tier.
- An old, nameless evil entity is bound within the sword. It is the source of the corruption. It is never identified — keep it a sealed mystery.

## The Curse
- Carrying or wielding the blade slowly eats the bearer's soul. Over time it plants urges to kill living things, making the bearer steadily more ruthless, until they "hunger" for the death of the living and begin raising the slain as undead — culminating in the bearer's own transformation into undeath (a Death Knight or similar).
- When wielded by an undead creature, its powers cost 0 hit dice — the curse rewards what it is trying to turn the bearer into. (Mechanics from S40 prep: spend up to 3 hit dice on a hit for extra Xd6 necrotic; spend 4 hit dice on a hit to force a DC 15 Wis save vs. frightened within 20 ft; spend 4 hit dice after killing a humanoid to raise it as a zombie for 1 hour.)

## True Origin
- Forged by [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow]] himself during the [[Chaos Wars]], as an attempt to corrupt champions of the Material realm. Keep the how vague — the point is the purpose and the era.
- [[Empress Morganna Eventide]] had Kharazoth forge it for [[Dread General Gerard Blackmarsh|Blackmarsh]] at the moment she raised him into undeath. That Kharazoth personally forged it is unrevealed to the party (see [[Empress Morganna Eventide]] restricted).
- Fits the broader pattern of Kharazoth's ambitions and the necrotic plane-leak corruption tied to the [[Chaos Wars]] / [[The Shattering]] breach (see [[Riftshard]]).

## Plot Hooks
- Open thread — what the blade wants from [[Ryo]]: unresolved. It killed his parents and now sits in his keeping. Whether it recognises Silmara blood, or is drawn to him specifically, is deliberately left open.
- The longer Ryo refuses to part with it, the more the question of whether that refusal is entirely his own becomes live. Left to the player, but the curse is patient.
