> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Moonlit Aegis]]*

## True Nature — a Growing Artifact
- The Moonlit Aegis is an artifact ("Selûne's Chosen"), not a static magic item. It grows in power across the campaign.
- Its face bears three sockets; only one is currently filled, with the moonstone [[Kai]] had long carried (looted early on at the Goblin temple, later revealed as one of the three blessed Stones of the Moon), set into the shield by [[Alyn the First]] as part of the consecration ritual. As further blessed moonstones are recovered and restored to it, the shield wakes new and greater powers.
- With one stone seated it grants +2 AC. This is the first rung of what it will become — keep the ceiling open. The public entry deliberately omits the rarity, the sockets, and the growth so none of this is telegraphed.

## Current Mechanics (one moonstone seated)
- Requires attunement. AC +2.
- Lunar Flare — Reaction, once per short rest. When a creature you can see targets the bearer with a melee attack, flash moonlight from the shield; the creature makes a DC 15 Constitution save or is blinded until the start of its next turn. On a success it averts its gaze and the attack proceeds normally. (Awakened by the first restored moonstone.)
- Moon Sense — Action, once per day. The bearer focuses on the shield and makes a DC 15 Wisdom save. On a success, they feel an instinctive pull toward the nearest blessed moonstone — direction only, no distance or precise location, like a tug of lunar intuition. On a failure the shield stays silent until next dawn. Works only on the material plane.

## The Moonstone Hunt
- The shield's moonstone is one of three blessed moonstones. In Session 49, Praetor confirmed two are known — this shield's, and one held by High Priest [[High Priest Oldir|Oldir]] at the High Sept of [[Val Miriel]] — and that "the shield will point the way" to the third. Moon Sense is literally that compass.
- The third blessed moonstone lies in the necromancer's keep — the ancient ruins at the heart of [[Borealia Forest]], now occupied by the new necromancer. Recovering it is bound up with that whole plot (the undead escalation, and the vampire lord of [[Val Solis]] who once patronised the original necromancer).
- More than three moonstones may exist. Praetor would not confirm the number, warning only that their "falling into the hands of the wrong people would be very diabolical."

## Origin Metal — Fully Cleansed
- Though forged from [[Dread General Gerard Blackmarsh]]'s cursed armour, the shield carries no lingering taint, vulnerability, or corruption. The redemption was complete — the darkness was drawn out, not merely contained. Treat it as wholly holy.
