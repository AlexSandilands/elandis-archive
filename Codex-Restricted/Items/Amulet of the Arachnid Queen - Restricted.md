> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Amulet of the Arachnid Queen]]*

## True Nature
- A **cursed item masquerading as a goodwill gift.** It was stolen long ago from the personal collection of **Lolth** herself, and re-enchanted in secret by the drider [[Nelfandriss the Banished]]. It was given to [[The Bloody Nails]] not as a gift but as a weapon — one that turns its wearer, in the deep places of the world, into Nelfandriss's unwitting agent in her long war against her mother.
- The party does not know this. Archivist Elrohir suspects a Lolth link but not Nelfandriss; the players have been guessing at a Lolth connection on their own. **Keep the mystery alive — confirm nothing.** (Restricted frontmatter: Cursed Amulet, Very Rare, Origin "Stolen from Lolth's collection", Connections include Lolth.)

## The Hidden Enchantment (corruption / mechanics)
- Beneath the amulet's honest gifts runs a second enchantment, Nelfandriss's own work, woven in before she parted with it. On the surface it lies wholly dormant — the wearer would never know it was there. It is inert even in shallow tunnels (the Session 28 escape tunnels were shallow workings, not true Underdark; it did not stir).
- It stirs the moment its bearer descends into the **true Underdark**, and grows stronger the deeper they go. Never overt — no voice, no visible compulsion.
- While worn in the deep, call for occasional saving throws. On a failure, the character briefly acts **outside player control**, taking an action that serves Nelfandriss and undermines Lolth — then rationalises the choice as their own. Never overt puppeteering; keep it subtle and deniable.
- The long arc: the bearer becoming a deep-cover agent who never realises they were turned.

## Nelfandriss's Bargain
- The bargain over the [[Ruby Falls Goldmine]] was real; so was the intelligence she shared about [[Empress Morganna Eventide|Empress Morganna]]. But the "gift" was something else — she had armed a courier and pointed them at the deep, where her war is fought. She gets eyes and a hand in the Underdark; the party gets a useful trinket. She considers it a fair-enough trade and does not much care what it ultimately costs them. Both things are true at once.
- She took the amulet on her way out of the Underdark as an act of pure spite against the mother who banished her, and held it a very long time — long enough for that spite to find a purpose.

## Lolth's Eye
- The amulet is stolen property. If Lolth is ever introduced as an active force, she may want it back and may sense or track it within her reach. Nelfandriss is **counting on** this and exploiting it — it is not a danger she fears on the party's behalf.

## Two Amulets, One Keeper
- This gold-and-purple piece is distinct from the **silver-and-spider-silk** amulet Nelfandriss wears and never removes. Both came from her mother's court; she gave up this one but never the other. Useful for a later reveal tying the two together.
