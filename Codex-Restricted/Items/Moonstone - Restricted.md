> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Moonstone]]*

## The Third Stone
- The third Stone lies in the necromancer's keep — the ancient ruins at the heart of [[Borealia Forest]], now occupied by the new necromancer. Recovering it is bound up in that whole plot: the undead escalation, and the vampire lord of [[Val Solis]] who once patronised the original necromancer.
- The [[Moonlit Aegis]]'s Moon Sense is literally the compass to it — "the shield will point the way," direction only, material plane only.

## What the Vault Holds
- The [[Vault of the Moon]] is a holy place blessed by [[Selûne]] — where [[Elaris Moonsong]] first sought her out and became her champion. What rests there now is her relic, the [[Moon's Tear]].
- The public faith venerates the Moon's Tear as Elaris's relic, but it is in truth Selûne's own — and the whole Stone-hunt is her mechanism for delivering it into the hands of her Chosen. Closing the public thread of "where the Moon's Tear now rests": it is in the Vault, behind the three Stones.

## True Origin
- The Stones were created by [[Selûne]] and given to her champion [[Elaris Moonsong]] — not merely "left behind" by Elaris as the public prophecy frames it. They are a God of Old's instrument, reaching into the world through her champion's relics.

## The Race for the Chosen
- The prophecy of the Moon's Chosen is no dead legend but Selûne's own standing mechanism — the means by which a God of Old, forbidden to act directly, may yet arm a mortal champion. The hunt for the Stones is the race for her Chosen.
- It runs against her dark sister [[Shar]], who works to corrupt [[Kai]] into a dark champion before Selûne can complete him (the black stars in his Starry Form). See [[Selûne]] restricted notes.

## How Many Stones
- [[Praetor'Varanous]] would not confirm whether more than three Stones exist, warning only that their "falling into the hands of the wrong people would be very diabolical." Left deliberately ambiguous — a hook to pull on later.

## Continuity Note
- [[Kai]]'s Stone is the same one looted at Grakthor Falls (S07), logged then as a plain, non-magical moonstone before the prophecy was designed around it. Canon now treats it as one of the three all along — a blessed Stone that simply had not yet revealed itself. Present it that way; do not flag the early appraisal as an error.
