> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow]]*

> The public entry is a deliberately thin, lore-voice minor entry. Everything below is the reserve truth — the full shape of who Kharazoth is, his goals, and his methods. None of this should leak into the public page.

## True Role & Endgame
- Importance is **Major**, not Minor. His full profession/role: *Ancient Dragon, Architect of the Second Shattering.*
- He is the architect, patient and methodical, of a **second world-ending Shattering** — the destruction and recreation of the current age. The precise mechanism and timing are still TBD; develop further before it becomes plot-relevant.
- He emerged from the Chaos Wars believing the world had been **reset improperly** and set about the long work of "correcting" it. Centuries passed; the world forgot him. That was the point.
- The [[Valtorran Empire]] is his instrument — its reach and resources the mechanism by which he intends to gather the power necessary for another Shattering.

## Appearance (in true dragon form)
- Enormous even by the scale of ancient dragons; crimson scales deepened to a dark arterial red at the edges, as though scorched by ages of dark magic.
- Eyes burn with a cold, pale violet light — the colour of sorcery rather than dragonfire.
- Tendrils of necrotic energy coil around his claws unprompted, the residue of the dark pact he struck to untether his soul from death.
- In mortal guise he is a different kind of threat — unrecognisable, precise, and patient, sliding through courts and cults to gather information and pull strings that won't be felt for years.

## Personality
- Patient the way geological processes are patient — not calm, but operating on a timescale where urgency is beneath him. Calculating, precise, devoid of mercy.
- Does not hate his enemies; he accounts for them, adjusts, and continues. Carries a quality of cold amusement, like something that has already decided the outcome and is watching the middle act unfold.
- A **groomer of instruments**: he doesn't raise armies, he identifies minds with the right mix of power and vulnerability and shapes them over years/decades into tools. He believes he is selecting for greatness; their total dependence on him is, to him, a feature.

## Nature of His Immortality
- Where a lich binds its soul to a vessel, Kharazoth bound his to **nothing at all** — a dark transmutation of the lichdom principle, stripped of the need for a phylactery.
- Destroy his body and his soul simply drifts, untethered, until the **Material Plane** reconstitutes it. No blade, spell, or army can end him permanently.
- **Kill condition:** bind a mortal soul to him at the moment of apparent death and have that mortal die before his body reforms — a sacrifice both precise and absolute. The party knows the mechanic. The soul-stone from the [[Vale of Eternal Night]] is the key resource; the artifact must be constructed and a willing sacrifice found. This is the campaign's endgame shape.

## History with Praetor'Varanous
- In the final battle of the Chaos Wars he clashed with [[Praetor'Varanous]] in the sky above the breaking world, drove a claw of black necrotic energy into the silver dragon's chest, and left him for dead — taking it as confirmation of his survival and supremacy.
- **Kharazoth believes Praetor is dead.** His parting blow was meant to be fatal; he has no reason to expect Praetor alive. As far as he is aware, the only being who understood his nature in that era was destroyed. The party now knows something their enemy does not — an asymmetry worth leaning into.

## Morganna's Grooming (unrevealed to party)
- Kharazoth identified [[Empress Morganna Eventide]] as a young woman with exceptional magical aptitude and formative wounds that made her receptive to a patient, guiding force.
- He cultivated her over years — appearing in various guises, providing tutelage, steering her ambitions — before revealing himself and proposing a different future.
- **He guided her into becoming a lich**, binding her soul to a hidden vessel and removing the one thing that could limit her: death.
- The party does not yet know this. When revealed it recontextualises her entire story — she is in some sense a victim, though one with considerable agency in what she has become.

## Disguise Behaviour
- Kharazoth regularly moves through the world in humanoid form to gather intelligence and cultivate useful people. The [[Red Priests]] of [[Valfyria]] are his most visible instrument, but serve him at a remove.
- The party could encounter him without knowing it. Consider introducing him as a charming, unnervingly perceptive NPC before the mask is pulled.
