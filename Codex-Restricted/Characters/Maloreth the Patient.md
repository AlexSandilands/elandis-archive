Maloreth the Patient is an ancient archdevil of the Hells and the prisoner sealed within the [[Soulstone Anchor]], the relic held in the deepest vault beneath the Veiled Citadel of [[Caer Nystral, The Dawnless City|Caer Nystral]]. He is a soul-hollower — a fiend who does not merely kill but unmakes a being and takes what is left — and he is old enough to have walked the world while soulstones were still being forged. For two thousand years he has waited inside the stone, unable to reform, biding his time for the one thing he is owed by his nature and denied by his cage: a way out.

## Description

#### Appearance

In his true form Maloreth is a towering, gaunt thing of ash-grey hide cracked through with ember-red, horned and unhurried, carrying the cold regal bearing of infernal nobility. But he has no face of his own — only the ones he takes. A soul-hollower keeps something of everyone he unmakes, and he wears whichever face serves the moment, settling features over the smoke beneath like a mask drawn from a vast and stolen collection. Those who meet him rarely see what he is; they see who he has decided to be for them.

#### Personality

He is patient the way only something immortal and caged can be — not calm, but operating on a timescale where haste is beneath him. Lawful and evil in the precise, contractual manner of the Hells, he is a schemer first and a monster second: he reads a mark, finds the want beneath the want, and offers exactly the shape of mercy or kindness or grief that will open the door. He does not rage at his imprisonment. He simply waits, and watches, and prepares the move he will make when someone finally comes close enough to use.

## Biography

#### Background

Maloreth is older than the Court, the pact, and the present age. In the time when soulstones were still being made — relics forged to cage the uncageable — he was already abroad in the world, hollowing the powerful and wearing their lives. His most consequential victim, by far, was the spirit who would one day rule the [[Vale of Eternal Night]]. Thousands of years ago Maloreth severed [[Mirileth]]'s spirit from her flesh and destroyed her body entirely, leaving her the discorporate, dispossessed thing she remained until [[Ellesandra Silmara]] found her and struck the body-pact that gave her form again. He did this and moved on, as such a creature does. He did not reckon on her coming back.

## The Binding

When the pact with [[Ellesandra Silmara]] gave Mirileth a body and far greater power than she had ever held as a spirit, she did not forget who had unmade her. She hunted Maloreth down and turned the cruelty of his own age against him: she bound his soul into a soulstone — the very kind of relic made to trap immortals who cannot otherwise die — and locked it in the most secure vault beneath her throne. There he has remained ever since, the [[Soulstone Anchor]]'s single prisoner.

The stone is Mirileth's secret prize and her secret terror at once. She keeps it because she will never again let him loose; she keeps it hidden because she cannot risk anyone learning it can be opened. She will kill, quietly and without hesitation, anyone who discovers the Anchor exists — for every soul who knows of it is a door that Maloreth might one day talk open.

## The Eviction Trap

A soulstone is the one thing that overrides a devil's return to the Hells. A slain devil's essence normally flees home and reforms over time; while Maloreth's soul is held in the Anchor, that road is closed to him, and he simply cannot come back. The stone is what has kept him gone.

This is the knot at the heart of the [[Soulstone Anchor]]'s role in the campaign. To forge the weapon that can permanently destroy [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow|Kharazoth]], the party needs an empty soulstone — and the Anchor is the only one known to exist. Emptying it means releasing Maloreth's soul, and the instant they do, it streams back to the Hells and begins to reform. An archdevil of his age reforms slowly, over years or decades, so this is not a fight in the vault but a consequence the party chooses: to end Kharazoth, they personally loose Maloreth the Patient back onto the world. The cell they need is occupied, and its tenant is every bit as dangerous as the enemy they mean to lock inside it.

## His Play

Maloreth's goal and the party's are, for once, the same: both need the stone empty. He never has to turn them against their own mission — he only has to make sure they finish it, and that he walks away from it owed.

His first move is the trick. If the party comes near the stone he reaches for them, and he tries to win a clean, willing release before they understand what they are freeing. Against this party he wears [[Ellesandra Silmara|Ellesandra's]] face for [[Ryo]] — a deliberate cruelty, since Ellesandra is fused to the very jailer who caged him — feigning gratitude and gentleness, presenting himself as a wronged thing owed a kindness, so that when he finishes reforming he can return as a power who remembers a debt and holds a claim on the Silmara line. The Ellesandra mask is not his nature; it is simply the right face for this mark, chosen as he always chooses, from the want beneath the want. If they see through it, he drops the face without embarrassment and bargains instead — freedom for a price, a name, a favour banked.

## Plot Hooks

- However the Anchor is emptied, Maloreth goes free; the only open question is the terms. Tricked into it, the party frees him unknowing, and he returns owed and unsuspected, with a quiet claim on Ryo's blood. Bargained with, the terms he extracts become the hook — a promise, a name, a favour he will collect when he is whole again.
- His slow reformation is a long fuse for a late-campaign or post-campaign threat: an ancient archdevil who remembers exactly who let him out, and exactly whose face moved them to do it.
- [[Mirileth]] would sooner see the party dead than see the Anchor opened. The mission Praetor set them and the secret the Star-Veiled Queen kills to protect are the same object — which makes the embassy that "solves a lot of problems" a walk toward the one thing in the Vale worth dying over.
- [[Praetor'Varanous|Praetor]] does not know the Anchor is occupied; he knows soulstones come from the Vale, not that a devil sleeps in this one. The tenant is a surprise waiting for the party at the worst possible moment.
