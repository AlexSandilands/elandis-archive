> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Ellesandra Silmara]]*

## Description (DM-only)
- **Appearance:** No physical description survives. Typically imagined in elven art as solemn and spare — a ruler who dressed for practicality over ceremony.
- **Personality:** Known by her decision, not her temperament. Walking alone into the Vale and returning suggests someone who had weighed the odds, accepted the cost, and moved anyway. Whether driven by desperation, strategic calculation, or something more personal has never been answered.

## The Pact (Full Truth — extremely restricted)
- [[Mirileth]] was a bodiless spirit — an archfae whose body had been destroyed and whose spirit had been severed by a powerful evil entity. To end the [[Vale of Eternal Night#The Long Hunt (~2000 years ago)|Long Hunt]], Ellesandra struck a bargain: Mirileth would take control of the Vale and bring the Hunt to heel, and in return Ellesandra would give Mirileth her body.
- The price was paid. Ellesandra surrendered her body; she and Mirileth now cohabitate it — two minds, one form. The archfae the Court calls the Star-Veiled Queen is the two of them together.

## Ellesandra's Fate
- She did return from the Vale after the Long Hunt, bearing the head of the Hunt's leader — the public legend is true. She died a few years later, and that death was the rest of the pact coming due: she left to return to the Vale and surrender her body to [[Mirileth]]. The circumstances of her passing are unknown — she likely used magic to make it appear natural. Marked Deceased by record, and in the ordinary sense she is; but she persists as a second mind inside the body that now rules the Vale as the Star-Veiled Queen.

## What Mirileth Knows
- The Star-Veiled Queen is the only living being present for whatever Ellesandra did or said inside the Vale. If the party ever has an audience with [[Mirileth]], she may be the only one who could speak of Ellesandra truthfully.

## Ryo's Lineage
- Ellesandra is confirmed as an ancestor of [[Ryo]]'s via the [[Silmara Family]] line. The party now knows the shape of the story; the pact's terms remain hidden.
