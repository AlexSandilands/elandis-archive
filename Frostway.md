## Nature of the Frostway:

- **Unpredictable:**  
    Every year, the freezing happens differently. Currents, storms, and the hidden flow of the deep sea beneath the ice cause **shifts, cracks, and upheavals**. No two crossings are ever identical.

- **Fragmented:**  
    The Frostway isn’t one solid stretch — it’s a **broken patchwork** of ice fields, frozen tide pools, narrow ridges, and isolated **"ice islands"** where the [[Frostwardens]] must find suitable paths for merchant caravans to cross safely.

- **Treacherous:**  
    Certain areas are infamous —
    - **The Shattered Fields**: huge swathes of broken, unstable ice where falling through is a constant danger.
    - **The Howling Narrows**: a narrow canyon between two black spires where icy winds create disorienting echoes.
    - **The Veiled Rift**: a deceptively smooth plain hiding thin ice sheets over deadly crevasses.

- **Time-sensitive:**
    - It takes **~10 days** to cross _with guidance and luck_. **7 days** if willing to risk more treacherous routes
    - Weather windows are short: blizzards, sudden temperature rises, or hidden melt pools can appear at terrifying speed.
    - The longer you're out there, the more the Frostway itself becomes an enemy.


## Monsters of the Frostway:

Not only is the terrain lethal — it's also _hunted_.
The [[Frostwardens]] have learned how to avoid the territories of these creatures, but occasionally there are encounters.

### Common Dangers:

| Creature                       | Notes                                                                                                                                            |
| ------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Polar Bears**                | Enormous and aggressive — adapted to the harshest ice. Hunt isolated or wounded travelers.                                                       |
| **Winter Wolves**              | Travel in packs; intelligent and sometimes seemingly orchestrated attacks on camps.                                                              |
| **Yetis**                      | Lurk in the black peaks; territorial and almost impossible to spot during a whiteout.                                                            |
| **Abominable Yetis**           | Singular terrors; tales speak of white behemoths that hunt by scent and can track a camp across miles of broken ice.                             |
| **Frost Worms** _(Remorhazes)_ | Rare, but some tunnels and warm burrows have been spotted near the Rift. The ground can suddenly collapse beneath you.                           |
| **Frost Giants**               | Ancient, few, and nomadic — believed to walk the Frostway only during the coldest seasons. Their footprints create dangerous valleys in the ice. |

## Rest Spots: "Havens of the Frostway"

Over the centuries, the [[Frostwardens]] (and before them, desperate merchants) have found or created Safe Havens:

- **Blackrock Shelters**:  
    Small caves or natural hollows carved into the great jagged rocks.  
    Reinforced with timber and stones by past travellers, often marked with old [[Frostwardens|Frostwarden]] sigils.

- **Old Cairns**:  
    Ancient stone cairns where travellers can light fires safely without fear of the flames weakening the ice.  
    (The older cairns sometimes have hidden stores of supplies left behind — or offerings to the dead.)

- **The Singing Arch**:  
    A natural stone arch among the Black Rocks that creates a low humming sound when the wind blows through. Believed to be a lucky place to camp.

- **The Hollow Isle**:  
    A larger, stable ice island with a sunken centre, protected from storms by sheer ice cliffs. Used for multi-day rests if blizzards trap travelers.


> [!quote] Frostwarden Saying
> _"Out on the Frostway, a roof over your head and a wall at your back is the difference between seeing tomorrow or being another whisper on the wind."_


## Environmental Threats

- **Sudden Cracks:** A safe-looking ice field might split during the night — ships frozen into the ice tilt and collapse into blackness.

- **Avalanches:** Built up ice and snow on the black rock peaks can sometimes break and collapse causing a cascade of falling rubble.

- **Blizzards:** Complete whiteouts where even sound becomes muted; navigation becomes almost impossible.

- **Thin Ice Zones:** Where only light foot traffic can pass safely — heavy loads or unwise fights can doom a whole group.

- **Aurora Disorientation:** Stunning auroras sometimes confuse night navigation, creating false horizons and optical illusions.
