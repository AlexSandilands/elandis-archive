# Gulf of Miriel — DM Brainstorm Notes

*Working notes for the Codex region entry. Untagged points are drawn from synopses / existing pages; **(new)** marks proposed lore to review.*

**Subtype:** Region · **Continent:** [[Valtorra]] · **Importance:** Major · **Status:** Active (Imperial-contested)
**Defining feature:** A great gulf on Valtorra's south-eastern edge whose only sea-gate is [[The Maw]], a permanent whirlpool — which makes the [[The Mawbreakers|Mawbreakers]] of [[Camaar]] the true gatekeepers of the Empire's southern shipping.
**Seat of power:** [[Val Miriel]] (Imperial governorship, ruling in the Miriel name)
**Connections:** [[Luminor River]] (artery north to the capital [[Val Luminor]]) · the open ocean beyond the Maw

---

## Geography & Landscape

- A large gulf indenting the **south-eastern edge of [[Valtorra]]**, open to the ocean only through [[The Maw]] at its mouth.
- **Four coastal cities** ring its shores. Clockwise from the south: [[Camaar]] on the **southern shore**, [[Farhaven]] on the **western shore**, [[Val Miriel]] in the **north-west** (the seat of power, where the [[Luminor River]] empties into the gulf), and [[Sabine]] on the **northern shore**.
- The [[Luminor River]] runs from the gulf north through the central plains to the capital, [[Val Luminor]] — so the gulf is the **seaward end of the Empire's central river artery**.
- [[The Maw]] — a permanent **whirlpool** at the gulf's **eastern mouth**, blocking its only exit to the open ocean. There is a navigable margin, but it is narrow, fast, and shifting; only seasoned Mawbreaker pilots run it reliably (Session 13).
- The **[[Isle of Solenne]]** — a large island in the centre of the gulf.
- *(new)* **Sea-stacks and reefs** crowd the approach to the Maw — black basalt spires the locals call the *Teeth*. Harpies nest on them *(canon — harpies near the Maw, Session 13)*.
- *(new)* Rich **fishing banks** along the sheltered inner shore; warm, wet, storm-prone climate — the gulf "boils" in the late-year gales, and fog rolls thick on autumn mornings.

## History

- [[Val Miriel]] was one of the **six human kingdoms of old** — alongside [[Val Noren]], [[Val Luminor]], [[Val Solis]], [[Val Aran]], and [[Val Aerie]] — and predates the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]] by roughly **a thousand years**.
- *(new)* The **Miriel line** were a sea-facing people: Val Miriel grew into the continent's great port and the key gateway to [[Val Luminor]] up the river. *(Their wealth and power came from the ocean and the trade it carried — but they weren't sea-raiders or "sealords"; they were a port kingdom.)*
- When the **Empire rose about a century ago**, it brought the old kingdoms under its control. The Empress gave each ruling king or queen an ultimatum: be executed, or continue to lead as a **governor — subservient to the Empire**. It was a deliberate move to keep familiar faces in place and blunt the populace's resentment of conquest.
- Val Miriel's crown thus became a governorship. Its last heir, **Vallarra Miriel**, governed the city; she married **[[Governor Tarlis Evandron|Tarlis Evandron]]**, who came to power through that marriage and still governs in the Miriel name after Vallarra died delivering their daughter, [[Vallania Miriel|Nia]].

## Peoples & Powers

- **The Empire** — seated at [[Val Miriel]]: Governor [[Governor Tarlis Evandron|Tarlis]], the Red Cloaks, the [[The Miriel Bastion|Miriel Bastion]] garrison. Holds the *law* of the gulf.
- **The [[The Mawbreakers|Mawbreakers]]** — based at the [[Camaar]] docks under [[Barak Stormrider|Barak]] (flagship [[The Albatross]]). Privateer-pilots with anti-Imperial sympathies who hold *de facto* naval supremacy in the Maw. Hold the *sea* of the gulf.
- *(new)* **Fishing communities** — the gulf's oldest working people; loyal to whoever lets them work, which is mostly the Mawbreakers.
- **[[Order of Ravens]]** nests operate in both Val Miriel and Camaar *(canon)*.

## Dangers

- [[The Maw]] itself — death to any pilot who misjudges it.
- Harpies on the *Teeth* sea-stacks near the mouth *(canon, S13)*.
- Imperial patrol galleys vs. Mawbreaker crews — the gulf is one incident away from open blockade.
- Late-year storms, fog, and the reefs of the inner approach.
