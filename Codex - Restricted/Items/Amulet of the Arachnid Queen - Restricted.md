The **Amulet of the Arachnid Queen** is a spider-shaped wondrous item of ancient Underdark make, prized for the easy dominion it grants over arachnids and the way it lets its bearer walk sheer stone and ceilings as though they were level ground. It is also a baited hook. Stolen long ago from the personal collection of Lolth herself, and re-enchanted in secret by the drider [[Nelfandriss the Banished]], it was given to [[The Bloody Nails]] not as a gift but as a weapon — one that turns its wearer, in the deep places of the world, into Nelfandriss's unwitting agent in her long war against her mother.

## Description

#### Appearance

The amulet is a spider wrought in dark, antique gold, poised above a large glowing magenta gem that forms its swollen abdomen, with teardrop-cut purple gemstones set around the body like a cluster of secondary eyes. It hangs from a plain black cord. The purple "eyes" catch and hold the light, and the whole of it carries a beauty that sits just on the wrong side of comfortable — the kind of pretty thing Nelfandriss would covet.

It should not be confused with the silver-and-spider-silk amulet Nelfandriss wears and keeps close at all times. Both pieces came from her mother's court; she gave up this one but never the other.

## Properties

While worn, the amulet grants its bearer a Climb Speed equal to their walking Speed — moving freely up, down, and across vertical surfaces and ceilings with hands free — and lets them trade basic messages with ordinary spiders. Once per day, the wearer can command lesser arachnids for an hour.

These are its honest gifts. Beneath them runs a second enchantment, Nelfandriss's own work, woven into the piece before she parted with it. On the surface it lies wholly dormant; the wearer would never know it was there. But it stirs the moment its bearer descends into the Underdark, and it grows stronger the deeper they go. It is never overt — no voice, no visible compulsion. Instead it waits for moments of weakness, and on occasion takes the wearer's hand entirely, steering them into some choice that serves Nelfandriss and undermines Lolth. The bearer feels none of it. They rationalise the act as their own and never sense whose hand was truly on them.

## History

The amulet was stolen from Lolth's own collection. When Nelfandriss was banished from the Underdark, she took it on her way out — an act of pure spite against the mother who cast her down — and held onto it for a very long time, long enough for that spite to find a purpose.

That purpose arrived with [[The Bloody Nails]]. Before parting with the amulet, Nelfandriss wove her hidden enchantment into it, then handed it over beneath the [[Ruby Falls Goldmine]] under the cover of a genuine treaty. The bargain over the mine was real; so was the intelligence she shared about [[Empress Morganna Eventide|Empress Morganna]]. But the "gift" was something else again — she had armed a courier and pointed them at the deep, where her war is fought. She gets eyes and a hand in the Underdark; the party gets a useful trinket. She considers it a fair-enough trade, and she does not much care what it ultimately costs them. Both things are true at once.

## Current Owner

The amulet belongs to [[The Bloody Nails]], who carry it as one of their most reliable tools and have no inkling of its true nature. So long as they stay on the surface it is exactly what it appears to be. The danger waits underground — and the party has already begun venturing into the kinds of places where the second enchantment wakes.

### [[The Bloody Nails|Campaign: The Bloody Nails]]

#### [[Session 24 - Nelfandriss The Banished]]

Deep beneath the [[Ruby Falls Goldmine]], rather than fight the immense, ancient drider they found guarding the depths, the party chose diplomacy. [[Nelfandriss the Banished]] revealed she was only defending her home from the [[Empress Morganna Eventide|Empire's]] intrusion, and a treaty was struck. As a token of the alliance — and, unknown to them, as the delivery of her hidden weapon — Nelfandriss gifted them the amulet and shared disturbing lore about [[Empress Morganna Eventide|Empress Morganna]].

#### [[Session 28 - The Underrun]]

Battered after a gruelling passage through the flooded tunnels beneath the city, the party used the amulet together with a *Rope of Climbing* to escape through a fissure in the cavern ceiling, emerging into the wintry grasslands north of the city. (These tunnels were shallow workings, not true Underdark; the second enchantment did not stir.)

#### [[Session 47 - Confluence of the Seven]]

At the Confluence of the Seven, [[Eryndor]] produced the amulet and Archivist Elrohir identified it immediately as ancient Underdark work — Lolth-related, he judged, though he drew no line to Nelfandriss. It surfaced alongside the party's recollection of the four planar rifts they had encountered, among them the Arachnid Queen's cave.

## Trivia

- The amulet's name comes from its form and its gifts rather than any inscription — to those who have studied it, the "Arachnid Queen" is presumed to be Lolth. They are closer to the truth than they know.
- Its violet gemstone "eyes" are arranged in a deliberate cluster, echoing the many-eyed heads of the spiders it commands.

## DM Notes

- **True nature:** A cursed item masquerading as a goodwill gift. Stolen from Lolth by Nelfandriss and re-enchanted to make the bearer an unwitting agent working Nelfandriss's war against her mother from the inside. The party does not know this; Elrohir suspects Lolth but not Nelfandriss; the players have been guessing at a Lolth link on their own. **Keep the mystery alive — confirm nothing.**
- **Corruption / mechanics:** The hidden enchantment is dormant on the surface and inert in shallow tunnels. It activates only in the true Underdark and escalates with depth. While worn in the deep, call for occasional saving throws. On a failure, the character briefly acts **outside player control**, taking an action that serves Nelfandriss and undermines Lolth — then rationalises the choice as their own. Never overt puppeteering; keep it subtle and deniable. The long arc is the bearer becoming a deep-cover agent who never realises they were turned.
- **Lolth's eye:** The amulet is stolen property. If Lolth is ever introduced as an active force, she may want it back and may sense or track it within her reach. Nelfandriss is **counting on** this and exploiting it — it is not a danger she fears on the party's behalf.
- **Two amulets, one keeper:** This gold-and-purple piece is distinct from the silver-and-spider-silk amulet Nelfandriss wears and never removes. Both came from her mother's court. Useful for a later reveal that ties the two together.
