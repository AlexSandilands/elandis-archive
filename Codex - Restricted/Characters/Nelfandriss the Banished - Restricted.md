> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Nelfandriss the Banished]]*

> The public entry deliberately obscures her origin (it says only "the daughter of a spider queen below" and that she "transformed herself through dark experimentation and was cast out"). The true backstory below is reserve — reveal only when dramatically appropriate.

## True Background (unrevealed to party)
- Nelfandriss is the **daughter of Lolth, the Spider Queen** — one of the most powerful divine entities of the deep. Born into that court hundreds of years ago, she was gifted, ambitious, and restless in equal measure. The insularity of the depths frustrated her; she wanted to communicate with the peoples of other realms — to learn, to collect, to be part of a world larger than her mother's domain.
- She turned to dark magic and transformed herself, merging her own form with that of a great spider — weaving together flesh and chitin, human and arachnid. She became the **first Drider**, creating something her mother had never sanctioned. Lolth viewed the self-transformation as corruption and defiance — a desecration of the form she had been given — and **banished her from the deep entirely.**
- Before she fled, Nelfandriss **stole her mother's amulet** — a powerful artefact of spider command. Whether it was revenge, leverage, or simply love for a beautiful thing, she has never said. She climbed upward through the tunnels, stopped when the sunlight began to burn her skin at the surface threshold, and made her home in the middle depths, raising children of her own. The driders that now occur naturally throughout the underdark are her descendants.

## The Amulet
- The ornate amulet of silver and spider-silk she keeps close at all times was **stolen from her mother's own collection** (Lolth's court). This is the *[[Amulet of the Arachnid Queen]]* gifted to the party.
- If Lolth is ever introduced as an active force in the campaign, the amulet's provenance could become a significant plot point — Lolth may want it back.

## Motivation Note
- Her willingness to deal with [[The Bloody Nails]] was rooted entirely in protectiveness: a treaty shielding her brood from imperial interference was worth more to her than any revenge on the miners. She stated clearly she is willing to make arrangements that protect her children. Not hostile by default — but ancient, powerful, and betrayed before.

## Plot Hooks
- She is bound by the treaty to allow the [[Green Gryphons]] to mine quietly. If the Empire overruns the Gryphons or the party breaks the agreement (even by accident), she becomes a threat.
- Conversely, if her children are threatened by a third party, she may reach out to the party as the only surface-worlders she has a compact with.
