**Ellesandra Silmara** is a legendary ancestor of the [[Silmara Family]] who ruled [[Sumara, The Shining City|Sumara]] during the conflict known as the [[Vale of Eternal Night#The Long Hunt *(~2000 years ago)*|Long Hunt]] — a sustained campaign by the riding-bands of the [[Vale of Eternal Night|Vale]] against the elves of the Shimmering Peaks. She is remembered above all else for a single act: walking alone into the Vale and returning with the head of the leader of the Hunt.

## Description

#### Appearance

No physical description of Ellesandra has survived in any accessible record. She is an ancestor some two thousand years removed from the present day, and what little the Silmara family lore preserves about her concerns what she did rather than what she looked like. She is typically imagined in elven art as solemn and spare — a ruler who dressed for practicality over ceremony.

#### Personality

Ellesandra is known by her decision, not her temperament. A leader who walked alone into the Vale of Eternal Night does not read as reckless — she returned — which suggests someone who had weighed the odds, accepted the cost, and moved anyway. Whether she was driven by desperation, by strategic calculation, or by something more personal has never been answered.

## Biography

The Long Hunt ground on for years, devastating settlements on both sides of the boundary between the Vale and Sumaran territory. Ellesandra ruled through it, bearing the weight of a war that showed no sign of ending by negotiation or force of arms. At some point, she made a decision that no record has ever fully explained.

She walked into the [[Vale of Eternal Night]] alone.

She came back carrying the head of the leader of the Hunt. What passed inside the Vale — what she saw, who she spoke with, what she gave or promised or did to emerge again — was never spoken of. The war ended. Soon after her return, the archfae [[Mirileth|Mirileth, the Star-Veiled Queen]] rose to take the throne of the Court, and a tenuous peace settled over the border that has held for two thousand years since.

The truth known to the DM: Ellesandra made a deliberate pact with the archfae queen [[Mirileth]] to end the conflict. The terms were that Mirileth would take control of the Vale and bring the Hunt to heel, and the war would cease. Ellesandra likely gave something in return — what that was remains a potential plot thread. She disappeared into the Vale and it is not recorded whether she left again by a different means, died there, or simply ceased to be spoken of. Her role is marked as Deceased by convention, but the manner of her death is unknown.

### [[The Bloody Nails|Campaign: The Bloody Nails]]

#### [[Session 50 - A New Morning in Sumara]]

[[Warden Sylvaris]] told [[Ryo]] and the party the story of the Long Hunt beneath the bioluminescent fields of the Greensward, naming Ellesandra as an ancestor of Ryo's line and recounting her solitary journey into the Vale — and her return with the Hunt-leader's head. The account was delivered as received history: what she did is documented; how she did it is not.

## DM Notes

- **The pact:** The full terms of Ellesandra's deal with [[Mirileth]] are unrevealed. This is a significant potential plot hook — Mirileth has held a two-thousand-year peace, but the original pact may have come with conditions that could become relevant when the party approaches the Vale as a Silmara embassy.
- **Ellesandra's fate:** The stub says she "disappeared alone into the Vale" and returned — but what happened after is unrecorded. She may have died in the Vale after the pact was struck, or may have returned to Sumara and simply faded from record. Either reading works.
- **What Mirileth knows:** The Star-Veiled Queen is the only living being who was present for whatever Ellesandra did or said inside the Vale. If the party ever has an audience with Mirileth, she may be the only one who could speak of Ellesandra truthfully.
- **Ryo's lineage:** Ellesandra is confirmed as an ancestor of [[Ryo]]'s via the [[Silmara Family]] line. The party now knows the shape of the story; the pact's terms remain hidden.
