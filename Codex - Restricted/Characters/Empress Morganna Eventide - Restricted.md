> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Empress Morganna Eventide]]*

Restricted-only frontmatter facts: Creature Type **Undead**; true Age **~130**; Profession **Empress, Lich**. Restricted infobox uses the Lich portrait (`Empress_Morganna_Eventide_Lich.webp`).

## The Truth of Her Nature
- She is a lich and genuinely looks twenty-one — **not** an illusion. The violet orb continuously drains life essence from those near her and channels it into her body, arresting biological decay entirely. A lich's body is normally visibly dead; hers is the exception, sustained by an unbroken chain of stolen vitality stretching back a century.
- The drain is passive and imperceptible. People leave her presence feeling vaguely tired and think nothing of it. Prolonged proximity is mechanically dangerous even outside combat.
- **The orb is her phylactery.** It sits in plain sight at every public appearance, carried openly, because she cannot leave it behind. Destroying the orb kills her permanently; while it exists and is intact, her body reforms after destruction. Any confrontation must grapple with the fact that she will defend the orb before anything else.
- The orb has three functions: phylactery, life-drain (sustaining her youth), and **bending the will** of individuals she focuses it on.

## Personality (True Reading)
- Not cruel for cruelty's sake. She wanted, above all, to be free — from family obligation, from mortality, from the shape her life had been pressed into. In trading that away she built a different cage: a century of obligation to [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow|Kharazoth]], a continent believing in a version of her that is mostly performance, and an empire requiring constant maintenance of controlled minds.
- Highly intelligent and comfortable with manipulation — not for enjoyment but as the most efficient tool available. The orb's will-bending has shaped how she thinks of people: as vectors of usefulness, to be aligned or removed. Whether anything warmer remains is a question she may no longer be able to answer herself.

## Background (Full Authored Lore)
Morganna was born into one of the ancient noble families descended from pre-Shattering civilisation — not that her relatives cared much about that heritage. She was the difficult one: too curious, too restless, too interested in ruins and old texts to fulfil the duties her family expected. They dismissed it as a rebellious girl shunning her station; she was looking for something they had long stopped thinking about.

As a teenager she explored ancient ruins in earnest, hunting the lost history of what her bloodline had once been. There she first encountered texts detailing the powers unleashed during the [[Chaos Wars]] — and among them, necromancy captivated her most. Where others saw death magic as abhorrent, she saw freedom: outliving her circumstances, preserving the history she loved, escaping the life her family kept pressing her back into.

In her early twenties, a heated argument with her brother turned fatal. She did not intend to kill him; the magic she was still learning did. Terrified of her family's response, she fled before dawn and did not look back, beginning a self-imposed exile in which she pursued forbidden knowledge with nothing left to lose. That exile drew her inward — into the **Shattered Wastes** at the heart of [[Aetheris]], the broken interior where the Dragon Queen had once torn her rift into the world and where pre-Shattering ruins lie thickest. It was the one place her obsession and her ruin pointed alike: a lawless expanse of wild magic and lost knowledge, where no family and no law would follow her.

It was in the Wastes that [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow]] found her. One of the three remaining red dragons still secretly active, Kharazoth approached in disguise, presenting himself as a mentor who recognised her potential. He was not interested in her wellbeing — only her usefulness. His ancient goal was to amass enough wealth to fund the ritual that would bring Tiamat into the world, and a power-hungry young necromancer with noble blood and nothing to lose was exactly the instrument he needed.

He guided her deeper into the dark arts, nurturing her ambition, letting her believe she was making the choices. When lichdom became realistic, he raised it — knowing she would want it, and knowing her vanity would never tolerate the decayed form lichdom usually produces. Feigning concern, he offered a solution: an enchanted violet orb of his own creation, serving as her phylactery while draining the life essence of others to sustain her youthful appearance — and, almost as an afterthought, able to bend the will of those around her.

The price was simple: Morganna would go into the world, amass power and wealth, and build an empire. The proceeds would flow back into Kharazoth's designs. He would give her eternity and the face she wanted; she would give him a continent. She accepted and set sail for [[Valtorra]].

## How the Conquest Actually Worked
- Not a military campaign but a demonstration of what a single lich with a soul-binding artefact can do given time. Each city fell the same way: she would speak to crowds — the orb amplifying her charisma into something that bypassed rational thought — and her first true converts, those the orb had fully bound, became the nucleus of loyalty around which the city organised.
- **The [[Hand of The Empire]]** were the earliest and most thoroughly controlled: first followers, minds wholly given to her will, the template for all who followed. Promising soldiers are still selected, trained, and mind-controlled in a final "graduation" ceremony the Empire frames as an honour. The orb's touch is always the last step.
- **The Voices of the Empire** (Imperial mages) were built on the same principle — loyal beyond question because their loyalty was never optional.
- **The Eyes of the Empire:** assassins operating entirely in shadow, unknown even to most within the Imperial hierarchy — even the Hands are not told they exist. They report directly to Morganna and operate outside normal hierarchy. Session 49's memory visions showed a hidden Eye among the Hands aboard a prison ship; the party named the falsity but the implication (Eyes infiltrate the Hands) may not yet have landed. Expect an Eye if the party meaningfully destabilises the Empire.

## Blackmarsh
- [[Dread General Gerard Blackmarsh|Gerard Blackmarsh]] was her first true lieutenant — too useful to lose to mortality. She killed him and raised him as a death knight, gifting him the blade *Dread's Hunger*, forged for the purpose by [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow|Kharazoth]] himself. He served faithfully for decades until destroyed by [[The Bloody Nails]].

## DM Notes
- **Not a glamour — genuine preservation:** Praetor concluded her appearance is "a glamour his visions could not pierce." The truth is stranger: there is nothing to pierce. Divination reads her as a living human, not an illusion concealing something else. If the party tries to dispel her "disguise," there is no disguise to dispel.
- **Kharazoth's leash:** Her empire-building serves Kharazoth's accumulation of wealth for the Tiamat ritual. Destroying Morganna does not solve the dragon problem, and Kharazoth has not yet revealed himself to the party. A century of Imperial wealth has been flowing somewhere — that thread is still open.
- **Redemption potential:** She originally sought freedom and the preservation of history, not conquest as an end. Kharazoth manipulated rather than converted her. Whether his hold is total or whether removing it could recover something of who she was is deliberately open. A confrontation where the party tries to reason with her could yield interesting results.
- **Unrevealed to the party:** That the orb is her phylactery. That Kharazoth personally forged *Dread's Hunger* and created Blackmarsh as a death knight. That the Eyes of the Empire exist. That her true age is ~130 and her origin is a pre-Shattering noble bloodline. That she accidentally killed her brother, which set all of this in motion.
