**Empress Morganna Eventide** is the lich-ruler of the [[Valtorran Empire]], a century-old undead sovereign who genuinely looks twenty-one — not because she hides behind an illusion, but because she never stops feeding. The violet orb at her side continuously drains life essence from those around her, channelling it into her body to keep decay at bay and her appearance unchanged. She did not conquer [[Valtorra]] through military genius or political cunning alone — she conquered it with that orb, and the century that followed has been sustained by the same mechanism: stolen life, bent minds, and a deal with a dragon who has never let her forget what she owes him.

## Description

#### Appearance

Morganna appears as a slight, pale woman of about twenty-one: long crimson hair, ice-blue eyes, always in white gowns of immaculate Imperial cut. The violet orb is constant — orbiting her hand, resting on a staff, drifting in the air nearby. It never leaves her, because it cannot. The orb is her phylactery, and it is also the mechanism keeping her body intact.

There is no illusion. She genuinely looks in her early twenties because the orb continuously draws life essence from those in her proximity and channels it into her, arresting biological decay entirely. A lich's body is typically dead in the most visible sense — Morganna's is the exception, sustained not by disguise but by an unbroken chain of stolen vitality stretching back a century. The effect is passive and imperceptible to those being drained. People leave her presence feeling vaguely tired and think nothing of it.

#### Personality

Morganna is not cruel for cruelty's sake. She is a woman who wanted, above all else, to be free — free from family obligation, free from mortality, free from the shape her life had been pressed into before she was old enough to choose otherwise. The irony is that in trading all of that away, she has built a different kind of cage: a century of obligation to [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow|Kharazoth]], a continent of subjects who believe in a version of her that is mostly performance, and an empire that requires constant maintenance of controlled minds to stay loyal.

![[Empress_Morganna_Eventide_Haunted_small.webp|right]]

She is highly intelligent and entirely comfortable with manipulation — not because she enjoys it, but because she long ago accepted it as the most efficient tool available to her. The orb's ability to bend wills has shaped how she thinks about people: as vectors of usefulness, to be aligned or removed. Whether anything warmer remains beneath that calculus is a question she may no longer be able to answer herself.

## Biography

#### Background

Morganna was born into one of the ancient noble families descended from pre-Shattering civilisation — not that her relatives cared much about that heritage. She was the difficult one: too curious, too restless, too interested in ruins and old texts to fulfil the duties her family expected. They dismissed it as a rebellious girl shunning her proper station. She was looking for something they had long stopped thinking about.

As a teenager she began exploring ancient ruins in earnest, hunting for the lost history of what her bloodline had once been. It was in those ruins that she first encountered texts detailing the powers unleashed during the [[Chaos Wars]] — and among those powers, necromancy captivated her most. Where others saw death magic as abhorrent, she saw freedom: the possibility of outliving the circumstances she had been born into, of preserving the history she had come to love, of escaping the life her family kept pressing her back into.

In her early twenties, a heated argument with her brother turned fatal. She did not intend to kill him. The magic she was still learning did. Terrified of her family's response, she fled before dawn and did not look back — beginning a period of self-imposed exile in which she pursued forbidden knowledge with nothing left to lose.
![[Empress_Morganna_Eventide_Ritual_small.webp|left]]

It was during this exile that [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow]] found her. One of the three remaining red dragons still secretly active in the world, Kharazoth approached her in disguise, presenting himself as a mentor who recognised her potential. The disguise was not the only deception. He was not interested in Morganna's wellbeing — he was interested in her usefulness. His ancient goal was to amass enough wealth to fund the ritual that would bring Tiamat into the world, and a power-hungry young necromancer with noble blood and nothing to lose was exactly the instrument he needed.

He guided her deeper into the dark arts, nurturing her ambition, letting her believe she was the one making the choices. When her power had grown enough that lichdom was realistic, he raised it — knowing she would want it, and knowing equally that her vanity would never tolerate the decayed form lichdom usually produces. Feigning concern, he offered a solution: an enchanted violet orb of his own creation, one that would serve as her phylactery while simultaneously draining the life essence of others to sustain her youthful appearance. It could also, he added almost as an afterthought, bend the will of those around her — making it rather easier to manage people.

The price was simple: Morganna would go out into the world, amass power and wealth, and build an empire. The proceeds would flow back into Kharazoth's designs. He would give her eternity and the face she wanted to keep. She would give him a continent.

She accepted, and set sail for [[Valtorra]].

![[Empress_Morganna_Eventide_small.webp|right]]
The conquest that followed was not really a military campaign — it was a demonstration of what a single lich with a soul-binding artefact can accomplish given time. Each city fell the same way: Morganna would appear, speak to the crowds — the orb amplifying her natural charisma into something that bypassed rational thought entirely — and her first true converts, the ones the orb had fully bound, would become the nucleus of loyalty around which the rest of the city organised itself. The [[Hand of The Empire]] were the earliest and most thoroughly controlled of these: the first followers, minds wholly given over to Morganna's will, who then became the template for all who followed. Promising soldiers are still selected from across the kingdoms today, put through rigorous training, and then mind-controlled in a final "graduation" ceremony that the Empire frames as a mark of honour. The orb's touch has always been the last step.


From the same principle, the Voices of the Empire were built — Imperial mages who provide advisory and controlling functions throughout the provinces, loyal beyond question because their loyalty was never optional. And then there are the Eyes of the Empire: assassins operating entirely in shadow, unknown even to most within the Imperial hierarchy, who ensure that the few individuals who cannot be persuaded quietly cease to be a problem. Their existence is not publicly known. Even the Hands are not told they exist.

[[Dread General Gerard Blackmarsh|Gerard Blackmarsh]] served as her first true lieutenant — capable, loyal, and too useful to lose to natural mortality. She solved the problem in the way she had learned to solve most problems: she killed him and raised him as a death knight, gifting him the blade *Dread's Hunger*, forged for the purpose by [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow|Kharazoth]] himself. Blackmarsh served the Empire faithfully for decades until his destruction at the hands of [[The Bloody Nails]].

### [[The Bloody Nails|Campaign: The Bloody Nails]]

#### [[Session 01 - The Rusty Nail]]

The founding of the [[Valtorran Empire|Crimson Empire]] framed the campaign's opening context — human cities felled, dwarven strongholds extinguished, elven lands found abandoned. The iron rule of Empress Morganna was established as the defining political reality of [[Valtorra]], a century of conquest that had reshaped the entire continent.

#### [[Session 24 - Nelfandriss The Banished]]

Deep in the flooded ruins of the [[Ruby Falls Goldmine]], [[The Bloody Nails]] encountered [[Nelfandriss the Banished]], an immense and ancient being who had witnessed the Empire's rise firsthand. She shared what she knew of the Empress — describing her as a young girl who arrived from a distant land and brokered a dark deal a century ago to entrap her own soul and extend her life indefinitely.

#### [[Session 34 - Mourning Bells]]

During a ritual on the [[Frostway]], [[Kai]] received a visitation from the moon goddess [[Selûne]], who warned of the corrupting influence of her divine sister and declared the Empress beyond redemption.

#### [[Session 44 - The Elven Archmage]]

Acting on intelligence recovered from a [[The Red Priests|Red Priestess]], [[Ayana Syndrosa]] led the party to a hidden sanctum where *Speak with Dead* was performed on the priestess's severed head. The ritual confirmed that the Red Priests operate out of [[Valfyria]] in direct service to the Empress — and that the Empress herself serves [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow]]. The name Kharazoth — a lieutenant from the [[Chaos Wars]], thought long dead — was enough to visibly shatter [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana]]'s composure.

#### [[Session 47 - Confluence of the Seven]]

At [[The Confluence of the Seven]] in [[Sumara, The Shining City|Sumara]], [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana]] briefed [[Illythia Voss]] on everything the party had uncovered: the Empress, her allegiance to [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow|Kharazoth]], and the operations of the [[The Red Priests|Red Priests]] out of [[Valfyria]].

#### [[Session 49 - The Silver Sage]]

In the [[Vault of Memories]] beneath [[Sumara, The Shining City|Sumara]], [[The Bloody Nails]] presented everything they knew of Morganna to [[Praetor'Varanous]], the ancient silver dragon. Drawing on centuries of accumulated scholarship, Praetor'Varanous reached a definitive conclusion: Morganna is a lich, her soul anchored to a hidden vessel and her appearance concealed behind a glamour his visions could not pierce. To kill her permanently, the phylactery must be destroyed before her body can reform. He warned that her true power may exceed anything the party has yet faced.

## Trivia

- Despite ruling for over a century, Morganna has never visibly aged. The Empire's citizens tend not to question this aloud.
- The violet orb has accompanied her since the day she landed on Valtorra's shores. It is the one detail that appears in every account of her, however brief — because it is the one thing she is physically incapable of leaving behind.

## DM Notes

- **The orb is her phylactery:** The party knows she's a lich with a hidden vessel — they do not yet know the orb itself is it. It sits in plain sight at every public appearance, carried openly, because she has no choice. Destroying the orb kills her permanently; while it exists and is intact, her body reforms after destruction. Any confrontation must grapple with the fact that she will defend the orb before anything else.
- **Not a glamour — genuine preservation:** Praetor concluded her appearance is "a glamour his visions could not pierce." The truth is stranger: there is nothing to pierce. The orb drains life essence from nearby creatures and uses it to genuinely sustain her body, keeping it biologically frozen at twenty-one. Divination struggles with it because it reads as a living human, not as an illusion concealing something else. This distinction matters if the party ever tries to dispel her "disguise" — there is no disguise to dispel.
- **The orb's dual function:** Beyond serving as her phylactery, the orb actively drains life essence from nearby creatures (sustaining her glamour) and can bend the will of individuals she focuses it on. The Hands of the Empire were the first created this way; the ritual has been refined and industrialised since. This means prolonged proximity to Morganna is mechanically dangerous even outside direct combat.
- **Kharazoth's leash:** Morganna's empire-building serves Kharazoth's accumulation of wealth for the Tiamat ritual. Destroying Morganna does not solve the dragon problem, and Kharazoth has not yet revealed himself to the party. The wealth the Empire has amassed over a century has been flowing somewhere — that thread is still open.
- **The Eyes of the Empire:** The party does not yet know this arm exists. They are covert, report directly to Morganna, and operate outside normal Imperial hierarchy. Session 49's memory visions showed a hidden Eye among the Hands aboard a prison ship — the party named the falsity but the implication (that Eyes infiltrate the Hands) may not yet have landed. If the party begins meaningfully destabilising the Empire, expect an Eye to appear.
- **Redemption potential:** Morganna originally sought freedom and the preservation of history, not conquest as an end in itself. Kharazoth manipulated rather than converted her. Whether his hold is so total she's past reaching, or whether removing his influence could recover something of the person she was, is a question worth leaving deliberately open. A confrontation where the party tries to reason with her — rather than simply destroy her — could yield interesting results.
- **Unrevealed to the party:** That the orb is her phylactery. That Kharazoth personally forged *Dread's Hunger* and created Blackmarsh as a death knight. That the Eyes of the Empire exist. That Morganna's true age is ~130 and her origin is a pre-Shattering noble bloodline. That she accidentally killed her brother, which set all of this in motion.
