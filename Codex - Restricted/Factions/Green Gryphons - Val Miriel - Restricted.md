## The Mine Deception

The party told [[Porta Hale]] that the [[Ruby Falls Goldmine]] had naturally collapsed and was empty of gold. This is false. The mine is operational — [[Nelfandriss the Banished]] and her followers are mining quietly under a private treaty the party negotiated directly. Porta and the chapter paid for what they were told was a failed investigation and closed the file. They have no outstanding interest in the mine.

[[Lord Norak Vara]], the contract's patron, is equally uninformed. He believes the mine is lost.

## Plot Hooks

- If the chapter ever reopens the mine question — through Vara pressing for another look, independent intelligence, or a second contract — the party's deception unravels. Their standing with the Val Miriel chapter, and potentially with the guild at large, would take a serious hit.
- Porta deflected an Imperial raid and then asked the party to leave. He doesn't know what they're hiding. If he ever found out the full extent of it — the mine, the treaty, the lie — his reaction would be a mixture of professional fury and grudging respect for the audacity.
