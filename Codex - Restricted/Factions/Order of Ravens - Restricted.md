The **Order of Ravens** is the preeminent criminal and intelligence network of [[Valtorra]] — a continent-spanning brotherhood of thieves, spies, and mercenaries bound together by a single shared symbol and a code of silence kept unto death. For centuries it sold its talents to the highest bidder and bent the affairs of nations to its own quiet profit. When the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]] rose and declared open season on its nests, the Order shed the neutrality it had always prized and became the sharpest blade of the resistance, running agents into the Empire's inner circles and lighting the fuse on rebellion wherever it could. Its head — the Grand Raven — is the merchant [[Gemma Finegold]], a truth no Nest Father has ever been told.

## Purpose & Goals

The Order exists to move what others cannot — secrets, contraband, and people — through a continent that has grown increasingly hostile to all three. For most of its history its only loyalty was to itself: it manipulated markets, courts, and rivalries for advantage, took no side in Valtorra's wars, and survived precisely because it could not be counted on by anyone.

The Empire changed that calculus. With the [[Hand of The Empire|Hands of the Empire]] hunting Ravens to the grave, neutrality became a luxury the Order could no longer afford. Today its aims are openly anti-Imperial: to gather intelligence on Imperial movements, to deny the Empire the unchecked consolidation of power, and to keep alive the rebellions that might one day unseat it. Under its current Grand Raven that purpose is sharpened by a personal edge — Gemma Finegold's own war with the Empire began the day it executed her mother in the streets of [[Silverdeep]].

## Structure & Leadership

The Order is a cellular network rather than a chain of command, built so that no single capture can unravel the whole. Ranks are earned and authority is deliberately compartmentalised:

- **Grand Raven** — the Order's supreme leader, whose identity is concealed from virtually every member, including the Nest Fathers. The role is held by **[[Gemma Finegold]]**, the Finegold caravan-master, elevated to it by her predecessor shortly before the campaign began. No Nest Father has ever seen the Grand Raven's face unobscured; even in rare direct meetings she appears hidden.
- **Nest** — a local chapter in a city where the Order keeps a presence.
- **Nest Mother / Nest Father** — the leader of a local Nest.
- **Talon** — an elite operative; the inner circle of a Nest.
- **Raven** — a full member.
- **Fledgling** — a new recruit, still proving their worth.

Membership is for life. Operatives stay until death or quiet retirement, both to preserve their craft and to protect the Order's secrets; execution is sanctioned for any operative who becomes a danger to the network.

Gemma's genius is in how she gives orders. Commands reach a Nest through fixed drop points whose locations are known only to the Nest Father — but she also delivers them in person, hidden inside her own trade. A Raven receives a trade letter or a passing word from a travelling merchant and never suspects that the merchant is the Grand Raven herself, issuing the order to their face. The system is built so that no single point of failure can betray the whole, and so that the one person who could betray all of it is the last anyone would suspect.

## Operations

- **Skyreach intelligence (Darmouth).** Ella Sable was captured attempting to steal the schematics of [[Skyreach]] from the Darmouth Lawmaster's office; Uric Graad and the party broke her out, and the intelligence was carried onward toward Camaar. This was Gemma's first operation as Grand Raven, and the recruitment of the Bloody Nails to carry it was her own initiative.
- **The Bastion heist (Val Miriel).** The Val Miriel Nest broke into [[The Bastion]] and made off with sensitive Imperial documents — a clean operation until the Fledgling Lia broke from the plan and attempted to assassinate [[General Varan Brado]] without sanction, an act that helped spark the Val Miriel riots and left her fate an open internal problem for Thaddeus.
- **The Val Luminor rescue.** A captured Raven was extracted from the dungeons beneath [[Val Luminor]]. She returned with word that full-blooded elves were being held and interrogated there — testimony that underpins [[Jarek]]'s belief that [[Eryndor]]'s mother may still be alive.

## Symbols & Iconography

The Order marks the cities it works in with hidden ravens — small representations of the bird worked into stonework, signage, and chalk, easily missed by the uninitiated. To those who can read them they signal safe houses, meeting points, and dangers to avoid. Field codenames work the same way: earned naturally as an operative proves themselves and bestowed informally, a piece of the Order that hides in plain sight.

## History

The Order was founded by [[Korvin Blackfeather]], its first Grand Raven, who unified Valtorra's scattered criminals under a single symbol after his legendary heist from a tyrant king — an exploit remembered as the **Raven's Flight**. For centuries the Order he built operated as a politically neutral power, manipulating societal dynamics for its own ends and never taking a side in the broader struggles of the continent.

The rise of the Valtorran Empire ended that long detachment. The Hands of the Empire declared open season on the Ravens, and nests were driven underground or wiped out. Roughly fifty years ago, under the direction of the Grand Raven **[[Ebon]]**, the Order shed its neutrality and became an active resistance — running agents into the Empire's inner circles, instigating uprisings, and working to keep the Empire from consolidating unchecked power. Ebon, a dwarf, was the architect of the Dwarven rebellions of that era; his long lifespan and his personal bond with the dwarven peoples made both his fifty-year tenure and those rebellions possible. The turn was never a comfortable one, and a cultural tension still runs through the Order between an old guard who would return to neutrality and those who believe the Empire has made it impossible.

Ebon found Gemma Finegold in Silverdeep not long after the Empire publicly executed her mother. She was a young woman already acting on her own — she had coordinated a clandestine rescue of several compromised Raven operatives, smuggling them out of the city and keeping sensitive intelligence (possibly intelligence that would have led the Empire straight to Ebon) out of Imperial hands, all without any idea who Ebon was. He knew exactly who she was. Over the years that followed he groomed her for the mantle: folding her into the Order's work, giving her steadily greater responsibility, and watching how she thought. He saw in her the qualities he had once had — a merchant's freedom to move, a wound deep enough to sustain a lifetime's commitment, and a mind that worked in networks and leverage rather than blades. When his age made the question of succession unavoidable, he gave the Order to her. He is now retired in Silverdeep, old even for a dwarf but alive, watching from a distance and trusting Gemma completely; he would surface only if the Order faced something existential, and even then only to her.

## Relations

- **[[The Finegolds]]:** Far more than an allied caravan company — the Finegolds *are* the Grand Raven's own network. Their caravans serve the Order as a smuggling route and an information line across the continent, and they carry Gemma's orders without the rank and file ever knowing the source. The relationship that the wider Order treats as a useful partnership is, in truth, the Grand Raven hiding in plain sight inside her own family trade.
- **[[Veiled Cubs]] (Camaar):** A quiet mutual respect — local, informal, and short of any formal alliance.
- **[[The Green Gryphons|Green Gryphons]]:** Avoided. The Order steers well clear of them.
- **Other rebel groups:** No formal alliances. The Order prefers independence and is wary of any entanglement that might expose the network.

## Trivia

- The Order's entire architecture is built around a single principle — that a network with no visible head cannot be decapitated. Every rank, drop point, and concealed identity exists to serve that one idea.
- A Raven's codename is never assigned; it is earned and then simply starts being used, so that even the naming of an operative leaves no paper trail.

## DM Notes

- **Unrevealed leadership:** Gemma Finegold is the Grand Raven. The Bloody Nails have worked for her, through her, from their very first job — the Skyreach mission was her opening move, and recruiting them was her own idea. They have never known whose orders they were following.
- **The figurehead's predecessor:** [[Ebon]] groomed and elevated Gemma; he is alive and retired in [[Silverdeep]], and would only resurface for an existential threat — and only to her.
- **[[Barak Stormrider|Barak]] (the engineered friendship):** [[Barak Stormrider]], captain of [[The Albatross]] and leader of [[The Mawbreakers]], has no connection to the Finegold family — Gemma sought him out. She deliberately targeted him, "bumping into" him in a Camaar tavern because she needed a reliable Mawbreaker connection. The friendship is genuine on his end; the introduction was engineered on hers. He does not know she is the Grand Raven.
- **Hidden agenda:** Beneath the resistance mission, Gemma runs the Order as a single, deniable instrument of her own — every node compartmentalised so that her hand never shows, even to her own commanders.
- **Plot hooks:** Elara Sya's testimony about full-blooded elves held beneath Val Luminor feeds directly into Jarek's belief about Eryndor's mother; the Order is the thread that could pull the party toward Val Luminor.
- **Open thread:** Whether the party ever learns Gemma is the Grand Raven — and how that revelation lands, given everything she has done alongside them.
