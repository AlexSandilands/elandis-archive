> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Order of Ravens]]*

## True Leadership
- The Grand Raven — concealed from virtually every member, including the Nest Fathers — is the merchant [[Gemma Finegold]], the Finegold caravan-master. She was elevated to the role by her predecessor [[Ebon]] shortly before the campaign began. No Nest Father has ever seen her face unobscured.
- Gemma's anti-Imperial purpose carries a personal edge: her war with the Empire began the day it publicly executed her mother in the streets of [[Silverdeep]].
- **The Bloody Nails have worked for her, through her, from their very first job.** The Skyreach mission was her opening move as Grand Raven, and recruiting them was her own initiative. They have never known whose orders they were following.

## How She Gives Orders
- Gemma delivers commands in person, hidden inside her own trade. A Raven receives a trade letter or a passing word from a travelling merchant and never suspects the merchant *is* the Grand Raven, issuing the order to their face.
- The system has no single point of failure that can betray the whole — and the one person who could betray all of it is the last anyone would suspect.

## The Finegolds (true relationship)
- Far more than an allied caravan company — the Finegolds *are* the Grand Raven's own network. Their caravans serve as the Order's smuggling route and information line, and carry Gemma's orders without the rank and file knowing the source. The wider Order treats this as a useful partnership; in truth it is the Grand Raven hiding in plain sight inside her own family trade.

## Ebon, the Predecessor
- [[Ebon]], a dwarf, was Grand Raven for ~50 years and the architect of the Dwarven rebellions of that era; his long lifespan and personal bond with the dwarven peoples made both possible.
- Ebon found Gemma in [[Silverdeep]] not long after the Empire executed her mother. She had already, on her own initiative, coordinated a clandestine rescue of several compromised Raven operatives — smuggling them out and keeping sensitive intelligence (possibly intelligence that would have led the Empire straight to Ebon) out of Imperial hands, all without knowing who Ebon was. He knew exactly who she was.
- Over the following years he groomed her for the mantle — folding her into the Order's work, giving her steadily greater responsibility, watching how she thought. He saw in her the qualities he had once had: a merchant's freedom to move, a wound deep enough to sustain a lifetime's commitment, and a mind that worked in networks and leverage rather than blades. When age made succession unavoidable, he gave the Order to her.
- He is now retired in Silverdeep, old even for a dwarf but alive, watching from a distance and trusting Gemma completely. He would surface only for an existential threat to the Order — and even then only to her.

## Barak (the engineered friendship)
- [[Barak Stormrider|Barak]], captain of [[The Albatross]] and leader of [[The Mawbreakers]], has **no** connection to the Finegold family — Gemma sought him out. She deliberately "bumped into" him in a Camaar tavern because she needed a reliable Mawbreaker connection. The friendship is genuine on his end; the introduction was engineered on hers. He does not know she is the Grand Raven.

## Hidden Agenda & Operational Detail
- Beneath the resistance mission, Gemma runs the Order as a single deniable instrument of her own — every node compartmentalised so her hand never shows, even to her own commanders.
- The Bastion heist: Lia's unsanctioned assassination attempt left her fate an open internal problem for Thaddeus.

## Plot Hooks & Open Threads

- Whether the party ever learns Gemma is the Grand Raven — and how that revelation lands, given everything she has done alongside them.
