> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Voice of the Empire]]*

## Structure & Leadership

The Voices operate on a hierarchy that mirrors the Empire's own rank structure, with a single head of the cadre advising [[Empress Morganna Eventide]] directly from [[Val Luminor]]. Below that, individual Voices are assigned to postings across the continent, each operating with considerable autonomy within their court but answering ultimately to the centre.

Unlike the [[Hand of The Empire|Hands]], who are deployed in cohorts and trained together in large formations, the Voices tend to operate alone or in pairs. The scarcity of mages powerful enough to serve makes large groupings impractical; a single Voice at a governorship is the standard deployment.

## Deployment

One Voice is assigned to each of the six governor's courts on [[Valtorra]], maintaining a permanent advisory presence at every regional seat of power. Beyond those fixed postings, Voices are occasionally deployed as battlefield assets and personal aides to senior military commanders, bringing arcane capability directly to the field when strategic objectives demand it. [[Marcelle the Broken]] exemplified this dual role, assigned to [[Dread General Gerard Blackmarsh]] during the Imperial siege of [[Val Noren]], where she served as both battlefield mage and trusted subordinate.

The head of the Voices holds the most prestigious posting: direct access to the Empress in [[Val Luminor]], advising from within the Citadel.

## Recruitment

The Voices draw their membership almost exclusively from humans and dwarves — the two peoples the Empire considers most reliable for the conditioning the programme demands. Dwarven Voices are often sourced through the Prismatic Academy in [[Glimmerforge]], which operates under Imperial leave; human Voices are trained at the [[Arcana Luminorium]] from a young age.

Elves are not recruited as a rule. [[Marcelle the Broken]] was a rare exception — an elven child acquired by the Empire and put through the full conditioning process — and the result, though operationally effective, was considered unusual enough that it was never systematically repeated. The Empire prefers recruits it can shape from youth; elves, as a people who have largely withdrawn from the material world, offer little opportunity for the kind of sustained acquisition the programme requires.

Mages of sufficient power are scarce. The cadre is never large.

## History

The [[Arcana Luminorium]] in [[Val Luminor]] pre-dates the [[Valtorran Empire]], having stood as an independent institution of magical learning since the age of reconstruction that followed the [[Chaos Wars]]. When [[Empress Morganna Eventide|the Empress]] took the continent city by city a hundred years ago, the Arcana Luminorium was one of the prizes — not taken by force but by quiet institutional capture, its faculty gradually replaced and its curriculum reshaped to serve the Empire's needs.

The Voices as a formal cadre emerged from that transformation. As the Empire consolidated control across [[Valtorra]]'s six kingdoms, soldiers and spies alone were insufficient to hold what had been taken; arcane authority was needed at every seat of power. The Voices became the answer — a small, elite corps trained not just for battle but for courts, for counsel, and for the particular skill of making power look inevitable.
