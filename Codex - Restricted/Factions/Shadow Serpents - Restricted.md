## Leadership & Core Crew

The named leaders the party has never met. Every Serpent the party has actually fought or routed has been a rank-and-file thug — the alley ringleader in Session 9 was an unnamed lieutenant, not one of these figures. On paper this is a genuinely dangerous crew: an assassin, a Whispers bard, a Goliath barbarian, a Gloom Stalker, and a Battlemaster. The entire joke of the faction lives in the gap between this roster and the thugs the party keeps swatting — the talent exists, it simply never shows up. Played straight, any of these five could be a real threat; played for laughs, they are the leadership that has somehow presided over the most one-sided feud in Old Camaar without once attending it in person.

- Serak the Venomous — leader. Half-Orc Rogue (Assassin). A towering, muscular figure with a scarred face and piercing green eyes, in dark serpent-patterned leather. Calculating, ruthless, strategic; holds grudges and seeks revenge on anyone who crosses his path. Expert in stealth, poison use, and close-quarters combat, with deadly precision with a blade — hence "the Venomous."
- Lira Nightshade — second-in-command and the gang's face. Human Bard (College of Whispers). A lithe woman with long dark hair and a captivating gaze, favouring stylish but practical dark clothing. Charismatic, cunning, persuasive; manipulates and deceives with illusion and enchantment magic and enjoys toying with her marks with a silver tongue.
- Grim "The Crusher" — enforcer and muscle. Goliath Barbarian (Path of the Ancestral Guardian). A hulking figure with stone-like skin and a menacing presence, wearing minimal armour and relying on natural toughness and raw strength. Loyal, straightforward, loves the thrill of battle; follows Serak's orders without question and takes pride in being the gang's protector. Powerful melee, damage resistance, abilities to shield allies.
- Vex Shadowstep — scout. Wood Elf Ranger (Gloom Stalker). A slender, sharp-featured elf in a cloak that seems to blend into the dark. Quiet, observant, pragmatic; scouts ahead and sets up ambushes, and prefers to work from the shadows and avoid direct confrontation unless necessary. Enhanced stealth, tracking, ranged proficiency.
- Kara Ironfist — bruiser and front-liner. Dwarf Fighter (Battle Master). A stout dwarf with a no-nonsense attitude and a love of combat, in heavy armour with a massive warhammer. Bold, stubborn, fiercely protective of her comrades; relishes testing herself against worthy foes and leads the charge. Mastery of combat manoeuvres, superior defence, battlefield resilience.

## The Alley Vendetta

The Serpents' repeated, doomed returns to harass the party are driven by Serak's personal vendetta. The party killed several of his gang in the Session 9 back-alley ambush, and the Serpents have been after retribution ever since. Serak holds grudges and hunts those who cross him — and few have crossed him as badly as this band.

## The Session 12 Raid

The black-hooded assassins who slipped into the party's inn rooms in the night during [[Session 12 - How to Start a Rebellion]] were the Shadow Serpents, on a retribution strike for their dead in the alley. [[Eryndor|Eryndor's]] Dagger of Warning woke the party and the attackers dove out the windows and escaped clean. The party never identified who attacked them that night, and has no idea it was the Serpents — so this beat is kept out of the public entry.

Note for continuity: that raid happened to coincide with the [[Finegolds]]' warning about a "Lady in a deep black cloak." The Lady is a separate, unrelated thread — the overlap was pure coincidence, not the Serpents. Don't conflate the two.
