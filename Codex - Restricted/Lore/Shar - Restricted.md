**Shar**, the Lady of Vengeance, is no faded name but a living **God of Old** — the dark sister and eternal opposite of [[Selûne]], a true and ancient power that the world has lost so utterly it does not know it has lost anything at all. She is a goddess of suffering, vengeance, and chaos, and her strength grows on cruelty wherever it is done; she serves no master and no cause but her own appetite for the world to hurt. And like her bright sister, she has lately begun to move — reaching, in the shadows, for the soul of [[Selûne]]'s chosen.

## Overview

Shar is one of the **Gods of Old**: the true gods of the cosmos, older than the [[The Shattering|Shattering]], lesser only than the [[World Tree]] and greater by far than the [[The Pantheon|Seven Divines]] the world mistakes for gods. She is [[Selûne]]'s twin and her perfect inverse — where the Moonmaiden is light, mercy, and forgiveness, Shar is the dark, the wound that will not heal, and the cold pleasure taken in another's pain.

What makes her dangerous is not an army or a church but her nature: she feeds on suffering directly, and so she needs no worshippers to grow strong, only a world that goes on hurting itself. For an age she has been content to sit in that long, patient dark and let mortals feed her as they always do. What is new is that she has stirred to *act* — and her chosen field is the same one her sister has moved onto: the young man [[Kai]], whom Selûne would make her champion of the light, and whom Shar means to turn.

## Divinity

### Lady of Vengeance

Shar's sphere is suffering, vengeance, spite, and chaos — and beneath them the older, colder things the deepest lore gives her: loss, secrets, and the primordial dark from which the light was a wound. Her defining trait is *how* she draws power: not from prayer or sacrifice but from **needless anguish**, at every scale. A massacre feeds her; so does a betrayal, a casual humiliation, a mean joke at someone's expense, a pointless little cruelty done for the pleasure of it. Every spiteful act in the world is, in effect, an offering she never had to ask for.

### A God of Old

Shar stands in the same hidden order as her sister — beneath the [[World Tree]], among the **Gods of Old** the cosmology names the True Gods, above the ascended [[The Pantheon|Seven Divines]]. But where the other old gods withdrew out of restraint, Shar's withdrawal is patience, not principle. She serves **no one**: not the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]], not [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow|Kharazoth]], not the Dragon Queen. A second [[The Shattering|Shattering]] would glut her on suffering beyond measure, and so their works please her — but the alignment is incidental, and she would as gladly see *them* suffer. Her only loyalty is to chaos and to her own appetite.

## Worship

Shar has no faith, and she has never needed one. She is not a god who must be fed by ritual; she is fed by the world's own malice, and the world supplies it freely. If she has servants at all, most do not know they serve her — every petty tyrant and casual cruelty does her work unbidden.

Her power is not constant through the month. Her **ascendant nights** are the inverse of her sister's: when the lesser moon **Sha** rides full while **Sel** is dark and gone — falling four times a year, on the first of each elemental season — she is at her strongest and Selûne at her weakest. No rite marks these nights, for no one keeps her calendar; but they are the nights her reach is longest.

## Myths & Deeds

### The Two Sisters

Shar and [[Selûne]] are the oldest of opposites, bound to the moon and to each other since before the [[The Shattering|Shattering]]: the light and the dark of the same sphere, mercy against malice, the open hand against the closed. The two moons are named for them both — **Sel** for the Moonmaiden, **Sha** for her shadow — and the nights are divided between them, each sister's full ride answered by the other's dark. Their struggle is older than any war the world remembers, and it has now found a new and narrow field: the soul of the mortal Selûne would raise as her Chosen. Where the bright sister offers Kai the path of light, Shar offers the slow seduction of the dark — to break him, or better, to *keep* him, and make of the moon's champion a thing of vengeance instead.

## In Memory & Belief

That Shar is forgotten even more thoroughly than Selûne is no accident, and no tragedy either: who, after all, raises a temple to spite? Where the Moonmaiden's worship lapsed into a saint's, Shar's may never have been open worship at all — a goddess who feeds on cruelty has no need to be loved, only indulged, and mortals have always indulged her without knowing her name. The void where her memory should be is the truest thing the world believes about her: she is the dark it does not look at, and she prefers it so.

## [[The Bloody Nails|Campaign: The Bloody Nails]]

#### [[Session 20 - The Streets of Val Miriel]]

When [[High Priest Oldir]] read the truth in [[Kai]]'s starry form, his warning named two powers, not one. Kai had been touched by [[Selûne]] — but her **dark sister, the Lady of Vengeance**, had fixed her gaze on him as well, and that, the old priest knew, was the graver danger.

#### [[Session 26 - A Midnight Romp]]

The mark of her hold showed plainly: half the stars of Kai's Starry Form burned **black**. Oldir recognised it for the Dark Sister's corruption, named Kai a Chosen already half-claimed, and urged him to walk the path of light to break her grip — the open statement of a contest the priest understood only the edges of.

## Trivia

- Shar needs no holy day, yet she has her nights all the same — the four a year when her moon rides full over a dark and absent Sel, the exact shadow of her sister [[Selûne]]'s Unshadowed.
- She is the rare god whose strength a faithful temple would do nothing to increase — only a crueller world would.

## DM Notes

- **Unrevealed truth:** Shar is a living God of Old / True God — [[Selûne]]'s dark twin, goddess of suffering and vengeance, who feeds directly on cruelty and needs no worshippers. The world knows none of this; even the party has only Oldir's fragment ("the Lady of Vengeance"). Keep the tier consistent with the restricted [[World Tree]], [[The Shattering]], and [[Selûne]] pages (World Tree ▸ Gods of Old ▸ Seven Divines).
- **Why now:** her stirring is the shadow of Selûne's — as the bright sister breaks her silence to raise a Chosen against a feared second [[The Shattering|Shattering]], Shar moves to corrupt that same Chosen ([[Kai]]) into a dark champion instead. The black stars are her work.
- **Allegiance:** none. Not [[Valtorran Empire|the Empire]], [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow|Kharazoth]], or the Dragon Queen — she serves only chaos and her own appetite. Their schemes feed her, so she may *aid or sabotage* any side if it sows more suffering; treat her as a wildcard, not a member of the dragon plot.
- **Plot hooks:** the tug-of-war over Kai (black stars vs the path of light); a moment of mass suffering (a massacre, a great betrayal) as a power-spike for her; the possibility she raises an unwitting agent or a true dark cult as the Shattering nears; her ascendant nights (Sha full / Sel dark) as windows when her reach is longest.
- **Open threads:** does she want Kai *turned* into her champion, or simply *broken* to deny him to Selûne? Is anyone already serving her unknowingly, feeding her through ordinary cruelty? What would a dark champion of hers actually become?
- **FR source to mine lightly:** Shar — darkness, loss, secrets, forgotten things, the offer of oblivion to the grieving, and the eternal twin-struggle with Selûne.
