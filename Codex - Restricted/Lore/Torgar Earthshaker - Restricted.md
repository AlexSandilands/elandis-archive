**Torgar Earthshaker** was a dwarf druid — the mortal champion chosen by **[[Grumbar]]**, the Earthlord, one of the Gods of Old — raised at the end of the [[The Shattering|Shattering]] to a lingering demigodhood. The world worships him as an eternal god of earth and stone; what it worships is an ascended mortal who stood in for the elder god the world has entirely forgotten.

## Overview

The truth behind the faith follows the same pattern documented in the restricted [[The Pantheon]] and [[The Shattering]] pages: Grumbar, the Earthlord, is a God of Old — a true, ancient power — who could not enter the Chaos Wars directly, so instead raised a dwarf druid as his champion. That champion held the line, gave everything at the Shattering's end, and ascended to the demigodhood the world now calls Torgar Earthshaker. Grumbar withdrew into silence behind him, and the worship that once flowed to the elder god has flowed, ever since, to his champion instead.

The relic, **Grumbar's Fist**, names the elder god openly — the clearest fragment of the buried truth in plain sight. In the mountain holds it survives as a soldier's oath whose source the faith no longer traces, exactly the kind of thread that unravels the whole history for anyone who pulls on it.

The [[The Stoneheart|Stoneheart]] of [[Val Noren]] is the chief seat of this displaced worship.

## DM Notes

- **The core secret:** Torgar Earthshaker was a mortal dwarf druid, champion of [[Grumbar]] (a God of Old), who ascended to demigodhood at the [[The Shattering|Shattering]]. The faith worships an ascended mortal, not an eternal god. Keep consistent with the restricted [[The Pantheon]], [[World Tree]], and [[The Shattering]] pages.
- **The relic thread:** *Grumbar's Fist* names the elder god openly in the faith's most sacred artifact. A cleric or scholar who begins connecting relic names to old-god names would find this thread waiting.
- **Grumbar's silence:** unlike [[Selûne]], there is no sign Grumbar is stirring — no Chosen, no fragment of direct presence in the campaign. Whether that silence is peace, withdrawal, or something else is deliberately open.
- **Open threads:** what Grumbar knows or wants; whether a second Shattering might move him as it has moved Selûne; how the Val Noren clergy would respond to the truth; the Stoneheart's crypts and the party's existing investment in the city as a potential future hook.
