**Selûne**, the Moonmaiden, is the goddess of the moon — and she is no faded memory but a living **God of Old**, one of the true and ancient powers that stand, unseen, behind the worshipped [[The Pantheon|Seven Divines]]. She is a goddess of moon and light, of forgiveness and the shelter of the weak; the world has forgotten her, and that forgetting was never an accident. What is new — and what should never have happened — is that she has begun, after an age of silence, to reach directly into the world again. She is afraid.

## Overview

Selûne is one of the **Gods of Old**: the true gods of the cosmos, older than the [[The Shattering|Shattering]], who by ancient habit do not act directly in the mortal world. They are the powers the deepest cosmology names the **True Gods** — real divinities, lesser than the [[World Tree]] that stands above them all, but greater by far than the [[The Pantheon|Seven Divines]] the world mistakes for eternal gods. Selûne is the genuine goddess of the moon; the Divine **[[Elaris Moonsong]]** whom the faithful pray to is the mortal champion she herself raised up, ascended and venerated until his worship buried hers.

That she is reaching into the world now — marking a mortal with her own hand, choosing a Chosen — is extraordinary, and a measure of her fear. The Gods of Old do not do this; the same restraint that keeps them hidden kept her silent for centuries. She has broken it because she believes a **second [[The Shattering|Shattering]] is coming**, and soon, and that the world will need a champion of the light to meet it.

## Divinity

### Lady of the Moon

Her sphere is the moon and its light, the stars, navigation, and the wanderers and the lost who steer by night — and beneath it, **forgiveness, redemption, and the protection of the innocent and the weak**. This much the public page has right; it is the gentleness, not the divinity, the world remembers.

### A God of Old

The truth the world has lost is the whole order of things. Above all stands the [[World Tree]]; beneath it, the **Gods of Old** — the True Gods, Selûne among them; and beneath *them*, the [[The Pantheon|Seven Divines]], who are not eternal gods at all but the seven mortal champions the Gods of Old empowered to end the [[Chaos Wars]] and seal the [[The Shattering|breach]]. The Seven ascended in their sacrifice to a lingering demigodhood, real powers that answer real prayers — and as the centuries passed and the old gods kept their silence, the world forgot the powers behind the champions and gave its worship to the saviours alone. Selûne's eclipse by Elaris is that whole history in miniature: the goddess raised the champion, the champion died a god, and the world kept the one it could see.

## Worship

Everything the moon faith keeps as Elaris's is, at its root, Selûne's. The rites she is owed survive inside his worship, their true author forgotten — and the calendar of the faith is keyed, secretly, to her.

The **Unshadowed** is the proof of it: the four nights a year when the greater moon Sel rides full while the lesser moon Sha goes wholly dark are the nights Selûne stands at her strongest and her shadow is blind — and so they are the faith's nights of grace and **the Pardon**, of forgiveness sought and given. The faithful keep the rite without knowing it is hers, or why mercy should be strongest when the dark moon vanishes. The **Moon's Tear** and the **prophecy of the Moon's Chosen** are likewise hers in truth: the prophecy is no dead legend but her own standing mechanism, the means by which a God of Old forbidden to fight may yet raise a mortal who can.

## Myths & Deeds

### The Champion of the Moon

In the time of the [[Chaos Wars]], when the Gods of Old chose champions rather than risk open war, Selûne raised the silver-haired elf cleric **[[Elaris Moonsong]]** as hers — one of the seven who joined their borrowed power to cast back the Dragon Queen and seal the [[The Shattering|breach]]. He was consumed in the working and ascended in death to the lingering godhood the world now worships. The goddess who made him she let the world forget.

### The Two Sisters

Selûne is not the only old power bound to the moon. Her dark sister — known to the world, where she is known at all, only as the **[[Shar|Lady of Vengeance]]** — is her shadow and eternal opposite: a God of Old who delights in suffering, vengeance, and chaos, and whose strength feeds on cruelty of every scale. The two moons are named for them both, **Sel** for the Moonmaiden and **Sha** for her sister, and the nights are divided between them — for every Unshadowed when Selûne stands full and her sister blind, there is its inverse, when Sha rides full over a dark and absent Sel. The sisters' struggle is older than the Shattering, and it has now found a new field: the soul of Selûne's would-be Chosen.

## In Memory & Belief

The world's forgetting of Selûne is near-total, and — this is the part the public page cannot say — it was, in a sense, chosen. The Gods of Old withdrew behind their champions and let their own names lapse; Selûne's silence is the same restraint that has kept the True Gods from striking their enemies directly since the war that wounded the [[World Tree]]. The scholar's footnote, the orphaned prophecy, the moons whose names no one can place — these are not the ruins of a dead faith but the embers of a living goddess who chose to be unseen.

What has changed is that she is unseen no longer. To break that silence — to mark a mortal, to move a Chosen across the board — is for a God of Old an act close to desperation, and it should be read as exactly that.

### [[The Bloody Nails|Campaign: The Bloody Nails]]

#### [[Session 04 - To Free a Raven]]

[[Kai]] first bore her mark in the temple at [[Darmouth]] — a holy symbol etched upon his hand by the goddess's own touch, the first move of a God of Old reaching directly into the world.

#### [[Session 20 - The Streets of Val Miriel]]

When Kai revealed his starry form to [[High Priest Oldir]] at [[The High Sept]], the terrified priest let slip the heresy his line has guarded in silence for generations: that the celebrated Seven Gods are "false idols masking the existence of the True Gods," ascended champions whose worship buried the older powers. He named Kai as touched by **Selûne** — a true goddess, older and greater than the Seven — and warned that her **dark sister, the [[Shar|Lady of Vengeance]]**, had also fixed her gaze upon him.

#### [[Session 26 - A Midnight Romp]]

Pressed again, Oldir recounted the full prophecy of the Moon's Chosen and, seeing the **black stars** scattered through Kai's Starry Form, recognised the mark of the Dark Sister's corruption taking hold. He named Kai a potential — if corrupted — Chosen, sheltered him, and urged him to walk the path of light to break her grip: the terms, though the priest did not know it, of the two sisters' contest over the boy's soul. The moonstone Kai bore, matching the gem in [[Elaris Moonsong - Restricted|Elaris Moonsong]]'s altar, was the first of the three Stones of the Moon.

#### [[Session 49 - The Silver Sage]]

[[Praetor'Varanous]] confirmed that the second of the three moonstones lies in Oldir's keeping at the High Sept — narrowing the road of Selûne's prophecy and the race for her Chosen.

## Trivia

- The two moons of [[Elandis]], **Sel** and **Sha**, are named for the Moonmaiden and her dark sister — the last of the goddess's true names still spoken daily, by a world that has forgotten they are names at all.
- The prophecy's "**Lady** of the Moon" is not a corruption: the Lady is real, and the male moon-god the faith venerates is merely the champion who eclipsed her.
- Her epithets — *Moonmaiden*, *Lady of the Moon*, *Lady of Night* — are older than the Seven Divines themselves.

## DM Notes

- **Unrevealed truth:** Selûne is a living God of Old / True God, not a faded memory — the genuine goddess of the moon, who *empowered* the Divine [[Elaris Moonsong]] (one of the ascended mortal Seven) and then let the world forget her. The tier (World Tree ▸ Gods of Old ▸ Seven Divines) and the fact that the Seven are ascended mortals are the core secrets; keep them consistent with the restricted [[World Tree]] and [[The Shattering]] pages.
- **Why now:** her direct contact with [[Kai]] is wildly out of character for a God of Old and is driven by her fear that a **second Shattering** is near. She is raising a Chosen against it — the prophecy of the three moonstones and the Celestial Cavern is her live mechanism, not dead legend.
- **The contest:** her dark sister [[Shar]] (the Lady of Vengeance) is working to corrupt Kai into a *dark* champion before Selûne can complete him — the black stars in his Starry Form. Oldir's "path of light" is literally the terms of that fight. See [[Shar]] for her side.
- **Plot hooks:** the hunt for the three Stones of the Moon (one with Oldir at the High Sept) and the hidden Celestial Cavern; whether other Gods of Old begin to stir as the second Shattering nears, Selûne's breaking of silence being perhaps the first; what completing — or losing — the Chosen costs Kai.
- **Open threads:** what exactly Selûne knows of the coming Shattering that the party does not; whether she can act any more directly than through a Chosen, or whether this is the limit of what a God of Old will risk; what she would owe, and to whom, for having reached in at all.
