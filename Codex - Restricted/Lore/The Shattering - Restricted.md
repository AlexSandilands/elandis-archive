**The Shattering** is the cataclysm that ended the old world and made the one [[Elandis]] knows today — the moment, some twenty-five centuries past, when the Dragon Queen [[Tiamat]] tore an opening between her realm and the mortal plane and loosed magic, dragons, and monsters upon a world that had been built to hold none of them. What followed was five hundred years of war and the near-extinction of every people alive, ended only by a sacrifice so total it reset the course of history — and left, beneath the comfort of legend, a wound that never fully closed and an enemy that never truly died.

## Overview

The mortal world — the Material Plane — was not magic-less by accident. It was *grown* that way: the one realm formed from the roots of the [[World Tree]] to be a place of order, logic, and peace, deliberately set apart from the magic that suffuses every other plane. The Shattering's true horror is that [[Tiamat]] poured raw chaos into a world engineered to be without it. She cracked the barrier between the planes, loosed arcane power, chromatic dragons, and monsters through the breach, and set in motion a five-century war to widen that wound until she herself could step through. She was stopped at the last by seven mortal champions — but the gods who armed them paid a price to stay their own hands, the seal those champions died to make was never whole, and the dragons who survived it are still, quietly, finishing what their Queen began.

## History

### The World Before

The age the Shattering destroyed was a world *without* magic — and that absence was by design. Of all the planes of existence, the Material Plane alone was grown from the roots of the [[World Tree]] as a realm of ordered peace and logic, the one place magic did not reach. Its peoples raised vast civilisations of iron and steam and pure learning, never knowing a spell or a monster; the ruins of those cities still litter every continent, their makers forgotten.

Against that ordered world moved [[Tiamat]], who sought to force her way onto the Material Plane and rule it. The deeper truth of the war is *why the gods did not simply stop her themselves.* The True Gods could not move directly against the Dragon Queen: open conflict between powers of that order risks a cosmic war — the very kind of war that, in the unimaginable past, had wounded the World Tree itself and led to its Sundering. Having seen once what gods at war can cost the fabric of reality, they would not pay that price a second time. So they did not fight Tiamat. They chose champions, and made of them something the world had never seen.

### The Chaos Wars

For five hundred years the [[Chaos Wars]] burned across the world — the ancient civilisations against the dragons and the monsters bleeding through the breach. Cities fell, knowledge was lost past recovering, and yet the chaos armed the world even as it ruined it: the spark of magic, once borne by perhaps one mortal in ten thousand, now woke in nearly one in ten. It gave the mortal host a fighting chance. It was not enough. As the war ground toward its end at the site of the original breach, the dragons fell back to defend the wound, and it began to look as though they would hold it.

### The Sacrifice of the Seven

Forbidden to strike Tiamat themselves, the gods did the next thing: they **imbued seven mortal champions with a measure of divine power greater than any follower had ever been granted** — raising them, for a time, to something near demigods. These are the "seven archmages" of the folk account. At the breach, the Seven joined their borrowed power into a single ritual of staggering magnitude and struck at the heart of the wound: they cast the Dragon Queen back into her own realm and sealed the tear in reality. The release was apocalyptic — it ended the war and killed near nine in ten of every living thing, mortal and dragon, for a vast span around the breach.

The Seven were consumed in their own working. But they were not, quite, ended. Their spirits linger still: unable to hold material form for long, yet no longer truly mortal either — they have *genuinely become demigods*, able to pass a thin thread of power to those who pray to them. This is the secret root of the world's religion. The pantheon mortals worship as the **[[The Pantheon|Seven Divines]]** are these seven ascended champions — real powers, answering real prayers, but *not* the True Gods who made them, and standing in for those distant powers in the minds of the faithful. They are, as a frightened [[High Priest Oldir|priest]] once put it to one of their own chosen, "false idols masking the existence of the True Gods" — though the falsehood is subtler than that: the idols are real, only mistaken for something greater than they are.

### Aftermath & Legacy

The seal was imperfect, and its flaws are the Shattering's living legacy. The wound did not close cleanly: scattered across the world remain the [[Riftshard]]s, fractures where the sealing failed. Each is not merely a charged crack but a *plane-leak* — a point where another plane bleeds into the Material, and magic worked nearby warps toward whichever plane is linked. (The Riftshard beneath a certain goblin temple was a Material-to-Faewild link, which is why the party's spells surged wild and unpredictable when cast there.) Every Riftshard points, like a compass, back toward the original breach.

The graver flaw is that **the dragons were not all killed.** A red, a green, and a black dragon at least survived the sealing and fled into the broken world — and rather than abandon their Queen's design, they took it up in secret, resuming the slow gathering of wealth and gemstones for the ritual that would tear the breach open again. Across two thousand years they have insinuated themselves into mortal society, ruling by proxy and by stealth. Foremost among them is **[[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow]]**, the *Architect of the Second Shattering* — the true power behind the [[Valtorran Empire]], working through the lich-empress [[Empress Morganna Eventide]] to amass over a century the wealth a second breach demands. The Shattering, in other words, is not finished history. It is a clock that is still running.

## In Memory & Belief

To the common world the Shattering is a campfire legend — a divine Dragon Queen who *gifted* magic, a seven-century war, the Seven Divines who banished the dragons. The longer-lived elves and some dwarves hold it nearer to history; a few of the well-read and the powerful know it for fact. None of them know what it truly was.

The gap between the folk tale and the truth is the most important thing about this entry. The world venerates the Seven Divines as eternal gods and never suspects they were mortal heroes of the Shattering, ascended in death; it does not know the True Gods exist behind them, nor why those gods held back; it does not know that the Material Plane was *made* magic-less and that the Shattering was a violation of its very nature; and it does not know that three of Tiamat's dragons still walk the world, finishing her work. The legend is not a lie so much as a mercy — the shape of a truth too large and too dangerous for the world to carry.

### [[The Bloody Nails|Campaign: The Bloody Nails]]

#### [[Session 02 - Midnight Fangs]]

On the road to [[Darmouth]], [[Lilliana Featherfoot]] sang the party "the Legend of [[Elandis]]" — the world of iron and steam, the divine Dragon Queen, the seven-century war ended by the Seven Divines — and [[Harland Finegold|Harland]] offered his preferred telling of Titans come to sunder the world. The party's first, unwitting brush with the truth, dressed as folk-song.

#### [[Session 20 - The Streets of Val Miriel]]

In a private audience at [[The High Sept]], [[High Priest Oldir]] saw [[Kai]]'s starry form and gave a terrified revelation: the celebrated Seven Gods are "merely ascended champions, false idols masking the existence of the True Gods." It was the first crack in the public myth — the hint that the pantheon is not what the world believes, and that something older stands behind it.

#### [[Session 44 - The Elven Archmage]]

[[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana]] grounded the legend in fact for the party — the [[Chaos Wars]], the Shattering of the planar barriers twenty-five hundred years ago, the chromatic dragons, the coming of magic.

#### [[Session 47 - Confluence of the Seven]]

The party carried to the Confluence the evidence of a *second Shattering in the making* — [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow|Kharazoth]], the Red Priests, and the dragon's century-long design — forcing the elves to weigh whether isolation could shield them from a wound that, last time, spared no realm.

#### [[Session 49 - The Silver Sage]]

Through a memory torn unbidden from [[Praetor'Varanous|Praetor]]'s mind, the party witnessed the Shattering's final battle as a survivor saw it: two titans clashing above a battlefield of hundreds of thousands, a city-sized rift bleeding red light, and [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow|Kharazoth]] driving a claw of black energy into the silver dragon's chest. Living proof that the war was real, and that one of its monsters endures.

## Trivia

- Common folk call it "the Split" or "the Splitting of the World" as often as the Shattering; scholars favour the older term.
- The recurring "seven centuries" of the folk legend is a corruption — the [[Chaos Wars]] lasted roughly five hundred years. The number seven migrated into the timeline from the Seven who ended it, who are themselves the very gods the tellers pray to.

## DM Notes

- **Unrevealed truth:** That the Material Plane was grown magic-less from the [[World Tree]]'s roots; that the True Gods exist *behind* the worshipped Seven and stayed their hand for fear of another Sundering; that the Seven Divines are the ascended champions of the Shattering, lingering demigods rather than eternal gods. The party has the *edges* of this (Oldir's "false idols," Praetor's vision) but not the whole.
- **Connections:** The Shattering is the spine that ties the campaign's threads together — [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow|Kharazoth]] and the Second Shattering, the [[Valtorran Empire]] as his funding engine, the [[Riftshard]]s, [[Praetor'Varanous]] as a living eyewitness, and the [[World Tree]] / Sundering as the older catastrophe that explains the gods' restraint.
- **Plot hooks:** The Second Shattering is the slow apocalypse the party is racing — every gemstone hoard, every Riftshard, every dragon thread leads back here. A Riftshard could be used (or weaponised) as a deliberate plane-door. The lingering Seven could, at need, manifest briefly to a chosen.
- **Open threads:** Who or what are the True Gods, and will they ever act? How close is Kharazoth to the wealth a second breach needs? Do the green and black survivors have their own designs, or do they serve Kharazoth? Could the original breach site be found — and what remains there?
