> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Seraphina Dawnbringer]]*

## The Truth Behind the Goddess
Seraphina Dawnbringer was, in life, a **mortal paladin** — the chosen champion of **[[Lathander]]**, the Morninglord, a God of Old whose sphere is dawn, renewal, and creative hope. At the end of the [[The Shattering|Shattering]] she was among the seven champions raised to a lingering demigodhood in the working that sealed the breach. The world prays to her as an eternal goddess; she answers, because she is real — but she is not eternal, and the God of Old behind her has been entirely forgotten.

When the Seven ascended and the Gods of Old withdrew, the world kept Seraphina and forgot Lathander entirely — his worship displaced so completely that **no seam remains in the sun faith** for the curious to find. The Dawnstar, her halberd, was a paladin's weapon before it became a holy relic; the sphere it now symbolises was Lathander's before it was hers. Follows the same pattern as restricted [[The Pantheon]], [[The Shattering]], [[World Tree]].

## DM Notes
- **Core secret:** mortal paladin, champion of [[Lathander]] (the Morninglord, a God of Old), ascended to lingering demigodhood in the Shattering alongside the other six. Her divine authority is rooted in Lathander's elder sphere.
- **Lathander's silence:** unlike [[Selûne]], who has broken the Gods of Old's restraint out of fear of a second Shattering, there is no present sign Lathander has stirred. His worship is so thoroughly displaced — no orphaned rites, no name surviving in any tradition — that there is no visible mechanism for him to act through. Whether that silence is chosen restraint or something else is open.
- **Seraphina's mortal character:** golden-haired, warm, relentlessly optimistic, a natural rallying point for those in despair. These traits are genuine, not invented by the faith. Keep any vision or record of her mortal life consistent.
- **Open threads:** What does Seraphina herself know of what she is? Whether the ascended Seven retain memory of their mortal origins — and whether she remembers Lathander — is unresolved.
