**Seraphina Dawnbringer** was, in life, a paladin — the chosen champion of **[[Lathander]]**, the Morninglord, a God of Old whose sphere is dawn, renewal, and hope. At the end of the [[The Shattering|Shattering]], she was among the seven champions raised to a lingering demigodhood in the working that sealed the breach. The world now prays to her as an eternal goddess; she answers, because she is real — but she is not eternal, and the God of Old behind her has been entirely forgotten.

## Overview

Seraphina Dawnbringer is one of the [[The Pantheon|Seven Divines]] — mortal champions raised to demigodhood at the [[The Shattering|Shattering]], each standing in for the elder God of Old who made them. Her patron was **[[Lathander]]**, the Morninglord: the genuine and ancient deity of dawn, renewal, and creative hope. When the Seven ascended and the Gods of Old withdrew, the world kept Seraphina and forgot Lathander entirely — his worship displaced so completely that no seam remains in the sun faith for the curious to find. The Dawnstar, her halberd, was a paladin's weapon before it became a holy relic; the sphere it now symbolises was Lathander's before it was hers. See also: restricted [[The Pantheon]], [[The Shattering]], [[World Tree]].

## DM Notes

- **Core secret:** Seraphina was a mortal paladin, champion of [[Lathander]] (the Morninglord, a God of Old). She ascended to lingering demigodhood in the Shattering alongside the other six. The world prays to her as an eternal goddess; the truth is that her divine authority is rooted in Lathander's elder sphere.
- **Lathander's silence:** unlike [[Selûne]], who has broken the Gods of Old's restraint out of fear of a second Shattering, there is no present sign that Lathander has stirred. His worship is so thoroughly displaced — no orphaned rites, no name surviving in any tradition — that there is no visible mechanism for him to act through. Whether that silence is chosen restraint or something else is an open question.
- **Seraphina's mortal character:** she was a paladin — golden-haired, warm, relentlessly optimistic, a natural rallying point for those in despair. These traits are genuine, not invented by the faith. If the party ever encounters a vision or record of her mortal life, that character should be consistent.
- **Open threads:** What does Seraphina herself know of what she is? Whether the ascended Seven retain memory of their mortal origins — and whether she remembers Lathander — is unresolved.
