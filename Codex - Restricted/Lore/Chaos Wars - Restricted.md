**The Chaos Wars** were the five hundred years of war that followed [[The Shattering]] — the long age of ruin in which the magic-less peoples of the old world fought for survival against the dragons and monsters [[Tiamat]] loosed through the broken barrier, and which ended only when seven mortals, raised to near-godhood by powers who dared not fight for themselves, spent their lives to banish the Dragon Queen and seal the wound in the world. It was the catastrophe that unmade the ancient civilisations and forged the [[Elandis]] of today — and it is not, despite the comfort of legend, truly over.

## Overview

The Chaos Wars are the five centuries of war that followed [[The Shattering]]'s breach, as [[Tiamat]] drove her brood to gather the gemstone wealth a ritual would need to widen the wound until she herself could step through and rule. That the world she attacked had been *made* magic-less — grown from the roots of the [[World Tree]] as a realm of order set apart from magic — is the Shattering's deeper subject; that entry and the restricted [[The Pantheon]] carry the full cosmology.

The two truths that bear on the *war* are these. First, *why the gods did not simply stop Tiamat themselves*: the True Gods — the **Gods of Old** beneath the [[World Tree]] — could not strike at a power of her order without risking another Sundering, so rather than fight her they raised seven mortal champions to near-godhood to end the war in their stead. Second, that their victory was incomplete: the seal those champions died to make was imperfect, not every dragon was slain, and the war the world believes ended twenty centuries ago is, in secret, still being fought.

## History

### The Road to War

The Chaos Wars grew out of [[The Shattering]] — [[Tiamat]]'s cracking of the planar barrier, which loosed magic and monsters into a world *grown* magic-less from the [[World Tree]]'s roots. That world and that breach are the Shattering's subject; what set the *war* in motion was that the breach was only Tiamat's first move. To enter the world in person she needed her dragons to amass vast hoards of gemstones — the arcane reagents for a ritual that would tear the planar door wide and lasting enough to admit her. The Shattering was the first crack; the Chaos Wars were the war to widen it.

### The First Onslaught

The opening of the war was sudden and catastrophic. The ordered, magic-less world had no answer to dragons falling from the sky or monsters pouring across its borders, and in those first violent years much of the old world simply fell — cities razed, whole peoples and the entirety of their learning swallowed in a single generation. Not every dragon served the Dragon Queen, however: the metallic dragons stood against her chromatic brood and fought alongside the mortal host, [[Praetor'Varanous]] among them, who made common cause with the elves of [[Sumara, The Shining City|Sumara]]. Among Tiamat's own commanders was the red dragon [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow]], an active and ancient force throughout the war — no mere foot-soldier but a lieutenant of her design.

### The Long War

What followed the collapse was a grinding, centuries-long attrition. The war was fought on *many fronts at once*, and by design: the dragons and their monstrous armies kept the scattered survivors perpetually busy and divided, so the peoples of the world could never gather their strength into one resistance. It was, for generations, enough merely to hold on. The tide turned on two counts — the spread of magic (from one mortal in ten thousand to nearly one in ten, at last a weapon to answer dragonfire) and the slow, hard-won unity of the surviving peoples, who began to make common cause across the fronts rather than bleed in isolation. It was in this long middle that some of the age's few enduring works were made — among them [[Arkangel]], the flying city, raised as a mobile repository of magical knowledge that no army could burn or besiege, and one of the very few intact creations of the war still aloft today.

### The Sacrifice of the Seven

The war ended where it had begun. Very nearly all the free peoples of the world gathered at the original breach for a final reckoning, and the dragons fell back to defend the wound. As the tide turned once more against the mortal host, the gods did the one thing left to them: forbidden to strike Tiamat directly, they **imbued seven mortal champions with a measure of divine power greater than any follower had ever been granted**, raising them for a time to something near demigods. These are the "seven archmages" of the folk account. At the breach, the Seven joined their borrowed power into a single ritual of staggering magnitude, cast the Dragon Queen back into her own realm, and sealed the tear in reality — a release so apocalyptic it ended the war in a stroke and killed near nine in ten of all living things, mortal and dragon, for a vast span around it.

The Seven were consumed in their own working — but they were not, quite, ended. Their spirits linger still, no longer mortal yet unable to hold material form for long: they had *genuinely become demigods*, able to pass a thin thread of power to those who pray to them. This is the secret root of the world's religion. The pantheon the world worships as the **[[The Pantheon|Seven Divines]]** are these seven ascended champions — real powers answering real prayers, but *not* the True Gods who made them, and standing in for those distant powers in the minds of the faithful. (See the restricted [[The Pantheon]] and [[The Shattering]] pages for the full cosmology: World Tree ▸ Gods of Old ▸ Seven Divines.)

### Aftermath & Legacy

The seal was imperfect, and its flaws are the war's living legacy. The wound did not close cleanly: scattered across the world remain the [[Riftshard]]s — not mere charged cracks but *plane-leaks*, points where another plane bleeds into the Material and magic worked nearby warps toward whichever plane is linked, each pointing back toward the original breach.

The graver flaw is that **the dragons were not all killed.** A red, a green, and a black survived the sealing and fled into the broken world — and rather than abandon their Queen's design, they took it up in secret, resuming the slow gathering of gemstone wealth for the ritual that would tear the breach open again. Foremost among them is [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow]], who survived the war's end by a dark transmutation of lichdom — binding his soul to nothing, so that his body can be destroyed but his spirit simply drifts until the Material Plane reconstitutes it. In the final battle he clashed with [[Praetor'Varanous]] in the sky above the breaking world and drove a claw of black necrotic energy into the silver dragon's chest, leaving him for dead (he does not know Praetor survived). Across the two thousand years since, Kharazoth has insinuated himself into mortal society as the *Architect of the Second Shattering* — the true power behind the [[Valtorran Empire]], working through the lich-empress [[Empress Morganna Eventide]] to amass the wealth a second breach demands. The Chaos Wars, in other words, are not finished history. They are a clock still running.

## In Memory & Belief

To the common world the Chaos Wars are a campfire legend, folded together with the Shattering into a single garbled tale — a divine Dragon Queen who *gifted* magic, a seven-century war, the Seven Divines who drove off the dragons. The longer-lived elves (whose archivists in [[Sumara, The Shining City|Sumara]] keep a true record) and some dwarves hold it nearer to history; a few of the well-read and powerful know it for fact. None of them know what it truly was.

The gap between the folk tale and the truth is the most important thing about this war. The world venerates the Seven Divines as eternal gods and never suspects they were mortal heroes of the Chaos Wars, ascended in death; it does not know the True Gods exist behind them, nor why those gods held back; it does not know the Material Plane was *made* magic-less and that the whole war was the consequence of violating its nature; and it does not know that three of Tiamat's dragons still walk the world, quietly finishing what their Queen began. The legend is not so much a lie as a mercy — the shape of a truth too large and too dangerous for the world to carry.

## [[The Bloody Nails|Campaign: The Bloody Nails]]

#### [[Session 02 - Midnight Fangs]]

On the road to Darmouth, Lilliana Featherfoot sang the party "the Legend of [[Elandis]]" — the world of iron and steam, the divine Dragon Queen who brought magic, the seven-century war ended by the Seven Divines. The party's first, unwitting brush with the truth, dressed as folk-song.

#### [[Session 44 - The Elven Archmage]]

[[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana]] grounded the legend in fact for the party — the Chaos Wars, the Shattering of the planar barriers twenty-five hundred years ago, the chromatic dragons, the coming of magic. In the same session, *Speak with Dead* on a [[The Red Priests|Red Priestess]]'s severed head revealed that [[Empress Morganna Eventide|the Empress]] serves an ancient entity named [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow]] — a name Ayana recognised from the Chaos Wars, a lieutenant thought long dead, and which visibly shook her.

#### [[Session 47 - Confluence of the Seven]]

When Kharazoth was named again before [[Illythia Voss]] — someone with direct memory of the war — she reacted with shock, identifying him as a figure "long thought dead" from the Chaos Wars. The party carried to the Confluence the evidence of a *second* Chaos War in the making: Kharazoth, the Red Priests, and the dragon's century-long design.

#### [[Session 49 - The Silver Sage]]

Through a memory torn unbidden from [[Praetor'Varanous|Praetor]]'s mind, the party witnessed the final battle of the Chaos Wars as a survivor saw it: two titans clashing above a battlefield of hundreds of thousands, a city-sized rift bleeding red light, and [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow|Kharazoth]] driving a claw of black energy into the silver dragon's chest. Living proof that the war was real — and that one of its monsters endures.

## Trivia

- The folk legend's "seven-century war" is a corruption: the Chaos Wars ran roughly five hundred years, not seven. The number *seven* migrated into the timeline from the Seven who ended the war — who are themselves the very gods the tellers now pray to.
- Every kingdom of the present age postdates the war. The oldest surviving realms, the human Cities of Old, were founded a thousand years and more *after* the last battle — there is no nation anywhere that remembers the world before.
- Kharazoth's epithet, "the Crimson Shadow," predates his apparent death at the war's end — the title was in use while he was a known, active force, not one given in absentia.

## DM Notes

- **Unrevealed truth:** That the Material Plane was grown magic-less from the [[World Tree]]'s roots; that the True Gods (the Gods of Old) stand *behind* the worshipped Seven and stayed their hand for fear of another Sundering; that the Seven Divines are the ascended champions of the Chaos Wars, lingering demigods rather than eternal gods; and that three of Tiamat's dragons survived to continue her work. The party has the *edges* of this (Oldir's "false idols" in Session 20, Praetor's vision in Session 49) but not the whole.
- **Connections:** This is the same spine as [[The Shattering]] — the two pages are one history split at the breach. Ties directly to [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow|Kharazoth]] and the Second Shattering, the [[Valtorran Empire]] as his funding engine, the [[Riftshard]]s, [[Praetor'Varanous]] as a living eyewitness, [[Arkangel]] as a surviving war-creation, and the [[World Tree]] / Sundering as the older catastrophe that explains the gods' restraint.
- **Plot hooks:** The Second Shattering is the slow apocalypse the party is racing — every gemstone hoard, every Riftshard, every dragon thread leads back to this war. Kharazoth believes Praetor died in the final battle; the party knows something their enemy does not. The original breach site, and whatever remains there, has never been found on-screen.
- **Open threads:** Who are the True Gods, and will they ever act? Do the surviving green and black dragons serve Kharazoth or pursue their own designs? How close is Kharazoth to the wealth a second breach needs? The "campaign against the Dread Kings" (referenced in Session 47 prep as a *separate* occasion the elves bled for the Material Plane) is a distinct event worth its own entry — keep it from being folded into this war.
