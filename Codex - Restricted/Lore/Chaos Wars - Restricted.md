> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Chaos Wars]]*

## The Cosmic Truth of the War
The world she attacked had been *made* magic-less — grown from the roots of the [[World Tree]] as a realm of order set apart from magic. That entry and the restricted [[The Pantheon]] carry the full cosmology. Two truths bear on the war itself:

- **Why the gods did not stop [[Tiamat]] themselves:** the True Gods — the **Gods of Old** beneath the [[World Tree]] — could not strike at a power of her order without risking another Sundering. Rather than fight her, they raised seven mortal champions to near-godhood to end the war in their stead.
- **Their victory was incomplete:** the seal those champions died to make was imperfect, not every dragon was slain, and the war the world believes ended twenty centuries ago is, in secret, still being fought. It is not finished history — it is a clock still running.

## The Sacrifice of the Seven (true account)
- Forbidden to strike [[Tiamat]] directly, the gods **imbued seven mortal champions with a measure of divine power greater than any follower had ever been granted**, raising them for a time to something near demigods. These are the "seven archmages" of folk account.
- The Seven were consumed in their own working — but not, quite, ended. Their spirits linger still, no longer mortal yet unable to hold material form for long: they had *genuinely become demigods*, able to pass a thin thread of power to those who pray to them.
- **This is the secret root of the world's religion.** The pantheon the world worships as the **[[The Pantheon|Seven Divines]]** are these seven ascended champions — real powers answering real prayers, but *not* the True Gods who made them, and standing in for those distant powers in the minds of the faithful. (See restricted [[The Pantheon]] and [[The Shattering]]: World Tree ▸ Gods of Old ▸ Seven Divines.)

## The Unfinished War — Surviving Dragons
- **The dragons were not all killed.** A red, a green, and a black survived the sealing and fled into the broken world — and rather than abandon their Queen's design, they took it up in secret, resuming the slow gathering of gemstone wealth for the ritual that would tear the breach open again.
- Foremost is [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow]], who survived the war's end by a dark transmutation of lichdom — binding his soul to nothing, so his body can be destroyed but his spirit drifts until the Material Plane reconstitutes it.
- In the final battle he clashed with [[Praetor'Varanous]] in the sky above the breaking world and drove a claw of black necrotic energy into the silver dragon's chest, leaving him for dead — **he does not know Praetor survived.**
- Across the two thousand years since, Kharazoth has insinuated himself into mortal society as the *Architect of the Second Shattering* — the true power behind the [[Valtorran Empire]], working through the lich-empress [[Empress Morganna Eventide]] to amass the wealth a second breach demands.
- **Metallic dragons** (cut from the public body's deeper read): the metallic dragons stood against Tiamat's chromatic brood, [[Praetor'Varanous]] among them, who made common cause with the elves of [[Sumara, The Shining City|Sumara]]. (This detail does appear in the public article; the reserve weight is on Praetor as living eyewitness.)

## The Riftshards (true nature)
- The [[Riftshard]]s are not mere charged cracks but *plane-leaks* — points where another plane bleeds into the Material, where magic worked nearby warps toward whichever plane is linked, each pointing back toward the original breach.

## What the World Does Not Know
The gap between the folk tale and the truth is the most important thing about this war. The world never suspects:
- the Seven Divines were mortal heroes of the Chaos Wars, ascended in death;
- the True Gods exist behind them, nor why those gods held back;
- the Material Plane was *made* magic-less, and the whole war was the consequence of violating its nature;
- three of Tiamat's dragons still walk the world, quietly finishing what their Queen began.

The legend is not so much a lie as a mercy — the shape of a truth too large and too dangerous for the world to carry. The party has the *edges* of this (Oldir's "false idols" in Session 20, Praetor's vision in Session 49) but not the whole.
