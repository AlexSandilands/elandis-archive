**Azara Flametongue** is not an eternal god. She was a tiefling sorcerer — the mortal champion chosen by [[Kossuth]], the Lord of Flames, the God of Old who held the sphere of fire before the world forgot him. At the [[The Shattering|Shattering]] she was consumed in the working that sealed the breach, and ascended in that sacrifice to the lingering demigodhood the world now worships as one of the [[The Pantheon|Seven Divines]].

## Overview

Azara is a real power: she holds the sphere of fire, answers prayer, and sustains the forge and the flame in the world. But she is a demigod, not an eternal god — the residue of a mortal sacrifice, not a first cause. Her relic, the **Emberheart** (a ruby orb ever warm to the touch), predates her divinity as a vessel for Kossuth's own principle.

The full structure — [[World Tree]] above all, the Gods of Old (the True Gods) beneath it, the Seven Divines beneath them — is laid out on the restricted [[The Pantheon]] page. [[Kossuth]] is the God of Old whose sphere Azara now stands in for: ancient, withdrawn, and silent. The world has forgotten him entirely, and prays to his champion in his place. This pattern is consistent with what the restricted [[The Shattering]] page records of how the Seven came to be.

## DM Notes

- **Core secret:** Azara Flametongue was a mortal tiefling sorcerer, champion of [[Kossuth]] (God of Old, primordial fire), who ascended at the [[The Shattering|Shattering]] to lingering demigodhood. Consistent with the restricted [[The Pantheon]], [[The Shattering]], and [[World Tree]] pages.
- **Kossuth:** exists as a root stub (`Kossuth.md`). Ancient, withdrawn, silent — the Lord of Flames. If he stirs as [[Selûne]] has (with a second Shattering approaching), that is a significant development.
- **The Emberheart:** its location is unknown to the faithful. As Kossuth's principle carried in concentrated form, recovering it could open a direct channel to that power with unpredictable implications.
- **Open threads:** does Azara retain anything of her mortal personality after centuries of abstract godhood? What is Kossuth's relationship to the silence? Does he know what the party is doing?
