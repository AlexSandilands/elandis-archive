The **Seven Divines** are the gods of [[Elandis]] — and they are not gods at all, or not in the way the world believes. They are the seven mortal champions who ended the [[Chaos Wars]], raised in their sacrifice to a lingering demigodhood; real powers that answer real prayers, but neither eternal nor first. Behind them, unworshipped and all but forgotten, stand the true and ancient **Gods of Old** — and the whole towering faith of the world is, in the end, the worship of seven saints mistaken for the gods who made them.

## Overview

The mortal world was not always given to the Seven. In the age before the [[The Shattering|Shattering]], the **Gods of Old** held their spheres openly — the true gods, the powers the deepest cosmology names the True Gods, who sit beneath the [[World Tree]] and above all that the world now worships. When [[Tiamat|the Dragon Queen]] tore the world open and the [[Chaos Wars]] burned for five centuries, those gods could not strike at her directly, for fear of the cosmic war that had once wounded the World Tree itself. So they chose champions instead: seven mortals, each raised up with a measure of divine power greater than any follower had ever been granted, who at the war's end joined their borrowed strength into the working that sealed the breach and cast the dragons down.

The Seven were consumed in that working — but not ended. Their spirits linger still, unable to hold material form for long yet no longer mortal: they have *genuinely become demigods*, able to pass a thread of power to those who pray to them. This is the secret root of the world's religion. The faithful pray to the Seven Divines as eternal gods, never knowing they were once mortal heroes, ascended in death — and never suspecting that the Gods of Old who made them are real, and stand behind the saints the world mistook for them. As a frightened [[High Priest Oldir|High Priest]] once put it: the Seven are "false idols masking the existence of the True Gods."

## The Seven Divines

Each of the Seven was, in life, the chosen champion of one of the Gods of Old — empowered by that elder god to carry their sphere into the war. When the Seven ascended and the old gods withdrew into silence, the world kept the champion and forgot the god. Each Divine's worship is, unknowing, the displaced worship of an elder power:

- **[[Seraphina Dawnbringer]]** (Sun) was the champion of **[[Lathander]]**, the Morninglord.
- **[[Elaris Moonsong]]** (Moon) was the champion of **[[Selûne]]**, the Moonmaiden — the clearest-documented of these pairings, and the one the party has brushed against.
- **[[Torgar Earthshaker]]** (Earth) was the champion of **[[Grumbar]]**, the Earthlord.
- **[[Azara Flametongue]]** (Fire) was the champion of **[[Kossuth]]**, the Lord of Flames.
- **[[Gale Winddancer]]** (Air) was the champion of **[[Akadi]]**, the Queen of Air.
- **[[Maris Wavecaller]]** (Water) was the champion of **[[Istishia]]**, the Water Lord.
- **[[Eldrin Starweaver]]** (Arcane) was the champion of **[[Mystra]]**, the Lady of Mysteries.

Fuller accounts of each Divine — their mortal lives, their deaths, and the elder god behind them — belong on their own pages.

## The Faith

The grandeur of the faith — the [[High Sept]], the [[The Stoneheart|Stoneheart]], the countless shrines — is raised, every stone of it, to the wrong tier of the divine order. The structure runs:

- the **[[World Tree]]** at the summit, the one genuinely transcendent god;
- beneath it, the **Gods of Old** (the True Gods), real and ancient and hidden;
- beneath *them*, the **Seven Divines**, the ascended champions the world actually prays to.

The whole religion rests two tiers below the powers that made it, and no temple of the Seven knows it. The knowledge is held by almost no one: [[High Priest Oldir|Oldir]] carries it as a guarded heresy passed down his line, and a scattering of the deepest scholars and longest-lived elves hold fragments. To teach it openly would be, in Oldir's view, too dangerous for the world to bear.

## In Memory & Belief

The faith's confidence is total precisely because the truth has been so completely buried. The world does not merely fail to worship the Gods of Old — it does not know they exist, has no memory that anything was worshipped before the Seven, and would hear the suggestion as madness or blasphemy. The founding story the faith tells is not even a lie so much as a half-truth worn smooth: the Seven *were* heroes, and *were* raised up — only not by nothing, and not into what the world imagines.

The gap is the most important thing about the faith. The world venerates seven ascended mortals as eternal gods; it does not know the True Gods stand behind them; and it does not know that at least one of those hidden powers ([[Selûne]]) has begun, out of fear of a second [[The Shattering|Shattering]], to reach back into the world she long ago left to her saint.

## Trivia

- The number *seven* the faith reveres is, in truth, only the count of the war's champions — not of the gods. How many of the Gods of Old there are, the world has no idea, for it does not know there are any.
- Every named relic of the faith — the Dawnstar, the Moon's Tear, and the rest — was a *mortal champion's* weapon before it was a holy artifact: the gear of heroes, sanctified by a worship that forgot they were ever wielded by hands.

## DM Notes

- **Unrevealed truth:** the Seven Divines are the ascended mortal champions of the [[The Shattering|Shattering]]/[[Chaos Wars]] — lingering demigods, not eternal gods — empowered by the **Gods of Old** (the True Gods) and standing in for them in the minds of the faithful. Each was the champion of one elder god (pairings above). Keep consistent with the restricted [[World Tree]], [[The Shattering]], [[Selûne]], and [[Shar]] pages (World Tree ▸ Gods of Old ▸ Seven Divines).
- **Who knows:** [[High Priest Oldir]] (a hereditary heresy) and a few of the deepest scholars / oldest elves. The party has only the edges — Oldir's "false idols" (S20), and what they've since learned of [[Selûne]] through [[Kai]].
- **Connections:** this is the spine the campaign's divine threads hang from — [[Selûne]] and [[Shar]] (Gods of Old now stirring), [[Kai]]'s arc as a Chosen, and the question of whether the *other* Gods of Old wake as a second Shattering nears.
- **Plot hooks:** the six elder gods behind the other Divines are undeveloped and waiting — any could stir as Selûne has. The relics-were-heroes'-gear angle is a quiet thread: the Seven's artifacts are scattered, and recovering one means handling a dead champion's weapon. What the Seven themselves know, want, or fear — and whether they can manifest more than a thread of power at need — is open.
- **Open threads:** how many Gods of Old are there? Do any still hold open worship somewhere remote? Are the Seven aware of what they truly are? Could the heresy, if it spread, break the faith — and who would want it to?
