**Borealia Forest** is a vast and ancient boreal woodland covering the northernmost reaches of [[Valtorra]], sprawling above the city of [[Val Aran]] toward the continent's ragged northern coast. Cold, dense, and given to deep shadow even in summer, it has carried a grim reputation for centuries — the place where a powerful necromancer once rose to infamy and left a taint the land has never fully shed.

## Geography & Landscape

The Borealia Forest begins just north of [[Val Aran]] and stretches toward the northern coast of [[Valtorra]], its true extent unmapped and largely unvisited. The trees are ancient and enormous — dark-barked conifers packed so tightly that light rarely reaches the floor — and the air inside runs colder than it should, even in the height of summer. Dead wood is everywhere: pale, stripped trunks standing among the living like sentinels. The forest floor is carpeted with deep moss and shadow, and little that requires sunlight grows there.

Deep in the heart of the woodland, far from any road or settlement, lie the ruins of an old stone fortification — half-swallowed by centuries of growth, its original purpose long obscured. These are the ruins where the original necromancer worked; items have occasionally been recovered from the site by those daring or foolish enough to venture that far in. The ruins now have a new occupant.

## History

The forest's reputation dates to an incident several centuries ago. A powerful necromancer — known to history only as **The Necromancer of Borealia Forest**, their true name lost — established a foothold in the ruins deep within and performed a ritual of significant and lasting consequence. The ritual tainted the land itself. When it was over, the dead did not stop walking. They never entirely have.

The city of [[Val Aran]] adapted. Its rangers learned to manage small numbers of undead as an accepted hazard of the northern woods, and the forest settled into an uneasy equilibrium that held for generations — if not comfortably, then at least predictably.

That equilibrium is now broken.

## Dangers

A new necromancer has taken up residence in the ancient ruins at the heart of the forest. This figure — an acolyte or aspirant inspired by the legend of the original — has begun replicating the old ritual. They are the cause of the recent escalation in both the number and aggression of the undead. The vision [[Berberis]] received at the Heartstone (Session 47) — *a frail figure completing a ritual in a ruined castle, the dead clawing upward through an endless dark forest* — depicted this new figure, not the historical one.

[[Berberis]] is aware that the undead are worsening and has left his grove to seek allies, but he does not yet know the cause. The grove of [[Berberis]], Quercus, and [[Prunus Serrulata - Restricted]] sits just south of the forest's edge, north of [[Val Aran]], and is itself increasingly exposed to the threat.

## DM Notes

- **The new necromancer:** Identity, motive, and full detail are deliberately undeveloped — this thread is for later in the story. The figure is presented as an aspirant inspired by the original's legend, but their exact nature is open.
- **The ruins:** The same site from which a Ring of Protection was recovered north of [[Val Aran]] (Session 22). A useful hook if the party ever traces that item back to its origin.
- **Berberis's arc:** Ending the dead walking the north is his personal goal. The revelation that a *new* necromancer is responsible — rather than just the original curse running its course — should land as a meaningful discovery when it comes.
- **Plot hooks:** The party could be drawn here via Berberis's quest, via rumours from [[Val Aran]], or via investigation into why the undead are worsening. The ruins are the likely endpoint.
