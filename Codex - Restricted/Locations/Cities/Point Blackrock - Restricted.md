> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Point Blackrock]]*

## Hidden Operatives

Mira Fenlock (the tavernmistress of the Last Respite) has been running an active resistance network out of her tavern since before the occupation — passing messages between rebel sympathisers, sheltering fugitives, and coordinating what quiet defiance remains in the town. She went very quiet after the party's breakout but the operation is ongoing. She is the settlement's most significant covert asset.

Nella Dorn (Keran Dorn's granddaughter at Frostflask Alchemy) has been quietly brewing explosive powders for rebel sympathisers since before the occupation — a dormant, unactivated cell that the party never encountered. She is still in town, still stocking, and still waiting for the right moment or the right cause to use what she has made.

The two do not appear to be in direct contact; they are parallel cells, neither aware of the full scope of the other.

## Aftermath of the Breakout

The Imperial soldiers sent onto the [[Frostway]] after the party's escaping column perished. Without a [[The Frostwardens|Frostwarden]] guide they had no means of navigating the ice in winter, and the crossing killed them. The locals who remained in town know the soldiers went out and never came back; some have drawn the obvious conclusion.

## Varik Surne's Fate

[[Commander Varik Surne]] almost certainly did not survive the fallout from the breakout. [[Dread General Gerard Blackmarsh]] was the obvious candidate to punish him for the loss of the prisoners, the destruction of the camp, and the failure to secure the Fox Crew — but Blackmarsh's own fate intervened before that accounting could happen. Surne was most likely dealt with instead by General Korvas ([[Vaeleran]]), in his capacity as a superior officer passing back through Imperial command, after departing [[Val Noren]]. The players have no knowledge of this. The irony — that Vaeleran's cover identity may have ordered the execution of the man who failed to stop him — is entirely below the surface.
