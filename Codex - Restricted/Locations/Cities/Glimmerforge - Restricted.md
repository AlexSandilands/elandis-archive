> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Glimmerforge]]*

## The Ummal Betrayal

- [[Thorgar Ummal|Thorgar]] and [[Hilda Ummal]] were not Imperial agents before the rebellion — they were a poor, low-status dwarven family who saw the rebellion as an opportunity. During the final battle at Glimmerforge's gates, they opened a hidden passage into the city from within, allowing Imperial forces to enter while the rebel army was engaged outside. The city fell because of them.
- The [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]] rewarded the Ummalls with the regency. Their authority is not merely collaboration — they are the direct cause of the rebellion's failure.
- The [[Cindercloaks]] are their personal enforcement corps, not a purely Imperial institution. The Ummalls created them to suppress dissent and hunt members of the [[Eternal Forge]] resistance. The Empire sanctions their existence; the Ummalls direct them.
- [[Dondar]] is the son of Thorgar and Hilda Ummal. He does not use the name Ummal. His twin brother [[Hrothgar]] leads the Eternal Forge resistance. Their younger sister [[Hvedra]] is alive but missing — seen walking freely in a torchlit tunnel alongside two others in a scrying vision (Session 49); her location is unknown.

## The Eternal Forge — The Real Stakes

- The [[Eternal Forge]] resistance named themselves after the physical forge deliberately: the Ummalls allow Imperial overseers to direct its output toward weapons production, which the resistance considers a desecration of a sacred site. Reclaiming the forge is as much a religious act as a political one for the movement's members.
- The sacred forge has not gone out in recorded history. Whether this is magical in origin or extraordinary maintenance is not established.

## The Royal Line

- A youngest Dwarven Prince named Goren was taken into the Underdark before the fall of Glimmerforge. [[Gromdal]] witnessed it but has no further details — he does not know who took the prince, for what purpose, or whether Goren survived.
- The Ummal Regency rests on the assumption that the old royal bloodline is extinguished. If Goren is alive, there is a legitimate claimant to the dwarven crown.

## The Prismatic Academy — Hidden Facts

- The permanent teleportation circle in the Academy's lower vaults is a guarded institutional secret. Its sigil is not shared with students.
- [[Dondar]] learned the sigil during his time at the Academy through means that were not authorised. He is among a very small number of people outside the faculty who hold it.
