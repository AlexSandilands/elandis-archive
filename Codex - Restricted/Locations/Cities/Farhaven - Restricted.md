> [!abstract] Public Entry: *[[Farhaven]]*

## Garrison & Governance

The garrison commander holds effective authority over all legal and military matters; the Mayor handles civilian administration and defers to him in practice. Both are unnamed — develop when relevant.

The commander enforces Imperial law visibly and harshly, partly to compensate for a garrison that is understaffed relative to a larger Imperial posting. The public executions at the junction on the day the party arrived are characteristic, not exceptional.

The Mayor is a civilian pragmatist who has made peace with Imperial rule. Whether that peace is genuine or resentful is an open thread.

## Undiscovered Locations

### Northern Quarter

#### Siren's Song *(inn — upscale)*

An elegant inn and boarding house overlooking the Northern Dock, offering the best rooms in Farhaven — sea-view suites, discreet staff, and a clientele of merchants and officers.

- Lady Evelina Moonshadow *(proprietor)* — a sophisticated woman with a mysterious past and connections among the influential; a potential ally or source for the right kind of information.
- Felix Seabreeze *(head butler)* — charming, meticulous, and discreet; ensures guests have everything they need and nothing they shouldn't.

#### Mystic Tides Apothecary *(apothecary — modest)*

A small aromatic shop stocked with herbs, potions, and remedies — the go-to for adventurers seeking healing supplies or uncommon ingredients.

- Nerida Selkie *(healer)* — a half-elf with an ethereal presence; knowledgeable in both conventional and magical medicine, known for a gentle manner.
- Thorne Saltshadow *(assistant)* — a former pirate with a shadowy past; skilled at brewing potions and sourcing rare or unusual ingredients.

### Northeast

#### Pearl of the Sea Library *(library — serene)*

A quiet library near the cliffs on the northeastern edge of the city, overlooking the gulf. Holds extensive collections on maritime history, navigation, and ancient lore.

- Librarian Cora Seacrest *(librarian)* — elderly, encyclopaedic knowledge of the collection; eager to assist researchers and hear travellers' accounts.
- Talon Wavewalker *(scholar's assistant)* — young and enthusiastic; specialises in ancient maritime legends and is always looking for new stories and artefacts.

### Central District

#### Green Gryphon Guildhall *(guildhall — reputable)*

The [[Green Gryphons|Green Gryphon]] chapter in Farhaven, positioned at the central crossroads where the main north-south road meets the east-west road to the Eastern Dock. The location is deliberate — a maximally visible, maximally trafficked corner for a mercenary guild whose business depends on being the first place people think of when they have a dangerous job.

#### Farhaven Armory *(smithy — reliable)*

A robust blacksmith's shop where weapons and armour are forged and repaired. The constant sound of hammers and hissing steam spills out into the street.

- Garrick Ironbeard *(smith)* — a burly dwarf with a thick beard and a reputation for the finest blades in Farhaven; gruff but fair, his work is widely respected.
- Lira Frostforge *(apprentice)* — a young half-elf with a talent for enchanting weapons; shy but passionate about her craft.

#### Navigator's Guildhall *(guildhall — reputable)*

A large building near the central crossroads where detailed charts and maps are maintained and sold. A hub for travellers, navigators, and anyone who needs accurate information about routes by sea or land.

- Master Cartographer Finn Tidescar *(cartographer)* — elderly, sharp-eyed, renowned for detailed and accurate maps; always eager to hear from travellers to update his charts.
- Cordelia Starmap *(apprentice)* — young and enthusiastic, excellent at celestial navigation and chart interpretation; dreams of exploring the world herself.

#### Coral Emporium *(curiosity shop — modest)*

A quaint shop crammed with curiosities from the sea — shimmering shells, intricate coral pieces, mysterious trinkets, and minor magical items connected to water.

- Oceana Waverunner *(proprietor)* — soft-spoken and elderly, she claims to communicate with sea creatures; her connection to the ocean runs deep.
- Kai Reefwalker *(supplier)* — Oceana's grandson, a skilled diver who supplies the shop's wares; adventurous, and willing to guide people to underwater locations.

#### Eversun Boutique *(tailor — fashionable)*

A clothing and accessories shop on the main road, its styles and fabrics inspired by the sea. Catches passing trade with its position on the north-south road.

- Isabella Wavecrest *(designer and tailor)* — flamboyant, detail-obsessed, and an excellent gossip; loves creating unique commissions.
- Rafael Tidewalker *(tailor)* — Isabella's quiet assistant, skilled at durable adventurer's clothing that still passes muster in polite company.

### Eastern Dockside

#### Sea Bounties Market *(market — bustling)*

A large open-air market adjacent to the Eastern Dock. Stalls overflow with fresh seafood, exotic spices from the trade ships, and local crafts.

- Marina Silverfin *(fishmonger)* — middle-aged and hearty; knows every fishing spot in the gulf and is a well-connected source of local knowledge.
- Elara Stormwind *(spice merchant)* — from distant lands, her stall a riot of colours and scents; loves to barter and often stocks rare herbs useful for potions or cooking.

### Southern Quarter

#### Driftwood Tavern *(tavern — modest)*

A cosy, dimly lit tavern built from driftwood and salvaged ship parts — a haunt for sailors and labourers looking to drink and trade stories.

- Captain Lyle Bracken *(proprietor)* — grizzled, one-eyed retired pirate; quick with a joke and slow to trust, with a thousand stories he may or may not share.
- Sophie Riptide *(barmaid)* — young and energetic, dreams of sailing; knows every rumour that passes through the tavern.

#### Whaleheart Shipyard *(shipyard — working)*

A sprawling vessel construction and repair yard along the southeastern shore near the Southern Dock.

- Captain Marlow Anchorwind *(foreman)* — a gruff retired naval officer who runs a tight operation; deep knowledge of ships, sea lanes, and moving cargo.
- Ella Driftwood *(shipwright)* — young, energetic, and always chasing innovative hull designs; passionate about building vessels that are both fast and durable.

## Plot Hooks

- Juliana's Textiles: Juliana survived the gallows and is running a shop in the city that tried to execute her. That takes a particular kind of nerve, desperation, or cover — and a party known for rescuing her would be recognised.
- The farmhouse with the Nail symbol on the western road: who lives there now? It could be a safe house, an abandoned property, or a family quietly trying to distance themselves from whoever painted it.
- The garrison commander enforces harshly to compensate for understaffing. If the party ever arrives openly, their reputation from the junction — rescuing a condemned prisoner by impersonating Imperial officers — will precede them. That might mean trouble, or leverage.
