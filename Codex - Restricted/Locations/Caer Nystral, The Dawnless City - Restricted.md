**Caer Nystral**, named **The Dawnless City**, is the capital of the [[Vale of Eternal Night]] and the seat of the Unseelie Court. Where [[Sumara, The Shining City|Sumara]] grows upward into prismatic light and is named the Shining City, Caer Nystral hunkers in black stone and grown blackthorn and is named the Dawnless. It is no accident of myth that the two are mirrors: Caer Nystral fills the wound the Sundering left in the world, and the Vale's eternal night is the shadow of that same blow. The Court calls it the Silmaras' crime and is not entirely wrong — only mistaken about how. The city is ruled from its central height by the archfae [[Mirileth|the Star-Veiled Queen]].

## Geography & Layout

Caer Nystral sits at the centre of the [[Vale of Eternal Night]], filling an immense bowl-shaped crater in the floor of the Vale. The Court tells the crater as grievance-myth — the place where the Silmaras' crime fell upon the Vale — but the literal truth is more terrible and is not spoken aloud anywhere in the city: **this is where the sky fell.**

When the first [[Ryordan Silmara]]'s act remade the borders of the Faewild — the Sundering of the World Tree — reality itself snapped over this spot. The sky came down, the light went out, and the land buckled and was gouged into the crater that holds the city. The Vale was plunged into the night it has never left, and the wound is centred here, at Caer Nystral, beneath the throne. The land's recoil left a central upthrust at the crater's heart, and it is on that height — the exact point the sky struck — that the **Veiled Citadel** stands. The shimmering veil through which starlight still bleeds down onto the Citadel is no mere enchantment; it is the seam of that wound, the one place the sky never fully closed.

The approach is the first defence. The crater floor around the city is given over to the same hazards that make the wider Vale lethal — *tideless mires* that never ebb, *witch-light bogs* whose pale lures lead nowhere safe, and **the Webs** of fae spiders far older and larger than anything on the Material Plane. Only three guarded **causeways** cross this killing-ground to the city's gates; everything else is mire, mound, and web.

From the outer edge inward, the city climbs through a series of concentric rings, each walled from the last and each a little higher than the one before, until the ground crests at the Veiled Citadel in the centre. A single ceremonial avenue, the **Midnight Procession**, runs from the Antler Gates straight up through every ring to the Citadel at the summit.

## The Rings of the City

Caer Nystral ascends to its throne as Sumara does — but in shadow, and inward. From the watched threshold to the height beneath the Veil:

- **The Barrow-Ring** — Beyond the main wall, the outermost ground belongs to the night hag covens, who dwell in mounds and barrows ringing the city. The oldest, coldest powers of the Court are kept here at the threshold rather than within it. The Hunt marches its condemned out to this ring, and few who are marched there return — a march that, to the prisoner, can be made to *look* like an escort toward the Court until it bends away toward the mounds.
- **The Thornwall & the Gates** — The main outer wall is a living rampart of grown blackthorn, impassably dense and quick to grow back faster than it can be cut. It is pierced by antler-arched gates of black iron — the **Antler Gates** to the south, where embassies are met, and the lesser **NW** and **NE Gates** — each held by sentries of the Hunt. An embassy's fate is often decided at the gate, before it ever sees the Court.
- **The Lower Warren** — Inside the main wall lies the teeming, lightless commons of the *Quiet Folk* — redcaps, quicklings, boggarts, and blights — knotted around the **Nightmarket**. It is not a market to browse but a warren to survive: crowded, dark, and quick to turn on the unfamiliar. It is also the one place in the city where a stranger might half-blend, and the most dangerous.
- **The Muster Yards** — Within the inner wall sits the Court's martial heart, the ground of the Hunt. Here are the **worg-kennels** of the Hunt's trackers and the **nightmare-stables** of the Fae Knights, flanking the **muster field** where the riding-bands form up before they ride out across the Vale. To reach the Citadel uninvited is to cross the Hunt's own ground.
- **The Court Tiers** — Higher still, cold and beautiful halls house the lesser archfae of the Court, each with a small retinue, each keeping its own counsel at the Queen's sufferance.
- **The Veiled Citadel** — At the crater's central height, on the very point where the sky fell, stands the throne of the Star-Veiled Queen beneath the seam of falling starlight; from its peak rises the **lunar spire** that anchors the dark moon over the Vale. Beneath it, cut into the wound itself, lies the most secure vault in the Vale.

## Architecture

Caer Nystral changes character as it climbs, so that a visitor can read their depth into the city by the materials around them. The outer **Lower Warren** is a close, organic sprawl of dark Vale timber — blackwood and silverleaf felled from the surrounding forests — roofed in grey slate and crowded so tightly that blackthorn, phosphor-fungi, and pale creeping flora knot through every gap between the walls. It is the nearest the city comes to looking lived-in: crooked, low, and green at the edges.

Inward and upward, the wood gives way to harder things. The **Muster Yards** and the **Court Tiers** are raised in black steel, dark stone, and black marble, and with each tier the design grows colder and more deliberate — straighter walls, sharper angles, roofs and spires drawn up into fae-wrong points like a thicket of antlers turned to architecture. The flora thins; the slate becomes steel; the silhouette becomes a blade.

At the summit, the **Veiled Citadel** is the end of that progression: a single mass of pure black obsidian, sheer and glass-dark, its antler-spires catching the pale light of the lunar spire along their edges. Where the Warren is grown, the Citadel is *cut* — and the whole city between them is the slow turning of one into the other.

## The Dark Moon

From the peak of the Veiled Citadel rises a **lunar spire** — a beam of pale light that climbs into the sky and anchors there a **dark moon**: a black orb that glows with the cold white light of an eclipse. It hangs fixed above the city, never crossing the sky as a true moon would, yet it still turns through the familiar phases — from a full orb glowing on every side to a thin crescent lit along one edge alone. Visible from most of the Vale, the dark moon and its spire are the surest sign of Caer Nystral seen from afar.

The dark moon is the Star-Veiled Queen's most public work. She anchored it not long after she took the throne, in the hope that a single shared light, hung where every corner of the Vale could see it, might draw her scattered and quarrelsome subjects together into one people.

## The Dwellers

No sun has touched Caer Nystral, and over uncounted generations its people have come to share its dark. Almost every native sees in the blackness without difficulty; an intruder who cannot is blind, and lit up to fae eyes like a struck match.

- **Fae Night Elves** — cold-skinned, dark-eyed kin of the Sumaran elves, folded far more deeply into fae nature; slow to laugh.
- **Centaurs & Fae Knights** — the Hunt: cavalry, wardens, and knights, riding dire-worgs and nightmare-mounts.
- **The Quiet Folk** — redcaps, quicklings, boggarts, and twig- and needle-blights, filling the Lower Warren and the household ranks.
- **The Deep Things** — the night hag covens of the Barrow-Ring and the lesser archfae of the Court Tiers, each holding their own ground at the Queen's pleasure.

There are no humans here, and no warmth. The city holds, at rest, some twelve thousand long-lived fae; when the Court is in full residence, that number climbs toward twenty-five or thirty thousand.

## Defences

Caer Nystral is held less by formal garrison than by the nature of the place itself.

- **The Hunt** — the Court's standing strength of cavalry, trackers, and knights, kennelled and stabled in the Muster Yards.
- **The Causeways** — only three crossings span the mires, the bogs, and the Webs of the crater floor, and every one is watched.
- **The Thornwall** — a rampart that regrows faster than any siege can clear it.
- **The Queen's eye** — little crosses the Vale unseen by the Star-Veiled Queen's magic; she permits no loose ends.
- **The dark itself** — the oldest defence, and the one no stranger can answer.

## Trivia

- Where Sumara is named the Shining City, Caer Nystral is named the Dawnless. The two cities are deliberate mirrors of one another, and the people of the Vale never let the comparison be forgotten.
- Its spires are shaped like antlers — sharp, asymmetric, and fae-wrong, with nothing quite square or true.
- The Veiled Citadel sits beneath the single seam of sky through which starlight still falls on the city; everywhere else, the only light is witch-light and the faint glow of phosphor-fungi.

## DM Notes

- **The true origin (restricted):** Caer Nystral's crater is where the sky fell when reality snapped during the Sundering of the World Tree — the cosmic act of the first [[Ryordan Silmara]] that remade the Faewild's borders. The blow extinguished the light, plunged the Vale into eternal night, and gouged the bowl the city fills. The Court's public belief — that this is collateral damage of the Silmaras' crime, held *with the conviction of grievance rather than evidence* — is the cover-myth over a literal, physical truth. The earlier "Dead Root / root of the World Tree the city rings" framing is **wrong and discarded**; there is no chasm-root at the centre, only the crater and its central upthrust.
- **Why it matters to the campaign:** Kharazoth's threatened "second Shattering" is a second Sundering of the World Tree. The party stands, in Caer Nystral, on the scar of the *first* one — a living illustration of what a second would do to the world, and (unknown to them) what it would unleash.
- **The Vault beneath the Citadel:** Cut into the wound below the throne is the most secure vault in the Vale, holding the [[Soulstone Anchor]] — kept, *not* used to power the city. The Anchor and what it imprisons are detailed in their own restricted entry; the Citadel is merely where it is kept.
- **The dark moon (public, but a loose thread):** The anchored dark moon and its lunar spire are open, visible lore — the Queen's stated act of uniting the Vale, not a secret. The thread left deliberately open: the spire rises from the same peak as the seam of starlight, the unhealed wound where the sky fell. Whether the Queen drew the moon's light *from* that seam — and what tampering with the Citadel might therefore do to the moon, or to the wound — is yours to decide if the party ever reaches the throne.
- **Asha Vayne:** [[Asha Vayne]] is alive within the Court — charmed and glamoured into the Queen's service as a cold knight of the Hunt, the real man recoverable beneath. He may lead the escort that brings a Silmara embassy in through the Antler Gates. See his restricted entry.
- **The Queen:** [[Mirileth|the Star-Veiled Queen]] is more than she appears, and the throne she keeps here sits directly over the secret the whole city is built around. See her restricted entry.
- **Plot hooks:** the embassy's reception at the Antler Gates (honoured escort vs. faked arrest and barrow-march); the descent through the rings toward the Citadel; the Vault and the Anchor as the true object of the journey; the recovery of Asha Vayne; and the slow revelation, to the party alone, of what the crater actually is.
