The Darkwood is the vast, monstrous forest that fills Valtorra's north-eastern corner — the region [[Lighthaven]] clings to the western edge of. On the surface it is a feared, fog-drowned wilderness that kills the unprepared; beneath that it is one of the most load-bearing places in the campaign, hiding the Sunken Temple, the emerald jewel [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow]] needs for the Second Shattering, and the riftshard that once unmade [[Vaeleran]]. Type: Location → Region. Importance: major.

## Established

- A dense, monstrous forest at Valtorra's far north-eastern edge; together with the Borealia Range it walls the continent's interior off from the coast. ([[Valtorra]])
- [[Lighthaven]] sits in a hollow hard against its western treeline, and the wood is listed as Lighthaven's Region. ([[Lighthaven]])
- The [[Emberspire]]'s light wards off "the horrors that spill from the wood" — the one thing that lets Lighthaven endure on its edge. ([[Lighthaven]])
- Fixed atmosphere: fog-shrouded, foreboding, "pitch-black depths." The party dragged [[Hugor]] into it under *Pass Without Trace*. (Sessions 28–29)
- Monster-haunted enough that Lighthaven keeps the Duskwardens to repel incursions, tells every child cautionary folktales about its edge, and treats the [[Darkwood Vanguard]] as heroes purely for going in and coming back. ([[Lighthaven]], [[Darkwood Vanguard]])
- Source of rare, valuable materials — hydra scale, elder-oak sap, monstrous components — the basis of Lighthaven's exotic trade, harvested by the Vanguard. ([[Darkwood Vanguard]])
- [[General Korvas]] was sent across the [[Frostway]] to the Darkwood to retrieve "the jewel" (Session 39) — and is heading there now.
- [[Ryo]] grew up in a treehouse home within the wood, raised by [[Jek]] of the Vanguard. The home was sealed against light leaking out. (Session 49 — see Restricted)

## Geography & Scale

- Roughly 150 miles long and 100 miles wide (about three hexes by two on the regional map, at ~50 miles to a hex). [[Lighthaven]] sits at its north-western corner; the wood runs east to the coast and reaches south toward [[Val Miriel]] and Sabine.
- Over so large an area the terrain is genuinely varied — not a uniform wall of dark pine. Real features include deep, grasping bramble-country, river gullies, and waterfalls.
- A massive lake lies at the centre of the forest, fed from beneath and draining out to the ocean by a river — the river the Vanguard and earlier prep reference.

## Ecology & Dangers

- The wood is exceptionally deadly because it is riddled with riftshards (see Restricted for what these are and why they matter); the monsters concentrate around them.
- Known and planned threats: basilisks; grasping brambles that lash out to wrap and drag victims away (2d8 piercing, Dex save to avoid, hard Strength check to break free); waterfall mist in the deep gullies that confounds the senses (Wisdom save).
- The rare flora and fauna that draw the Vanguard in are the same wealth that makes the wood worth the risk.

## History

- During the [[Chaos Wars]], an ancient civilisation lived in this region. The Darkwood overgrew their ruined world long after the age fell — the forest is younger than the catastrophe, grown up over the bones of what came before. ([[Emberspire]] restricted lore)
- [[Lighthaven]] and the [[Emberspire]] are the surviving western edge of that lost world: a town built on forgotten ruins, beneath a tower the ancients raised.
- At the forest's heart stood one of that civilisation's great places of worship — a temple to one of the gods of old — now drowned beneath the central lake as the Sunken Temple. (see Restricted)

## Inhabitants

- The [[Darkwood Vanguard]] work only the shallow reaches near Lighthaven; no one holds the deep wood.
- [[Ryo]]'s old treehouse home stands somewhere within, where [[Jek]] hid and raised him.
- A reclusive faction of merfolk live in the drowned heart of the wood. (see Restricted)

## Atmosphere

- Perpetual gloom and fog; pitch-black beneath the canopy. Feared across the region, it reads to Lighthaven less as a forest than as a thing held at bay — the dark the Spire's light is aimed against.

## Restricted

- The wood is riddled with [[Riftshard]]s — fractures where other planes bleed into the Material. Riftshards attract and breed monsters; this is the real reason the Darkwood is so deadly, and exactly what the [[Emberspire]] was raised during the [[Chaos Wars]] to hold back. The forest is monstrous because of what leaks into it.
- At the centre, drowned beneath the great lake, lies the Sunken Temple: an ancient hall of worship to one of the gods of old, from the same lost Chaos-Wars civilisation whose ruins [[Lighthaven]] is built upon.
- The temple holds a particularly large Riftshard, linked to the Plane of Water. It is the lake's true source — water gushes from it to fill the basin above. It is the inverse twin of [[The Maw]], whose own riftshard pulls water the other way, into the Plane of Water.
- A faction of merfolk live in the temple and revere the Riftshard. Their daily ritual casts a spell that opens it to gush water and top up the lake — a spiritual ceremony that also, deliberately or not, keeps the water level high enough that the temple stays submerged and hidden. They are hostile to any intruder barring very smooth talking; it is their home and their religious centre. (Faction is so far just a concept — unnamed, no leadership yet. Needs its own entry.)
- Set into a great altar at the centre of the temple's massive central hall is a large emerald jewel — one of a matched pair. It was a gift to the ancient civilisation here during the Chaos Wars; mined from the deepest parts of the dwarven mines in the [[Silver Mountains]]. Its sister is a red jewel now in [[Glimmerforge]].
- [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow]] needs both jewels to empower his ritual for the Second Shattering. He already controls the red one — the Empire holds the dwarven kingdom. The emerald is the missing key, which is why [[Dread General Gerard Blackmarsh|Blackmarsh]] sent [[General Korvas]] to retrieve it (Session 39). Korvas is bound for the Sunken Temple now.
- The merfolk do not know what they are guarding. They worship the Riftshard, not the jewel, and have no idea the emerald set in their altar is a key to the apocalypse.
- The Sunken Temple's great Riftshard is the same one that once shattered [[General Korvas]]'s Imperial conditioning and left the blank-slate man who became [[Vaeleran]] (see Korvas restricted). So Blackmarsh is unknowingly sending Korvas back to the very place that unmade him. Open thread: whether proximity to the Riftshard fractures his freshly-restored conditioning a second time.
- [[Ryo]]'s childhood treehouse was sealed against light leaking out — a deliberate hiding, tied to his elven royal bloodline rather than to the temple plot. His presence in the wood is incidental to the jewel; the Darkwood is simply where [[Jek]] hid and raised him.
