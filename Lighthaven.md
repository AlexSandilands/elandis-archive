## Details

- Population: ~1000
- Imperial Presence: Low
- Local Leader: Gideon Valtros
- Defining Trait: The Emberspire, a large ancient tower that predates the current town, used for creating immense light when an attack from The Darkwood is occurring
- Current Conflicts: Perpetual defence against monsters of The Darkwood
- Claim to Fame: Extremely fertile farming lands to the west, and access to exotic materials from The Darkwood (which the Darkwood Vanguard retrieve)

### Overview

Lighthaven is a small but resilient town located on the eastern edge of civilization, near the boundary of The Darkwood, a dense and foreboding forest teeming with monstrous creatures. The town lies in a natural dip in the terrain, often shrouded in thick fog that lends an eerie atmosphere to its quiet streets.

While life in Lighthaven is fraught with danger, its fertile farmlands to the west sustain a modest economy, supplemented by the Darkwood Vanguard, a guild in the town who are experts in navigating the Darkwood to retrieve exotic materials from flora and fauna to use for trade with travellers and merchants along the northern highway.

### History

Lighthaven was built upon the ruins of an ancient city, its name and origins long forgotten. Crumbling remnants of old walls and collapsed structures can still be seen in some parts of the town, particularly near The Emberspire, the ancient lighthouse-like tower that dominates the eastern skyline.

The Emberspire is said to predate even the earliest recorded settlements in the region, and though its original purpose is unknown, the townsfolk have long relied on its beacon to ward off incursions from The Darkwood. Over generations, the knowledge of how to light and maintain the spire’s great brazier has been preserved by the Emberwardens, a dedicated group of keepers who ensure it remains a steadfast defense against the horrors of the forest.

Though the ruins suggest the town was once part of a grander civilization, most locals regard the past with little curiosity, focusing instead on the challenges of the present. Occasional expeditions into the ruins have yielded fragments of forgotten lore and artifacts, but no clear answers about the ancient city have ever emerged.

### The People

The people of Lighthaven are a hardy and mistrustful lot, shaped by the constant threat of monster attacks and the oppressive shadow of The Darkwood. Most are fiercely loyal to one another, but their hospitality toward outsiders is limited. Stories of betrayal and disaster from wandering travellers have reinforced this cautious outlook over generations.

Despite their wariness, the townsfolk are proud of their resilience and resourcefulness. Farmers till the rich soil west of town, producing abundant crops that support the local economy. Skilled artisans like blacksmiths and carpenters keep the town running, while brave members of the Darkwood Vanguard venture into the forest to retrieve valuable materials and eliminate dangerous creatures.

### Culture and Beliefs

While the townsfolk are not particularly devout, they hold a quiet reverence for The Emberspire and the Emberwardens. It is common for families to place small lanterns in their windows at night, symbolizing solidarity with the spire’s light. Folklore and cautionary tales about The Darkwood are passed down to children, warning them to stay far from its edge.

The people of Lighthaven also celebrate a seasonal festival on the Winter Solstice called The Emberlight Vigil, where the town gathers to light smaller braziers around the spire, commemorating their survival and honoring those lost to the forest’s dangers.

### Relations with the Empire

Lighthaven technically falls under the jurisdiction of [[Valtorran Empire|The Empire]], but the townsfolk largely see the imperial presence as a burden rather than a boon. The local guards, particularly the Duskwardens, handle most of the town’s defense, and the imperial soldiers stationed at the barracks are viewed as ineffectual outsiders. Mayor Valtros openly defies imperial influence, favoring policies that prioritize the well-being of Lighthaven’s residents.

### Darkwood Attacks

The Emberspire provides security against the attacks of monsters from the Darkwood.
A list of example monsters that often attack are:
- **Werewolf** – A classic shapechanger whose curse transforms a villager into a savage beast under the full moon.
- **Giant Spider** – Enormous, venomous arachnids that spin sticky webs among the trees, ambushing travelers.
- **Vine Blight** – Animated, creeping vegetation that uses its thorny tendrils to strangle and trap intruders.
- **Goblins** – Cunning and vicious, these small humanoids often set up ambushes and raiding parties from forest hideouts.
- **Dire Wolf Pack** – Larger and more ferocious than ordinary wolves, their coordinated hunts can overwhelm a village’s defenses.
- **Bugbear** – Hulking goblinoids that use brute force and stealth, striking unexpectedly from the shadows.
- **Shadow** – Mysterious, incorporeal undead that sap the strength and will of their victims, emerging from the gloom.
- **Displacer Beast** – These panther-like monstrosities have an uncanny ability to appear shifted, making them deadly and elusive predators.
- **Ettercap** – Cruel, spider-like humanoids that craft traps and ambushes, using both venom and cunning to overcome foes.

## Locations

### The Emberspire

>[!tip]+ Description
> Rising above the eastern edge of Lighthaven, The Emberspire is a relic of an ancient civilization. Its towering, blackened stone structure is both majestic and ominous. Intricate carvings of flames and celestial symbols adorn its base, hinting at its long-forgotten purpose. Inside, winding stone staircases lead to the massive brazier at the summit, where the Emberwardens ignite its beacon during incursions.
> 
> The brazier itself does not actually provide external light, instead there is some persistent enchantment on the tower such that when the flames reach a certain temperature and luminosity the top of the tower will magically start to glow.
> 
> There is a skill required by the Emberwardens to be able to achieve the correct balance in the brazier. The surrounding ruins add an air of mystery, with crumbled arches and overgrown pathways suggesting stories lost to time. 
>
> The Emberwardens wear deep golden cloaks during the vigil, but usually just a dark grey with the typical gold stripe down the back

#### NPCs

1. Elda Arkwright (Human, Female): The senior Emberwarden, Elda is a stern woman in her 50s with an unshakable sense of duty. Her scarred hands show years of lighting the great fire. She often gives wisdom to the younger wardens.
 
2. Brynn Hallowfell (Halfling, Male): A young and ambitious Emberwarden apprentice. Brynn is eager to prove himself but struggles with the weight of responsibility. His nimbleness makes him an excellent runner during emergencies.

### Blacksmith: Ironclad Forge

>[!tip]+ Description
> The Ironclad Forge is a beacon of warmth and industry in the heart of Lighthaven. Its sturdy stone foundation and thick timber walls speak to its dwarven craftsmanship.
> 
> The forge constantly hums with the rhythmic clang of metal against anvil, the air thick with the scent of coal and molten steel. Weapons, armor, and tools are displayed proudly on racks, ranging from functional farm implements to intricate decorative blades.
> 
> Locals trust this forge not just for its quality but for the blunt wisdom of its master blacksmith.  
  
#### NPCs

1. Dhorik Ironbrow (Dwarf, Male): The master blacksmith, Dhorik is gruff but deeply respected for his skill. His sturdy build and braided grey beard make him a local icon.

2. Maren Willowby (Human, Female): Dhorik’s apprentice, Maren is a cheerful woman in her late 20s. She balances the forge’s intensity with her warm demeanor, creating decorative trinkets alongside weapons and tools.

### Tavern: The Foggy Tankard

>[!tip]+ Description
> Nestled on the main road, The Foggy Tankard is a haven for travelers and townsfolk alike. Its dimly lit interior is cozy, with a roaring hearth, sturdy oak tables, and walls lined with hunting trophies and faded maps. The air is filled with the aroma of spiced stew and the chatter of patrons sharing tall tales.
> 
> A polished wooden bar dominates one side of the room, where mugs of frothy ale are constantly being filled. Outside, a sign depicting a tankard amidst swirling mist swings gently in the breeze. 

#### NPCs

1. Tobias Gristle (Human, Male): The jovial barkeep with a belly as big as his laugh. Tobias is a master of spinning tales, often embellishing monster attacks to entertain patrons.

2. Harris Farfoot (Halfling, Female): The tavern’s quick-witted waitress. Harris is known for her sharp tongue and uncanny ability to overhear everything happening in the tavern.

### General Goods: The Lighthaven Mercantile

>[!tip]+ Description

>
>Lanterns, rope, farming tools, and basic rations line the walls, while a glass case near the counter displays rarer items like healing potions or unusual trinkets. The shop’s wooden floor creaks underfoot, adding to its rustic charm. The scent of fresh parchment and dried herbs fills the air, hinting at the mix of goods available for trade.  
   
#### NPCs

2. Eleanor Tresh (Human, Female): Wife of Elias, manages the books

3. Quinn Tresh (Human, Male): Eleanor’s teenage son, Quinn is often bored with life in Lighthaven but dreams of leaving to become an adventurer.

### Market: The Embermarket

>[!tip]+ Description
>Encircling the base of The Emberspire, the Embermarket is a bustling marketplace, its vibrant stalls a striking contrast to the tower's imposing presence.
>
>Farmers display baskets of fresh produce, and merchants offer wares ranging from hand-carved trinkets to simple textiles.
>
>A few vendors sell rare goods brought by brave adventurers venturing into The Darkwood. 
>
>The market is alive with chatter and the occasional laugh, though an undercurrent of unease lingers, as if the shadow of the forest is never far from mind.  
   
#### NPCs

1. Arlen Greyvine (Human, Male): A kindly farmer who sells the freshest vegetables and fruits. He’s a beloved figure in town for his generosity and humor.

2. Myra Pindlebock (Halfling, Female): A traveling merchant who specializes in oddities and minor magical curios. Myra has an enigmatic charm that keeps people coming back.

3. Ilya Caross (Tiefling, Male): A shady merchant who is the front for [[The Darkwood Vanguard]], selling valuable monster parts that were retrieved
 
4. Jek (Gnome, Male): An older explorer who has been venturing into [[The Darkwood]] for years, retrieving exotic materials. Not associated with [[The Darkwood Vanguard]]

### Imperial Garrison: The Shadowguard Barracks

>[!tip]+ Description
>The Shadowguard Barracks is a modest but well-fortified structure of stone and timber. It serves as the base for both the imperial soldiers and the local Duskwardens, the latter holding the respect of the townsfolk.
>
>The main hall is adorned with maps of the region and racks of weapons ready for use. Tension lingers in the air between the imperials and locals, their uneasy alliance forged out of necessity.
>
>A small training yard in the back echoes with the sounds of drills, while the barracks’ central hearth serves as a gathering point for weary guards.
>
> **Group Name: The Duskwardens**:
> The local guard trained specifically to repel incursions from The Darkwood.

#### NPCs

1. [[Captain Lucius Draegor]] (Human, Male): The imperial captain, Lucius is a stern, arrogant man in his 40s with short black hair shaved around the sides of his head. His armour is always too clean. He is resented for his dismissive attitude toward Lighthaven's struggles.

2. Sergeant Kael Morryn (Human, Male): The head of the Duskwardens. Kael is a stoic and pragmatic leader in his 30s who earns loyalty through bravery and action.

>
>The apothecary also features a small garden out back, where rare and exotic plants grow under careful watch.

2. Hugor Thistlebane (Halfling, Male): Thalia’s assistant, Hugor is a chatterbox with a knack for identifying rare herbs. He’s often seen foraging near the forest’s edge.

### Town Hall: The Spirehold

>[!tip]+ Description
>The Spirehold is a squat but imposing structure of gray stone, with banners of the town crest hanging from its walls. 
>
>Its interior is austere, with polished wooden furniture and a large central chamber where decisions about Lighthaven are made. Ledgers and maps clutter the mayor’s office, where heated debates with the imperial presence are a common sight.
>
>A small meeting hall within the building is often used for addressing the townsfolk during emergencies, its acoustics carrying the mayor’s voice clearly even in crowded gatherings.  
   
#### NPCs

1. Mayor Gideon Valtros (Human, Male): The fiercely loyal mayor, Gideon is in his 50s and is known for his deep mistrust of outsiders. He is a strong advocate for the locals' rights.

2. Mira Caldry (Human, Female): Gideon’s efficient and diplomatic assistant. Mira often tempers the mayor’s fiery rhetoric.

### Guild Building: The Darkwood Vanguard

>[!tip]+ Description
>A sturdy stone-and-timber building located near the eastern edge of town, close to The Darkwood.
>
>The hall is adorned with trophies of past expeditions: the skulls of monstrous creatures, pelts of rare beasts, and weapons forged from exotic materials.
>
>Inside, a roaring hearth warms the main gathering room, where adventurers plan their forays into the forest and recount their harrowing tales over mugs of strong ale.
>
>The hall includes an armory, a map room filled with marked routes and dangers, and a workshop where harvested materials are processed or traded.  
   
#### NPCs

1. Garrick Thornshield (Dwarf, Male): The guildmaster of the Darkwood Vanguard. Garrick is a grizzled veteran with a missing eye and a prosthetic hand fashioned from the claws of a Darkwood beast. His deep voice commands respect, and he has a talent for both strategy and diplomacy.

2. Silva Greybough (Human, Female): A seasoned scout and herbalist who specializes in navigating The Darkwood’s treacherous terrain. Silva has a keen eye for valuable materials and an encyclopedic knowledge of the forest's dangers.

3. Ilya Caross (Tiefling, Male): A shady merchant who is the front for [[The Darkwood Vanguard]], selling valuable monster parts that were retrieved. Normally found at the Embermarket selling goods.
