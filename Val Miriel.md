Val Miriel — **Major** City. The largest trade city on the continent and its religious heart, a vast walled port on the [[Gulf of Miriel]] straddling the mouth of the Luminor River. One of the six human **Cities of Old**, conquered by the [[Valtorran Empire]] roughly a century ago and now ruled, in the Empire's name, by the surrendered royal line of House Miriel.

## Established

- A large walled **port city** on the [[Gulf of Miriel]], built around the mouth of the **Luminor River**, which runs NW→SE through the city and out to the sea. (Val Miriel notes; [[Gulf of Miriel]]; [[Aeolian Chasm]] confirms the Luminor's eastern branch runs through Val Miriel into the Gulf.)
- The **main port** through which supplies are shipped upriver to **[[Val Luminor]]** — making it strategically vital, and the reason the Imperial presence here is so heavy.
- The **largest trade city on the continent**, and its **religious centre** — the [[The High Sept|High Sept]], cathedral of the Seven, stands here.
- One of the **six human Cities of Old** — ancient kingdoms founded during the rebuilding of civilisation after the **Chaos Wars**; the city is 1,000–1,500 years old.
- Conquered by the [[Valtorran Empire]] ~100 years ago. The royal **House Miriel** surrendered and was installed as **Governors**, so the populace would still see a familiar ruling family rather than an Imperial outsider — but bound to abide by Imperial rule.
- Ruled by **[[Governor Tarlis Evandron]]**, father of **[[Vallania Miriel]]** ("Nia"). (His personal story lives in his own page.)
- The [[Order of Ravens — Val Miriel]] runs a nest here, led by Nest Father **[[Thaddeus Karr]]**.
- The party spent a major arc here (Sessions ~20–28): the streets, the Governor's daughter, the Inferno at Serenity Plaza, the **Riots of Val Miriel**, and the **Underrun**.

## Geography & Layout

A genuinely huge city — it takes a couple of hours to walk from one side to the other, and roughly **30 minutes between each of the four Ways**. Home to **hundreds of thousands**: a human-majority "City of Old" core, but cosmopolitan as any great port, with plenty of other species among its citizens.

**The causeways.** The city's spine is its great paved causeways:

- A **main causeway runs west→east**, entering at **Valena's Portal** (the great gate in the NW) and crossing the city to the **eastern gate**. (A third gate sits to the SW.)
- Four **Ways** run the length of the city, north-to-south, perpendicular to the main causeway. Each begins at a great building at its **northern end** and runs down toward the **southern waterfront**:
  - **Military Way → [[The Red Bastion]]** (the army and garrison)
  - **Royal Way → [[The Governor's Keep]]** (rule and administration; formerly Miriel Castle, still called Miriel's Keep by old families)
  - **Divine Way → [[The High Sept]]** (the Seven; clergy and scholars)
  - **Artisan Way → [[Theater of Dreams]]** (craft, wealth, and culture)

**The river.** The **Luminor** cuts NW→SE through the heart of the city, dividing it into a western bank (Military and Royal Ways) and an eastern bank (Divine and Artisan Ways), joined at the centre by **Valena's Bridge**, where the main causeway crosses. The river is the city's shared artery — everyone's life touches it.

**The docks.** At the southern, seaward end where the Luminor meets the Gulf lie the docks, with a clear social gradient: the **Grey Docks** (toward the Military Way side) are the rough, working, dangerous end of the city; the **Green Docks** (toward the Divine Way side) are cleaner and more reputable. **Mawbreakers Hold** sits out by the Green Docks.

**Naming note:** Valena's Portal and Valena's Bridge are named for **Valena**, a founding-era ancestor of House Miriel — forebear of [[Vallania Miriel]] and her mother **Vallarra Miriel** — possibly dating to the city's founding centuries ago.

## History

Val Miriel is one of the **six human Cities of Old**, the ancient kingdoms raised as civilisation rebuilt itself in the long aftermath of the **Chaos Wars**. It has stood for 1,000–1,500 years, ruled across the centuries by House Miriel, whose seat was **Miriel Castle**.

When the [[Valtorran Empire]] rose roughly a century ago and swept the continent, Val Miriel was conquered — but the royal family **surrendered** rather than see the city burn. Rather than impose an Imperial outsider on a proud old populace and risk unrest, the Empire kept House Miriel in place as **Governors**: a familiar face on the throne, ruling in the Empire's name and bound to its law. The arrangement holds to this day under [[Governor Tarlis Evandron]] — old Miriel walls now wearing newer Red Empire fortifications, an old kingdom administered as an Imperial possession.

## Government & Imperial Presence

- **Government:** Imperial governorship held by House Miriel — [[Governor Tarlis Evandron]] rules from [[The Governor's Keep]], with his daughter [[Vallania Miriel]] at his side. Legitimacy of the old line, leash of the Empire.
- **Imperial Presence: heavy.** Because Val Miriel is the supply port for [[Val Luminor]], the Empire keeps a strong hand here — the garrison of [[The Red Bastion]] is a real military weight, not a token. The Governor arrangement is the soft glove; the Bastion is the iron fist behind it.

## The People

Hundreds of thousands of citizens. A human city at its heart — one of the old human kingdoms — but a great trade port draws every kind of people, so the streets, markets, and docks are cosmopolitan and crowded. Daily life turns on the river: trade, the docks, and the [[#The River Market]] touch nearly everyone.

## Culture & Faith

- **The religious centre of the continent.** The [[The High Sept|High Sept]] — the great circular cathedral of the **Seven** — stands on the Divine Way, drawing pilgrims and clergy from across the land. Worship of the Seven is the civic faith. (See [[The Pantheon]].)
- **The river is the city's identity.** More than infrastructure — it's the shared life of the place, which is why the River Market grew into what it is.

### The River Market

A defining civic fixture, not a footnote. The River Market is a **permanent** market spanning a long stretch of the Luminor on both banks ([[#Riverfront & Docks|River Market (E)]] and **(W)**), straddling the water near Valena's Bridge. Because the river is central to everyone's life and nearly everyone passes through, the market has grown to hold **something for everyone** — goods of every kind, from every quarter of the city and every ship that docks. For most citizens it's a regular, ordinary part of the week.

## Districts

The city reads naturally as its four Ways plus the riverfront. Each Way takes its character from the great building at its head. Establishments below are kept **inline at full detail**; named NPCs link out to their own pages where they have one, and stay described inline where they don't.

### Military Way (west bank → Grey Docks)

The soldier's quarter, running from [[The Red Bastion]] down the western bank to the rough **Grey Docks**. Drill yards, barracks, mercenary trade, and — toward the water — the hardest, most dangerous end of the city.

**The Green Griffon** *(guild hall)* — the Val Miriel chapter of the mercenary guild.
- **[[Porta Hale|Captain Porta Hale]]** — burly, iron-grey, no-nonsense leader of the branch.
- **Vira "Goldfinger" Loris** — shrewd halfling who runs the guild's finances and supplies.
- **Rina "Hawkeye" Talon** — young half-elf, the guild's top scout and archer.
- **[[Teresa Martari|Teresa "T" Martari]]** — purple-skinned tiefling, greatsword, enjoys wrestling bears.
- *Quests on offer:* the Vacant Ship (a crewless merchant ship found drifting); the Gold Mine (a benefactor's goldmine gone silent — became the Ruby Falls Goldmine, Session 23).

**The Coal** *(smithy)* — messy, unglamorous, and the real article: a keen eye sees the arms and armour here far outclass the Silver Forge's. Favoured by people who actually fight.
- **[[Ingrid]]** — master smith. Large "Nord"-like woman, Russian accent, no bullshit.

**Fur and Fang** *(exotic goods)* — a dim, rustic shop of cured hides, rare furs, claws, horns, and mounted trophies, iron-banded door carved with wolves and snakes.
- **[[Gaara|Gara "Grimhide" Morrow]]** — grizzled retired hunter, lost a leg to a dire wolf; stern, deeply knowledgeable about wild beasts.
- **[[Kira|Kira "Softpaw" Voss]]** — Gara's daughter and assistant; quiet, observant, takes the occasional dangerous hunt; more personable with customers.
- *Quest:* "The Hydra" — Gara's heard of a large lizard-like monster that nearly killed hunters in the forest north of [[Farhaven]].

**The Rusty Anchor** *(tavern — low-end)* — rough dockside tavern down by the Grey Docks: creaky, dim, stale ale, a clientele of sailors, mercenaries, and dubious characters. The place to buy cheap drink and underworld information.
- **Old Man Corwin** *(bartender)* — grizzled veteran, few words, deep knowledge of the city's dark corners, fiercely protective of his tavern.
- **Finn the Fence** *(regular)* — shifty, smooth-talking rogue and information broker; well-connected, charming, cunning. *(Plot-weight NPC — candidate for his own page if he recurs.)*

### Royal Way (west bank → riverfront)

The seat of rule and old money, running from [[The Governor's Keep]] down the western bank. Nobility, administration, and the city's well-heeled.

**Miriel's Rest** *(establishment near the Keep — discovered, detail TBD)* — named on the city map along the Royal Way; flesh out when the party engages it.

**The Alchemist's Retreat** *(alchemy shop — high-end)* — well-regarded; the nobility come here for tonics and elixirs.
- **Alchemist Lysander** — brilliant but eccentric, fond of risky experiments.

### Divine Way (east bank → Green Docks)

The quarter of faith and learning, running from [[The High Sept]] down the eastern bank to the cleaner **Green Docks**. Clergy, scholars, pilgrims — and, at the river's edge near the sea, some of the city's more pleasant waterfront.

**Librarium Valoris** *(great library)* — a grand old library of thousands of ancient texts and scrolls; scholars come from across the land to study here.
- **Librarian Anselm** — elderly scholar with an encyclopaedic command of the city's history.

**River's Watch** *(tavern — mid/upper)* — on the Divine Way side of the Luminor, near the river mouth, looking out over the water toward the sea. Not noble-class, but a clear cut above the Rusty Anchor: stone-and-timber, big windows over the river, polished floors, a warm fire, a sophisticated and welcoming air. *(The party spent significant time here — strong candidate to graduate to its own [[River's Watch]] page; if so, the heading links out and this inline entry stays put.)*
- **Owner Aric Laris** — tall, handsome, late thirties; impeccable manners, inherited the tavern, knows his regulars by name, a good listener.
- **[[Lila Spring|Barmaid Lila Spring]]** — spirited, quick-witted, sharp tongue and warm heart; dreams of owning her own tavern.
- **Chef Isolde** — middle-aged, cheerful, curly auburn hair; blends traditional recipes with her own twists; treats her kitchen staff like family.

### Artisan Way (east bank → riverfront)

The quarter of craft, wealth, and culture, running from the [[Theater of Dreams]] down the eastern bank. The finest shops, the high-end trade, and the city's cultural life.

**The Silver Forge** *(armoury — fancy)* — beautiful, ornate, noble-favoured weapons and armour you probably wouldn't want to actually fight in.
- **Arsumas Ellighten** — half-elf, long silver hair, perfect makeup, large silver apron; campy, finds used gear distasteful.
- Three assistants — **Markul, Yiffa, Tom** — who do most of the actual work.

**The Gilded Crow** *(tavern — high-end)* — luxurious, in the heart of the Artisan Way: golden accents, velvet seating, tapestries, a grand chandelier, live classical music, gourmet food and the finest wines.
- **Proprietor Lady Marielle** — former noblewoman, graceful and refined; sharp wit, keen business sense, trades discreetly in the city elite's gossip.
- **Head Chef Tomas** — culinary genius from a distant land; exotic flair, friendly, loves sharing travel stories.

**The Bridge of Stars** *(curio shop)* — a mysterious shop of magical artifacts and curiosities; a place of wonder and intrigue.
- **[[Felix|Proprietor Felix]]** — charming, enigmatic shopkeeper with a knack for finding the perfect item for a customer.

**Potion Seller** *(shop — named on map, detail TBD)* — out toward the eastern gate; flesh out as needed.

### Riverfront & Docks

The shared centre of the city — the Luminor, Valena's Bridge, and the [[#The River Market]] (the permanent market spanning both banks, E and W) — and the seaward docks where the river meets the Gulf.

- **Grey Docks** — the rough, working, dangerous waterfront (Military Way side); the Rusty Anchor's territory.
- **Green Docks** — the cleaner, more reputable docks (Divine Way side).
- **Mawbreakers Hold** — out by the Green Docks. *(Named on map; detail TBD — note the [[Aeolian Chasm]] entry references "The Mawbreakers" as allies of the Bloody Nails; check whether the Hold is connected before publishing.)*

## Landmarks (own POI pages)

The four great buildings and the Underrun are full landmarks with their own interiors, casts, and plot weight — each becomes its own Point of Interest page, teased and linked from here. Rich existing notes to carry into those pages:

- **[[The Red Bastion]]** (Military Way) — red-walled fortress, patrolled parapets, a steel portcullis on a rolling mechanism, a training courtyard, and a great echoing central chamber ringed by balconied floors and corner spiral stairs. The Imperial garrison's seat.
- **[[The Governor's Keep]]** (Royal Way) — sprawling fortress overlooking the Gulf of Miriel; ancient Miriel walls blended with newer Red Empire fortifications. Formerly Miriel Castle. Seat of [[Governor Tarlis Evandron]] and [[Vallania Miriel]].
- **[[The High Sept]]** (Divine Way) — the religious centre of the continent. A vast circular cathedral (coliseum-like); seven statues of the Seven at the entrance; an outer ring of alcoves and altars; seven great arched doors descending to a central chamber of terraced seating, each section dedicated to one of the Seven. Before the altar to [[Elaris Moonsong]] stands a grey, silver-veined wood cabinet holding a **Moonstone**. Led by **[[High Priest Oldir]]**; **Sister Lara** lives here.
- **[[Theater of Dreams]]** (Artisan Way) — grand ornate theatre, the cultural centrepiece of the city. **Director Selene**, a passionate and gifted director.
- **[[The Underrun]]** — the city's vast underground network of tunnels and reservoirs, fed naturally by the Luminor, that washes the city's waste out to reservoirs beyond the walls. **Foreman Darius** oversees the cisterns. *(An in-world secret — most citizens don't know its extent; the party does, from Session 28.)*

## Structure (for the City codex reference — NOT part of the published entry)

This is the design we agreed for **all** City entries; capture it in `.claude/skills/codex/references/locations/city.md` and the City template, then delete this section before /codex publishes:

- **Tiered hub-and-spoke.** The city doc is the canonical at-a-glance hub for everything in it.
  - **Tier A — Landmark:** a great structure with its own interior, cast, and plot weight (players go *inside*). → its **own POI page**; teased + linked from the city doc.
  - **Tier B — Establishment:** a shop/tavern with flavour and a proprietor, no interior map. → **inline** under its district, full detail.
  - **Tier C — Mention:** a name with a line of colour. → named only in the district text.
- **Graduation by link-out, not by moving content.** When a Tier B establishment or an inline NPC earns a fuller page (lots of scenes, a recurring character), it stays inline **at the same level of detail** — the heading/name simply becomes a wikilink to the deeper page. Split-out pages are *supplementary depth, never replacements*.
- **NPCs** follow the same rule: described inline in their establishment's section; name links out to a character page where one exists.
- **Evergreen.** City docs are timeless portraits. Events ride in a **Campaign Appearances** section by default; only a genuinely transformative event graduates to its own short in-body section (DM's judgment per event).
- **Restricted companion doubles as a staging area for undiscovered locations.** For a city the party hasn't explored, the *public* doc lists only what's been discovered; not-yet-found establishments wait in the **Restricted** doc, and the DM **promotes** them into the public hub as the party explores. Preserves the fun of exploration. (Plus normal DM-only plot truths.)
- **Required body order:** opening → infobox → **Geography & Layout** (mandatory first body section — gates, causeways/Ways, river, docks; the map in prose) → History → Government & Imperial Presence → The People → Culture & Faith → **Districts** (one subsection per district, establishments inline) → **Landmarks** (links to POI pages) → Factions present → Campaign Appearances.
- **Infobox fields:** Type, Region (parent [[Region]] — keep even if unknown), Population, Government, Imperial Presence, Status.
