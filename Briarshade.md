A vast, hostile region of the [[Faewild]] governed by the [[Court of Fallen Leaves]] (Unseelie / Autumn Allied) — a predatory, colourless wood that feeds on fear, sorrow, and the vibrancy of those who wander into it.

## Established
- Governed by the Court of Fallen Leaves, Unseelie / Autumn Allied. (prep)
- Completely greyscale — all colour is absent throughout the biome; this is a natural property of the court and the region, not a deliberate curse. (prep, Session 45)
- When the party entered, all colour visibly drained from their clothing and gear. (Session 45)
- Colour is a commodity within the Briarshade — scarce, prized, almost narcotic to the local fey. (prep, Session 45)
- Faewild distances are strange here; the party only skirted the outer edges. Travellers who lose their way deeper in could be lost for days or weeks. (DM)
- [[Grinn, The Pale]] guards the only clear path through the outer Briarshade and demands a Toll of Colour to allow passage. (Session 45)
- The party paid Grinn with Kai's happy memory of wild-shaping into a dog and fouling [[Neros]]'s bed; the memory was permanently erased from Kai's mind. (Session 45)
- Grinn rewarded them with a Briar Light — a piece of enchanted black sap that provides diffuse illumination without casting shadows. (Session 45)

## Geography & Atmosphere

- **Scale:** A vast Faewild region; the party only crossed the outer fringe. The interior is largely unknown and potentially endless by mortal reckoning.
- **Lighting:** A colourless, ashen twilight. No distinct sky colour — just a dim, washed-out grey gloom that smothers everything. Shadows stretch to unnatural lengths and twitch with a life of their own even without wind.
- **Flora:** Dense with ancient, gnarled, leafless trees that constantly weep thick, sweet-smelling black sap. The ground is heavily carpeted in pale, thorny vines that move uncomfortably like grasping fingers. Bioluminescent fungi provide the only consistent ambient light, their pale glow the closest thing to warmth in the wood.
- **Sensory:** Air is freezing, perfectly still, and heavy with creeping dread. The soundscape is the crunch of frost underfoot and a near-constant overlapping chorus of faint whispers that always sound as though they are right beside the listener's ear.

## The Greyscale Curse

The Briarshade's colourlessness is not an imposed spell but a fundamental property of the region and its court. Colour simply does not exist here — it has been bled out of the landscape entirely. As a consequence, any vibrancy that enters the Briarshade (a mortal's bright cloak, a painted shield, a vividly joyful memory) becomes something rare and precious. The local fey treat colour the way mortals treat gold — as currency, as status, as something worth taking.

- Standard and magical Darkvision fail completely within the Briarshade. The darkness is dense, almost physical.
- Fae-native light sources (such as the bioluminescent fungi or the black sap of the trees) are unaffected by the curse and remain functional. They also do not trigger the shadow mechanic (see below).

## The Light Trap

Any hard shadow cast by a *mortal* light source — torch, lantern, *Light* cantrip — detaches from the ground and becomes a hostile Living Shadow. These entities swarm, and attempting to destroy them with directed light only spawns more, creating a compounding loop. The shadows drain strength on contact; travellers who linger with mortal light will be weakened until they cannot move.

The practical solutions locals and the well-informed know:
- **Diffused light:** muffling a lantern or cantrip to produce only soft, unfocused dim light prevents hard shadows from forming.
- **Cross-illumination:** three or more light sources in a tight geometric formation can wash out shadows between them.
- **Fae light:** harvesting the native sap or fungi provides illumination that plays by fae rules, casting no mortal shadows at all. Only some trees produce the usable sap, and the locals know which ones and how to retrieve it safely.

## Inhabitants

The Briarshade is home to malicious, opportunistic fey who thrive on the suffering of travellers: Boggles, Redcaps, Quicklings, and Meenlocks. They are entirely patient and predatory — they do not attack armed, wary groups head-on. Instead they harass, steal, and isolate individuals, letting the hostile environment wear down their prey's mind before moving in.

All of them prize colour as currency. A vibrant object or a warm, happy memory carries real value here.

## Magical Hazards

- **The Lure:** The flora and ambient magic work in tandem to mimic familiar voices — loved ones, lost companions, people the traveller has wronged — calling desperately from the darkest parts of the wood, attempting to draw individuals away from safety.
- **Nightmare Inducement:** The Briarshade actively resists peaceful rest. The ambient whispers and the oppressive atmosphere produce terrifying, hyper-realistic nightmares drawing on the sleeper's deepest fears and past traumas. Long rest in the Briarshade is rarely restful.
- **Living Shadows (Dormant):** Even when not triggered by mortal light, the ambient magic can cause shadows to detach when a traveller lowers their guard to sleep — lingering to pilfer small shiny items or whisper the sleeper's worst insecurities back at them.

## Notable

- **[[Grinn, The Pale]]:** A goblin-like fey toll-keeper who blocks the only clear path through the outer Briarshade, demanding a Toll of Colour from anyone who wishes to pass and be pointed safely through.
- **Briar Light:** Enchanted black sap from certain Briarshade trees. Provides diffuse illumination without casting any shadow, mortal or fae. A known resource to those who know how to find and harvest it.

## Restricted

- The seat of power for the Court of Fallen Leaves is assumed to lie somewhere in the deep Briarshade — but its exact location is unknown. If the party ever ventures further in, this is where the court proper would be found.
- The Court's leadership is deliberately left mysterious; do not define it unless the party pursues it directly.
