**Government:** The Council of the Seven Circles

## Overview

Hidden in the exact center of The [[Shimmering Peaks]], Sumara—known as The Shining City—is the last great bastion of ancient elven civilization. Built into the luminous white stone of the caldera, the city is a masterpiece of magical architecture. Sweeping spires of glass and alabaster rise seamlessly from the natural rock, connected by delicate, arcing bridges of solidified light. The city exists in a state of perfectly curated harmony, standing in stark contrast to the chaotic, overgrown wilds of the Faewild that surround its protective mountain ring.

## Government Structure

Sumara is governed by a delicate balance of power between the ancient Royal Bloodline and the democratic meritocracy of "The Circles." The Royals serve as the ultimate arbiters, symbolic figureheads, and keepers of the oldest planar magics, while the day-to-day governance, defense, and survival of the city are managed by the leaders of the Seven Circles.

## The Seven Circles

Elven society within Sumara is strictly divided into seven disciplines, or "Circles," ensuring that every aspect of their isolated civilization functions flawlessly.

**1. The Circle of the Weave (Magick)**

- **Function:** Responsible for maintaining the city's magical wards, researching planar travel, and training arcane casters. They regulate the use of high magic to prevent drawing the attention of wild Archfey.
- **Leader:** _Archmage Illythia Starwhisper._ A brilliant but highly rigid traditionalist who took over leadership after her predecessor abruptly left the city.

**2. The Circle of the Blade (Warfare & Defense)**

- **Function:** The military arm of Sumara. They train the border guards, patrol the inner bridges, and maintain the martial traditions of the elven people.
- **Leader:** _Commander Kaelen Nightbreeze._ A stoic, heavily scarred veteran who views the Faewild as an active siege against their people.

**3. The Circle of the Dawn (Healing & Restoration)**

- **Function:** The physicians, clerics, and alchemists of the city. They tend to the physical and spiritual health of the populace, heavily relying on the unique flora of the Faewild to brew potent remedies.
- **Leader:** _Elder Silaqui Amastacia._ A deeply empathetic, soft-spoken cleric who rarely leaves the city's vast botanical infirmaries.

**4. The Circle of the Scroll (History & Prophecy)**

- **Function:** The archivists and lore-keepers. They maintain the vast libraries of Sumara, preserving the true history of the Material Plane, the Chaos Wars, and the elven lineage before their exile.
- **Leader:** _Archivist Elrohir Gemflower._ An ancient, absent-minded scholar who is more comfortable among crumbling tomes than living people.

**5. The Circle of the Scales (Law & Diplomacy)**

- **Function:** The judges and diplomats. They resolve internal disputes, regulate the distribution of resources, and theoretically handle diplomacy with any independent Fey courts that approach the borders.
- **Leader:** _Justiciar Naivara Siannodel._ A sharp-tongued, fiercely pragmatic elf whose rulings are absolute and final.

**6. The Circle of the Loom (Artifice & Architecture)**

- **Function:** The builders and enchanters. _(Added to account for the city's survival)._ They construct the physical infrastructure of Sumara, weaving magic into stone and glass to expand the city without disturbing the natural lay of the land.
- **Leader:** _Master Artisan Fenian._ A perfectionist who views the entire city as a single, ongoing art project that must not be rushed.

**7. The Circle of the Root (Nature & Agriculture)**

- **Function:** The druids and agriculturalists. _(Added to account for the city's survival)._ Since the city is cut off from the Material Plane, this circle is responsible for harmonizing with the volatile magic of the Faewild to grow food and safely harvest resources without angering the land.
- **Leader:** _Warden Sylvaris._ A wilder, less formal elf who spends most of their time in the agricultural terraces at the city's lower edges.****
