# Darmouth

A major Imperial port city on [[Valtorra]]'s southern coast, purpose-built by the [[Valtorran Empire]] roughly fifty years ago as the planned southern gateway to the continent's interior.

## Established

- Founded by Imperial decree approximately fifty years ago — not an organic settlement but a calculated investment (DM)
- Located on the southern ocean coast at the foot of the [[Aeolian Mountains]], near the entrance to the [[Aeolian Chasm]] — not on the [[Gulf of Miriel]] (campaign map)
- The layout is a military grid: straight cobblestone streets, colonial timber buildings, lantern posts — "more like an imperial garrison than a home" (Session 03)
- The main street runs dead-straight from the northern gate to the harbour, roughly a mile to two miles; [[Market Square]] sits at the midpoint (x_Session Prep/Session 3)
- The [[Temple of the Seven Divines]] stands on a hill to the east, its spires visible from the gate (x_Session Prep/Session 3)
- A sewer network runs beneath the city (Session 04)
- The [[Temple of the Seven Divines]] contains a hidden subterranean escape passage, used by [[Sister Arianna Brightstar]] to get the party out of the city after the jailbreak (Session 04)
- [[Seraphina]], a [[Hand of the Empire]], was spotted operating in Darmouth during Session 03
- The party freed [[Ella Sable]] and recovered stolen [[Skyreach]] schematics from the [[Darmouth Bastille]] via the sewers in Session 04

## Geography

Darmouth sits on the open southern ocean coast at the base of the [[Aeolian Mountains]]. To the north, the mountains rise to the [[Aeolian Chasm]], above which [[Skyreach]] was being constructed. The harbour faces the open sea; the mountains block any easy overland route to the interior.

## History & Strategic Significance

The Empire founded Darmouth with a single strategic vision: once [[Skyreach]] completed a bridge across the [[Aeolian Chasm]], a direct overland highway would run from Darmouth's docks northward through the mountains and on to [[Val Luminor]] and the continent's interior. This route would bypass the [[Gulf of Miriel]] and the [[Mawbreakers]] entirely, making Darmouth the Empire's primary southern supply hub and stranding the Mawbreakers' stranglehold on the gulf's shipping.

It was this vision — not mere vandalism — that drove the [[Mawbreakers]] and the rebellion to destroy Skyreach. Without the bridge, Darmouth is cut off from the interior by the mountains, its grand ambitions reduced to a well-garrisoned backwater.

## Current State (post-Skyreach)

The destruction of [[Skyreach]] has stalled major investment in Darmouth. New plans for the supply route are being drawn up, but construction is frozen and merchants who anticipated a booming trade hub have grown wary.

The Imperial military presence has significantly increased. The Empire has pieced together that the [[Skyreach]] schematics were stolen from the [[Imperial Lawmaster's Office]] here, that escaped prisoners from the Darmouth Bastille passed through the city, and that rebel sympathisers are embedded among the population. The [[Rusty Nail]] symbol has begun appearing on walls and doors across the city. The response has been a harsh crackdown: increased patrols, arbitrary detentions, and a climate of suspicion that has made Darmouth feel less like a colonial boom-town and more like an occupied city.

## Inns & Taverns

### The Iron Anchor
Quality: Modest (5s per night)
A popular, bustling tavern where sailors and locals gather to swap stories and enjoy a drink.
- Proprietor: [[Captain Barnaby "Old Salt" Hargrave]] — A retired sailor with a wealth of knowledge about the sea and the [[Mawbreakers]].

### The Seafarer's Rest Inn
Quality: Comfortable (8s per night)
A clean, comfortable inn catering to travelers and merchants. It prides itself on offering a warm and welcoming atmosphere.
- Owner: [[Eliza Farthington]] — A kind-hearted widow.

### The Wharf Rat
Quality: Poor (1s per night)
A seedy, dimly lit tavern frequented by pirates, smugglers, and other unsavory characters.
- Owner: [["One-Eyed" Jack Flint]] — A rough and gruff man known to turn a blind eye to illegal activities in exchange for a few extra coins.

### The Golden Hook
Quality: Aristocratic (4g per night)
A renowned seafood restaurant serving freshly caught fish and other delicacies.
- Chef/Owner: [[Henri Leclair]] — Charismatic and reputable for his innovative and flavorful dishes.

## Guilds & Organizations

### The Green Gryphons Mercenary Guild
A well-established guild where adventurers and mercenaries can find work, training, and camaraderie. The guild is known for taking on dangerous and high-paying jobs.
- Leader: [[Commander Alaric Brack]]
	- Description: Human, middle-aged, heavy set, shaggy red hair, braided beard (Viking style).

### The Tilted Scales Merchant Guild
The hub for the city's wealthy merchants and traders. It serves as a place for negotiations, agreements, and the setting of trade policies within the city and beyond.
- Guildmaster: [[Lysandra Goldspindle]] — A shrewd and intelligent businesswoman. Known for her knack for diplomacy and negotiation, she constantly strives to ensure fair commerce.

### The Azure Sail Shipwrights
A reputable shipbuilding company responsible for constructing and repairing many of the vessels that sail the southern coast.
- Shipwright: [[Morgan Redwood]] — Highly skilled in the craft.

## Shops & Commerce

### Market Square
A bustling marketplace filled with stalls offering fresh produce, fish, spices, and various imported goods. A lively place for locals to shop, trade, and socialize.

### Ironsong Forge / Lumberloft
A well-regarded blacksmith known for crafting high-quality weapons and armor, situated next to the lumber yard.
- Blacksmith: [[Thora Ironsong]] — Talented smith, eager to discuss her craft.
- Woodworker: [[Freya Ironsong]] — Thora's twin sister who works in the Lumberloft next door.
- Description: Both are gorgeous gnomes in their late 20s, with wavy brown hair tied up into pigtails with matching ties.

### The Silver Waves Alchemist
An alchemy shop offering a wide variety of potions, ingredients, and magical supplies.
- Owner: [[Agatha Nightshade]] — A mysterious and enigmatic alchemist.

### The Albatross Trading Company
A prominent trading company with connections throughout the Empire.
- Owner: [[Jasper Harrington]] — An ambitious merchant always seeking new opportunities and partnerships.

### The Tidal Pearl
A fine jewelry store specializing in pearls and other precious gems found in the region. Caters to the wealthy and discerning clientele of Darmouth.
- Proprietor: [[Lady Isabella Montague]] — Elegant and refined.

### Sundew General Store
A friendly, family-run general goods store that offers a wide range of everyday items and supplies.
- Owners: [[Oliver Sundew]] and [[Maribel Sundew]] — A cheerful husband-and-wife team.

## Government & Law

### Town Hall (Governor's Hall)
The civic center of Darmouth, representing the might and prosperity of the Empire. It's where the city's officials conduct affairs.
- Governor: [[Eamon Hightower]] — A true Imperial loyalist, appointed because Darmouth was built from scratch by the Empire. He runs the city as an extension of Imperial will rather than a governed community.

### The Imperial Lawmaster's Office
Positioned near the center of the city, this formidable edifice is where order is maintained and Imperial laws are interpreted. The [[Skyreach]] schematics were stolen from this building by [[Ella Sable]] and the [[Order of Ravens]] in Session 03.
- Lawmaster: [[Adelia Veridhart]] — A no-nonsense, hardworking woman with an unerring sense of justice, known for fair and impartial judgment.

### Darmouth Guard Barracks
A well-fortified and orderly building housing the city's guard and local law enforcement.
- Captain: [[Gavric Stonehelm]] — Commands the guards with a firm but fair hand.

### Darmouth Bastille
Located on the outskirts, this is a grim and foreboding fortress where those who break the Empire's laws are confined. Thick stone walls and constant patrols make it inescapable. The party broke into the Bastille via the sewers in Session 04 and freed [[Ella Sable]].
- Warden: [[Korban Slate]] — A stern, unyielding, and incorruptible man who maintains strict order with relentless efficiency.

### Harbor Master's Office
A stately building overseeing the comings and goings of ships.
- Harbor Master: [[Linus Clearwater]] — Ensures the smooth operation of the port.

## Faith & Knowledge

### Temple of the Seven Divines
A beautiful and serene temple dedicated to the worship of the Seven. Contains a hidden subterranean escape passage used by the party in Session 04, facilitated by [[Sister Arianna Brightstar]]. [[Kai]] experienced a divine vision here involving a goddess who claimed him as her champion.
- High Priestess: [[Sister Arianna Brightstar]] — Wise and compassionate, she offers guidance and blessings to those who seek the aid of the goddess.

### The Mariner's Library
A quiet, well-stocked library dedicated to the collection and preservation of maritime knowledge, charts, and lore.
- Librarian: [[Seraphina Blackwood]] — Knowledgeable and always eager to assist visitors in their research.

## Restricted

- The [[Order of Ravens]] still maintains a cell in Darmouth. The party has not returned since Session 04, so players are unaware of its current status or composition.
- The Imperial crackdown specifically targets anyone believed to have sheltered or assisted escaped prisoners — [[Eliza Farthington]] at the Seafarer's Rest, the [[Ironsong]] sisters, and others who interacted with the party are potential pressure points the Empire could exploit.
- The temple escape tunnel is known to the Order of Ravens through [[Sister Arianna Brightstar]]'s involvement; it remains a live asset.
