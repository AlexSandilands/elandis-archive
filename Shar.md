Shar — **Major** Lore entry, subtype **Deity**. Brainstorm notes (spun off from [[Selûne]]). The dark sister: a **God of Old** like Selûne, her shadow and eternal opposite. Where Selûne is the forgotten *good*, Shar is the forgotten *threat* — and a sharp contrast case, because she has **no public worship at all**: the world doesn't know her name, only the fragment "the Lady of Vengeance" that [[High Priest Oldir]] gave [[Kai]]. Almost the entire entry is `## Restricted`.

## Established (from the vault)

- Known only as **the Lady of Vengeance** / **the Dark Sister** — [[Selûne]]'s dark sister. [[High Priest Oldir]] warned [[Kai]] she had "taken notice" of him (Session 20). *(Session 20, High Priest Oldir)*
- Her mark on Kai: the **black stars** in his Starry Form (Session 26) — half his stars burned black, which Oldir read as her corruption taking hold; he urged Kai to "walk the path of light" to break her grip. *(Session 26)*
- The secondary moon **Sha** is named for her (forgotten etymology — see [[Calendar of Elandis]]). *(Calendar)*

## What she is

- **A God of Old**, sister and opposite to [[Selûne]]. The moon's shadow to Selûne's moonlight. Based on FR's **Shar** (darkness, night, loss, secrets, the primordial dark, comfort-through-oblivion) — kept light.
- **Portfolio (this world's twist):** suffering, vengeance, and chaos. Her power **grows from needless anguish** — not just atrocity but the small stuff: a cruel joke, a petty prank, casual spite. The more pointless cruelty in the world, the stronger she gets.
- **Allegiance: only herself, and chaos.** She is *not* aligned with the Empire, [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow|Kharazoth]], or [[Tiamat]]. A second [[The Shattering|Shattering]] would glut her on suffering, so their works feed her — but she serves none of them; the overlap is incidental.

## Worship & belief (public level)

- **None openly.** She has no temples, no clergy, no faith — the world doesn't even hold her as a half-remembered old god the way it dimly does Selûne. Her name survives only in the moon **Sha** (its origin lost) and, now, in the warning a frightened priest gave one marked man. This near-total absence of public presence is the entry's defining feature.

## Restricted

- **She is contesting Selûne's Chosen.** The black stars are Shar working to corrupt [[Kai]] into a *dark* champion before [[Selûne]] can complete him as hers. The two sisters are fighting over his soul; Oldir's "path of light" is literally the terms of that contest. (Cross-reference Selûne's restricted notes.)
- **Why now:** Selûne broke her age-long silence because she fears a **second Shattering**; Shar's sudden interest in Kai is the shadow side of the same moment — where Selûne reaches for a champion, Shar reaches to turn him.
- **Her win condition is suffering itself**, at any scale. She doesn't need the world to end; she needs it to *hurt*. That makes her a wildcard threat distinct from the dragon plot — she might aid or sabotage anyone if it sows more anguish.
- **Ascendant nights:** the inverse of Selûne's Unshadowed — when **Sha is full and Sel is dark** (the 1st of each elemental season, 4×/year; see [[Calendar of Elandis]]). Her strongest nights, though no rite marks them. Possible name TBD.

### DM notes / hooks / open threads
- **Plot hooks:** the tug-of-war over Kai (black stars vs the path of light); whether she ever manifests an agent/cult of her own as the second Shattering nears; using a moment of mass suffering (a massacre, a betrayal) as her power spike.
- **Open threads:** does she want Kai *turned*, or just *broken*? Is there a mortal already serving her unknowingly (feeding her with cruelty)? What does a "dark champion" of hers actually become?
- **FR source to mine lightly:** Shar — loss, secrets, forgotten things, the offer of oblivion to the grieving; the eternal twin-struggle with Selûne.
