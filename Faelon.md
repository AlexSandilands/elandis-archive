A small mining settlement at the foot of the southern mountain range, half a day's ride west of [[Camaar]]. Minor location — visited by the party during the Salla the Brave side quest (Sessions 10.5–12).

## Established
- Half a day's ride inland (west) of Camaar, at the base of the southern mountains (Session 9 prep, Camaar.md)
- Faelon is the trade waypoint between the mountain mines and Camaar's docks: raw minerals and stone flow down to the port; provisions and equipment go back up (Camaar.md)
- The inn is called **The Redleaf Inn**, named for the Everred Forest nearby; run by Dammond and Ania; 2s per room per night (Session 11 prep)
- An old man named Torvin drinks at the Redleaf bar and tells the local folk tale of Salla the Brave (Session 11 prep)
- **Tara** — old woman, twin sister of the late Salla the Brave; lives in a dilapidated house in poverty on the edge of the village; found closure in Session 12 when the party returned her sister's bones and locket, and spoke of leaving the village afterward (Sessions 10.5, 12)
- A local exchange sells spell components and general trade goods; the party purchased obsidian and silver dust there (Session 12)
- **Tumbler's Crag** is nearby — jagged rocky terrain north of the town within the Everred Forest (Sessions 11–12)

## Geography

- Sits at the base of the southern mountain range, surrounded by the **Everred Forest** — a stand of trees that remain a deep, vivid red year-round, regardless of season. Locals accept it as simply the way things are; outsiders find it striking.
- The Everred Forest runs between the town and the crags above it, thinning as the treeline gives way to the rocky high country
- Tumbler's Crag lies to the north, a jagged stretch of rocky terrain within or just beyond the forest

## The Mines

- Primary industry is ore extraction: common metals, stone, and some raw obsidian; the mountain seam is old and reliable but not spectacular
- No gemstones — those come from the Silver Mountain range further north up the continent's spine
- Mining families have worked the same seams for generations; the operation is collective and informal, not company-owned

## People & Governance

- Faelon attracts people who want to leave society behind — it is emphatically not a city, and residents prefer it that way
- No Imperial presence; the Empire doesn't extend its reach this far into the mountains and the locals have no interest in inviting it
- Governed by nothing more formal than reputation: **Durras Velt**, a weathered half-dwarf who has been in the mines longer than anyone else can remember, is the de facto authority — he sets work schedules, arbitrates disputes, and speaks for the town to outsiders. No title, no office; just the person everyone defers to
- Serious disputes get settled at the Redleaf over ale

## Atmosphere

- Quiet, cold, and self-sufficient; the rhythm of the place is the shift bell from the mines and the smell of woodsmoke
- The red forest is the town's one striking feature — locals barely notice it anymore, but it unsettles newcomers
- The folk tale of Salla the Brave has circulated for decades; as of Session 12, the party resolved her spirit, though few townspeople know the full truth
