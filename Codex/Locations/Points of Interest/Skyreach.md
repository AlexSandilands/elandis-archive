Skyreach was a secret Imperial viaduct raised over the [[Aeolian Chasm]] in the far south of [[Valtorra]] — the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire's]] answer to the treacherous sea routes it could not control. A decade in the making and built on the labour of the enslaved, it was designed to bridge the great gorge and carry armies and supplies overland from [[Darmouth]] into the continental heartland, bypassing the [[Mawbreakers|Mawbreakers']] grip on [[The Maw]]. It never carried a single cart. In the winter of 2507, a rebel force breached its walls, scaled its four-hundred-foot pillars, and brought the colossus down in a cataclysm that shook the southern frontier.

## Location & Geography

Skyreach occupied the narrowest crossing point of the [[Aeolian Chasm]], roughly fifty miles west of [[Darmouth]], where the gorge walls drew close enough for a viaduct to be viable. At this reach of the canyon the cliffs were sheer and high, snow-dusted in winter, with the Aeolian River running far below along the canyon floor.

The complex was more than a bridge. A great stone wall sealed off the river gorge at the eastern approach, blocking any force from advancing upriver without first breaching the garrison. Behind the wall lay the compound — barracks, guard towers, and the slave camp where the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]] held its prisoners and workforce. From the compound floor, the viaduct's massive stone pillars rose four hundred feet to the height of the gorge rim, each hollow, with a spiral staircase running through its interior and traps set into the landings to deter any intruder who made it past the gates. At their summit the bridge deck crossed the void to connect the eastern bank to the western cliff — though the uppermost gatehouse was still unfinished when the structure fell.

## History

Construction began in secret, sometime in the decade before 2507. The [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]] funnelled prisoners, dissidents, and enslaved captives into the work camp that grew beneath the rising pillars, labouring them under armed guard to raise the arches that would vault the gorge. The project was deliberately concealed: its blueprints were locked in the Lawmaster's archive in [[Darmouth]], and the few letters referencing it spoke only in guarded terms — of "forward payments" and increasing prisoner capacity, with instructions that the work must be completed "before winter."

The strategic logic was sound. Without a crossing at the [[Aeolian Chasm|chasm]], [[Darmouth]] was effectively cut off from the continental interior, and the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire's]] only southern alternative — the port of [[Val Miriel]] and its river routes — was vulnerable to the [[Mawbreakers|Mawbreakers']] influence over [[The Maw]]. Skyreach was meant to make [[Darmouth]] the primary southern port and drive new supply highways straight across the gorge into the central plains of [[Valtorra]]. Once the viaduct opened, the [[Mawbreakers]] would lose their strategic leverage entirely.

Intelligence operatives of the [[Order of Ravens]] and their allies in [[Darmouth]] had caught wind of the project and moved to steal the schematics. In the autumn of 2507, a rebel contact named [[Ella Sable]] was captured while attempting to lift the blueprints from the Lawmaster's office. After a daring rescue, the stolen documents reached [[Barak Stormrider]] of the [[Mawbreakers]], whose engineer [[Gromdal]] studied the plans and identified the structural weak points that could bring the viaduct down — the joints at the mid-pillar landings and the underside of the unfinished bridge deck.

## The Fall of Skyreach

In the early hours of the 18th Water, 1st 2507, a rebel fleet led by [[Barak Stormrider]] and [[The Albatross]] reached the [[Aeolian Chasm]] after a weeks-long voyage south. While the [[Mawbreakers]] launched a loud frontal assault on the labour camp to draw the garrison's attention and free the slaves, a small party infiltrated the fortress walls under supernatural silence — scaling the perimeter, clearing the western gatehouse, and winching open the main iron gate to admit the rebel army. The alarm was raised as the gate opened, and the assault began in chaos.

The party climbed the four-hundred-foot interior of the viaduct's pillars, fighting through defenders and triggering traps along the way, and planted gunpowder kegs at the structural weak points [[Gromdal]] had identified. [[Eryndor]] descended on a conjured fly to lash the final charges to the underside of the bridge deck. With the fuses burning, the party dropped from a lower balcony and fought their way to the extraction point — where they found [[Barak Stormrider|Barak]] gravely wounded and an elite Hand of the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]] blocking their escape. They defeated the warrior, pressed a rusty nail — taken from their old prison ship — into his forehead, and fled to [[The Albatross]] as the countdown reached zero.

Skyreach collapsed in a thunderous cataclysm of rubble and dust that was visible for miles. The bridge deck sheared away, the pillars fractured and fell into the gorge, and the great wall crumbled as the shockwave tore through the compound. Thirty [[Mawbreakers|Mawbreaker]] sailors died holding the line to let the prisoners escape. The ruins remain in the [[Aeolian Chasm|chasm]] to this day, the shattered arches and toppled pillars a lasting monument to the rebellion's first great strike against the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]].

## [[The Bloody Nails|Campaign: The Bloody Nails]]

#### [[Session 01 - The Rusty Nail]]

Among the wreckage of the Imperial prison ship they had escaped, the party found a letter in the captain's quarters addressed to "Captain Armin" from an unknown correspondent. The letter spoke of a forward payment to "increase prisoner capacity" and insisted the work at Skyreach must be completed before winter — the first intimation any of them had of the project.

#### [[Session 03 - The Streets of Darmouth]]

[[Gemma Finegold]] revealed her rebel ties in [[Darmouth]] and introduced the party to [[Uric Graad]] of the [[Order of Ravens]], who explained that [[Ella Sable]] had been captured attempting to steal Skyreach schematics from the Lawmaster's office. The party resolved to break her out and recover the intelligence.

#### [[Session 08 - The High Road]]

At the [[Mawbreakers|Mawbreakers']] compound in [[Camaar]], the stolen package from [[Darmouth]] was opened and its contents revealed: detailed blueprints for Skyreach. [[Barak Stormrider]] laid out the plan that would define the months to come — use the schematics to find the structure's weak points, sail south, and bring the viaduct down before the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]] could finish it.

#### [[Session 12 - How to Start a Rebellion]]

In a war room aboard the [[Mawbreakers|Mawbreakers']] docked fleet, [[Barak Stormrider]] and [[Gromdal]] briefed the party on the demolition plan. The mission had two arms: a frontal assault on the labour camp by the [[Mawbreakers]] to draw the garrison, and a covert infiltration by the party to open the gate and plant gunpowder kegs at the viaduct's structural weak points. The window was narrow — the work had to be done before winter closed the sea routes.

#### [[Session 15 - Operation Skyreach]]

After the long voyage south, the party arrived at the gorge and discovered that Skyreach was not merely a construction site but a fortress, its great wall blocking the river. Under the cover of a freezing, moonless night — and the shroud of a Pass Without Trace — they ambushed the wall patrol, breached the western gatehouse, and winched open the main iron gate to admit the charging [[Mawbreakers|Mawbreakers]]. A guard reached the alarm bell before they could stop him, and the fortress erupted into noise as the rebel assault began.

#### [[Session 16 - The Fall of Skyreach]]

The assault reached its climax as the party fought through the burning slave camp and climbed the viaduct's four-hundred-foot pillars, planting charges at every structural joint [[Gromdal]] had flagged. At the extraction point below, they found [[Barak Stormrider|Barak]] near death and a Hand of the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]] waiting — an elite warrior who, to the party's shock, appeared to recognise [[Vaeleran]], calling him by a different name. After a brutal fight, [[Vaeleran]] delivered the killing blow. [[Eryndor]] drove a rusty nail into the Hand's forehead — the Bloody Nails' first mark — and the group fled to [[The Albatross]] as the charges detonated. Skyreach fell, and with it the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire's]] southern supply strategy.

## Trivia

- The rusty nail driven into the defeated Hand of the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]] at Skyreach became the first act that gave [[The Bloody Nails]] their name — [[Eryndor]] had kept the nail from their original prison ship.
- Thirty [[Mawbreakers|Mawbreaker]] sailors died at Skyreach, roughly half the crews that held the river crossing while the prisoners escaped.
- The uppermost gatehouse of the viaduct was still unfinished when the bridge fell, meaning Skyreach had never carried a single crossing before its destruction.
