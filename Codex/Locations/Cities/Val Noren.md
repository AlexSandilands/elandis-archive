Val Noren is the northernmost of [[Valtorra]]'s ancient human city-states — one of the six Kingdoms of Old, and the first to break free of the [[Valtorran Empire|Crimson Empire]] in living memory. It stands on a rugged island off the far north-east coast of the continent, its high stone walls pressed against mountain foothills, its heart cut by the glittering Renite River. For over a thousand years, the city has drawn its wealth and its identity from the ore veins of those mountains — [[Renite Steel]], the most coveted blade-metal in the known world — and the faith its people keep in [[Torgar Earthshaker]] has given it a martial-spiritual character unlike any other city in Valtorra. When the Imperial siege was broken in 2507, [[King Marius Noren]] declared it the Shield of the North.

## Geography & Layout

Val Noren occupies the largest island in the chain running north-east from [[Valtorra]]'s coast into the frozen sea. Mountains dominate the northern and central interior of the island; the city was built at their southern base where the land drops to a rocky shoreline. The Renite River descends from those heights, running east to west through the city before reaching the sea, and the glitter of its ore-flecked waters is visible from any point along its banks.

The city is encircled by high ancient stone walls, pierced by a southern gate toward the coast and an eastern gate at the wharf district. The river bisects the interior, dividing the city into a northern bank — the higher, older ground, dominated by the Keep and the great temple — and a lower southern bank where the markets and working trades cluster. Two river gate towers, one on each bank, control movement across the water within the walls. On the eastern side, Chandlers' Wharf handles the coastal and river trade that keeps the city supplied through the long months when the sea freezes.

Access to the mainland is by sea during the warm seasons and across the shifting ice of the [[Frostway]] in winter. [[Point Blackrock]] is the nearest mainland transit point — a crossing of roughly a week on guided ice when conditions permit. Both routes carry risk; the sea is treacherous against ice and storm, and the Frostway has its own dangers. This near-isolation has shaped the city and its people in equal measure.

## History

Val Noren is one of the six ancient human Kingdoms of Old — the city-states that rebuilt [[Valtorra]] in the long aftermath of the [[Chaos Wars]]. It predates the [[Valtorran Empire]] by over a thousand years. Its origins are more organic than civic: the Noren family held an early claim to the ore veins in the mountains above the site, and the settlement that grew around their mines attracted generation after generation of smiths, miners, and traders drawn to the remarkable quality of the metal. As the settlement matured, the Norens formalized their rule, built walls, and established the dynastic kingdom that gave the city its name.

For centuries Val Noren traded [[Renite Steel]] south to the rest of the continent. Its island position and considerable fortifications kept it outside most of the continent's conflicts. When the [[Valtorran Empire|Crimson Empire]] rose under [[Empress Morganna Eventide]] roughly a hundred years ago, Val Noren fell alongside every other human kingdom — not through any particular weakness, but because nothing on the continent withstood the conquest. The Noren royal line was preserved under occupation and repurposed as Imperial governors rather than kings.

The rebellion came later here than on the mainland. [[King Marius Noren]], guided over years by his advisor [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana Wynne]], expelled the Imperial garrison and declared independence. The Empire's response was swift: [[Dread General Gerard Blackmarsh]] arrived with an army of ten thousand soldiers and a siege that would last the better part of a year. The Loyalists held the northern half of the city — the Keep, the temple, and a secondary barricade line — while Imperial forces held the southern half and maintained their camp outside the walls. The siege was broken in 2507 when the river gate towers fell and the Loyalist army crossed on a pontoon bridge under fog, and Marius declared the city the Shield of the North.

## Government & Imperial Presence

Val Noren is ruled as a free kingdom under [[King Marius Noren]], the first of his line to hold the title of king rather than Imperial governor since the conquest. His council includes [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana Wynne]] as chief advisor, [[Captain Erendir Thalos]] commanding the city guard, and Marshal Kaelen Dravorn leading the rebel forces that became the city's standing army. Imperial authority has been expelled entirely; the roughly four thousand prisoners taken at the liberation were the last formal Imperial presence in the city.

The Keep's great hall, converted into a refugee shelter during the siege, is slowly returning to civic use as the city rebuilds. Marius's attention has turned outward — toward [[Val Miriel]] and the broader fate of the north.

## The People

Roughly fifty thousand souls live within Val Noren's walls. It is a human city in its ancient identity, though decades of occupation and the siege have left it scarred: the southern half bore the brunt of the Imperial hold, and reconstruction is a long project. For most of the city's history, daily life has turned on two things — the forge and the faith. The Noren are not a seafaring people in the manner of [[Val Miriel]]; they are mountain and forge people, insular by geography and hardened by winter.

They are known beyond the island for their reserve with outsiders and their stubbornness under pressure. When the only routes in and out are frozen sea and treacherous ice, self-sufficiency is not a virtue but a necessity, and it became the city's defining character long before the Empire arrived.

## Culture & Faith

[[Torgar Earthshaker]] is the city's dominant god, and his worship in Val Noren takes a form distinct from the rest of Valtorra. Here, faith and craft are the same thing. Temple walls are hung with weapons and armour — not trophies but offerings, expressions of devotion in the material that Torgar governs. The forge is a sacred space. The finest smiths are considered touched by the god, and the act of working metal carries a reverence that outsiders sometimes find strange.

The combination of deep faith, specialist craft, and a wealth built on a single remarkable material has given the city a reputation — and a self-image — of uncompromising quality. A Val Noren blade is not just a weapon; it is a statement.

### The Stonesworn

The [[Stonesworn]] are Val Noren's guild of master smiths and the highest institutional expression of the city's combined faith and craft. They hold the most prestigious [[Renite Steel]] commissions, train apprentices in both metallurgy and religious practice, and maintain the armory of [[The Stoneheart]] temple. Their mark on a blade is among the strongest guarantors of quality in Valtorra. Membership is selective and their standards exacting; the guild predates the [[Valtorran Empire]] by centuries.

### Renite Steel

The Renite River carries a faint mineral shimmer even on overcast days — tiny specks of ore washed down from the mountain veins above the city, suspended in the current. The ore itself must be mined from those veins, an operation the Noren family controlled for centuries and the Empire seized during occupation. The refined metal takes a sharper edge than conventional steel while remaining notably lighter, and in the hands of a skilled smith it holds that edge without the brittleness that usually follows. A Stonesworn-forged [[Renite Steel]] weapon is the most reliable way to own one — though the ore reaches smiths elsewhere too, at considerable cost.

## Districts

Val Noren divides naturally into four areas, shaped by the Renite River and the rising terrain.

### The Keep Ward

The high ground of the northern bank, where the city's Keep stands against the mountain. The nobility of Val Noren — those houses that remained through the occupation — maintain their residences here. Lady Elira Norven, Lord Veyric Halstrom, and Baroness Maedra Sornel each hold property in the Ward; their politics and the question of what a post-liberation Val Noren owes its opportunistic supporters are an ongoing concern for Marius's council.

### The Stoneheart Quarter

The densest concentration of ancient stonework in the city, centred on the great temple and the smithing yards that cluster around it. The smell of coal smoke is permanent here. This was rebel-held territory throughout the siege, and the Quarter's stones absorbed much of the heaviest fighting.

#### Ashen Forge *(smithy — Stonesworn guild forge)*

An ancient smithy, its roof blackened from siege fires but its forges still burning. The [[Stonesworn]] have worked iron and [[Renite Steel]] on these anvils for generations. In the days following the liberation, the divine crafting of the Moonlit Aegis took place here — the smith who forged it woke from the work with no memory of doing so.

- Helda Coalbraid (master smith, Stonesworn) — tireless and blunt, celebrated across the city for the Moonlit Aegis.
- Milo Drass (apprentice) — teenage, eager, usually in the way.

#### Ember Hall *(guildhall — rebel coordination post)*

A broad timber-roofed hall, once the guild house of the city's chandlers before that trade declined. During the siege, rebels used it for maps, planning, and stored weapons — its candlelit rooms became a symbol of defiance.

- Serra Duskvale (rebel coordinator) — veteran, pragmatic, quick to cut through argument.
- Bran "One-Eye" Korrin (messenger) — scarred, reliable, slips through lines in ways nobody else can explain.

### The Cloisters and the Wharf

The eastern face of the city, where the Renite River approaches its mouth and the ground levels toward the waterfront. The two river gate towers flank the main river crossing within the walls here.

#### Saint Aveline's Cloister *(monastery and infirmary)*

A centuries-old monastery dedicated to a healer-saint, half-ruined by siege bombardment. Its stone cloister and chapel served as the city's main infirmary throughout the occupation — crowded with cots, refugees, and the smell of incense struggling against blood. The armour purification that produced the Moonlit Aegis was performed within these walls.

- Brother Calem (priest) — weary, selfless, spent of nearly everything but still present.
- Dara Ironleaf (druid, herbalist) — runs makeshift gardens on the roof; supplies what the healers need.

#### Chandlers' Wharf *(river warehouses — trade and supply)*

A row of waterfront warehouses once used for fish oil, wax, and salt. During the siege, rebel smugglers ran supplies into the city at night along the river, slipping past Imperial patrols under cover of dark.

- Ferren Latch (wharfmaster turned smuggler) — missing three fingers from a deal gone wrong; reliable now.
- Ina Brook (boatwoman) — young, fearless on the water, runs messages along the river.

### The South Quarter

The lower, rougher bank. Imperial forces held this half throughout the siege, and it shows: buildings are partially demolished, the market hall was converted into a refugee camp, and the main order of business is reconstruction. The Bell Tower of Calmor Street, its bells shattered by Imperial stone-throwers and converted into a rebel sniper post, is the quarter's most recognisable feature.

The North Market Hall — called the Shattered Market during the occupation — still functions as a tent city of barter stalls and salvaged goods. The Eastgate Granaries are crammed with displaced families. At Widow's Fountain nearby, Erya Talven keeps spirits alive with folk songs and stories of the siege.

#### The Nail & Tankard *(tavern — rebel gathering place)*

A formerly ordinary inn, its signboard now painted with a red nail. The cellars hold weapon caches; the common room holds rumour, celebration, and the occasional heated argument about who did what in the siege.

- Jareth Holloway (innkeeper) — stout, fiery, turns every toast into a speech.
- Selka Morrin (courier) — sharp-eyed, in and out with news.

## Landmarks

### The Stoneheart

[[The Stoneheart]] is the great temple of [[Torgar Earthshaker]] at the city's centre — an ancient structure set within a stone courtyard that holds a towering statue of the god, depicted looking upward with one fist raised. The main doors open to a long hall leading to an octagonal central chamber, with two lateral wings. It predates the [[Valtorran Empire]] and, by local tradition, is the oldest continuously active place of worship in the north of Valtorra.

### The Ratways

The old storm drains and cisterns beneath Val Noren — reeking of torch smoke and mildew — make up a city-wide network that generations of smugglers have picked their way through, but never fully mapped. The Ratways are labyrinthine enough that even their most practiced guides know only their own routes; entire sections go unexplored for decades at a stretch. That obscurity cuts both ways: during the Imperial siege, it was a Ratways tunnel that gave Imperial agents a path beneath the rebel barricades and into the Keep, allowing them to abduct [[King Marius Noren]] while the defenders held the surface. "Rook" Lerris guides those who need to move unseen; Imka Vey fills known sections of tunnel with traps and deadfalls against anyone who follows.

## Factions

### Mawbreakers

The [[Mawbreakers]], the rebel sailing company out of [[Camaar]], operated in Val Noren throughout the siege — bringing the party through the occupied city, coordinating rebel supply, and ultimately leading the pontoon bridge operation that broke the Imperial hold. [[Barak Stormrider]] has other seas to sail, but his relationship with [[King Marius Noren]]'s court is one of mutual regard.

### Stonesworn

The [[Stonesworn]] are Val Noren's guild of [[Torgar Earthshaker]]-devoted master smiths and the guardians of the city's combined craft and faith tradition. See Culture & Faith above.

## [[The Bloody Nails|Campaign: The Bloody Nails]]

#### [[Session 01 - The Rusty Nail]]

Val Noren is the campaign's origin point. Under Imperial occupation, the city was a departure port for the Empire's prison ships running south to labour camps. The party's characters were among those prisoners, held in the hold of a ship that departed Val Noren before the campaign began. Their escape at sea, and the campaign itself, began there.

#### [[Session 35 - The Frostway]]

After crossing the [[Frostway]] with the surviving Frostwardens, the party arrived at the outskirts of Val Noren and observed it from a distance: high walls, a frozen dock, thousands of Imperial tents encircling the city, and a pillar of black smoke rising from within the walls — a cathedral set alight outside rebel territory.

#### [[Session 36 - General Korvas]]

The party infiltrated the occupied city, navigating Imperial patrols through back alleys and trap-laden buildings before being captured by [[Dread General Gerard Blackmarsh]] and [[Marcelle the Broken]]. They narrowly avoided execution before [[Barak Stormrider]] and a squad of [[Mawbreakers]], disguised in Imperial armour, fought the soldiers back and extracted them.

#### [[Session 37 - March of the Dead]]

The party met [[King Marius Noren]] in the rebel Keep and found him personally ensuring the safety of displaced civilians. He spoke with them about the siege and the threat of the [[Red Priests]]. That night, Imperial agents used a coordinated undead assault on the city's barricades as a diversion while a Red Priest breached the Keep and abducted Marius.

#### [[Session 39 - The Stoneheart]]

The party fought through undead-held tunnels and crypts beneath [[The Stoneheart]], found Marius bound and under guard in the royal vault, and freed him from a Red Priest's custody.

#### [[Session 40 - General Blackmarsh]]

The confrontation with [[Dread General Gerard Blackmarsh]] in Val Noren ended in the general's defeat. During the fight Marius cast Healing Word to pull [[Kai]] back from the edge of death. The party escaped through the Ratways.

#### [[Session 41 - Reclaim the Reclaimer]]

A war council in the Keep. Marius, [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana Wynne]], and [[Barak Stormrider]] laid out the strategic situation — approximately four thousand Imperial soldiers remaining, two [[Hand of The Empire|Hands of the Empire]] among them. The two-pronged liberation plan was agreed: the party would take the river gate towers from the inside while the [[Mawbreakers]] built a pontoon bridge under fog.

#### [[Session 42 - Liberation of Val Noren]]

In a coordinated pre-dawn operation, the party infiltrated and silenced both river gate towers without raising a general alarm. The Mawbreakers, under Barak's command, constructed a pontoon bridge beneath a massive fog bank while thousands of Loyalist soldiers waited to cross. As the fog cleared, the full battle broke across the bridge — the party sustained the Loyalist vanguard and targeted Imperial leadership while Marius led the charge and took a wound from the Imperial commander. The remaining [[Hand of The Empire|Hands of the Empire]] committed suicide rather than surrender; the Imperial soldiers broke and yielded. Marius declared Val Noren the Shield of the North.

#### [[Session 43 - The Shield of the North]]

The morning after liberation. Marius received the party in formal audience — rewarding them with twenty thousand gold in platinum, Greater Healing Potions, and a family heirloom [[Renite Steel]] dagger. [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana Wynne]] taught [[Dondar]] the Teleportation Circle sigil for Val Noren and [[Glimmerforge]]. [[Kai]] visited [[The Stoneheart]], negotiated permission to teach Selune's rituals to the acolytes, and then purified the Dread General's cursed armour in a vision of the Astral Plane. The refined metal was forged into the Moonlit Aegis by Helda Coalbraid at the Ashen Forge in a divine fugue state.

## Trivia

- The prefix *Val-* runs through the names of the continent's six ancient human kingdoms — Val Noren, [[Val Miriel]], Val Luminor, Val Solis, Val Aran, and Val Aerie. It is a relic of the Kingdoms of Old; the most common interpretation is simply "seat" or "hold," making each name a variant of "the hold of its people or its founder."
- The Renite River gives its name both to the ore and to the city's primary export. The mineral shimmer in the water is genuine: in the right light, a slow bend of the Rennite catches suspended ore dust and looks, briefly, like scattered sparks.
- The [[Stonesworn]] tradition of hanging weapons as temple offerings is unique to Val Noren. In other cities, [[Torgar Earthshaker]]'s temples are typically decorated with stone tools and geological specimens; the martial element is considered a northern variation rooted in the city's centuries of forge-work and ore-extraction.
