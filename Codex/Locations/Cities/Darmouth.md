**Darmouth** is a purpose-built Imperial port on the southern coast of [[Valtorra]], founded roughly fifty years ago as the planned sea-end of the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire's]] most ambitious supply highway. Where the old cities of Valtorra grew across centuries of trade and organic settlement, Darmouth was engineered — its military grid of straight cobblestone streets and colonial timber buildings laid down by Imperial surveyors with the economy of people who needed a port and needed it to work. The harbour opens onto the open southern sea; the gate faces north toward the Aeolian Mountains and the unfinished bridge of [[Skyreach]] that was meant to make this city the continent's premier inland trade hub. In [[Skyreach|Skyreach's]] absence, it is a well-garrisoned port with grand ambitions that have nowhere yet to go.

## Geography & Layout

Darmouth sits on the southern coast of [[Valtorra]] at the foot of the Aeolian Mountains, on the open sea south of the [[Aeolian Chasm]] and well beyond the sheltered waters of the [[Gulf of Miriel]]. It is a city built on flat ground between the mountainside and the waterfront, and its layout makes this immediately legible.

A single broad cobblestone avenue runs dead-straight from the northern gatehouse to the harbour a mile and a half away, the spine of the whole city. Streets branch off it at perfect right angles, forming a disciplined grid across every block. Weathered colonial wooden buildings line every road, built in a uniform style that speaks of speed and function rather than craft; lantern posts stand at measured intervals. The grid gives the city its character — or rather its absence of one. Where the old Valtorran cities accumulated centuries of alleys, irregular plazas, and organic growth, Darmouth was designed from nothing and shows it.

Three areas define the city's north-to-south axis. The Civic Quarter around the northern gate is where the Empire's administrative and military presence concentrates. At the midpoint, Market Square opens into the city's commercial and social heart, surrounded by its trading blocks. At the southern end, the Harbour fronts the docks and the open sea. East of the main avenue, a hill rises apart from the grid, occupied entirely by the Temple of the Seven Divines and its surrounding grounds, its spires the tallest structures visible from the gate. On the western fringe, beyond the last grid block, the Darmouth Bastille stands on the outskirts in deliberate isolation from the city it serves.

## History

Darmouth was founded by the [[Valtorran Empire]] roughly fifty years ago, driven by a single strategic calculation: if the [[Aeolian Chasm]] could be bridged, a direct overland highway would run from the southern ocean coast north through the mountains to [[Val Luminor]] and the heart of the continent. Caravans could sail to Darmouth, offload, and move goods directly inland — bypassing the [[Gulf of Miriel]] and the [[Mawbreakers|Mawbreakers']] stranglehold on its shipping entirely. Darmouth was built as the sea-end of that future road, long before [[Skyreach]] was anywhere near complete.

For decades, investment flowed steadily into the city. The port grew, the merchant guilds established themselves, and the population — drawn by Imperial promises of a ground-floor stake in the continent's new great trade hub — expanded steadily. The military grid was not incidental to this vision: unlike the maze of the old cities, Darmouth could be policed, governed, and held. The design prioritised control, and the Empire had no objection to that.

The destruction of [[Skyreach]] cut this vision at its root. Without the crossing, there is no highway; without the highway, Darmouth handles a fraction of the traffic it was built for. The Empire has not abandoned the city — new plans for the southern supply route are slowly being drawn up — but the sense of a city that was promised a future and is still waiting for it sits heavily on the streets.

## Government & Imperial Presence

Darmouth was built by the Empire and is governed entirely in its name. Governor Eamon Hightower holds the city's administration — unlike the old-kingdom governors elsewhere on [[Valtorra]], Hightower has no dynastic claim to the seat; he was appointed on Imperial merit alone and governs accordingly. Lawmaster Adelia Veridhart oversees the city's courts and law with a reputation for unsentimental fairness that makes her as likely to act against a corrupt guardsman as against a smuggler. Guard Captain Gavric Stonehelm commands the watch.

Since the destruction of [[Skyreach]], Imperial military pressure in Darmouth has grown substantially. The Empire knows that the Skyreach schematics were stolen from the Lawmaster's Office here, that escaped Imperial prisoners passed through the city, and that rebel sympathisers are embedded among the population. Patrols have increased, arbitrary detentions are routine, and the Rusty Nail — the mark of the rebellion — has begun appearing chalked on walls and door-posts across the city. The response has been a sustained crackdown that has shifted Darmouth's atmosphere from ambitious colonial port to something closer to an occupied city.

## The People

Darmouth is young by Valtorran standards, and its population shows it. Where the old cities of the continent carry centuries of distinct culture, tradition, and rooted identity, Darmouth's settlers came in waves of Imperial-sponsored migration — human families, merchants, and tradespeople drawn by the promise of early access to the Empire's planned southern hub. The result is a city that feels less settled than most: practical in character, generally loyal to whoever maintains order, and without the deep civic traditions that older places take for granted.

The sailor population of the harbour adds rougher edges to the mix. Imperial soldiery is a constant presence, and the effect on the city's mood is considerable — a background tension that was always there but has grown considerably since the crackdown began. Merchants who staked their futures on Darmouth's promised growth feel stranded. Ordinary citizens have found themselves subject to scrutiny that feels less like law enforcement and more like collective suspicion.

## Culture & Faith

Daily life in Darmouth turns on the port and the market. Market Square is the city's social centre — the place where everyone from dockworkers to merchants to visiting sailors passes through in the course of an ordinary day, and where news and rumour move as freely as goods. Tides, arrivals, and departures structure the week.

The Temple of the Seven Divines on the hill to the east is the city's principal place of worship and its most architecturally distinguished structure, a marked contrast to the practical timber construction that dominates everywhere else. Faith in Darmouth is broad rather than deep — the Seven are acknowledged, the Temple maintained, and High Priestess Sister Arianna Brightstar well regarded — but Darmouth was not built around religion the way the older cities were, and it lacks the deep devotional culture of a [[Val Miriel]] or a [[Val Aerie]].

## Districts

### The Civic Quarter

The northern end of the city, clustered around the gatehouse, is where the Empire's administrative and military weight concentrates. The broad main avenue enters through the gate and passes the Governor's Hall and the Green Gryphons chapter in its first few blocks; the Imperial Lawmaster's Office stands in the north-western grid, a deliberately severe building visible from most of the quarter; and the Guard Barracks occupy the western edge, housing Darmouth's watch. The atmosphere here is businesslike and watchful, and has grown considerably more watchful since the recent crackdown.

#### Governor's Hall *(civic administration)*

The civic centre of the city — a sturdy timber-and-stone building just inside the gate, conspicuous for the Imperial seal above its entrance and little else. Working offices, not a monument.

- Eamon Hightower *(governor)* — a true Imperial appointee with no dynastic claim to the seat, courteous in manner and unyielding in his loyalty to the Empire.

#### Imperial Lawmaster's Office *(lawmaster's court)*

The formidable building where the Empire's law is interpreted and administered in Darmouth. Cases are heard here, warrants issued, and the city's legal order enforced. Its storage rooms hold confiscated materials and evidence — or did, until a particular theft made the security of those rooms a matter of Imperial concern.

- Adelia Veridhart *(lawmaster)* — no-nonsense and hardworking, with an unerring sense of justice and a reputation for impartiality that is genuinely earned.

#### Green Gryphons *(mercenary guild hall)*

The Darmouth chapter of the [[Green Gryphons]] mercenary guild occupies a hall on the main avenue in the Civic Quarter, well-positioned for both gate traffic and city contracts. The hall handles work ranging from caravan escort and building security to whatever difficult job the city's guilds and merchants need handled without fuss.

- Commander Alaric Brack *(chapter master)* — a middle-aged, heavy-set man with shaggy red hair and a braided beard, who runs the Darmouth branch with the temperament of a veteran soldier who has made peace with civilian life.

### Market Square and the Trading Quarter

The midpoint of the main avenue opens into Market Square — a broad open plaza of packed stone that is the city's loudest, busiest, and most lived-in space. Permanent and semi-permanent stalls fill most of it, trading in fresh fish, produce, spices, imported harbour goods, and whatever the surrounding merchants have on offer. The surrounding blocks hold the city's principal commercial establishments, each within a short walk of the square.

#### The Seafarer's Rest *(inn — comfortable)*

A clean, well-maintained inn a block off the square, catering to travelling merchants and ship officers. It has a reputation for reliability and a genuinely welcoming atmosphere — something that stands out in a city not always known for warmth.

- Eliza Farthington *(owner)* — a kind-hearted widow with a sad but warm smile and patience for difficult guests.

#### Sundew General Store *(general goods)*

A family-run shop carrying the full range of everyday supplies, with a reputation for treating customers like neighbours. Consistently stocked and consistently friendly.

- Oliver Sundew and Maribel Sundew *(owners)* — a cheerful husband-and-wife team.

#### The Silver Waves Alchemist *(alchemy)*

An alchemy shop offering potions, reagents, and magical supplies. The stock is solid and the proprietor knows her trade, though she keeps the specifics of her methods to herself.

- Agatha Nightshade *(owner)* — a self-contained and somewhat mysterious alchemist who inspires confidence without encouraging curiosity.

#### The Albatross Trading Company *(merchant trading)*

A prominent trading company with connections throughout the [[Valtorran Empire]], dealing in bulk goods and brokering contracts between merchants and suppliers. Positioned on the main avenue, a block north of the square.

- Jasper Harrington *(owner)* — an ambitious merchant always in search of new partnerships, who speaks the language of profit with practised fluency.

#### The Tilted Scales Merchant Guild *(merchant guild)*

The hub of Darmouth's merchant class — a place for negotiations, trade agreements, and the commercial policies that govern the city's markets. Since the failure of [[Skyreach]], the guild has lobbied hard for any intelligence on what the Empire plans to do next about the southern supply route.

- Lysandra Goldspindle *(guildmaster)* — a shrewd and intelligent businesswoman with a diplomat's manner and an unerring sense of where the profit is going.

#### Ironsong Forge / Lumberloft *(smithy — mid-grade and woodworking)*

A smithy and adjacent lumber workshop sharing a courtyard on the southern edge of the trading quarter. The forge produces weapons and armour of genuine quality; the lumberloft handles custom woodwork.

- [[Thora Ironsong]] *(blacksmith)* and [[Freya Ironsong]] *(woodworker)* — twin gnomish sisters in their late twenties, hair tied back in matching pigtails: Thora's bright blue, Freya's soft pink. Thora is eager to discuss her craft at length; Freya is quieter but no less skilled.

#### The Tidal Pearl *(jeweller — high-end)*

A refined jewellery shop a block south-east of the square, specialising in pearls and precious gems from the southern sea. Its clientele are the city's wealthier merchants and visiting Imperial officials.

- Lady Isabella Montague *(proprietor)* — elegant and precise, with an eye for quality and a manner calibrated for discerning customers.

#### The Mariner's Library *(library)*

A quiet, well-stocked library south-west of the square, dedicated to maritime knowledge, charts, and sea lore. It is the closest thing Darmouth has to a scholarly institution.

- Seraphina Blackwood *(librarian)* — knowledgeable and always ready to assist researchers, with particular depth on southern sea routes.

### The Harbour

The southern end of the main avenue opens onto Darmouth's docks — a long stretch of timber piers and stone quays where the city's purpose is most visible. The harbour handles fishing boats, coastal traders, and larger merchant vessels, with the full social range of a working waterfront: skilled tradespeople, rough labourers, sailors in from long voyages, and people who ask very few questions about the ships they crew.

#### The Iron Anchor *(tavern — modest)*

A popular, bustling tavern on the harbour front, with low ceilings, a long bar, and a loyal regular crowd of sailors and dockworkers. One of the better places in the city to learn what ships are coming and going.

- Captain Barnaby "Old Salt" Hargrave *(proprietor)* — a retired sailor with a wealth of knowledge about the sea, the southern routes, and the workings of the [[Mawbreakers]].

#### The Golden Hook *(restaurant — aristocratic)*

A renowned seafood restaurant on the western edge of the harbour, markedly more elegant than its neighbours. Freshly caught fish and locally sourced delicacies, at prices aimed at the city's wealthier visitors and Imperial officers.

- Henri Leclair *(chef and owner)* — charismatic and innovative, with a reputation among the city's well-heeled circles for exceptional cooking.

#### The Wharf Rat *(tavern — poor)*

A seedy, dimly lit dive on the waterfront, frequented by the harder edges of harbour society. Few questions are asked here, and fewer are answered.

- "One-Eyed" Jack Flint *(owner)* — a rough and gruff man who turns a blind eye to dealings he finds profitable to ignore.

#### Azure Sail Shipwrights *(shipbuilding)*

A reputable shipbuilding company on the western waterfront, responsible for the construction and maintenance of many vessels working the southern sea routes. Their work is considered dependable.

- Morgan Redwood *(master shipwright)* — highly skilled and quietly proud of the craft.

#### Harbour Master's Office *(port administration)*

The administrative building overseeing arrivals and departures in Darmouth's docks. All ships register here; all departures require its clearance.

- Linus Clearwater *(harbour master)* — a meticulous administrator who keeps the port's operations running without incident.

## Landmarks

### Temple of the Seven Divines

The Temple of the Seven Divines stands on the hill east of the main avenue, its elegant spires the highest point in Darmouth and visible from the northern gate. It is the city's principal place of worship and its most architecturally distinguished building — a clear contrast to the practical timber construction that fills the grid below. The hill and its grounds sit entirely apart from the city's military geometry, and the temple draws citizens from every district for services, counsel, and the particular solace of a space not built for efficiency.

- Sister Arianna Brightstar *(high priestess)* — wise and compassionate, a counsellor to much of the city's population, and the keeper of the temple's quieter responsibilities.

### Darmouth Bastille

The Bastille sits on the western outskirts of the city, beyond the grid — a grim fortress of thick stone walls and constant patrols. It is where the Empire confines those who break its laws in this part of the south, and its location at the city's margin is deliberate: present enough to be felt, separated enough to keep its business from the streets. The sewer network runs beneath it, a detail that has not always appeared on the warden's maps.

- Warden Korban Slate *(warden)* — a stern and incorruptible man who maintains the Bastille's routines with relentless efficiency.

## Factions

### Green Gryphons

The [[Green Gryphons]] maintain a chapter hall in the Civic Quarter under Commander Alaric Brack. The Darmouth branch handles contracts across the city and the surrounding southern region, with a particular volume of work guarding the coastal road.

### Order of Ravens

The [[Order of Ravens]] has a presence in Darmouth, operating quietly rather than from any established nest. The city's rebel network made itself known to the party early in the campaign, and the Imperial crackdown since [[Skyreach|Skyreach's]] destruction has pushed it further underground.

## [[The Bloody Nails|Campaign: The Bloody Nails]]

#### [[Session 03 - The Streets of Darmouth]]

The party entered Darmouth with [[Finegolds|the Finegold caravan]], their status as escaped Imperial prisoners making every conversation at the gate a calculation. In the city they outfitted themselves — [[Neros]] commissioning a quarterstaff from the Ironsong sisters, the group purchasing a regional map from the Sundew General Store — and registered with the [[Green Gryphons]] for a contract to clear the goblin clan terrorising the coastal road. The city's mood was darker than it appeared: a figure in a red-lined black cloak moved through the Market Square crowds, and before the day's end the party watched [[Ella Sable]] chased down and arrested by city guards in a back alley. That night at the Seafarer's Rest, [[Gemma Finegold]] revealed she had known the party were escaped prisoners since they met on the road — and introduced them to [[Uric Graad]] of the [[Order of Ravens]], who explained what was really at stake.

#### [[Session 04 - To Free a Raven]]

The party broke [[Ella Sable]] out of the Darmouth Bastille. Entering through the sewers, they bluffed their way through the prison's guards with four of them in confiscated uniforms — [[Ryo]] wearing the red cloak of a Hand of the Empire — and retrieved Ella and the stolen [[Skyreach]] schematics before retreating through the maintenance tunnel with the alarm a breath behind them. [[Gemma Finegold]] and [[Uric Graad]] met them outside the walls with healing and a clear path out. The party made their final exit through the Temple of the Seven Divines, where [[Kai]] was drawn into a celestial vision — and where Sister Arianna Brightstar guided them through the temple's subterranean passage and out beyond the city perimeter.

## Trivia

- Darmouth's military grid was policy, not aesthetic: unlike the old Valtorran cities with their centuries of alleys and organic growth, the grid can be policed and held. The design prioritises control over character, and it shows.
- The entire city was built to serve a road that does not yet exist. Every quay, every guild hall, every colonial block was laid down on the assumption that [[Skyreach]] would complete the crossing of the [[Aeolian Chasm]]. Without it, the harbour is useful — but the grand vision has been indefinitely deferred.
