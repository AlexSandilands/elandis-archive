**Commander Varik Surne** was an Imperial officer who served as military governor of [[Point Blackrock]] during the [[Valtorran Empire]]'s occupation of the frontier settlement in 2507. Cold, calculating, and always mindful of the pressure bearing down from above, he administered the occupation from the commandeered half of the [[Point Blackrock#The Frosthearth|Frosthearth]] with the efficiency of a man who understood that failure would not be forgiven.

## Biography

Surne was the Empire's man in the north: not a frontline general but an administrator and enforcer, tasked with holding [[Point Blackrock]] stable while [[Dread General Gerard Blackmarsh]] drove the campaign deeper into enemy territory across the [[Frostway]]. He oversaw four military crossings of the ice, moving approximately ten thousand Imperial soldiers toward [[Val Noren]], coercing [[The Frostwardens]] into guiding them — knowing full well that several crews who crossed never returned.

He divided the Frosthearth with Warden-Master Harren Vol, and the arrangement suited him. Vol served as a pressure valve, absorbing the resentment of the remaining townsfolk; Surne could focus on logistics, intelligence, and watching for threats to the occupation from his side of the divided lodge.

## [[The Bloody Nails|Campaign: The Bloody Nails]]

#### [[Session 33 - A Camp Alight]]

When [[Vaeleran]] entered [[Point Blackrock]] invoking the identity of [[General Korvas]], Surne was the one who recognised the opportunity beneath the surface. He had Vaeleran escorted to the Frosthearth and received him with the careful warmth of a man managing a valuable asset — openly amused at Korvas's reported memory loss, and quick to note that [[Dread General Gerard Blackmarsh|General Blackmarsh]] would be very eager to see his old comrade once the campaign reached its conclusion. He offered a deal: wait one week for Imperial reinforcements to arrive, then cross the ice together for the final push on [[Val Noren]]. Vaeleran bluffed back, offering to lead a crossing immediately and accelerate the campaign. Surne was still weighing the proposal when the southern edge of his camp ignited.
