**Ryordan Silmara** is a full-blooded elven ranger and the last surviving heir of the [[Silmara Family]], the royal bloodline of the Sumaran elves and one of the oldest lines in [[Valtorra]]. The son of [[Ratha Silmara]] and [[Rhennaya Silmara]], he was surrendered as an infant into the care of a gnomish stranger by parents who knew what was coming, and woke decades later in an unnamed forest with only a signet ring and no memory of who he was — a mystery that has driven, haunted, and ultimately defined every step of his journey since. He goes by Ryo.

## Description

#### Appearance

Ryo has the silver hair and sharp features of his bloodline, and moves through the world with the deliberate quietness of someone long accustomed to being noticed for the wrong reasons. He dresses practically, favouring dark travelling clothes with no insignia or ornamentation save for a single worn signet ring — the seal of the Silmara line — that he wore without understanding for decades before learning what it meant. He habitually keeps his hood up in populated areas, his silver hair being the kind of detail that draws eyes and invites questions he would rather avoid. The Shield of Living Memory returns to his arm if discarded, and he carries [[Dread's Hunger]] with the wariness due a blade he does not trust and cannot quite bring himself to relinquish.

#### Personality

Ryo is contained in the way that people become contained when they have spent most of their life navigating danger alone. He is not unfriendly, but he keeps his own counsel and does not readily offer what he feels. He has a sharp eye for leverage — he negotiates, he watches, he waits — and when he decides to act he is precise and final about it. There is a current of something colder beneath the surface: a willingness to execute that has surfaced more than once when a situation demanded it. Against that, those who have spent time with him find a man of unusual steadiness, someone who keeps his word and does what needs to be done without needing recognition for it. He does not want the throne, and he means it; but the weight of a name he never chose seems to be settling onto his shoulders whether he wants it or not.

## Biography

#### Background

Ryo carries the blood of the [[Silmara Family]], the royal line whose history stretches back to the age of the [[Heartstone]] itself — that petrified remnant of a World Tree root now buried beneath the [[Royal Citadel of Sumara]], the pulse of elven magic in the Faewild city above. The name Ryordan is old in that lineage. The first to bear it was an elven soldier — remembered as Ryordan Silmara, First of his Name — who stood at a cosmic battle alongside vast celestial beings, sundered the root of the World Tree to save it from annihilation, and bound his bloodline to it in the act, tying every Silmara descendant to the World Tree's life and magic in perpetuity. That binding passes, in unbroken line, to Ryo.

His parents, [[Ratha Silmara]] and [[Rhennaya Silmara]], were the king and queen of the Sumaran elves in the years before the great elven exodus. When the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]] rose and the elves withdrew from [[Valtorra]], Rhennaya led her people into the Faewild — but not before she and Ratha surrendered their infant son to a gnomish man in darkness, pressing his name into the stranger's keeping: *"His name is Ryordan. You should probably just call him Ryo."* The shorter figure — his mother — patted the child's head, and walked away. Not long after, [[Dread General Gerard Blackmarsh]] killed them both.

Ryo woke, approximately eighty years later, in a forest with no memories and nothing to identify him but the signet ring on his hand. He did not know what the ring was. He did not know his name had any weight behind it. He built a life in the margins of an empire hostile to his kind, moving carefully, hiding his ears, learning to survive as the last full-blooded elf anyone had seen in a generation.

### [[The Bloody Nails|Campaign: The Bloody Nails]]

#### [[Session 01 - The Rusty Nail]]

Ryo was among the prisoners freed in the jailbreak that sparked the formation of the group, recovering his longbow from the confiscated gear and stepping immediately into the role he would hold across the entire campaign: the archer, the tactician, the one who watches before he acts.

#### [[Session 04 - To Free a Raven]]

In the sewers beneath [[Darmouth]], Ryo donned the red-lined cloak of a Hand of the Empire and carried the deception past the prison guards, providing the disguise under which the party freed members of the [[The Order of Ravens|Order of Ravens]].

#### [[Session 08 - The High Road]]

Meeting privately with [[Barak Stormrider]], Ryo revealed his true elven heritage for the first time — a confidence that marked the beginning of a longer pattern of selective trust. Barak noted he hadn't seen a native elf in nearly a century.

#### [[Session 15 - Operation Skyreach]]

Sharing his story before the assault on [[Skyreach]], Ryo identified his persecution plainly: a full-blooded elf, hunted by the Empire for his race alone. It was the first time he had said it in so many words to the party.

#### [[Session 16 - The Fall of Skyreach]]

[[Lirien]] identified the signet ring Ryo had worn his whole life as the seal of the [[Silmara Family]] — the ruling bloodline of the lost elven city of [[Ithilmara]]. Ryo received the information without knowing what to do with it.

#### [[Session 30 - The Darkwood Ambush]]

Interrogating the captured [[Captain Lucius Draegor]], Ryo learned the name of the man who had sold him to the Empire: [[Dhorik]], the dwarven blacksmith of [[Lighthaven]]. With the intelligence secured, he executed the Captain. When the group retreated into the woods afterward, his tendency to isolate under pressure surfaced, prompting a confrontation with [[Vaeleran]] about the costs of a lone-wolf approach to their shared work.

#### [[Session 44 - The Elven Archmage]]

The [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]]'s reaction to Ryo's ring forced a confrontation with [[Ayana Syndrosa]], who had previously wiped his memory out of distrust. Believing him to be a spy, she pinned him to a wall and teleported the rest of the party into the air above [[Val Noren]]. When Ryo convinced her of his honesty, she saved the others and led the group to [[Ithilmara]], where he placed the ring into an ancient dais and activated a dormant portal — confirming his descent from the elven royal line and stepping the party through into the Faewild.

#### [[Session 46 - The Shining City]]

At the Confluence of the Seven, [[Ayana Syndrosa]] confirmed what the ring had suggested: Ryo was the son of [[Ratha Silmara]] and [[Rhennaya Silmara]], sole surviving heir to the elven throne. Then, in the same breath, Ayana made a connection that stunned both young men — [[Eirieneth Silmara]], who had stayed behind in Valtorra when the elves withdrew, was Rhennaya's sister. [[Eryndor]] was Eirieneth's son. Ryo and Eryndor were first cousins, both carriers of the Silmara blood.

#### [[Session 47 - Confluence of the Seven]]

At the Locus Arcanum, Ryo silently showed [[Illythia Voss]] the signet ring. Her composure cracked: *"Your parents were Ratha and Rhennaya. When were you born? How do you exist?"* Ryo disclosed that he had woken eighty years ago in a forest with only the ring and no memories. Later, he stepped the cipher runes of the [[Vault of Memories]] — spelling BLOOD — and the door cracked open. Descending into the Heartstone, he saw the vision the Vault held for him: two hooded elves surrendering an infant to a frightened gnome, and then a figure in black armour driving a sword through both his parents at once. He passed portraits of his parents — Ratha standing beside Rhennaya, young and happy — for the first time in his life.

#### [[Session 48 - The Vault of Memories]]

Inside the Vault, the party navigated a series of memory-chambers and trials. Ryo crossed a blind bridge over a void guided entirely by Eryndor's voice, made a running leap across a ten-foot gap with barely enough clearance, and fought off pursuing stone defenders while others completed the passage. Both levers pressed, the bridge solidified.

#### [[Session 49 - The Silver Sage]]

[[Praetor'Varanous]] called Ryo back to the memory-dais one final time and showed him a vision he had never seen: his mother handing him as an infant to a gnomish man in the dark, speaking the name aloud — *"His name is Ryordan. You should probably just call him Ryo"* — and then walking away. Then visions of the first Ryordan Silmara, First of his Name, the elven soldier at the cosmic battle with the World Tree. Then the death of his parents, seen whole, on Blackmarsh's blade. Afterward, in the kitchens of the [[Royal Citadel of Sumara]] — an ancestral home he had never set foot in before — Ryo poured [[Eryndor]] his first drink from a cellar more than a century untouched, and asked, quietly, never to be called Ryordan again.

#### [[Session 50 - A New Morning in Sumara]]

Walking through the Heartstone Commons, the city of [[Sumara, The Shining City|Sumara]] noticed the Silmara heir in its streets: a grey-haired farmer named Gav received strange unbidden words — *"Ryordana has returned, the daughter of the Silmara"* — when he saw the group come down. In the Diminished Trove, Ryo took back [[Dread's Hunger]] from [[Illythia Voss]] rather than part with it, sliding it into the bag of holding without explanation. [[Warden Sylvaris]] confirmed that the [[Royal Citadel of Sumara]] was his by right, and warned him specifically to keep his hood up over his silver hair before crossing into the [[Vale of Eternal Night]].

## Trivia

- The name Ryordan carries weight stretching back to the cosmic age of the World Tree. The first Ryordan Silmara, First of his Name bound the Silmara bloodline to the sundered root that now lies as the [[Heartstone]] beneath Sumara — meaning every Ryo who has come since has been, in some sense, a custodian of that binding.
- [[Praetor'Varanous]] smelled the blood of Silmara before seeing Ryo and Eryndor, and was briefly taken aback that there were two of them.
- Ryo has never lived in [[Sumara, The Shining City|Sumara]]. The kitchen of the [[Royal Citadel of Sumara]] lit its own fireplace when he and Eryndor entered — old enchantments detecting guests in a home that had waited a century for its heir.
