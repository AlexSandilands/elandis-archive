## Summary

The last of the morning in [[Sumara, The Shining City|Sumara]] was spent saying goodbye. Over fae coffee in [[Illythia Voss|Illythia]]'s apartments, [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana]] confessed that she had tried to scry on [[Eryndor]]'s mother in the night and found only a wall where the woman should have been — and she asked whether the loss should be carried to the [[The Confluence of the Seven|Confluence]] at all, for [[Eirieneth Silmara|Eirieneth]] had given up her elven name for a quiet human life and might not thank them for dragging her back into it. *"I don't doubt,"* [[Eryndor]] answered, *"that she would choose this over a cell."* With his blessing the matter would be raised. Ayana herself was needed back in the waking world, where old [[King Marius Noren|Marius Noren]] would be wondering where she had gone, but she would linger a few days yet to see them return from the dark. Carried by griffins over the [[Heartstone]] and up the cliffs, the party climbed into the archives of the Circle of the Scroll, and there — with a little coaxing and a great deal of fortune — an old archivist drew back the curtain on the oldest history of all. He told them of [[Ryordan Silmara|Ryordan, first of his name]], the low-born elf who rose to command gods and armies, and who, to save all creation, took two swords to the [[World Tree]] itself and sundered it from the world. As the tree withdrew, the sky cracked and a piece of it fell upon the northern Vale, drowning it in endless night; and Ryordan, by some bargain never fully recorded, bound his bloodline to the tree forever. That is why the Silmara may command the World Gates. That is why a city of the wronged — [[Caer Nystral, The Dawnless City]] — grew in the crater where the sky had fallen.

In the Ironsward they found their cuffs waiting with the promised favour. [[Skyguard Caeryn]] — the same who had clapped them in irons the day they arrived — owed [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana]] a debt, and grudgingly handed over five sets of [[Zephyr Cuffs]] on a promise of their swift return. He spoke, too, of the lost patrol: how sixty years past [[Kaelen Vayne|Commander Vayne]]'s son and his company had clashed with the Hunt at the forest's edge, how the dead were many on both sides, and how no body of [[Asha Vayne|Asha]]'s was ever found. Then came the climb to the high peaks and the leap into open air. The cuffs caught them and bore them north faster than any of them had ever moved, the [[Shimmering Peaks]] vanishing behind them the instant they crossed the [[Liar's River]] — until a speck on the horizon swelled into a Roc the size of a house, hunting them down the sky. They landed hard and ran. An arrow took the great bird's eye; roots dragged it screaming from the air; [[Dondar]]'s magic shrank it to a trembling mouse; and when at last it shook free and stooped on them again, a ball of fire bloomed across its face and broke its nerve. It fled into the distance with one eye glaring back, and the party stumbled at last to the wall of eternal night.

They did not cross it blindly. [[Kai]] sent his little bat through the veil and watched through its eyes as a different world opened on the far side — a beautiful, cold darkness of silver needle-trees and glowing mushrooms, a river of pure black water, and far off across the plain a great beacon: a black moon hung in perpetual eclipse, ringed in white fire, with a single luminous beam rising from the earth to meet it. No watcher on the other side could see back through the shroud, the bat found, which meant their battle with the Roc had gone unseen. With that comfort the party raised a sheltering dome at the very threshold and settled to rest, the warmth of the Shining City at their backs and that last white light burning in the dark ahead. They would not steal into the Vale like thieves, they decided, but walk in openly — envoys of a kind, carrying a secret worth more than any of them dared yet spend. *"We either do this with balls or with blood,"* [[Berberis]] said. Beyond the wall, the beacon waited.

## Details

### Farewells in the Locus Arcanum

* The party gathered with [[Ayana Syndrosa]] and [[Illythia Voss]] at the [[Locus Arcanum]] for a final morning before leaving [[Sumara, The Shining City|Sumara]].
* [[Kai]] and the party tried "fae [[joffee]]" — the Feywild's version of joffee, which (unlike the Material Realm's) is caffeinated and genuinely perks the drinker up.
* **Character Moment:** Asked her age, [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana]] revealed she is **700 years old** despite appearing to be only in her late forties; her silver hair is natural, not from age.
* **Major Plot Beat — the failed scrying:** [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana]] disclosed she had tried to scry on [[Eirieneth Silmara|Eirieneth]] the previous night and could not see her at all, despite knowing her well.
* Ayana asked whether [[Eryndor]] wanted the matter known beyond the group, warning that [[Eirieneth Silmara|Eirieneth]] had chosen to leave elven society and might be forced back into it if freed and her line made known.
* [[Eryndor]] judged she would choose freedom over a cell and that they could not attempt the rescue without more resources; he gave his blessing for [[Illythia Voss|Illythia]] to raise it at the next [[The Confluence of the Seven|Confluence]].
* [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana]] said she must soon return to the Material Realm, as [[King Marius Noren|Marius]] would be looking for her, but agreed to wait a few days to see them safely back from the Vale.
* They discussed using the Citadel's World Gate to send Ayana home on their return, and to visit [[Praetor'Varanous|Praetor]] again at the same time — [[Kai]] passed on that he missed her.
* [[Kai]] asked whether the Veil over the Vale could ever be lifted; [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana]] said it was beyond her, dating to the time of [[Ryo]]'s ancestor and namesake.
* The party resolved to research at the Circle of the Scroll before risking the Vault of Memories again.
* **Character Moment:** [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana]] mentioned that [[Ryo]]'s father had been a high-ranking member of the [[Circle of the Blade]] before giving up the post to marry into the Silmara family.
* **Character Moment:** [[Kai]] urged [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana]] and [[Illythia Voss|Illythia]] to marry, offering a god's blessing; Ayana laughed it off — after centuries together, marriage was *"the least on our minds."*
* On parting, Ayana recalled an old, ill-advised bargain [[Kai]] had once struck with a top-hatted fey called [[Grinn, The Pale|Grinn]] in the [[Briarshade]], calling it *"very, very foolish."*
* [[Illythia Voss|Illythia]] arranged griffins to ferry the party to the base of the Mnemorium Canton.

### The Circle of the Scroll

* Griffin-riders of the mage guard flew the party past the [[Royal Citadel of Sumara|Royal Citadel]] and [[Heartstone]] to a waterfall cliff, where vast magical stone elevators slide endlessly up and down the rock face.
* A hunched old faun librarian, **Silvana**, led them to the public archives; at the desk sat an elderly elven archivist, **Aaroun**.
* **Aaroun** was reluctant — knowledge of [[Caer Nystral, The Dawnless City]] and the Vale is closely guarded and rarely sought.
* [[Ryo]] and [[Eryndor]] talked their way in: claiming [[Kaelen Vayne|Lord Kaelen]]'s sanction (a deception) and arguing that as outsiders they were *"comparatively expendable"* and nothing would trace back to him.
* [[Kai]] cast Enhance Ability for Intelligence on the group and the archivist; the search roll was exceptional, unlocking nearly everything the archives held.
* **Major Lore — the Sundering:** They learned of [[Ryordan Silmara|Ryordan, first of his name]], a low-born elf and adventurer who rose during a cosmic catastrophe to command the aid of gods and celestials and lead armies across the realms.
* The [[World Tree]] once physically extended into every realm, its roots threading the cosmos itself; it was under attack and being corrupted.
* To save all of creation, [[Ryordan Silmara|Ryordan]] swelled to titanic size, wielded two swords, and **sundered the World Tree** so it would no longer be physically present in the realms; the tree then withdrew from reality.
* **Major Lore — the fall of the sky:** As the tree retracted, reality shattered above the [[Shimmering Peaks]] (where the root's tip is still buried) and a piece of the sky fell upon the northern Vale — then home only to a minor fey court — gouging a vast crater and wreathing it in a dome of eternal night.
* **Major Reveal — the binding of the Silmara:** As part of the Sundering, [[Ryordan Silmara|Ryordan]] irrevocably bound his bloodline to the World Tree — the reason the Silmara can control the World Gates and have reigned as royalty for millennia.
* [[Caer Nystral, The Dawnless City|Caer Nystral]], was built by the wronged denizens of the Vale in the crater where the sky had fallen, a deliberate mirror to the Shining City.
* **Major Lore — the city map (on an exceptional roll):** A near-perfectly circular city ringed by a wall of black steel, stone, and vines, laid out in concentric tiers:
  * **The Barrow-Ring** — outside the wall; mounds, webs, and barrows where "the old ones" dwell — hags and deep things.
  * **The Lower Warren** — just within the wall; home to lesser beings: redcaps, quicklings, boggets, spriggans.
  * **The Muster Yards** — the martial court, where the Hunt resides: armories, barracks, stables, kennels.
  * **The Court Tiers** — rising rings of buildings for the lesser archfae and nobles.
  * **The Veiled Citadel** — black obsidian walls soaring above the city's centre.
* The later sketch also showed the **Antler Gates** (the main entrance), smaller side gates, and the **Midnight Procession**, a great avenue rising to the upper courts.
* **Major Lore — soulstones:** No record named the "Soulstone Anchor," but the term *soulstone* appeared — some archfae are too powerful to be slain and will rematerialize stronger on another plane unless killed near a bound or hated item; a soulstone traps such a being's soul while its body is destroyed.
* The text implied the Soulstone Anchor [[Praetor'Varanous|Praetor]] seeks is likely one such stone, and a powerful one.
* **Aaroun** refused to let them copy the restricted map; [[Kai]] memorised it instead.

### The Ironsward — Zephyr Cuffs and the Lost Patrol

* In a specialised side-armory on the second tier of the Ironsward, the party found **[[Skyguard Caeryn]]** waiting and impatient — the same skyguard who had cuffed and jailed them on their arrival in [[Sumara, The Shining City|Sumara]].
* Owing [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana]] a favour, [[Skyguard Caeryn|Caeryn]] lent them five sets of [[Zephyr Cuffs]] on the promise of their return within a couple of days.
* **Lore — the Lost Patrol:** [[Skyguard Caeryn|Caeryn]] recounted that patrols toward the Vale were called off some sixty years ago after [[Kaelen Vayne]]'s son was lost.
* A patrol strayed too near the forest's edge and clashed with a Hunt patrol; the dead were heavy on both sides, and the sigils on a fallen centaur suggested an important Hunt figure had died there too.
* Many expected bodies — including [[Asha Vayne|Asha]]'s — were never recovered; rather than risk further escalation, [[Kaelen Vayne|Kaelen]] pulled the patrols back to the city and has blamed himself ever since.
* [[Skyguard Caeryn|Caeryn]] described the Hunt: mostly four-legged — centaurs, wargs, displacer beasts — fast, ground-covering, and heard before they are seen, by horn and howl.
* **The Cuffs explained:** charged with a measure of magic (one second-level slot), each set grants a single glide that lasts until the wearer touches ground, resetting after a full day's rest; the glide ends if the wearer is knocked out, restrained, or brought to a standstill mid-air — there is no safety net.

### The Glide and the Roc

* After the break, the party left without meeting the Council again, and chose not to approach [[Kaelen Vayne|Kaelen]] — [[Warden Sylvaris|Sylvaris]] and [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana]] had both advised against telling him their purpose.
* They followed the river north from the Ironsward to a high mountain tarn, crossing to a vantage where the world opened up: the foothill plains, the circular [[Liar's River]], and far off the shimmering black wall of the [[Vale of Eternal Night|Vale]].
* [[Berberis]] read the weather with druidcraft — gentle winds, no storm — and the party attuned to the cuffs inside a Tiny Hut.
* By rough reckoning (about five miles of glide per thousand feet of height) they judged they could reach most of the way to the Vale's edge before landing.
* **Character Moment:** [[Berberis]] leapt first — climbing a boulder, flashing the party a rude two-handed farewell, and falling backward off the edge; the others followed.
* The cuffs hauled their arms wide and bore them north at breakneck speed; the [[Shimmering Peaks]] warped away into the far distance the instant they passed over the [[Liar's River]].
* **A speck on the horizon** grew into a **Roc** — a bird larger than a house, with a wingspan of dozens of metres — gaining on them from the east.
* They angled down and the cuffs flared to land them gently; the Roc stooped after them, talons spread, and the party broke into a run for the Vale's edge.
* [[Berberis]] tried to bind the Roc to the earth with roots; the first attempt failed by a hair.
* **Standout Moment:** [[Ryo]] put an arrow clean through one of the Roc's eyes, half-blinding it; [[Kai]] threw up a fog cloud for cover.
* In the running fight that followed: [[Berberis]] dragged the Roc from the sky with roots (Earthbind) and enchanted [[Ryo]]'s bow; [[Kai]] took his Starry Archer form.
* [[Ryo]] dropped a swathe of grasping overgrowth to slow the grounded bird to a crawl.
* **Major Beat — the mouse:** [[Dondar]]'s polymorph transformed the Roc into a tiny mouse, lost amid the vines.
* [[Kai]] sent his bat familiar to carry the mouse high and drop it; the bat hauled it ~150 feet up before the mouse bit the little creature to death, and the mouse fell — the impact's damage carrying over as it reverted to the Roc on landing.
* Battered, the Roc took wing again and stooped a final time; on [[Berberis]]'s shout that *"animals don't like fire,"* [[Dondar]] caught it full in the face with a ball of flame.
* **Standout Moment:** Between its pierced eye, the indignity of the mouse, and the fire (which this beast feared doubly), the Roc lost its nerve and **fled**, glaring back with its one good eye as [[Ryo]] put two more arrows into the same socket.
* The party reached the wall of eternal night, no longer pursued.

### The Threshold of the Vale

* [[Kai]] listened at the ground for the tremor of the Hunt and heard nothing.
* **Major Reveal — beyond the veil:** [[Kai]] sent his bat through the black shroud and saw the far side through its eyes — a perpetual twilight of dark, star-strewn sky, silver needle-trees, ancient moss, glowing mushrooms, and a river of pure black water; *"a beautiful darkness,"* unlike the menace of the [[Briarshade]].
* **The beacon:** Miles away across the plain hung a **great black moon in perpetual eclipse, ringed in white fire**, with a single luminous silver beam rising from the ground to meet it — an unmistakable landmark in the dark.
* Crucially, the bat found that the shroud cannot be seen *through* from the inside — meaning no watcher in the Vale had witnessed the fight with the Roc.
* **Character Moment:** [[Ryo]] swapped his Circle of the Weave robe for plain clothes and [[Kai]] stowed his bright robe, the party agreeing to hide their colours and the Silmara connection.
* The party debated their approach and chose to enter **openly, as envoys / a neutral third party**, rather than sneak in — reasoning that stealth would only mark them as untrustworthy to the Hunt.
* They acknowledged the leverage and the danger of travelling with a Silmara — two, even — and what it might cost to reveal it.
* [[Kai]] raised an eight-hour sheltering dome at the very edge of the veil, blended into the mountainside, as a fallback.
* **Cliffhanger:** The party settled to take a long rest at the threshold — the warmth of the Shining City behind them and the white beacon-moon burning in the eternal night ahead — resolving to walk into the [[Vale of Eternal Night|Vale]] come morning. *"We either do this with balls or with blood."*
