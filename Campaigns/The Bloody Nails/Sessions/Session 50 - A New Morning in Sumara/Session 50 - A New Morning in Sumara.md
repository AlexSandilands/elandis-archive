## Summary

Morning broke warmer than usual over [[Sumara, The Shining City|Sumara]], and the [[Royal Citadel of Sumara]] woke with it. [[Kai]] clanked through royal cupboards to find fae eggs with purple whites and green yolks, and built a passable breakfast over them while [[Eryndor]] pelted his back with ball bearings from the corner he'd slept in. [[Dondar]] woke from a nightmare with a shout the others heard down the hall. When they crossed the Bridge of Recollection and stepped into the Heartstone Commons, the city noticed them — eyes followed, gossip moved with them, and a graying old farmer named Gav whispered that strange words had landed in his mind when he saw the group come down: *"Ryordana has returned, the daughter of the Silmara."* [[Berberis]] tapped the side of his nose and said nothing. Gav's whole face changed.

The errands took most of the morning. At the Ironweave Blacksmith, Ferethis pressed [[Dondar]]'s new fae-metal headband onto his brow — a quiet thing that fit perfectly over the [[Rhun'zar]] — and handed over a second item wrapped in leather and a small silver pin. Across the city in the Etherean Canton's Diminished Trove, the ancient Arcanist Levvel threaded purple light around the [[Emberblade]] and went still. *"It's one of the more powerful items I think I've ever seen."* She told [[Eryndor]] to set down his component pouch and channel his magic through the sword instead, and when he did it was like a key turning in a lock — a vast ocean of power beneath the blade, the steel pulsing with a faint glow, and the unmistakable sense that the sword could yet grow. The party also showed her [[Dread's Hunger]]. She read it only briefly before flinching away — through the blade she had seen [[Ratha Silmara|Ratha]] and [[Rhennaya Silmara|Rhennaya]] killed, and offered to take it from them; [[Ryo]], after a beat, slid it back into the bag of holding. [[Dondar]] left with a Wand of the War Mage; [[Kai]] left with a needle bound for a calligrapher at the Pale Brush.

Down in the Greensward, deep enough that the air went warm and humid, [[Warden Sylvaris]] looked once at [[Ryo]] and said, *"Cut directly from the root, you are."* [[Berberis]] gave her a feather token — a piece of the Material Plane — and they walked it out into the centre of the bioluminescent fields and planted it; in moments a sixty-foot oak stood there, healthy, and the Warden hugged its trunk. Over its roots she told them the long story: a war called the Long Hunt, an ancestor of [[Ryo]]'s named [[Ellesandra Silmara]] who walked alone into the [[Vale of Eternal Night|Vale]] and returned carrying the head of the Hunt's leader, and the archfae who rose to rule the Vale soon after — [[Mirileth|the Star-Veiled Queen]] — whose tenuous peace had held for two thousand years. The city they were bound for was [[Caer Nystral]], the Dawnless City; the son of [[Kaelen Vayne]] had vanished on its borders sixty years ago, and Sylvaris doubted the queen's style would have let him simply die. The party left a sending stone in her hands for the Council and climbed back up to the [[Locus Arcanum]], where they found [[Ayana Syndrosa]] off-duty for the first time in forty years, lounging in a dressing gown beside [[Illythia Voss]]. She agreed to find them Zephyr Cuffs for the descent. Before they left, [[Berberis]] floated a different idea entirely — not to slip into the Vale, but to walk in as a Silmara embassy. *"That,"* he said, *"solves a lot of problems."*

## Details

### A Warmer Morning

* The party woke in the [[Royal Citadel of Sumara]] to a noticeably brighter, warmer light spilling over the Shimmering Peaks — unusual for the perpetual Faewild twilight.
* [[Kai]] went to the royal kitchens and found pantries stocked with bread, oats, fresh fruit, and eggs whose whites were purple and yolks were green.
* [[Eryndor]], having slept off the wine in the corner, threw a handful of ball bearings at [[Kai]]'s back, then asked for breakfast.
* [[Dondar]] woke screaming from a nightmare — a new occurrence, only recent.
* [[Berberis]] sent his owl ahead to investigate the shouting, then followed it to the kitchen.
* **Character Moment:** [[Kai]] cooked fae-egg French toast for the whole party while [[Ryo]] eventually drifted in from his hangover.
* [[Kai]] experimented with using Prestidigitation to make ordinary water taste like the royal wine

### Down into the Commons

* The party descended from the Citadel across the Bridge of Recollection — a long span over the gulf that surrounds the [[Heartstone]] itself, the sundered root of the [[World Tree]] on which the Citadel sits.
* People in the Heartstone Commons stopped to stare; word of the lights in the Citadel the night before had already begun to spread.
* **Vendor encounter — Gav:** A grey-haired farmer selling purple carrots whispered to [[Berberis]] that strange words had landed in his mind on seeing them — *"Ryordana has returned, the daughter of the Silmara."*
* [[Berberis]] confirmed nothing aloud but tapped the side of his nose; Gav's hope was visible.
* Gav described the people's view of [[Kaelen Vayne]]: a pillar after the loss of the Silmaras, who had held the city together through the worst of the skirmishes with the Hunt.
* **Lore — The Hunt:** Gav named the Hunt as the visible face of the Unseelie Court of the [[Vale of Eternal Night]] — elves, centaurs, redcaps, wargs.
* **Overheard:** [[Kai]] caught two passersby — a short girl and a pixie against a wall — gossiping about lights in the Citadel windows the night before. *"Surely it wouldn't be Vayne moving in, would it?"*
* At the Moonglass Atelier, [[Kai]] bought a simple yellow Luminary-style robe; [[Ryo]] picked up a blue cloak in the colours of the Circle of the Weave.

### The Ironweave

* Ferethis handed [[Dondar]] his commissioned fae-metal headband — leather-bound, fitted over the Rhun'zar, sitting directly above his arcane focus. He felt a quiet interaction between the headband and the arcane the moment he put it on.
* **Cost:** 250 gold for the headband.
* Ferethis brought out the second commission as well — a small leather parcel secured with a silver pin. [[Dondar]] received it without opening it. **Cost:** 100 gold.
* [[Kai]] collected his simple deer half-mask. **Cost:** 50 gold.

### The Diminished Trove

* In the Etherean Canton, the party visited the Diminished Trove, run by the elderly Arcanist Levvel, pristine in blue robes.
* The shop's stock was slim — most powerful items had gone with refugees through the Emerald Ceremony or been claimed by the city's defenders.
* **Available stock (rolled):** two potions of fire breath, one potion of heroism, one potion of gaseous form, a medallion of thoughts, an eye-emblazoned magical shield granting advantage on initiative and Perception, a pair of sending stones, and a Wand of the War Mage (+1).
* **Major Reveal — the [[Emberblade]] awakens:** [[Eryndor]] asked her to identify the sword. She wove a ritual of purple thread around it, then stopped and stared. *"It's one of the more powerful items I think I've ever seen."*
* She told [[Eryndor]] to put aside his component pouch and channel a spell through the [[Emberblade]] itself. He cast illusory script; the blade pulsed and glowed with arcane light.
* **Cite:** *"There is a vast potential to the sword that you're only just starting to scratch the surface of. If you continue to use it and channel great magic through it, it may grow with you."*
* **Reward Obtained:** [[Dondar]] purchased the Wand of the War Mage. **Cost:** 2500 gold (requires attunement).
* **Major Plot Beat — [[Dread's Hunger]] identified:** [[Berberis]] presented the cursed sword for a brief, hesitant identify.
* Arcanist Levvel gasped during the reading — through the blade she had seen [[Ratha Silmara|Ratha]] and [[Rhennaya Silmara|Rhennaya]] killed. *"That blade has a long, long history. A lot of evil and darkness."*
* She offered to take the sword and either cleanse or destroy it. [[Berberis]]' passive insight read her as genuinely shaken and trustworthy; [[Ryo]], however, returned it to the bag of holding rather than part with it.
* **Lore — The Hunt's fighting style:** Levvel described the Hunt's patrols along the boundaries — Fae Knights, centaurs, dire wargs, displacer beasts — and warned they are excellent trackers who cannot easily be outrun or thrown off scent.
* **Reward Obtained:** [[Ryo]] purchased three healing potions, [[Kai]] one, and others rounded out the stock. **Cost:** 50 gold each.
* **Reward Obtained:** [[Kai]] purchased an Illuminator's tattoo needle and made plans to take it up the hill to the Pale Brush, a calligrapher said to inscribe spell tattoos. **Cost:** 250 gold.

### The Greensward

* The party descended through the tiers of the city — the architecture growing increasingly interwoven with the living trees — and reached the Sanctum Canoporium at the lowest point of the Greensward Canton.
* They were granted immediate audience with [[Warden Sylvaris]], who was found in a nursery tending potted saplings under a balcony.
* On seeing [[Ryo]], Sylvaris crossed the room, looked him directly in the face, and said: *"Cut directly from the root, you are. There's definitely no doubt about it. That old fool Kaelen, I don't know how he could possibly have seen otherwise."*
* **Character Moment — the Oak:** [[Berberis]] produced a feather token enchanted with a Material Plane oak. Sylvaris took it reverently — *"I can sense something inside there, something yearning to grow"* — and led the party out into the bioluminescent fields.
* She chose a central crossroads between four fields as the place; [[Berberis]] drove the feather into the soil and a sixty-foot Material-Plane oak sprang into being.
* Sylvaris ran to its base, pressed her ear against the trunk, hummed to it, and pronounced it healthy. *"I will do everything I can to keep this one alive."*
* They sat in the roots and spoke openly.
* **Major Lore — The Long Hunt:** Some factions of the Hunt believe the Silmaras caused the Sundering and the darkness that followed; some believe the Vale folk were the rightful inhabitants of the Shimmering Peaks.
* About two thousand years ago a faction of the Hunt launched a multi-year campaign — the Long Hunt — raiding the villages around the base of the peaks at devastating cost.
* **Major Reveal — [[Ellesandra Silmara]]:** An ancestor of [[Ryo]]'s, Ellesandra went alone into the [[Vale of Eternal Night]] and returned carrying the head of the leader of the Hunt. Nothing was ever spoken of how or why.
* **Major Reveal — [[Mirileth]], the Star-Veiled Queen:** Soon after Ellesandra's return, the current archfae rose to rule the Vale. Her name is Mirileth. She has held a tenuous peace for the two thousand years since.
* **Asha Vayne thread:** Sixty years ago, in a border skirmish, [[Kaelen Vayne]]'s son [[Asha Vayne|Asha]] was killed — or assumed killed; his body and party were never recovered. An important member of the Hunt fell in the same skirmish. Sylvaris doubts [[Mirileth]]'s style would have permitted a simple disappearance: *"I think he has at this point given up hope. But it's not her style, I don't think. Wouldn't be surprised if there was more to the story."*
* **Major Lore — [[Caer Nystral]], the Dawnless City:** The mirror of [[Sumara, The Shining City|Sumara]]. A few thousand permanent residents; up to twenty thousand at court. Centaurs, Fae Knights, some elves, redcaps, boggarts, blights, nighthag covens, minor archfae — held together by the Queen. The artifact [[Praetor'Varanous|Praetor]] sent them to retrieve almost certainly lies there.
* Sylvaris admitted that as a leader she should advise [[Ryo]] not to go — his return alone is *"extremely valuable"* — but she would not stop him. She warned [[Ryo]] specifically to keep his hood up over his silver hair when he crosses into the Vale.
* **Recommendation — Zephyr Cuffs:** Sylvaris suggested the party seek out a set of *Zephyr Cuffs*, enchanted bracers used by a local sport that allow a wearer to leap from the peaks and glide; she pointed them to [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana]].
* **Sending Stones — strategic placement:** The party decided to leave a sending stone with Sylvaris for the Council and to reserve stones for distribution to allies on the Material Plane.
* They briefly checked stones already in their possession.
* **Character Moment — the Royal Citadel:** Sylvaris confirmed the [[Royal Citadel of Sumara]] is [[Ryo]]'s by right and offered to help him reshape it however he wished. The kitchen's food had appeared through old magical enchantments that detected guests.
* **Character Moment — flowers:** As they took their leave, [[Berberis]] grew Material Plane wildflowers around the new oak's base. Sylvaris knelt, smelled them, and grew fae flowers among them — a bed of mixed worlds at the foot of the tree.

### The Locus Arcanum

* The party climbed back through the city to the [[Locus Arcanum]] in the Etherean Canton and were shown straight through to [[Illythia Voss|Illythia]]'s apartments.
* **Character Moment:** They found [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana]] reclined in a chair in a dressing gown with a glass of wine, hair brushed loose — the look of a woman who had been on edge for forty years and had finally taken the load off. [[Illythia Voss]] was nearby in casual clothes, making breakfast.
* [[Berberis]] cut straight to it: they needed to get to the [[Vale of Eternal Night|Vale]].
* [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana]] joked about helping them raid the Ironsward for griffins to spite [[Kaelen Vayne]] — then declined, mock-deferring to [[Illythia Voss|Illythia]], who heard her from the kitchen and called out *"yep, I heard that."*
* **Reward Arranged — Zephyr Cuffs:** [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana]] sent a Sending and reported back: a contact has five sets available, either to rent or to buy.
* **Mechanic — the Cuffs:** Worn at wrists and ankles; charged with a second-level spell slot per night; allow gliding from a height. Knockdown, restraint, or zero movement during a glide ends the effect — and the wearer falls.
* [[Kai]] served prestidigitation-flavoured water to [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana]] as a "wine that won't get you drunk." She approved.
* **Cliffhanger / Strategic Question:** The party debated whether to enter the Vale stealthily or to walk in as a formal Silmara embassy — [[Berberis]] argued an embassy *"solves a lot of problems"* and could open the door to far more than a stolen soulstone.
* [[Eryndor]] floated the further suggestion of declaring [[Ryo]] regent before they left, to give the embassy weight.
* The session paused before the descent, with the cuffs to be collected and the choice still open.
