## Campaign Introduction

Welcome to [[Valtorra]], one of the four great pillars of the world of [[Elandis]]. For centuries, the ancient cities of this continent thrived in a fragile but enduring harmony, their stories woven together by trade and tradition. That peace shattered a century ago when a lone woman arrived on the eastern shores, bringing with her a tide of blood and iron that would rewrite history.

The human cities fell first, their defences crumbled by an unstoppable force. The dwarven strongholds of the mountains followed, their ancient forges quenched. Yet, when the conqueror reached the elven territories, she found only echoes - the [[The Valtorran Elves|Elves]] had vanished entirely, leaving their ancestral homes silent and abandoned. From these ruins, The [[The Valtorran Empire|Crimson Empire]] was born, ruled by the iron will of [[Empress Morganna Eventide|Empress Morganna]].

For a hundred years, the Empire has tightened its grip. In the last decade, the air itself has grown heavy with fear; the laws are harsher, the taxes steeper, and citizens vanish in the dead of night without explanation. It was under this shadow that a prison ship set sail from the northern island city of [[Val Noren]], heading south toward a labour camp. Within its damp, iron-barred cells, stripped of their belongings and clad in common peasant green, five strangers await a grim fate.

## Summary

The campaign began in the damp, oppressive hold of a prison ship sailing from [[Val Noren]] toward the port city of [[Darmouth]]. The player characters, stripped of their belongings and clad in common peasant garb, found themselves incarcerated by The [[The Valtorran Empire|Crimson Empire]]. However, fate intervened in the form of a violent storm. A bolt of lightning struck the ship, killing the high-ranking imperial officer [[Draedan Coldsteel]] instantly. As the vessel began to take on water, [[Eryndor]] managed to procure a rusty nail, using it to skilfully pick the lock of their cell. Amidst the rising chaos, [[Neros]] showed a moment of compassion by befriending the ship’s dog, ensuring the animal wasn't left to drown as the party scavenged the storage hold for basic armaments.

The escape to the surface was a desperate scramble. Upon reaching the top deck, the party encountered frantic imperial sailors attempting to launch the lifeboats. [[Kai]] utilized the *Command* spell to force the sailors to yield, allowing the group to secure a boat and cast off just as the prison ship was swallowed by the churning waves. The group spent a harrowing night tossed by the sea before washing ashore on a jagged coastline. They found refuge in a nearby sea cave, listening through the night to the distant, violent groans of the shipwreck being pulverized against the rocks by the tide.

In the morning, the party returned to the wreckage to search for their confiscated gear. They discovered a group of Goblins picking through the debris and swiftly eliminated them to reclaim their equipment. While looting the Captain’s Quarters, they found a significant cache of gold and a mysterious letter addressed to "Captain Armin" from an individual identified only as "V." The letter spoke of a "forward payment" to increase prisoner capacity and emphasized that the work at [[Skyreach]] must be completed before winter.

Leaving the shore behind, the party trekked through the dense coastal woods until they reached the main east-west trade road. There, they encountered a merchant convoy belonging to [[The Finegolds]]. The caravan had recently been harried by goblin raiders, leaving their healer, [[Maeve Greycloud|Maeve]], injured. Seeing a mutual benefit, the party struck a deal with the caravan leader, [[Gemma Finegold|Gemma]]: the group would provide protection for the remainder of the journey in exchange for safe passage and transport to the nearest city, beginning their journey toward rebellion.

## Details

### The Great Escape

* **The Incident:** [[Draedan Coldsteel]], a "[[Hand of the Empire]]," was killed by a lightning strike while heading to the top deck. His body fell just out of reach of the cells.
* **Breaking Out:** [[Eryndor]] found a rusty nail and successfully picked the cell lock (DC 10).
* **The Ship's Dog:** [[Neros]] used Animal Handling to befriend the ship's dog, who followed the party throughout the escape.
* **Scavenging:** The party successfully searched the storage room before the water rose too high, recovering leather armour, shortswords, and a locked chest containing 76 gold pieces.
* **The Lifeboat:** On the top deck, [[Kai]] cast *Command* on the remaining sailors, successfully securing a lifeboat for the party.

### Survival on the Beach

* The party washed ashore during the peak of the storm. Everyone managed to stabilize and find a shallow cave for shelter.
* During the night watch, the party heard the ship finally break apart on the reefs and heard the distant chattering of goblin scavengers.
* **Morning Skirmish:** The group ambushed the Goblins searching crates on the sand, clearing the area to safely loot the wreck.

### Loot and Revelations

* **Gear Recovery:** The party recovered their primary equipment, including [[Dondar]]'s spellbook, [[Vaeleran]]'s longsword and chainmail, and [[Ryo]]'s longbow.
* **The "V" Letter:** Recovered a letter from the Captain's Quarters mentioning a secret project at [[Skyreach]].
* **Valuables:** Found 125 gold pieces and a large diamond worth 300g hidden in the Captain's desk.

### The Finegold Encounter

* After traveling a few hours from the coast, the party reached the road and flagged down [[The Finegolds]] caravan.
* The caravan was traveling from [[Camaar]] to [[Darmouth]].
* The party met [[Gemma Finegold]] and [[Maeve Greycloud]], noting the merchants were poorly defended against the local goblin threat.
* **The Deal:** The party joined the caravan as hired guards, providing them a perfect cover to enter the next city without drawing imperial suspicion.
