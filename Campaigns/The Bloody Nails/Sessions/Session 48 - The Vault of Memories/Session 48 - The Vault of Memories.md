## Summary

Resuming from the session 47 cliffhanger, the party found the [[Vault of Memories]]' stone guardian utterly immune — [[Dondar]]'s opening fireball engulfed it entirely without leaving a mark. The mechanic emerged through trial and error: the chamber's rotating lightning beams could stun the construct if it walked through them, opening a brief damage window. The party learned to read the rotation and bait the golem into the arc, with [[Kai]]'s Lunar Spear and [[Berberis]]' Shillelagh grinding it down. When [[Eryndor]] drove the [[Emberblade]] into a weakened joint and channelled Booming Blade for the first time, something stirred inside the sword — a silent, wordless acknowledgement, the first hint of something deeper within the blade. The floor then gave way, dropping the party into a lower chamber swarming with stone defenders. [[Berberis]] delivered the killing blow — a full-weight strike to the head — just as a lightning charge built to full discharge between the pylons, crackled, and died harmlessly as [[Dondar]]'s beard went briefly, quietly static.

The vault door opened onto a hexagonal antechamber with two cipher-locked passages: *FALSE MEMORIES* and *HAVE FAITH*. The party decoded the new runes and entered the latter — a vast chamber dropping away into a purple void, with an invisible bridge only the onlookers could see while the person crossing was completely blind. [[Kai]] went first and fell. [[Ryo]] followed, guided step by step by [[Eryndor]], and made the crossing with a running leap across a ten-foot gap, barely catching the far edge. [[Dondar]] polymorph'd into a giant badger when his spell slots ran dry, fought off one grappling stone defender on the bridge, and was then hauled over the edge by a second in a final act of golem spite. [[Berberis]] went last, navigating a winding elevated path with [[Eryndor]] calling every turn and [[Ryo]] cutting down a pursuing golem at the last second. Both buttons pressed simultaneously, the bridge formed solid — and [[Kai]] and [[Dondar]] fell from the ceiling, having been suspended in the void for the better part of half an hour, landing on the completed bridge.

The passage beyond opened into a chamber so vast the far walls were lost to darkness: enormous columns, worn statues of elven kings and queens, a carved [[World Tree]], a colossal female figure with elven ears. As the party moved deeper, something drew attention to a massive beast-like shape half-hidden among the statues — too large to be sculpture, too still to be alive. Then an eyelid shifted. An ancient, cat-slit eye opened and fixed on them. Wings — longer than should have been possible — unfurled. Muscles that had not moved in centuries finally stirred, and a dragon rose to its full height above the party: towering, serpentine, staring down at five exhausted adventurers at the end of one of the longest days of their lives.

## Details

### The Stone Guardian

* The party entered the chamber immediately from the session 47 cliffhanger; [[Dondar]]'s fireball engulfed the golem completely and was entirely shrugged off.
* The golem opened with *Slow*, halving movement and restricting actions for most of the party.
* [[Ryo]] held out the [[Silmara Family|Silmara]] signet ring and declared his lineage — the golem did not respond.
* **Encounter Mechanic:** The golem is immune to all damage and effects by default; it can only be stunned by walking through the rotating lightning beams between the chamber's four pylons, opening a damage window.
* [[Kai]] sent a Sending to [[Ayana Syndrosa]] asking whether to preserve or destroy the guardian — she replied: *"The Heart Stone is very likely testing you. Whatever it is, you need to solve the puzzle to get through."*
* The party learned to position the golem into the beam's path; [[Kai]]'s Lunar Spear and [[Berberis]]' Shillelagh dealt the heaviest damage during stun windows.
* **Character Moment:** [[Eryndor]] used Booming Blade with the [[Emberblade]] for the first time; the sword seemed to stir — a soundless acknowledgement, the first hint of something deeper within the blade.
* The golem collapsed backwards and the floor crumbled — Phase 2 began in a lower chamber with more pylons and stone defenders spawning from the rubble.
* [[Dondar]] took 45 damage from a natural 20 golem strike; [[Kai]] cast Sanctuary on him to protect him from further attacks.
* Stone defenders grappled [[Berberis]], [[Ryo]], and [[Eryndor]] in turn; all escaped or were destroyed.
* **Killing Blow:** [[Berberis]] shattered the golem's head just as the lightning built to full discharge — it crackled up and died harmlessly; [[Dondar]]'s beard stood briefly, statically on end.

### The Crossroads

* Beyond the guardian's chamber: a hexagonal antechamber with two cipher-sealed stone doors.
* Left door: *FALSE MEMORIES* — Right door: *HAVE FAITH*.
* The cipher alphabet had subtly shifted from the previous session's version; [[Eryndor]] and [[Kai]] cracked the new substitution quickly.
* The party chose the *Have Faith* door; the *False Memories* door was not attempted this session.

### The Bridge of Trust

* A vast chamber dropping into a purple void; all magic and abilities were stripped from the party on the entry side.
* Stepping onto the jutting ledge stole the player's vision entirely; the rest could see an invisible bridge form across the void but could not cross a force field to help.
* **Goal:** Two players must reach the far side and press two levers simultaneously to open the forward passage.
* **Attempt 1 — [[Kai]]:** Guided by [[Berberis]] and others; failed a Dexterity save at the bridge's edge and fell into the void.
* **Attempt 2 — [[Ryo]]:** Guided primarily by [[Eryndor]]; successfully crossed a ten-foot gap with a running leap, barely landing. Reached the far side and waited by the levers.
* **Attempt 3 — [[Dondar]]:** Polymorphed into a giant badger to deal with grappling golems; fought off one defender but a second hauled them both over the edge.
* **Attempt 4 — [[Berberis]]:** [[Eryndor]] guided him through a winding path including a climb up an elevated section; a pursuing golem nearly caught him before [[Ryo]] stepped in and cut it down.
* **Resolution:** [[Ryo]] and [[Berberis]] pressed both levers simultaneously; the bridge solidified; [[Eryndor]] walked across; [[Kai]] and [[Dondar]] fell from the ceiling onto the completed bridge, roughly half an hour after their falls.

### The Dragon Chamber

* The door beyond opened into an unfathomably large chamber: several hundred feet long, massive columns, darkness at the edges.
* Statues lined the room — elven kings and queens with crowns and circlets, a [[World Tree]], a colossal female figure with elven ears, and a massive kneeling beast-like form.
* The party was deep into their resource reserves — no long rest since before the chess game, most members critically low on spell slots.
* **Cliffhanger:** The beast-like statue shifted. An eyelid opened. A cat-slit eye swivelled and locked onto the party. Ancient muscles began to move, wings unfurled, and a massive serpentine dragon rose from among the statues, towering above them. Its colour was not revealed. Session ends.
