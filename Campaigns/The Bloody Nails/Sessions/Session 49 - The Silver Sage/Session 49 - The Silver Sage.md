## Summary

The dragon's voice rolled through the great chamber like a slow avalanche — *"Little mice in my hole. It has been a long time, and I am so hungry"* — before he sniffed the air and stilled. *"I smell the blood of Silmara. No. There are two of them. This was unforeseen."* Wings folded; a cloud of silver mist gathered around his vast form and resolved into an ancient, hollow-cheeked elven figure leaning hard on a gnarled staff, the same black curse-brand burned through his chest. He named himself [[Praetor'Varanous]] and led the party deeper into the [[Vault of Memories]], where he raised a silver-liquid dais from the stone and asked them to walk him through the threads that had brought them here. Each remembered scene held a deliberate falsity — windows where there were none in [[Ryo]]'s old treehouse, a village too crowded around a five-year-old [[Eryndor]], a hidden Eye disguised among the Hands aboard the prison ship — and as the party named the lies aloud, the dragon nodded and the truths re-anchored themselves in his fraying mind.

When [[Berberis]] dared to look back into the pool, the surface flashed red and tore open onto a memory the dragon had never meant to share: two titans clashing above a battlefield of hundreds of thousands, a city-sized rift bleeding red light, and a crimson dragon driving a claw of black energy into Praetor's chest with a parting *"So long, Praetor."* The brand on his ribs began to make sense — and so did the brand they had once peeled from the back of a red-robed cultist. From that revelation the conversation turned harder. The Empress, Praetor concluded after weighing all they knew, was almost certainly a lich, her soul anchored in some hidden vessel and her face hidden behind a glamour the visions could not pierce. [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow]] had taken the same dark path centuries earlier — but bound his soul to *nothing*, and so could not be killed by ordinary means. There was, the dragon admitted, one path: a single weapon he could enchant — sword, arrow, nail, anything the wielder chose — anchored to a soul-stone from the [[Vale of Eternal Night]]. Strike Kharazoth with it, and a mortal soul could be bound to him; should that mortal then die before his body reformed, he would be ended forever. *"Sacrifice is great and real. But it is the only thing I've found that could destroy him."*

After a long pause, the dragon called Ryo back to the dais one last time and showed him a younger memory still — a hooded woman handing a swaddled infant to a gnomish man in the dark. *"His name is Ryordan. You should probably just call him Ryo."* Then visions of the first [[Ryordan Silmara]], the elven soldier who had bound his people to the sundered root of the world tree, and finally the death of [[Ryo]]'s parents on Blackmarsh's blade. [[Dondar]] asked, almost in spite of himself, after a name he had never spoken aloud — *Hvedra* — and saw in the pool a short dwarven figure with long brown hair walking freely down a torchlit tunnel beside two others, alive and unbound. The party rose through the ceiling on a stone platform, found a soft blue orb hanging in the entry alcove, and watched [[Ayana Syndrosa]]'s flickering image tell them the council would ask nothing more of them tonight. *"Take the night."* In the kitchens of an ancestral home neither he nor [[Eryndor]] had ever seen, [[Ryo]] poured the first drink the half-elf had taken in his life from a cellar more than a century untouched, asked to never again be called Ryordan, and the party scattered to balconies, gardens, and quiet rooms beneath three strange Faewild moons.

## Details

### The Silver Sage

* The session opened directly on the Session 48 cliffhanger; the dragon rose to full height, addressed the party as *"little mice"*, and pretended hunger before revealing it was a joke.
* He sniffed and detected *"the blood of Silmara"* twice — initially confused, then surprised: *"No. There are two of them. This was unforeseen."*
* He revealed the same black, lightning-veined curse-brand burned across his chest as he transformed into an elven form: ancient, frail, silver-haired, leaning on a gnarled staff.
* He introduced himself as **[[Praetor'Varanous]]** and led the party deeper into the chamber to a stone dais with a basin of swirling silver-liquid light.

### The Pool of Insight

* Praetor framed the exercise as anchoring his confused memories: *"Memory is unreliable, especially mine. To pass it, we must first uncover the falsities."*
* **Memory — Ryo's treehouse home in the Darkwood:** [[Ryo]] improvised the falsity — the windows. He had never cut windows; the home was sealed against light leaking out.
* **Memory — Eryndor at five years old:** A village deep in the [[Whispering Woods]], his father frantic, his mother already missing. [[Eryndor]] named the falsity: too many houses, too many people. His family lived more isolated on the outskirts.
* **Memory — the prison ship at the start of the campaign:** The woman the party originally believed to be a [[Hand of the Empire]] was in fact an [[Eye of the Empire|Eye]] in disguise — the assassin they later understood her to be.
* **Memory — Vallania in the goblin cave:** [[Vallania Miriel|Nia]] had been imprisoned and barely breathing, not fighting goblins on her feet.
* **Memory — Lenny the goblin running into the woods:** Ellie was missing from the vision; the two goblins fled together.
* **Memory — Tumbler's Crag and the locket:** The skeleton highlighted in the moonlight should have been a child's, not an adult's. [[Salla the Brave|Salla]] died young.
* **Memory — Skyreach and the defeat of the Hand:** The rusty nail was driven into his forehead, not his chest.
* **Memory — the wagon ride with Lia:** [[Lia]] picked Eryndor's pocket for the dagger, not gold.
* **Memory — the Finegold ambush:** [[Harland Finegold]]'s body was found buried in a field, not propped against the stones.
* **Memory — Vallania presenting the party to her father:** Tarlis Miriel arrived alone, with no flanking guards.
* **Memory — Serenity Plaza fire elementals:** before the fire, a street preacher had been the trigger that caused the guards to arrive and only then did the fire erupt. [[Dondar]]'s minor illusion of a nail had sealed the event.
* **Memory — confronting the dwarven smith Dhorik:** Hugor was not present; he had long since fled after [[Ryo]] frightened him.
* **Memory — [[Kai]]'s ceremony to Selune on the Frostway:** There was no crowd of refugees. Only [[Alyn the First]] answered the call.
* **Memory — the Frostwarden crews:** The crew leading them through the snow was the **Fox Crew**, not the Bear Crew (who were lost) nor the Wolf Crew (slaughtered by the worms).
* **Memory — Blackmarsh corrupting Vaeleran:** The gemstone was purple, not green; [[Vaeleran]] never knelt.
* **Memory — [[Ayana Syndrosa]] revealing herself as elf:** She pinned only [[Ryo]] to the wall and teleported the rest away — they were never lined up against it.

### A Glimpse Behind the Curtain

* [[Berberis]] proposed an Insight check to peer back into Praetor's own memory through the pool; the DM granted advantage for the idea.
* The pool flashed red and pulled the party into a vision of two titanic dragons clashing above a battlefield of hundreds of thousands, with a rift the size of a city tearing open and bleeding red light.
* The crimson dragon drove a claw of black energy into the silver dragon's chest with a parting line: *"So long, Praetor."*
* Praetor slammed his staff and dispelled the vision, embarrassed to have been read.
* [[Berberis]] handed him the parchment copy of the runes carved into the back of the red-robed cultist they had killed; Praetor confirmed it evoked the same language as his own curse-brand, though not the same symbol.
* **Major Reveal:** The crimson dragon was [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow]]. Praetor's curse was Kharazoth's parting gift; the [[Silmara Family]] had bound him to the [[Vault of Memories]] to keep the death-magic from killing him, but to step beyond the vault is to die within hours.

### A Council in the Vault

* Praetor turned the conversation: *"What is it that you all want?"*
* [[Berberis]]: end the dead walking the north — the necromancer in the ruins of [[Val Noren]].
* [[Ryo]]: undecided; only days into knowing his heritage; not ready to commit to a throne.
* [[Eryndor]]: rescue his mother [[Eirieneth Silmara|Eirieneth]] from the Empress's tower in [[Val Luminor]]; admitted he had given up hope before today.
* [[Dondar]]: liberate his people. *"The Empire have taken our holy city. I would like it back. And the traitors within."* — the first time he had spoken aloud of [[Glimmerforge]] to the party.
* **Character Moment:** [[Kai]] noted this was the first time [[Dondar]] had ever revealed any of this. *"No one ever asked,"* the dwarf replied.

### The Empress and the Lich

* The party shared what they knew of [[Empress Morganna Eventide|Empress Morganna]]: arrived young from the east, has not aged, served by the [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow|Crimson Shadow]], known to the Spider Princess of the Underdark.
* They handed Praetor the **assassin's blood-paper** found on the Eye on the prison ship.
* He scryed the other end and reported: the recipient is currently inside the **Citadel of [[Val Luminor]]**, very close to the Empress, almost certainly the head of the Eyes of the Empire.
* [[Berberis]] also offered the Dread Sword for inspection. Praetor refused to touch it: *"I do not need to inspect that closer. I was around at the time of its creation."*
* **Major Reveal — the Empress is a Lich:** From all the gathered evidence, Praetor concluded Morganna is a lich — soul bound to a hidden vessel — and her unaging glamour is hiding a far older form. To kill her, the vessel must be destroyed before her body can reform. He warned the party that her power may exceed anything they could imagine.

### The Path to Kill Kharazoth

* **Major Reveal — Kharazoth's immortality:** Where a lich anchors its soul to an item, Kharazoth bound his to *nothing*. He cannot be killed by ordinary means; he simply reforms.
* **The path Praetor offers:** he knows an enchantment he can place upon a chosen weapon — sword, arrow, **nail**, anything that draws blood — paired with a soul-stone anchor.
* The wielder of this artifact can kill Kharazoth temporarily by piercing his body and dying with the artifact still inside him.
* **The permanent kill:** before striking, the soul of a mortal is bound to the artifact; if that mortal then dies before Kharazoth reforms, Kharazoth is destroyed forever.
* Praetor named the cost plainly: *"Sacrifice is great and real. But from all of my time and all of my research, it is the only thing I've found that could possibly destroy him forever."*
* He left the question of *who* the bound mortal would be entirely open.
* **Crafting time:** *"Some months."*
* **Soul-stone location:** A soul-stone anchor is known to lie deep in the [[Vale of Eternal Night]] to the north — Unseelie territory. The party connected this immediately to [[Kaelen Vayne]]'s missing son, listed MIA in the same Vale.

### Ryo, Ryordan, and the Death of His Parents

* After the break, Praetor turned again to [[Ryo]], pressing him gently on whether he meant to claim the throne.
* [[Ryo]] declared he would not stay in [[Sumara, The Shining City|Sumara]]: *"I have no interest in politics."*
* **Vision — Ryo's surrender:** Two cloaked figures handed an infant Ryo to a gnomish man. *"His name is Ryordan. You should probably just call him Ryo. Save the anonymity."* The shorter figure — his mother — patted his head and walked away.
* **Major Reveal — his true name:** *"Your mother called you Ryordan."*
* **Vision — Ryordan Silmara, First of His Name:** An elven soldier standing among vast blue-skinned, winged celestial entities at a cosmic battle, a great root piercing earth and sky. Praetor named him as the Silmara who bound his people to the sundered root of the world tree and held the cosmos in balance.
* **Vision — the death of his parents:** Replayed from the vision in Session 47 — his father [[Ratha Silmara|Ratha]] holding his wounded mother [[Rhennaya Silmara|Rhennaya]], [[Dread General Gerard Blackmarsh]] stepping in with the Dread Sword and running both through with a single thrust. **Confirmed:** Blackmarsh was their killer.
* **Character Moment:** *"At least we got their killer."*

### Hvedra, Moonstones, and Time

* **Dondar's question — Hvedra:** Praetor scryed and showed [[Dondar]] a vision of three dwarves walking down a torchlit tunnel, one short, one with very long brown hair trailing down her back. The dwarf added the details himself; Praetor confirmed the vision was of the present. **She appears to be alive, walking freely, and not a captive — which Dondar found troubling.**
* **Kai's question — the third Moonstone:** [[Kai]] presented the Moonlight Shield. Praetor confirmed two stones already known (the shield and the one held by **High Priest [[High Priest Oldir|Oldir]] at the High Sept of [[Val Miriel]]**) and that *"the shield will point the way"* to the third — but only on the material plane.
* Kai asked whether more than three Moonstones existed; Praetor declined to answer beyond noting that *"falling into the hands of the wrong people would be very diabolical."*
* **Time:** Time in the vault flows at the same rate as outside.
* Praetor sent his regards to [[Ayana Syndrosa]] (*"I always enjoyed her company"*) and accepted, in passing, [[Dondar]]'s offer of a future chess game with Master Grumble.
* For the journey north, Praetor advised consulting the people of [[Sumara, The Shining City|Sumara]] for knowledge of the [[Vale of Eternal Night|Unseelie Court]].

### The Blue Orb

* The stone platform raised the party back through the ceiling and into the Royal Citadel basement; the vault contraption sealed behind them.
* In the alcove a **soft blue orb** hung in the air, unfolding into a flickering hologram of [[Ayana Syndrosa]] (with [[Illythia Voss]] briefly waving in frame).
* **Cite:** *"I will ask no more of you. Only come when you feel like you're ready. Take the night to yourselves."*
* **Character Moment:** Ayana admitted, openly, that part of her drive had been selfish — that the party had reunited her with someone she cared deeply about, and that she would forever be thankful.

### The Royal Citadel

* **Ryo's request:** *"Please don't call me Ryordan. Just call me Ryo."*
* [[Berberis]] sought out one of the Citadel's interior gardens and curled up at the base of a tree for the night, planning a later visit to the Greensward Canton.
* [[Kai]] found a balcony with a view of the moon — and counted **three moons** above [[Sumara, The Shining City|Sumara]], one of them faint and pink, the Faewild sky unlike anything on the Material Plane.
* [[Eryndor]] sought out [[Ryo]]; the two of them found the kitchens, where the fireplace lit itself.
* **Character Moment — first drink:** From the Royal Wine Cellars, [[Ryo]] poured [[Eryndor]] his first ever drink. *"And it was there in the kitchen of your ancestral home that you both shared a drink together for the first time."*
* The two of them spoke openly: distrust of even [[Ayana Syndrosa]]; the resolution to find [[Eryndor]]'s mother; the need to help [[Dondar]] with his unspoken brother; getting back at [[General Korvas|Korvas]]; the search for [[Kaelen Vayne]]'s son's fate.
* They noted the practical thought of finding a teleport circle in [[Sumara, The Shining City|Sumara]] to ease return travel.
* [[Dondar]] drank alone, meditated, and went to bed with all his thoughts on his family.
* As [[Eryndor]] drifted off, his mind wandered back to the [[Emberblade]] in the closet — the half-stirring he had felt in the previous session lingered. Something is there, *to look into anyway.*
* **Reward Obtained — Long Rest and Level-Up:** The party took a full long rest in the Royal Citadel and advanced to **Level 10**.
