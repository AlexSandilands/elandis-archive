# Session Title Proposals — The Bloody Nails

Three naming registers are offered for every session so you can pick whichever column reads best to you and apply it wholesale. Each column keeps a consistent tone across all 48 sessions.

- **Column A — Punchy.** One to three words. A single image, a single object, a single blow. These are meant to hit like a struck bell: terse, evocative, no connective tissue.
- **Column B — Chapter.** Three to six words in the classic fantasy-novel chapter style — the register of *A Game of Thrones*, *The Wheel of Time*, *The Locked Tomb*. Often a concrete noun phrase ("The Gates of Darmouth", "Blood on the Altar"), sometimes a cadenced clause. Reads well aloud, feels like a page-turn.
- **Column C — Thematic.** More literary and poetic — the voice of chapter titles in Le Guin, Bakker, or Mieville. Favours "of"/"and" constructions, abstract nouns, images held in tension. Takes the emotional beat of the session and polishes it into a phrase.

Session 10.5 was a short interlude session — a side-quest bridge into the Faelon / ghost-girl arc. It's titled accordingly in each register.

Pick a column and I can rename the folders to match.
