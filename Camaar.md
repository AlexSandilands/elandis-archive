A major port city on the southern shore of the [[Gulf of Miriel]] — the [[Mawbreakers|Mawbreakers']] stronghold, the gulf's oldest commercial harbour, and the scruffiest city in the Empire's reluctant ledger.

## Established
- Southern shore of the Gulf of Miriel, at the foot of a mountain range (Gulf of Miriel codex; map)
- Sprawling, ancient city of stone — "haphazardly built stone structures stretching along the coast like a maze, bearing the marks of centuries of unplanned expansion" (Session 8 prep)
- Contrasts sharply with the ordered colonial style of [[Darmouth]] — ancient and unplanned rather than Imperial in character (Session 8)
- Informally divided: Old Camaar (north) and New Camaar (south), with the Docklands as a third distinct zone (Camaar.md / brainstorm)
- The Mawbreakers operate their primary base here, at a robust stone stronghold overlooking the docks, with a hidden basement war room (Sessions 8, 12)
- Slim governance: a mayor's office and town guard exist but are weak; main Imperial military force is at [[Val Miriel]], not Camaar (brainstorm)
- The [[Order of Ravens]] has an active nest in the city, operating out of Old Camaar (Order of Ravens codex)
- Active supply trade via [[Faelon]] (half a day's ride inland) to the southern mountain range (Session 9 prep)
- [[General Varan Brado]] makes periodic shows of force; the locals resent it (brainstorm)

## Geography

The city occupies a stretch of the southern gulf shore backed by a mountain range, giving it a sheltered harbour on one side and rugged high country on the other. It sits relatively close to [[The Maw]] — the whirlpool at the gulf's eastern mouth — making it the first safe anchorage inside the gulf for any ship that has just run the passage. The mountains behind it are the source of the mineral trade that gave the city its founding purpose; [[Faelon]], a small inland town at the mountain base, is the waypoint through which goods travel between the mines and the docks.

Three informal zones, without hard boundaries:

- **Old Camaar (North):** The original settlement, grown organically over centuries and now largely neglected. Buildings lean and sag; law has little presence or effect here. The shadow organisations of the city — the [[Veiled Cubs]], the [[Order of Ravens]], the [[Shadow Serpents]] — operate here, using the dark streets as cover. No hard line separates it from New Camaar, but people know not to walk through it after dark.
- **New Camaar (South):** The commercial expansion that grew as trade prospered. Better maintained, home to merchants and tradespeople, and most of the city's formal shops and services.
- **The Docklands:** The waterfront, distinct from both halves. The [[Mawbreakers|Mawbreakers']] stronghold overlooks the harbour, and the dockside taverns and facilities serve the sailors and captains who keep Camaar alive.

## History

Camaar is ancient — roughly contemporary with the Kingdoms of Old and founded under the jurisdiction of the [[Val Miriel|Miriel Kingdom]] long before the [[Valtorran Empire|Empire]] existed. It was never a kingdom in its own right, but a working port with a specific purpose.

The founding logic runs several threads at once. The minerals in the southern mountains needed an outlet to the gulf. Ships coming through The Maw needed an immediate safe harbour for repairs and resupply — Camaar is the first anchorage on the inner shore after the passage. The mountains supply freshwater down to the coast, making the site ideal for provisioning ocean-going ships. And Camaar served as a useful shadow port: a place to trade without the full oversight and tariffs of Val Miriel. The [[Aeolian Chasm]], cutting through the mountains directly south, has always been a land-trade barrier; the fact that the Empire chose to build [[Skyreach]] to bridge it reflects a commercial frustration that is centuries old.

Centuries of uncoordinated expansion produced the current city: mismatched rooflines, streets that fold back on themselves, and an older northern district that has simply been left behind as commerce pushed south. The Mawbreakers' dominance is less a historical event than a structural inevitability — the pilots who guide ships through the Maw have always lived in the port those ships limp into.

## Governance

Camaar has a mayor's office and a town guard, but neither holds much weight. The mayor is not a formal Imperial appointee — technically a local position — but is functionally subservient to the Empire and expected to report upward. Real governance is thin. The main Imperial military presence in the Gulf sits at Val Miriel; the Empire has not found it worth stationing a dedicated garrison in Camaar.

The [[Mawbreakers|Mawbreakers]] fill the gap. They are not formally the law, but people look to them when the mayor's office or town guard cannot or will not act. In the Docklands especially, the Mawbreakers are the authority that matters.

## Character & Culture

Camaar is a sailors' town. Its people are predominantly workers, sailors, and merchants — practical and largely indifferent to political allegiances beyond the immediate. The city has a lived-in roughness from centuries of unplanned growth and minimal civic investment.

The Empire barely meddling is precisely what the city prefers. Camaar sits in a comfortable grey zone: close enough to Imperial supply lines to benefit from trade, far enough from Val Miriel that day-to-day life proceeds without much interference. [[General Varan Brado|General Brado's]] periodic shows of force — troops in the streets, naval vessels at anchor — remind the city that the Empire has not forgotten it. The locals receive these visits with quiet resentment. The Mawbreakers are broadly respected as the city's real authority; their roots go back further than the Empire, and their control of The Maw makes their presence feel less like imposition and more like a natural feature of the place.

## Trade

Camaar is the southern gulf's primary working port. Its harbour handles goods routed through The Maw by the Mawbreakers, as well as overland trade from the mountains. [[Faelon]] is the waypoint: minerals and raw mountain goods come down to the docks; provisions and equipment go back up in return.
