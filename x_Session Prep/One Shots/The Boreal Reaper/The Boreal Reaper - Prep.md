#### Crossing the Narrow Ice Path

**Description:**  
As the party ascends the foothills leading to Icehowl Peak, they come upon a narrow ledge carved into the mountainside. The path is barely wide enough for a single file. A steep rock wall looms on one side, while on the other, a sheer drop plunges hundreds of feet into a rocky ravine. Blasts of icy wind make footing treacherous.

**Presenting the Danger:**

- The trail is coated with slick ice, and occasional patches of loose rock threaten to give way underfoot.
- The wind howls across the ledge with enough force to knock an unsteady traveller off balance.
- Snowfall or swirling fog could limit visibility to only a few feet.

**How the Party Might Detect It:**

- A **Survival DC 12** check might let them know that high winds and icy footing make this an extremely dangerous pass.
- On a **Perception DC 12** or higher, they might notice a hairline crack in the ledge indicating it isn’t entirely stable.

**Possible Solutions:**

1. **Use Rope and Pitons**:
    - The party can set pitons into the rocky wall and secure rope to create a makeshift railing.
    - Each member crossing can hold onto the rope, granting advantage on checks made to avoid slipping.
2. **Find an Alternate Route**:
    - A **Survival DC 20** check might reveal a longer but safer path around the ledge. This may cost extra time but reduces risk.
3. **Move One at a Time**:
    - Characters might coordinate crossing in single-file to reduce stress on the path.
    - Each person crossing makes an **Acrobatics or Athletics check (DC 15)** to keep footing.
> [!question] Trigger
> If nothing was done to alleviate the danger of weight on the path then the ledge will break

**Outcome & Consequences:**

- **Success:** The party navigates the ledge, perhaps exhausted or slowed, but unharmed.

- **Failure (Slipping)**: Another party member may attempt a **Dexterity saving throw** to grab them—if roped, they have advantage. A fall could result in significant damage or even be fatal, emphasizing the seriousness of the environment.
> [!info]- Action
> - Falls 60ft (6D6 damage)
