## Scene 1: The Mountain Road

> [!summary]+ Details
>
> Well-maintained stone road up through the [[Shimmering Peaks]]. The party has not yet seen the city.
> This will be the last opportunity for the party to discuss before heading into the city

**NPC: Thenrel Arith** — an elf road worker, middle-aged by elf standards, lean and practical-looking. Wears a dull-green work tunic and carries a chipping mallet and a supply pack. He has been tending this road alone for thirty years. He is not lonely — he is content. He has not thought about the possibility of visitors in decades.

- **Description:** weathered hands, ink-stained fingertips (he keeps a personal log of every repair he makes), 
- a small jar of Faewild flowers tucked into his belt because Thenrel notices small things.
- **Reaction:** he hears footsteps on the road and turns around expecting to re-mark a waystone. He sees the party. He goes completely still. The mallet falls out of his hand. He looks at it. He looks at them. He looks at the mallet.

He cannot stop staring. He answers any questions haltingly, honestly. He runs ahead — not because he's panicking, but because he genuinely cannot help himself. He has to tell someone.

## Scene 2: Cresting the Hill — First Look at Sumara

> [!cite]+ Read Aloud
> *Cresting a hill between two peaks, the road curves and the mountains part — and there it is.*
>
> *[[Sumara, The Shining City|Sumara]].*
>
> *It fills the entire caldera below you like something poured into a bowl of white stone. The city doesn't sit on the land — it grows from it. Tier upon tier of alabaster and glass cascading down the mountain walls, connected by arching bridges of what you can only describe as frozen light. Spires catch the Faewild sky and refract it into prisms. Somewhere far below, a silver waterfall roars from the heights and disappears into channels of glowing blue water. In the center, rising from a narrow spire of natural stone, a citadel you could describe no other way than: eternal. As though the mountain always knew it would be here.*
>
> *And above the city — floating, slowly drifting — are three enormous structures, vast as temples, hanging in the air as though they have simply forgotten to fall.*
>
> *You hear Ayana draw a sharp breath beside you.*
>
> *She says nothing. But her hand finds Ryo's arm, and holds it.*

> [!info]+ Floating Buildings on Arrival
> - **The Locus Arcanum** (Weave) - crystal sphere, orrery rings
> - **The Luminarium** (Dawn) - glass and copper conservatory, domed roof
> - **The Palisade Eternal** (Blade) are floating - A squat angular fortress tower of dark grey stone, covered in carved battle reliefs
> - **The Oraculum** (Scroll) - tower of deep indigo stone, large crystal lens at the top

[[Sumara, The Shining City#The Seven Cantons|Cantons]]

Let the moment breathe. Give players time to react. Child-Ayana may react with something childlike but real: *"It's so pretty"* — but there's something underneath it that the players might notice if they're paying attention.

## Scene 3: Entering the City

> [!summary]+ Details
>
> They descend toward the Heartstone Commons entrance — a grand natural arch in the caldera wall, carved with reliefs of the World Tree.

- Citizens: Elves in flowing clothing, pixies darting between market stalls, sylvans (taller, bark-textured skin, hair like leaves), fungrals (small, cheerful, mushroom-capped folk rolling barrels and carrying baskets), satyrs
- The crowd doesn't become immediately hostile — confused, awed, then anxious
- Give 2-3 minutes of in-world time (party walking, looking around, being stared at) before the patrol arrives

## Scene 4: The Arrest

Three Faewild Wyverns descend from the Palisade Eternal — sleek, iridescent-scaled, smaller than Material Plane wyverns, built for speed and agility, with a faintly luminescent blue-green shimmer to their scales.

Their riders: **Vanguard Wardens** — elite elven soldiers of the Ironsward, fitted silver-and-grey armour, crests of the Circle of the Blade.

**Lead Warden: Warden Caeryn** — young, nervous but disciplined.

- *"By order of Commander Vayne and the Council of the Seven Circles, you are required to present yourselves for questioning. Please come peacefully."*
- Not unkind. Just very out of his depth with this situation.
- If pushed: *"I am trying to do this with dignity. Please. Do not make me use force in the middle of the Commons."*

> [!info]+ Recommended Path
> Allow themselves to be taken. Fighting Vanguard Wardens in the Commons would be catastrophic politically. Child-Ayana will be cooperative and excited: *"Are we being arrested? Woooo!"*

> [!danger]+ If They Resist
> The city goes quiet. More wardens on rooftops. The crowd pulls back. Make clear this is not a fight they want.

## Scene 5: The Ironsward Canton — Interrogation & Healing Ayana

> [!summary]+ Setting
> A clean but severe receiving hall in the Ironsward Canton. Stone floors, long table, oil lamps.

**The Interrogation** — Warden Caeryn takes their details:
- *"How did you cross the Liar's River?"*
- *"What is your purpose here?"*
- *"Where did you come from on the Material Plane?"*
	- Elf follow up question
- Who is this child

> [!info]+ Noticing Ayana
> At some point during questioning — when child-Ayana says something oddly knowing, or when she absent-mindedly begins to form a spell — a warden whispers to another: *"Wait... is that child... is she warded? That looks like Gossamer Dust."*

**Sending for Elder Pip:**
- **Warden-Elder Thessan** (grey-haired, long experience, Caeryn's superior) recognizes Gossamer Dust
- Sends for **Elder Pip Glimmermoss** — an ancient, tiny, wing-worn pixie healer from the Circle of the Dawn. 200+ years old. Warm but fussy, all business, speaks in rapid clipped sentences.
- The cure: a small vial of deep green liquid she produces from somewhere in her many pockets. Has Ayana drink it. Within a minute, she grows back — memories flooding in all at once.

> [!danger]+ Moment: Ayana Restored — Name Reveal
> Ayana sits up slowly. Takes a breath. Looks at her hands. Then at the room. Then directly at Caeryn.
>
> *"Caeryn. You've gotten taller, but it seems your wits have grown duller!"*
> Caeryn finds his voice: *"Lady Syndrosa?"*
>
> *"Don't just stand there with your mouth open. You look like your father when he's confused. How is Rellian, by the way? Still growing those awful root vegetables on his terrace?"*
>
> — **This is the first time the players hear the name Syndrosa.**
>
> She rebukes him gently for not recognising her. She asserts herself: the Bloody Nails are under her protection. They are not prisoners. She asks for privacy and wine
> *"I have been eight years old for nearly 24 hours. I need wine."*

- Ayana uses a Sending to contact Illythia
- **Set time: tonight at moonrise** (gives the party most of the day to explore)
- Condition: Ayana requests her presence remain secret until the Confluence begins. She will travel cloaked and hooded. *"I want to speak before they have time to prepare speeches."*

## Scene 7: Exploring the City

The party has several hours before moonrise.

### The Heartstone Commons

#### Petal & Provision

**Proprietor: Sporryn** (fungral, male, enthusiastic, pale-cream mushroom cap)

- Sells Faewild herbs, alchemical supplies, dried mushroom rations, simple rope and tools
- Fascinated by the outsiders — will ask a hundred questions in rapid sequence

#### The Fading Stars *(Tavern)*

**Proprietor: Torgrun Brasswick** — a male dwarf, 180 years old, ruddy-faced, enormous beard braided with small Faewild flowers his wife puts there every morning. Cheerful, nostalgic, sad around the eyes.

**His wife: Syriel Brasswick** — an elf of the Circle of the Root, taller than him by a good two feet, graceful, warm, deeply fond of him.

Torgrun came to Sumara with the great Exile because he refused to be separated from Syriel. He has not seen another dwarf in a century.

When he sees Dondar: he goes completely still. Then very quietly: *"Well. I'll be the deep roots of the mountain."*

> [!info]+ Torgrun's Questions for Dondar
> He will want to know about the dwarven holds. Is [[Glimmerforge]] still standing? What of the clans? Has the Empire broken them or are they fighting back? His voice doesn't crack until he asks about Glimmerforge specifically. That's where he's from.
>
> He won't push. He will sit down, pour two drinks, and wait.

> [!tip]+ Gossip at The Fading Stars
>
> - *"Did you hear the Palisade has been docked in the low position for three days? Vayne is making a point."*
> - *"The Scroll circle says the Oraculum needs restoration work. Convenient timing, don't you think."*
> - *"My cousin in the Luminary says Elder Silaqui had visitors last week. Scales people. She looked upset after."*
> - *"Someone saw a light in the Citadel last night. The Royal Citadel. That place has been sealed for a hundred years."*

### The Fundament Canton

#### The Ironweave *(Blacksmith — key for Faewild metal items)*

**Proprietor: Ferethis** — a lean, quiet elven smith who works exclusively with Faewild metals. Suspicious of outsiders but will warm if the party shows genuine interest in craft.

> [!info]+ Faewild Metals
> Three types available:
> - **Starfall Iron**: Faintly luminescent, forged from ore shot through with trapped starlight. Slightly warm to the touch.
> - **Heartwood-Bound**: Wood-and-metal hybrid construction. Lighter than it looks. Organic, almost alive.
> - **Moon-Quenched Steel**: Deep silver-grey, quenched in Liar's River water under moonlight. Cold to the touch at all times.

> [!info]+ Rollable Faewild Property Table
> Each item's special property is unknown until it is first used in earnest (first attack made, first hit taken). Roll 1d8 when triggered:
>
> | d8 | Property | Effect |
> |---|---|---|
> | 1 | **Dreamweight**- | The item is impossibly light when it counts. +1 to attack rolls. On a natural 1, the weapon acts of its own accord — makes an additional attack against the nearest creature (could be an ally). |
> | 2 | **Starfall Temper** | In complete darkness, the item sheds dim light in a 5-foot radius. Crit range increases by 1 (e.g. 19–20). |
> | 3 | **Living Memory** | The item remembers its owner. If disarmed or dropped, it returns to your hand at the start of your next turn (no action required). |
> | 4 | **Fae-Stitched** | The item resists being removed. Advantage on Strength saves against forced movement. Drawback: cannot be removed quickly — requires a full minute to doff armour or sheathe the weapon. |
> | 5 | **The Hunger**- | The weapon wants to be used. If you haven't made an attack on your turn, make a DC 12 Wisdom save or use your bonus action to make one regardless. On hits, add 1d4 extra damage. |
> | 6 | **Verdant Rebound** | Armour woven with root-memory. The first time you drop below half HP in combat, vines briefly erupt — the attacker must make a DC 13 Strength save or be restrained until end of their next turn. |
> | 7 | **Echo Metal** | The weapon remembers every swing. Once per combat, when you miss an attack, you may reroll it. |
> | 8 | **Moonquench** | Simply excellent. +1 AC (armour) or +1 to damage rolls (weapon). No tricks. Extremely rare. |

## Scene 8: The Weave's Canton — Meeting Illythia Voss

As moonrise approaches, Ayana leads the group (cloaked) to the Etherean Canton.

**Illythia Voss** meets them at the base of the Locus Arcanum as it descends to dock.

- **Description:** tall even for an elf, silver-streaked dark hair pulled back practically, deep blue robes, an arcane focus braided into her hair rather than carried. Precise, calm, with warmth underneath — but tonight she is wound tight.
- Will send the nearby arcanists away and request their presence in her quarters

> [!danger]+ The Meeting — Ex-Lovers
> Illythia stops walking. A long beat. Then:
>
> *"40 years... and not one message."*
> Ayana begins: *"Illythia, it was better this way, there was no way of knowing if..."*
>
> Illythia slaps her. Not hard enough to injure, hard enough to mean it.
>"Illy!?"
> A beat. Then Illythia pulls her in and kisses her.
> Another beat.
> *"You left me in charge of the Circle. I have been furious, terrified, proud and lonely every single day, but not one message"*
>
> Ayana: *"I know."*
> *"Never again."*
> *"Okay."*
>Why are you here?
>- Look to Ryo for confirmation?
>- Suggests the group explains the evidence found
>- Dragons, Chaos wars
> Illythia takes a breath, smooths her robes, and looks at the party with an expression of composed professionalism: *"Right. Shall we go over the council?"*

> [!info]+ Illythia's Briefing
> - Kaelen Vayne has the support of Naivara Siannodel (Scales) and Elrohir Gemflower (Scroll)
> - The three remaining — Dawn (Silaqui), Loom (Fenian), Root (Sylvaris) — are uncertain
> - Kaelen has been the de facto council head for a decade
> - *"He is not evil. He is a man who has decided that the best way to protect people is to control everything. You've met the type. His son was lost to the Vale of Eternal Night and he's been different ever since"*
> - The party should expect dismissal, condescension, and hard questions. That is fine. Be ready.

## Scene 9: The Confluence of the Seven

> [!summary]+ Setting
> The seven floating buildings dock together high above the city at moonrise, joined to the peak of the Royal Citadel — a crystalline platform open to the sky. Bridges of light extend between each building and the central platform. The whole city watches from below.

> [!read]+ Opening Read Aloud
> *Seven great structures drift in from the darkness, guided by unseen hands. They lock into place one by one with a sound like a chord struck on an instrument the size of a cathedral. The Citadel's peak opens — a platform of pure crystal rising slowly to join them. From below, the whole city glows.*

**Council Members:**

| Name | Circle | Disposition | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Illythia Voss** | The Weave | Supportive | Must appear neutral; Ayana's ex-lover |
| **Kaelen Vayne** | The Blade | Hostile | Cold, dangerous-when-quiet; politically formidable |
| **Elder Silaqui Amastacia** | The Dawn | Open | Gentle, genuinely moved; most likely first to swing to Ryo |
| **Archivist Elrohir Gemflower** | The Scroll | Allied with Kaelen | Ancient, dry; argues via historical record |
| **Justiciar Naivara Siannodel** | The Scales | Allied with Kaelen | Cold precision; legal arguments only |
| **Master Artisan Fenian** | The Loom | Cautious | Watches Silaqui and Sylvaris for cues |
| **Warden Sylvaris** | The Root | Suspicious | Especially focused on planar crossings |

### Act 1: The Threat — Kharazoth

Ayana removes her hood. Let the room react.

- The threat she has uncovered.
- The Empire is not what anyone believes. It is being directed by an agent of Chaos
- **[[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow]]**, an ancient red dragon surviving from the Chaos Wars. The Empress serves him.
- Believes he intends to start the a second chaos war

> [!danger]+ Interruption — "How Did You Get Here?"
> Before she can finish, someone cuts her off. Kaelen. Or Naivara. Or both, overlapping.
>
> *"How did you get here?"*
>
> A beat. Then Elrohir, quietly: *"You came from the Material Plane. You crossed the barrier. Commander Vayne — the wards—"*
>
> Kaelen: *"You have opened a rift. You have done exactly what we have spent a century preventing. You walked back into this city through a tear in the fabric of our protection, and now you want us to discuss dragons?"*
>
> Naivara: *"Lady Syndrosa — if that claim is even valid after a twenty-year absence — you have potentially compromised the security of every living elf in this city. Do you understand the gravity of what you've done?"*
>
> The room turns. Even Silaqui is troubled. This is the accusation that must be answered.

> [!check]+ Ayana's Defense
> *"I opened no rift. I crossed no planar tear. I used no magic to return."*
>
> Silence.
>
> *"Then how?"* (from Elrohir, academically curious despite himself)
>
> Ayana turns. She gestures to Ryo.
>
> *"He brought me here."*

---

### Act 2: Ryo's Claim

The attention shifts entirely. Ayana explains who Ryo is — or who she believes him to be. The son of Ratha and Rhennaya Silmara. The ring is presented.

> [!danger]+ Kaelen Vayne — Response
> *"The royal line is dead. I grieve it as we all do. But this — this young man, raised on the Material Plane by mortals, carrying a ring that could have been made by any competent artificer — this is not a restoration. This is a story. And stories, however moving, do not govern cities."*
>
> He will call Ryo "boy" at some point. He will not shout. He is more dangerous when quiet.

**Challenges posed to Ryo:**

> [!danger]+ From Naivara (Scales)
> *"The law requires recognition and a Rite of Ascension. Neither has occurred. Legally, you are a claimant. Nothing more."*

> [!danger]+ From Elrohir (Scroll)
> *"The historical record shows that every crisis our people have endured originated with a unilateral royal decision. The Chaos Wars. The Exile. The deaths of Ratha and Rhennaya. Why should we believe this time would be different?"*

> [!danger]+ From Kaelen (Blade)
> *"Even if your blood is true — what gives you the right to speak for a people who have never met you? You don't know our language. You don't know what we built here in silence and survival."*

> [!danger]+ From Sylvaris (Root)
> *"You came through the barrier. Planar crossings weaken the walls between worlds. How many cracks did you open to reach us?"*

> [!check]+ From Silaqui (Dawn) — gently
> *"Tell me about your mother. Rhennaya was someone I admired greatly. Tell me something only her child would know."*
>
> If Ryo answers well (DC 14 Persuasion or good roleplay): Silaqui visibly softens. She does not speak against the claim. Fenian watches her. Sylvaris is unmoved but thoughtful.

**The Ring's Rejection:**
- Kaelen: *"The Empire's artificers are not without talent. This proves nothing except that someone knew the ring's design."*
- Naivara agrees: *"Without the Rite of Ascension, the ring is symbolic at best."*

**The Verdant Vault Suggestion** — comes from Elrohir, a small concession from an unexpected direction:

> [!check]+ Elrohir's Concession
> *"If the claim is genuine, there is one proof that cannot be forged. The Verdant Vault. The doors respond only to Silmara blood. If this young man can open them, the argument is resolved."*

> [!info]+ The Verdant Vault — Council Briefing
> - Located beneath the Royal Citadel in the stone below the Bridge of Recollection's foundation
> - Sealed for 100 years. No one has entered since the Exile.
> - Only a Silmara royal can cause the doors to open
> - Magical properties unpredictable and ancient — *"Do not touch anything you do not understand"* (Elrohir)

---

### Act 3: Kaelen Exits — and the Stakes Remain

With the succession question tabled pending the Vault, Kaelen moves to adjourn. Ayana stops him.

*"We are not done. I did not cross two continents and a realm boundary to settle a succession question. Half the matter has not been heard."*

She returns to Kharazoth. To the evidence the Bloody Nails have gathered. To what it means that the Empire is being shaped by an ancient dragon toward a single end.

> [!danger]+ Kaelen Storms Off
> He stands. Slowly.
>
> *"I will not sit at this table and watch our society unravel for a ghost story. If there is evidence, submit it to the Scroll for assessment. Elrohir will review it in due course."*
>
> He gathers his things. Before leaving — quietly, to the room, not to Ayana:
>
> *"When you are done with the theatre, I will be at my Canton. Governing the people who actually depend on me."*
>
> He leaves. Naivara and Elrohir exchange a look. They do not follow him — but they do not commit either.

**Session End:** The council agrees, tentatively, to reconvene after the Verdant Vault is opened. Ryo must prove his blood. And whatever is inside that Vault may answer far more than just the question of succession.
