## Scenario 1: Audience with the Dragon

> [!summary]- Details
> - Where: [[Vault of Memories]] — the dragon's chamber, deep within the Heartstone
> - When: Continues directly from Session 48 cliffhanger
> - Key NPCs: [[Praetor'Varanous]]

### Scene 1: First Impressions

*Continues from cliffhanger — the dragon has just risen above the party.*

**[[Praetor'Varanous]]** — ancient silver dragon, bound to the Heartstone for centuries; vast, serious, dry humour rare but devastating when it lands

> [!cite]+ Read Aloud
> *"Little mice in my home..."* He inhales — slow, ancient, terrible.
>
> *"It has been a long time. And I am... so... hungry."*

- He lets the threat land. Watches what they do.
- Sniffs.
  - *"I smell the blood of Silmara."*
- Sniffs again. A pause — something genuine breaks through.
  - *"...No. There are two of them."*
- The pose drops. He resumes his elven form — gaunt, silver-robed, leaning on a gnarled staff. Curse-brand visible at the open collar.
  - *"I imagine you have many questions. As do I. Come."*

> [!info]- If the party reads him as hostile and acts first
> Praetor doesn't fight. He stands very still, lets the spell or arrow strike — it does nothing — and then, mildly: *"Are you finished?"* Then proceed.

---

### Scene 2: The Pool of Insight

Praetor leads the party deeper into the chamber. He bangs his staff once against the stone, and a wide circular dais rises out of the floor — its centre a deep dish filled with swirling silver liquid-light.

- *"Memory is unreliable. Especially mine — though I have had the time to learn its shape. To parse it, we must uncover the falsities first. Show me what you have done. I will see, and we will see together."*
- The Pool replays scenes from the party's journey. Each contains one deliberate error. Players call out the falsity aloud.
- This is the recycled **False Memories** material — frame Praetor as *learning* about them, not testing them. He is curious, occasionally amused, sometimes silent.
- No checks. No punishment for wrong guesses — the Pool simply doesn't react.

> [!check]+ New Memories — Lead With These
>
> **Memory: Ryo's home in the tree.** *Ryo improvises the falsity from his own backstory.* Ask him explicitly what is wrong with the vision; whatever he says becomes canon.
>
> **Memory: Eryndor as a child.** A village deep in the [[Whispering Woods]]. Eryndor wakes as a five-year-old. His mother is missing from the cottage. He runs out into the street, where there is a commotion of villagers gathered around something. *Eryndor improvises the falsity from his own backstory* — or you can suggest one tied to Eirieneth's disappearance if he stalls.

> [!info]- Selecting from Session 48
> All 16 prepared memory scenes from Session 48 are available — pick on the fly. Lead with the new two, then drift through the strongest emotional/character beats: Salla, Selune's ceremony, Blackmarsh restoring Korvas, Ayana's reaction to the ring, the prison ship, the Faewild rifts. Skip freely. Aim for 4–6 total beyond the new pair.

> [!info]- Praetor's reactions
> Vary them — slight tilt of the head when something interests him; a long silence after Salla; the ghost of a smile after Ellie & Lenny; nothing at all after Blackmarsh, which lands harder than commentary would.

### Scene 3: A Council in the Vault

Once the Pool is finished, Praetor extinguishes it with a gesture and turns to face them. The flippancy is gone.

> [!cite]+ Read Aloud
> *"You have been driven hard. By prophecy, by enemies, by your own choices. I would like, for a moment, to be the one asking. What is it you want?"*

This is the agency-restoring scene. Praetor's job is to ask, not answer. The players drive.

**Threads to surface — let players raise them; if stuck, Praetor probes:**

| Thread | Praetor's prompt |
|---|---|
| Sumara's politics | *"What will you do about the city above us?"* |
| [[Kaelen Vayne]] | *"The Blade is a man with a wound. What do you intend?"* |
| [[Ryo]]'s heritage | *"You wear the ring. Do you mean to wear the crown?"* |
| Eryndor's mother | *"There is a woman in a tower. The Empress holds her. What is she to you?"* |
| [[Kharazoth the Crimson Shadow\|Kharazoth]] | *"And the shadow that has not died — how do you intend to end him?"* |
| The Empire | *"What of the world above the Liar's River?"* |

> [!check]+ Big Reveal — How to Kill Kharazoth
> Surface this once the conversation reaches Kharazoth. Praetor leans on his staff.
>
> - Kharazoth is, in effect, immortal. He survived [[The Shattering]] through dark magic — soul bound to himself, no phylactery required. Wherever he is, he reforms.
> - **The path:** Praetor — who has carried Kharazoth's curse on his own scales for three centuries — knows the shape of the binding. He can guide the crafting of a vessel.
> - The vessel must **pierce his body**. A one-time-use weapon. The form is the party's choice — a sword, a spear, an arrow, a nail, anything that draws blood.
> - When the weapon strikes: a mortal's soul is bound to the vessel; Kharazoth's soul is bound to that mortal.
> - **Kharazoth dies permanently only if that mortal dies before his body reforms.**
> - Leave the question open. Praetor does not name the mortal. He looks at the party and waits.

> [!info]- Kaelen options — only if players are stuck
> Praetor offers these as suggestions, not directives. Float only what the conversation invites.
>
> - **The lost son.** Kaelen's son was taken by the [[Vale of Eternal Night]]. He may yet live. A rescue could turn the man.
> - **The soul-stone anchor.** The Unseelie of the Vale are rumoured to keep an ancient soul-stone — an artefact some old archfae was experimenting with long ago. Such a stone could form the seed of the vessel that kills Kharazoth.
> - **Pre-empting a coup.** Kaelen may move toward a military stand if the council shifts toward restoration. Engaging him before that happens — politically or personally — may be required.
> - **The combined quest.** A journey into the Vale that retrieves both his son *and* the soul-stone — solves two problems with one passage.

> [!info]- Praetor's posture throughout
> He does not push, does not direct. He listens. When asked directly what *he* would do, he says: *"I have had centuries to consider. The luxury of a long view is that one stops mistaking it for wisdom. The choice is yours."*

## Scenario 2: Reprieve

> [!summary]- Details
> - Where: [[Vault of Memories]] entrance → [[Sumara, The Shining City|Sumara]] / Royal Citadel
> - When: When the party signals they are ready to leave the Vault
> - Key NPCs: [[Ayana Syndrosa]] and [[Illythia Voss]] (via projection only)

### Scene 4: The Blue Orb

When the party reaches the Vault's entrance, a soft blue orb hangs in the air, slowly turning. As they approach, it unfolds — Ayana's image flickers into focus.

> [!cite]+ Read Aloud
> *"I have spoken with the Council. We will leave you be this evening — there will be no more questions tonight, no more demands. Illy and I will return to the Locus Arcanum. The Royal Citadel is yours; take whatever rooms you wish, eat, rest, talk. When you are ready — and only when you are ready — come and find us."*
>
> A pause. Her composure softens.
>
> *"I know what we have asked of you. What I have asked. Take the night."*

- The orb dims and drifts apart.
- This is permission, not a prompt. Make it explicit they have downtime — no clock, no urgency.

### Scene 5: The Royal Citadel

If the party stays in the Citadel for the evening, run it loose. No mechanics, no checks. Whatever they want.

- Quiet rooms. Balconies overlooking the city. Hot baths. Food. Wine. A library if anyone asks.
- Each player can take a moment — a private conversation, time alone, time with another PC, prayer, study, sleep.
- This is the breathing-room scene. Do not pull them back into plot unless they reach for it.

- **Emberblade**

> [!info]- If players ask for things
> The Citadel staff oblige quietly. Anything reasonable. No questions asked.
