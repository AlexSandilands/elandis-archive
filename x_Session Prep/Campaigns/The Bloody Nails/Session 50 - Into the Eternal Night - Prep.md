## Scenario 1: The Last Morning in Sumara

> [!summary]- Details
> - Where: [[Sumara, The Shining City|Sumara]] — Royal Citadel down through the Greensward
> - When: Morning after Session 49. Party rested, Level 10.
> - Key NPCs: Ferethis, [[Torgrun Brasswick]], Warden Sylvaris, [[Illythia Voss]]

### Scene 1: A Quiet Wake

- The Citadel staff have left fresh clothes, food, water. No one speaks unless spoken to.
- Free roleplay over breakfast — kitchens, balconies, gardens. PCs find each other or don't.
- A liveried elf brings [[Dondar]] a sealed envelope mid-morning: *Ferethis at the Ironweave has completed your order. At your leisure.*

### Scene 2: The Ironweave — Dondar's Items

**Ferethis** — lean, quiet elven smith; warms only to those who show genuine interest in craft.

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Fae Metal Headband (leather-bound) | Sits over a Rhun'zar; conceals Dondar's wizard focal point |
| Fae Metal Hairpin | Inscribed *"Hvedra"* in elvish — wrapped, no comment |

- Ferethis offers no questions about the inscription. He saw the name. He understood it was not his to ask.
- *"Faewild metal does not forget what it is given. Be careful what you say in front of it."*

### Scene 3: Torgrun's Curiosia — A Dwarf in the Garden

**[[Torgrun Brasswick]]** — dwarf, 180 years old; came to Sumara with the Exile rather than be parted from his elven wife. Hasn't seen another dwarf in a century.

> [!question] Trigger
> [[Dondar]] enters the shop.
> > [!info]- Action
> > Torgrun goes very still. *"Well. I'll be the deep roots of the mountain."* He pours two drinks. Waits.

- Torgrun asks after [[Glimmerforge]] specifically — that's where he's from. His voice will not crack until he does.
- He does not offer counsel; he's been away a century, and he knows it.
- If Dondar opens up at all: Torgrun quietly hands him a small piece of Glimmerforge stone he's kept by the till for a hundred years. *"In case you go back. Take a piece of here, leave a piece of there."*

### Scene 4: The Greensward — Berberis and the Meridian of the Root

The lowest tier of Sumara: bioluminescent terraces, glowing fungi, soil that smells of rain. The Nexus — the **Sanctum Canoporium** — is so overgrown the line between building and forest is gone.

**Warden Sylvaris** — Meridian of the Root, wilder than the other Meridians, suspicious of outsiders. Did *not* vote with Kaelen on opening the Vault.

> [!question] Trigger
> Berberis walks into the Greensward.
> > [!info]- Action
> > Sylvaris finds him within minutes. The conversation is not formal — they walk the terraces while it happens.

- Sylvaris has read the council records on Berberis. He's curious about a druid raised on the Material Plane.
- He probes: the Empire's blight, the dead at [[Val Noren]], the Faewild bleeding through the broken seams.

> [!check]+ The Quiet Offer
> If the conversation goes well, Sylvaris extends a tentative offer:
> - A standing welcome in the Greensward whenever Berberis returns.
> - A formal invitation, *when the time comes*, to walk the path of the Root — and possibly to stand as a junior voice within the Circle. Not now. *"When you have closed the wound that brought you here."*
> - He will not say it aloud, but: he sees Berberis as the kind of outsider voice the Greensward has been missing for a century. **Long-term hook — possible future Confluence seat.**

### Scene 5: Loose Ends

While Dondar and Berberis are anchored, the others can pursue their own threads. Free-form.

| Where | Who | What's there |
|---|---|---|
| [[The Diminished Trove]] | Arcanist Levvel | Shelves nearly bare; a scroll or two, minor curios. Will answer Vale lore questions at length. |
| The Mnemorium Canton | Archivist Elrohir Gemflower | Ancient, dry. Will brief them on the Long Hunt, [[Asha Vayne]], the Star-Veiled Queen. |
| The Fading Stars | Lyris Thornweave | Gossip. Mentions no Sumaran has volunteered to enter the Vale in living memory. |
| The Ironweave | Ferethis | Already covered if they came for Dondar's items. Faewild metal stock otherwise. |

### Scene 6: The Question of the Vale

When the party reconvenes, [[Illythia Voss]] meets them on the Bridge of Recollection. Composed, but the warmth is there.

**Vale briefing (Sumara's records):**

- The Vale lies in eternal night under [[Mirileth|the Star-Veiled Queen]], an archfae of unknown age.
- *The Hunt* — centaur riding-bands with dire worgs as trackers — patrol the boundaries. Fae Knights on nightmares for matters of honour.
- The denizens hold the Sumarans responsible for the darkness. Grievance traces to the Sundering, 2000 years ago.
- 60 years ago [[Asha Vayne]] — Kaelen's son — was killed in a border skirmish. A senior Hunt leader fell with him. *Some among the elves still believe he survived.*
- The party will not be welcomed. They will be **hunted**.

> [!cite]+ Read Aloud
> *"You will not pass quietly. The Hunt will find you — they always find someone, eventually. What you can choose is how, and where, and on what ground."*

> [!info]- The Cuffs of Gliding
> Sumara has no road to the Vale; the elevation drops a thousand feet from the caldera rim to the forest floor below. The standard method is a set of enchanted bracers — **Cuffs of Gliding** — that turn a fall into a controlled descent.
>
> **Acquisition options:**
> - **Buy outright** — 3,000gp per pair (Ironweave, or the Loom).
> - **Borrow with deposit** — coin or item of equivalent value, refunded on return.
> - **Convince the Citadel** — argue royal courtesy. Illythia will provide if the party makes the case (Ryo, the ring, the mission). She would prefer this; she does not say so unless asked.

## Scenario 2: The Crossing

> [!summary]- Details
> - Where: Northern descent from Sumara → forest → the threshold of the Vale
> - When: When the party departs
> - Key NPCs: None — wilderness travel

### Scene 7: Over the Edge

> [!cite]+ Read Aloud
> *The Cuffs warm at the wrist. The wind takes you — slow, fae-light, the prismatic spires of Sumara falling away behind. Ahead the forest unfolds for miles. The horizon goes dark earlier than it should.*

- Descent takes hours; then days of foot travel through the northern Faewild wilds.
- They pass out of Sumaran territory and into the dimming twilight that marks the Vale's outer reach.
- Time of day stops mattering. Light fades: gold to silver to ash to nothing.

### Scene 8: The Roc

Trigger at DM's discretion — **mid-glide** (Roc spots them descending against open sky) **or** later as a **travel hazard** (Roc patrols the canopy and catches their scent).

- The Roc is hunger, not vendetta. No tactics beyond grab-and-eat.
- **If mid-glide:** missed grabs send PCs into uncontrolled descent. Cuffs save the fall but burn a charge or require a DC 14 DEX save to re-stabilise.
- **If in the forest:** the Roc tears at the canopy from above. Ranged attackers only have line of sight when it dives.
- If the party breaks line of sight in heavy canopy, the Roc gives up. Not a chase to the death.

### Scene 9: The Whisper Wind

As they near the Vale proper, the air shifts. A breeze carries voices.

> [!cite]+ Read Aloud
> *"...should have been three of us..."* — *"...the lights, the lights..."* — *"...Asha?..."*

- Pick a fragment per player — something half-known to them, in a voice they almost recognise.
- No mechanical effect. Don't explain it. Let it sit.
- A known Vale hazard: sometimes useful, more often not.

## Scenario 3: The Vale

> [!summary]- Details
> - Where: Outer [[Vale of Eternal Night]], north toward [[Caer Nystral]]
> - When: After the party crosses the threshold
> - Key NPCs: The Hunt — Centaur Warden, Centaur Troopers, Dire Worg

### Scene 10: Across the Threshold

There is no marker. The party simply notices, at some point, that the sky above is full dark — not twilight. The trees have changed: silverleaf, blackthorn, phosphor-fungi glowing faint green at the base of every trunk.

> [!example]+ Description
> *The trees grow taller here. Silver leaves catch no light but reflect every movement. Glowing fungi line the trails — green where the path is true, blue where it is not. The horns of the Hunt, if they sound, will sound long before they are close.*

**Hazards en route — random or set, as needed:**

| Hazard | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Nightshroud Fungus** | Black spore-puff. Disturbance triggers a low burst of necrotic damage in a 20ft cloud. Withering, not fire. *(Reflavour of Fireball Fungus.)* |
| **Darkness Pits** | Voids in the forest floor disguised as ordinary shadow. A creature stepping in falls 30ft into a still, lightless dark — restraining, not crushing. *(Reflavour of Quicksand pits.)* |
| **Whisper Wind** | Already used in Scene 9; may recur as ambient flavour. |

### Scene 11: The Hunt Closes

The Hunt **will** find them. Stealth determines how — not whether.

> [!check]+ Stealth shapes the encounter
> Group stealth check (DC 16, scaling with terrain).
> - **Success by 5+:** Party picks the ground. Surprise round on the Hunt. Choose a defensible cleft or thicket.
> - **Success:** Even start. Standard initiative, no surprise.
> - **Failure:** Hunt has surprise. Party caught in open ground.
> - **Failure by 5+:** Hunt rides them down at speed. Surprise round + Troopers open with **Trampling Charge** while the Warden directs.

**Encounter composition:**

| Enemy | Count | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Centaur Warden** | 1 | Commander. Magic-touched; sees through illusion. Won't fall for misdirection. |
| **Centaur Trooper** | 5 | Heavy cavalry. Open ground favours them; trees and rough terrain do not. |
| **Dire Worg** | 1 | Tracker. Pack tactics with the troopers. The reason they were found. |

> [!info]- The Warden's voice
> Refuses parley *before* combat. After — if the party is winning and offers — they may listen.
> *"You are far from your shining city, elf-friends. What did you imagine waited for you here?"*

> [!info]- Asha Vayne thread (post-combat)
> If asked about Kaelen's son, the Warden gives no direct answer. But goes still for a half-beat. *"That was a long time ago. The Hunt remembers what it kills."* The hesitation is the tell.

### Scene 12: After the Hunt

If the party wins, they have a window. Bands rotate; another will find them eventually, but not at once.

- Bodies, gear, the Warden's **signal-horn** on the field. Horn could be forged into a Hunt signal later.
- The Worg's collar bears the Queen's mark: a stylised antler over a downturned crescent.
- **Soft hook for next session:** [[Caer Nystral]], *The Dawnless*, lies further north. The soulstone anchor is held in the most secure vault of its Citadel.

> [!info]- Pacing
> If the Hunt fight runs long and the party is bloodied, **end the session here** — they hold the ground, the next horn has not yet sounded, the Vale presses in. If there's still time, push forward into the deep forest and let the size of the land they're in start to land.

## Notes

- **Pacing target:** Sumara ~half the session. Crossing + Vale entry + Hunt fight the second half. End at the Hunt's defeat or the start of the deeper Vale.
- **Asha Vayne is a thread, not a beat.** Surface in Elrohir's briefing OR Illythia's debrief. Surface again as a half-tell from the Warden post-combat. Don't push.
- **Cuffs of Gliding is a character moment.** Whether they buy, borrow, or ask is the first time they exercise the weight of the council's recognition. Let the choice land.
- **Sylvaris's offer to Berberis** is long-term — possible future Confluence seat. Plant; don't name it loudly.
- **The Hunt is not a wipe.** Warden is the threat; troopers are bodies. If the party is in trouble, the Warden withdraws to gather a larger band (escalation for later).
- **Praetor's advice in S49 was: consult the Sumarans before going.** The morning round honours that.
- **Caer Nystral and the soulstone are next-session material.** Don't rush.
