## Soldiers on the Road

### Setting the Scene

Location: A wide, open road stretches ahead of the players, with fields and low grassland on either side. The weather is clear, the sun high, casting long shadows.

Time of Day: Early afternoon.

### Noticing the Regiment

As the players travel along the road, their journey is interrupted by the distant sound of disciplined marching and the clinking of armor. The encounter begins with a perception check to notice the regiment before they are too close.

Perception Check:

- DC 10: Players hear the rhythmic sound of marching soldiers in the distance. They see a dust cloud rising far ahead on the road.
- DC 15: Players can distinguish the individual figures of the soldiers. They notice the glint of sunlight on armor and weapons.
- DC 20: Players spot the "Hands of the Empire," the five captains on horseback, leading the regiment. They can make out the empire's banner and possibly overhear snatches of orders or conversation from the regiment.

### Encounter Description

As the players continue, they see the regiment clearly now. One hundred soldiers in well-maintained armor march in unison, led by five captains on horseback, the "Hands of the Empire." These captains are distinguished by their ornate armor and the imperial insignia they bear. The regiment is moving steadily toward the players' direction.

The sight is imposing: a display of military power and discipline, moving with a purpose that speaks of control and authority.

### Options for the Players

1. Avoiding the Encounter:
	- Given the open nature of the area, avoiding the regiment is challenging but possible. The players must act quickly to find a solution.
	- Stealth Check:
		- Group Stealth Check (DC 20): Players can try to hide in the tall grass or any minor undulations in the terrain. Success means the regiment marches past without noticing the players. Failure results in the regiment spotting them as they attempt to hide. 

2. Interacting with the Regiment:
	- Parley: If the players decide to confront or talk to the regiment, they may need to think quickly and choose their words carefully.
	- Persuasion/Deception Check:
		- DC 15: The players can convince the captains they are harmless travelers or even that they are on a mission of their own for the Empire.
		- DC 20: The players can persuade the captains to share information about their destination or purpose.
	- Intimidation Check:
		- DC 20: The players can attempt to intimidate the soldiers into letting them pass unchallenged, but this could lead to increased tension and potential conflict if the check fails. 

3. Combat:
	- Engaging the Regiment: Engaging the regiment in direct combat is highly dangerous. The players should consider their options carefully as they are outnumbered and outmatched.
	- The captains, "Hands of the Empire," are formidable foes and will lead the soldiers efficiently. The encounter could become a deadly battle if not handled wisely.

### Outcomes and Consequences

- Avoidance: If the players successfully avoid the regiment, they can continue their journey without delay or conflict. They might overhear information about the regiment’s purpose if they listen closely during the stealth check. 
- Successful Interaction: A successful interaction can yield useful information or even assistance, depending on the players' approach and the captains’ disposition. 
- Failed Interaction: Stripped of possessions and taken as prisoners back to Camaar 
- Combat: If combat ensues, the players will face a daunting challenge. The "Hands of the Empire" captains will coordinate their forces effectively, potentially leading to a tactical retreat or a dire battle for the players.

### Soldiers

#### Captain Aurelia Varis

- Appearance: Aurelia is a tall, imposing woman with short, cropped black hair and piercing green eyes. She wears ornate plate armor adorned with the Empire's crest and rides a black warhorse. Her demeanor is stern and commanding, and she moves with the confidence of a seasoned warrior.
- Personality: Often speaks in short, direct sentences. She values efficiency and may quickly assess the players’ strengths and weaknesses.  

#### Captain Darius Faelthorn

- Appearance: Darius is a rugged, muscular man with a mane of curly brown hair and a thick beard. His hazel eyes are sharp and constantly scan the surroundings. He wears practical, well-worn armor and rides a sturdy brown horse. He carries a large, decorated shield strapped to his back.
- Personality: Uses a gruff, no-nonsense tone. He appreciates bravery and directness in others. 

#### Captain Lyra Kellen

- Appearance: Lyra is a slender, agile woman with long, flowing auburn hair and keen blue eyes. She wears a lighter set of armor designed for mobility and speed, and rides a swift, grey horse. Her demeanor is calm and observant, often assessing situations with a quick glance.
- Personality: Speaks softly but with purpose. She is more likely to ask questions than to give orders. 

#### Captain Thaddeus Voss

- Appearance: Thaddeus is a tall, lean man with neatly trimmed blond hair and a goatee. His grey eyes are calculating and often reflect a hint of amusement. He wears polished armor that gleams in the sunlight and rides a white stallion. He has a charismatic presence and a smile that rarely fades.
- Personality: Often speaks with a charming smile and uses elaborate language. He enjoys verbal sparring and diplomacy. 

#### Captain Sabina Rook

- Appearance: Sabina is a sturdy, middle-aged woman with short, greying hair and intense brown eyes. She has a scar running down her left cheek, a mark of her many battles. She wears functional, no-nonsense armor and rides a strong, dark bay horse. Her expression is usually serious, reflecting her no-nonsense attitude.
- Personality: Speaks bluntly and to the point. She focuses on the practical aspects of any situation.
