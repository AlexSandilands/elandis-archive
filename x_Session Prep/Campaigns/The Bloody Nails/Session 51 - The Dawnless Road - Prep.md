## Scene 1 — The Locus Arcanum

**Where** [[Locus Arcanum]], Sumara · **Opens** exactly where Session 50 ended · **Goal** close out Sumara, kit up, set the approach.

- Morning after. [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana]] is off-duty for the first time in forty years; [[Illythia Voss|Illythia]] is here. Warm, unhurried, dressing-gown-and-wine.
- The party collects **five Zephyr Cuffs** from Ayana's contact (rent or buy).
- Ayana and Illythia tried to scry [[Eirieneth Silmara|Eirieneth]] and hit a wall — her binding-cuffs suppress magic, so divination slides off her. The honest read: **alive and held close**, not lost. *(Ayana cast Eirieneth's original silver-to-black hair glamour — this is personal for her.)*
- Threads that may surface: whether to tell the [[Sumara, The Shining City#The Confluence of the Seven|Confluence]] about Eirieneth, or declaring [[Ryo]] regent before they leave. Improvise these if they come up.

**[[Ayana Syndrosa]]** — Confluence agent; finally relaxed, the cuffs and the scry are hers.
**[[Illythia Voss]]** — Ayana's partner; dry, at ease, asides from the kitchen.

#### Zephyr Cuffs
`Worn at wrists + ankles · charge with a 2nd-level slot (lasts the day)`
- While charged, **glide from any height** — no fall damage, drift forward as you descend.
- The glide **ends** if you're knocked prone, restrained, or dropped to 0 movement mid-air.
- This is the safety valve for the Roc (Scene 2): a cuffed PC who falls just glides down.

## Scene 2 — The Descent & The Crossing

**Where** the caldera rim down into the [[Vale of Eternal Night|Vale]] · **After** they leave Sumara · **Goal** get them across to the deep-Vale road, with the Roc beat and the night-crossing en route.

- They pick a way down off the caldera — their call:

| Route | Feel | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Glide off the rim (cuffs) | Fast, dramatic — the intended way | Showcases the cuffs; sets up the Roc mid-air |
| Overland over the bridge past [[Grumble, The Anniahilator\|Grumble]] | A day or two, near [[Liar's River]] | Chess-game callback — waves them through, or wants one more game |
| Hug the inner bank near the [[Shimmering Peaks]] | Slower, safer | Delays the crossing |

- The **Roc** finds them somewhere on the descent (any route) — a hungry snatch, not a fight.
- Past the threshold, the warmth of Sumara just stops. The **night-crossing** begins

> [!cite]+ Read Aloud — crossing the threshold *(optional)*
> *The warmth of Sumara thins behind you, and then it is simply gone. The canopy closes; the moons do not follow. Ahead the trees stand black against a darker black, and the air tastes of cold iron and old roots. You have crossed into the night that never ends.*

#### The Roc — non-combat snatch
Hungry CR 11 raptor. Wants a meal, not a fight.
`AC 15 · HP 248 · fly 120 · Talon +13 (grapple, escape DC 19)`

**Altitude clock** (its initiative):

| Round | Height | Fall if dropped |
|---|---|---|
| Grab | ~30 ft | 3d6 (survivable) |
| +1 | ~120 ft | 12d6 (clears the canopy) |
| +2 | gone | long fall |

**Ways out**
- **Break the grapple** — grabbed PC, DC 19 Athletics or Acrobatics → free, then falls (see clock).
- **Force a drop** — 30+ damage in one round (CON 13 to cling; auto-drops on a crit, or a radiant/fire burst).
- **Drive it off** — 50+ damage in one round, *or* fire / thunder / bright Faewild light → WIS 13 or frightened. In canopy, simply breaking line of sight ends the chase.
- **Safety valve** — a cuffed PC who falls just glides down safe, so a mid-glide snatch is tense, not lethal.

#### Crossing the Vale — night navigation
*Proposed DCs — tune to taste.* The road is half-swallowed and the dark eats landmarks, so the crossing is a navigation challenge, not a straight march.
`Per leg: navigator rolls DC 15 Wisdom (Survival) · others may Help`

- **The light trade-off:** carry light → **advantage** on the check, but the Hunt and the Court's eyes spot them sooner / on worse terms. Travel dark → no advantage, slower, but unseen.
- **Success** — advance a leg, no hazard.
- **Failure** — lose time and trigger a hazard (roll/pick below).
- **Fail by 5+** — they stray toward a watched chokepoint; the Hunt finds them on the *Hunt's* terms (feeds Scene 3).

## Scene 3B — The Barrow-Ring & the Crone *(Hardliner branch)*

**Where** the **Barrow-Ring** — a heath of old burial mounds off the deep-Vale road · **Reached if** the Hardliner band marches them off to be "dealt with" (Scene 3) · **Goal** the floor drops out: disarmed, deep in the dark, and *sold* to something older than the Court. Plant a new player against the Queen.

> [!cite]+ Read Aloud — the Ring
> *The road gives out onto a heath of humped, grass-grown mounds — nine of them in a slow, sagging ring, each crowned with a leaning stone and a snarl of antlers gone grey with the years. At the centre, the largest barrow gapes: a low mouth of turf and root, framed by ribs of pale bone, and somewhere down inside it a fire is burning green and throwing no warmth at all. The Hunt does not take you in. They cut your bonds, set your weapons in a heap at the threshold — and leave you there.*

**The hand-off.** The hardliners don't bloody their own hands. They march the party to the Ring and *hand them over* — bound, weaponless — to the thing that lives in the central mound, the way they've quietly handed over every inconvenient body the Court would rather forget. To the band this is disposal; a tithe paid, a problem buried. **What they don't know is that she stopped being their servant a long time ago** — and that a *living Silmara* changes everything the moment the hood comes off.

#### Mallt Sootmarrow — the Barrow-Crone
The crone of the Ring; a Vale-hag who was old when the Antlered Queen was new. *(A nod to Mallt-y-Nos of the Hunt — fitting.)* Coven-less but lair-deep: the barrow answers her.
`Lair-empowered hag · AC 17 · HP ~135 · Int/Cha caster · effectively CR ~9 inside her Ring`
- **Two faces** — appears as a bent, sloe-eyed old woman *or*, when it suits her, a tall lady crowned in dead antlers. She chooses which you see.
- **Knows you** — reads surface thoughts and tastes fears; this is where her "I know exactly what you are, little Silmara" punch comes from.
- **Barrow-wights** — 3–6 of the original mound-dead rise from the alcoves on her word *(use Wight / Specter stats)*. Bound to the Ring; they do not pursue past the stones.
- **The Ring holds** — anyone who bolts onto the heath finds it loops back: same mounds, same fire, the dark folding them home. A *soft* cage (illusion/short teleport), meant to make the first "just run" **fail** and land the dread — not a hard wall forever.
- **Don't let a stab solve her.** In her own barrow treat her as functionally legendary — resists, guises, the wights between her and the party. A straight fight here is a loss; the scene is a *negotiation at knifepoint*.

#### The barrow — her hall
- A low crawl through turf and root opens into a long earthen hall, the ceiling a net of live roots, the air cold with crushed sloe and old soil.
- **Green witchfire** in a broken stone hearth — light, never heat. Shadows move wrong.
- Walls lined with **burial alcoves**, the barrow-dead still in them (her wights-in-waiting). Strung between: charms of finger-bones, milk-teeth, antler-tine, and trinkets she scries through.
- At the head, the shattered **bier of a barrow-king** serves as her seat.

#### Disarmed & screwed — let it land
Count it out for the table so the helplessness is *felt*, not assumed:
- **Weapons, focuses, components** — stripped on the road, now heaped at the barrow mouth under her eye and warded with bone-charms. Reach for them and the wights stir.
- **Casters** — between the wards, the wights, and how fast she is, there's no clean turn to fight out of. (Tie it to the Court's magic-suppressing binding if you want the echo of [[Eirieneth Silmara|Eirieneth's]] cuffs.)
- **Zephyr Cuffs** — she's *curious* about the fae-work and lets them keep them; it costs her nothing, because down here there's nothing to glide off and the Ring won't let them walk out. (Quietly leaves a thread for later.)
- **The beat:** no weapons, no clean casting, surrounded, miles deep in the night that never ends. *You are not getting out of this room by force.* Then she speaks.

#### What Mallt wants — the turn
- She has **no love for the Antlered Queen** — an old grievance. She was of the Vale *before* the Court; now the Hunt feeds her, but only scraps, only on the Queen's terms, and she is bone-tired of it.
- A breathing **Silmara** is the first real card she's held in an age. She will **not** waste that on a quiet killing.
- Her play: use the Silmara as **leverage** — a chip to work *against the Queen*, and a thing to **dangle in front of Sumara**. Hand them back for a price, threaten to, sell the secret of where Sumara's lost Silmara is — every road opens once she's holding one.
- So she **countermands the hardliners**: *these are mine now.* The band either yields (she has a hold over them) or this becomes the night a hag and a hunt-band fall out over the party — chaos the party can use.

#### Playing her / the outs
- She's **not** a rescuer — she's a worse captor with a *use* for them, which is exactly why they stay alive and whole. That's the lever they bargain on.
- **Bargain** — fed, unharmed, *valuable*. Buys time and information; she'll talk, she likes an audience.
- **Her price** — she wants something carried, a promise sworn in the old way, or what they know of Sumara's court. Quest hook, on her terms.
- **Escape seed** — wights are Ring-bound; the hardliner band outside is now rattled (she took their prize); the Cuffs + a distraction = a run for the dark.
- **The thread** — she sends a finger-bone token off toward Caer Nystral *or* toward Sumara to open her "negotiation." However the scene ends, **Mallt is a recurring third power in the Vale now** — one who hates the Queen and has held a Silmara.
