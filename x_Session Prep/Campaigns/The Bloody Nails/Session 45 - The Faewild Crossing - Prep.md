## The Gossamer Woods

> [!summary]+ Details
> 
> - Landing zone in the Faewild (Court of Morning Dew).
> - Golden hour lighting, candy-floss trees, giant glowing mushrooms.
> - Air smells of vanilla and ozone; ground is springy like a mattress.

### The Pixie Trap

> [!question] Trigger
> Small pixie nervously waves from behind a leaf and beckons someone over
> 
> > [!info]- Action
> > 
> > - [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana]] steps in aggressively to swat it away and takes the full hit.
> >     
> > - **Effect:** Concentrated _Polymorph_ + _Confusion_. She physically reverts to a 6-to-8-year-old child.
> > - She is extremely confused, joyful, playful and doesn't understand most questions
> > - High-level spells misfire into soap bubbles, confetti, or turn rocks into teacups.
> > - Cannot be dispelled normally; the party must now babysit her.

### Encounter: The Möbius Thicket

- The party enters a clearing with a massive glowing tree stump (like a sundial) and a still pond.
- Leaving the clearing for 10-15 mins in _any_ direction results in them seamlessly stepping back into the exact same clearing.

> [!info]+ Clues & Escalation
> 
> 1. **Fauna:** A glowing beetle or sprite flies through the clearing entirely _backwards_.
> 2. **Physics:** Dropping a stone in the pond makes ripples go _inward_.
> 3. **Reflections:** Player reflections face away from them.
> 4. **Tracks:** Investigation/Survival reveals their own tracks leaving the clearing are facing backward (heel-first).

> [!info]+ Solution
> 
> - The party must face the glowing stump and **walk backwards** out of the clearing into the brush.
> - Must maintain this for a few minutes without turning around to break the spatial loop.

## The Briarshade

> [!summary]+ Details
> 
> - Court of Fallen Leaves (Unseelie). Gnarled trees, grasping vines, creeping dread.
> - **The Greyscale Curse:** The entire biome is leached of color. It is heavily obscured in magical, monochrome gloom.
> - **Blindness:** Standard and magical _Darkvision_ fail completely here. The darkness feels heavy, like ink.

### Travel mechanics: The Darkness

> [!Danger] Navigating Without light
> - Travel speed is reduced to **1/4 pace**.
> - The navigator must make a **DC 18 Survival check** every hour.
> - _Failure:_ The party becomes lost for 1d4 hours and triggers a random Unseelie encounter (Boggles, Redcaps, or Meenlocks) with the enemy having advantage on stealth.

### The Light Trap: Living Shadows

If the players create any light source (torch, lantern, _Light_ spell) to see, it cuts through the gloom—but triggers the biome's trap.

> [!info]+ The Shadow Spawn Mechanic
> 
> - **The Trigger:** Any hard shadow cast by a mortal light source detaches from the ground/trees and becomes a hostile **Living Shadow** (Use standard Shadow stats, but give them 1 HP like a 4e Minion).
> - **The Swarm:** For every light source ignited, 1d4 + 1 Shadows peel off the environment every 10 minutes of travel.
> - **Destroying Them:** A player can use an Action or Bonus Action to shine their light directly on a Shadow. The intense direct light instantly destroys the 1 HP Shadow.
> - **The Catch (The Loop):** Shining a directional light to destroy a Shadow causes the light to swing, casting _new_ hard shadows behind the player or against the trees. Destroying a shadow this way instantly spawns **1d2 new Shadows** at the edge of the light's radius.

> [!Danger] Combat:
> 
> - Every time a Shadow hits a player, it deals 1d4 Necrotic damage and drains **1 point of Strength**.
> - Strength drain recovers only on a Short or Long Rest. If they get bogged down, they will physically weaken until they are paralyzed and consumed.

### Puzzle Solutions: How to Travel Safely

To survive the Briarshade, the players must figure out how to navigate without casting "hard" shadows.

> [!check]+ Solution 1: Cross-Illumination
> 
> - **The Logic:** If you have multiple light sources overlapping from different angles, you wash out the shadows cast by the objects between them.
> - **The Execution:** The party must ignite **3 or more light sources** and walk in a strict geometric formation (like a triangle or square) with the lights on the outside.
> - **The Result:** They cast no hard shadows outward. They can travel at half-pace safely, but if their formation breaks (someone trips, or they have to squeeze single-file), shadows will instantly spawn.

> [!check]+ Solution 2: Diffused Light
> 
> - **The Logic:** Bright light casts hard shadows; dim, diffused light casts soft, fuzzy shadows that cannot "detach."
> - **The Execution:** A player wraps a lantern or a _Light_ spell in a thick cloak or paints over the glass with mud, allowing only a 5-foot radius of Dim Light to bleed through.
> - **The Result:** They avoid spawning monsters and can see just enough to travel, reducing the Navigation check to a manageable DC 13.

> [!check]+ Solution 3: Fey Bioluminescence
> 
> - **The Logic:** Mortal light creates mortal shadows. Fae light plays by Fae rules.
> - **The Execution:** A player harvests the sickly glowing fungi or the glowing black sap native to the Briarshade and uses it as a torch.
> - **The Result:** Because the light belongs to the biome, it does not agitate the shadows. They can travel safely, though the light may occasionally whisper to them (causing minor creepy roleplay moments).

### Random Encounters: The Briarshade

> [!tip] DM Note:
> How to use these
> - Roll a d6 every hour of travel, or trigger one automatically if the navigator fails their DC 18 Survival check. These are designed to test the party's nerves and potentially give them hints for the shadow puzzle.

#### 1. The Toll of Color (Social / Bargain)

Because the Briarshade is entirely greyscale, vibrant colors are a highly prized, almost narcotic commodity for the local fey.

> [!info]+ Encounter: Grin the Pale
> 
> - **The Setup:** Sitting on a petrified, thorny log blocking the only clear path is a Meenlock (or a twisted, pale goblin-like fey) wearing a tattered grey top hat. He introduces himself as "[[Grinn, The Pale|Grinn]]."
> - **The Interaction:** Grinn doesn't want to fight; he knows the shadows will eat the party eventually anyway. He politely demands a "Toll of Color" to let them pass safely and point them in the right direction.
> - **The Cost:** A player must willingly give up something vibrantly colored.
>     - _Physical:_ If they hand over a colorful item (a red cloak, a painted shield), Grinn touches it, draining the color until it is dull grey, and eats the extracted pigment like candy.
>     - _Mental:_ If they have no items, he asks for a "colorful memory." The player must narrate a happy memory. Grinn inhales it. The player keeps the factual memory, but permanently loses the emotional warmth attached to it (great for roleplay).
> - **The Reward:** Grinn honors the deal. He steps aside and gives them flawless directions, granting **Advantage on the next two Navigation Survival checks**.

#### 2. The Echoing Thicket (Psychological Hazard)

This encounter leans into the lore that the Briarshade mimics familiar voices to separate the party and break their light formations.

> [!Danger]+ Encounter: The Voices in the Dark
> 
> - **The Setup:** As they squeeze through a dense patch of thorny vines, the wind dies completely. Suddenly, from the pitch-black woods just beyond their sightline, they hear a familiar voice crying out for help.
> - **The Hook:** Tailor this to the characters. They might hear [[King Marius Noren]] yelling for a medic, [[Illaris Sharin]] screaming Kai's name, or a child from [[Val Noren]] sobbing.
> - **The Mechanic:** Everyone must make a **DC 15 Wisdom Saving Throw**.
>     - _Success:_ They recognize the voice is slightly off—a perfect mimicry, but lacking a soul. They can ignore it.
>     - _Failure:_ The player is magically compelled to step toward the voice. They use their reaction/movement to wander 15 feet into the dark, immediately breaking whatever "light formation" they were using to suppress the shadows.
>     - _Consequence:_ If a light formation is broken, the shadows instantly detach and swipe at the isolated player (automatic 1d4 necrotic damage and 1 Strength drain) before the party can pull them back.

#### 3. The Weeping Treant (Resource / Puzzle Hint)

This functions as an interactive set-piece that can actively help the party solve the overarching "Living Shadow" puzzle if they are struggling.

> [!check]+ Encounter: The Bleeding Bark
> 
> - **The Setup:** The party finds a massive, ancient Treant that has been partially petrified by the Unseelie magic. It is trapped in the greyscale gloom, weeping thick, glowing black sap from deep gashes in its bark.
> - **The Interaction:** The Treant communicates via weak, localized telepathy. It is freezing and in agony. It begs the party for "warmth," but warns them that "the hard light bites."
> - **The Puzzle:** The party must figure out how to warm the Treant _without_ casting a hard shadow (which would spawn shadow-monsters that attack the tree).
>     - _Solutions:_ They could use body heat (hugging the tree), wrap it in bedrolls, use a heavily muffled/diffused magical heat source, or cast a spell like _Prestidigitation_ to simply warm the wood directly.
> - **The Reward:** If warmed safely, the Treant sighs in relief. A large chunk of the glowing black sap solidifies and drops to the ground. This gives the party a piece of **Fey Bioluminescence**—a light source that does _not_ cast hard mortal shadows, perfectly solving their travel mechanic for the rest of the biome!

## The Reveler's Glade

> [!summary]+ Details
> 
> - Court of the High Sun (Seelie). Hyper-saturated colors, sweltering heat, bioluminescent jungle.
> - **The Seelie Curse:** The biome is aggressively hospitable. Refusing a gift or a dance is a grave insult, and the air itself is intoxicating.
> - **Emotional Amplification:** All emotions are magically magnified tenfold.

### Travel Mechanics: The Euphoria Spores

> [!Danger] Navigating the Overgrowth
> 
> - **The Trap:** Using slashing weapons or fire on the flora bursts the spore pods.
> - **The Save:** If pods are burst, or if the party travels at a normal/fast pace, everyone must make a **DC 16 Constitution saving throw** every hour.
> - _Failure:_ The character becomes Intoxicated (applies the **Poisoned** condition, but flavor it as an overwhelming, reckless euphoria). They have disadvantage on all Wisdom and Intelligence checks.
> - _Complication:_ Intoxicated characters will refuse to take the "boring" or safe path, forcing the party to navigate dangerous terrain or delay their travel to corral them.

### Puzzle Solutions: How to Travel Safely

To survive the Glade's atmosphere, the party must figure out how to navigate without breathing the spores or agitating the flora.

> [!check]+ Solution 1:
> Filtration
> - Tying wet cloths over their mouths and noses (or using spells like _Create Water_ to dampen masks) grants Advantage on the Con saves, but they must still move at a Slow pace. [!check]+ Solution 2: The Gentle Path Using blunt weapons to gently push vines aside, or using cold/necrotic magic to quietly wither the plants rather than violently chopping them, prevents the spores from releasing entirely.

### Encounters: The Reveler's Glade

#### 1. The Feywild Tea Party (Social / Etiquette Puzzle)

The party arrives at a beautiful, unbreakable crystal bridge spanning a deep gorge. The entire bridge is taken up by an opulent, never-ending tea party hosted by a Summer Eladrin Noble. They cannot pass without joining, but Fey etiquette is a minefield.

> [!info]+ Encounter: The Obligation of Tea
> 
> - **The Host:** Lord Veren of the High Sun. He insists they sit, eat, and converse before crossing.
> - **The Rule:** You cannot say "Thank You" (it implies a debt in the Faewild), you cannot refuse food directly (it is an insult), but eating the food charms you into staying forever.
> - **Mechanic: Social Progress Clock**
>     - _Progress Clock (4):_ They successfully navigate the etiquette and are allowed to cross.
>     - _Consequence Clock (4):_ Lord Veren is insulted, or a player falls under the food's spell.
> 
> **Actions to push the clock (DC 15 Charisma/Insight/Deception):**
> - _15+ (+1 Progress):_ Complimenting the host lavishly without saying "thank you."
> - _20+ (+2 Progress):_ Successfully sleight-of-handing the food into a pocket without eating it, or offering a "gift" of equal conceptual value (like a beautiful song or a secret) to bypass the meal.
> - _< 15 (+1 Consequence):_ Saying the words "Thank You" or directly refusing a cup of tea.
> - _< 10 (+2 Consequence):_ Getting frustrated and attempting to flip the table or threaten the host.
> 
> **Failure Consequence:** If the Consequence clock fills, Veren demands a "Toll of Joy." The party must permanently surrender a happy memory, or suffer **1 level of Exhaustion** from being magically forced to dance a frantic jig for an hour before being dismissed.

#### 2. The Wall of Vexation (Psychological / Roleplay Hazard)

This leans entirely into the Glade's "Emotional Amplification" mechanic.

> [!Danger]+ Encounter: The Emotional Barricade
> 
> - **The Setup:** The path narrows between two massive, impassable cliffs. Blocking the path is a shimmering, semi-transparent wall of glowing pink thorns.
> - **The Interaction:** Striking the thorns with weapons or magic causes the wall to instantly reflect the damage back as Psychic damage.
> - **The Puzzle:** The wall feeds on repressed emotion. Because the Glade forces everything to the surface, the wall acts as a pressure valve. It will only open if a character genuinely unleashes a strong, repressed emotion.
> - **The Solution:** A player must roleplay a genuine outburst. They must confess a secret to the party, yell out a frustration they’ve been holding onto, or express profound, unfiltered grief (perhaps Ryo regarding his parents, or Ayana in her child-state throwing a magical tantrum about her lost power).
> - _Mechanic:_ If the roleplay is genuine, the wall shatters into harmless flower petals. If they try to fake it (Deception check), the wall lashes out, dealing 2d6 psychic damage.

#### 3. The Riptide Parade (Mobility Complication)

This is an environmental hazard disguised as a party.

> [!check]+ Encounter: The Stampede of Satyrs
> 
> - **The Setup:** A massive, roaring parade of Satyrs, Centaurs, and enchanted beasts sweeps horizontally across the party's path. It is a literal current of bodies, hundreds deep, moving at incredible speed while playing drums and panpipes.
> - **The Hazard:** Waiting for it to pass will take days (it's a Feywild parade; it doesn't end). Trying to just walk through it requires a **DC 17 Strength (Athletics) check**. On a failure, the character is swept up in the crowd, taking 2d6 bludgeoning damage from the trampling hooves, and is carried 60 feet in the wrong direction.
> - **The Solution (Lateral Thinking):** > - _Join the Band:_ A character can make a high Performance check to start playing an instrument or singing a wildly different tempo. The crowd, distracted by the new tune, parts to surround the player, allowing the party to walk through the "eye of the storm."
>     - _Illusion:_ Casting a massive illusion of a legendary Archfey or an even bigger party off to the side will cause the parade to abruptly veer off course, clearing the path.

## The Shimmering Peaks

> [!summary]+ Details
> 
> - Buffer zone to the Elven City. Deep indigo sky, shifting auroras.
> - A massive circular mountain range of white stone.
> - A roaring river of liquid starlight flows in a perpetual loop around the base of the mountains.

### The River Wards

> [!info]+ Spatial Distortion
> 
> - **Water:** Touching the water summons starlight elementals that drag them under (Str/Athletics save).
> - **Distance:** Flying over or walking along the bank to bypass the bridge causes the mountains to recede into the distance.
> - **Solution:** They must stand directly in front of the ancient stone bridge to cross.

### The Bridge Warden

- **Grumble the Annihilator:** 12ft obsidian troll in dark robes, hood up. Booming voice.
- Slams his fist and demands they complete the "Ancient Rites" or face "dire consequences."
	- Permanently banished from the [[Shimmering Peaks]]
	- Soul taken forever

> [!info]+ The Game
> 
> - Grumble is actually very friendly and just bored. The "Rites" are just a game.

### The Pieces (White Marble vs. Obsidian)

> [!info]+ Pawns: Goblin Warriors
> 
> - **Chess Move:** 1 square forward (or 2 on first move). Captures 1 square diagonally forward.
> - **D&D Action:** _Javelin Throw._ Range: 3 squares (60 ft)

> [!info]+ Knights: Blink Dogs
> 
> - **Chess Move:** "L" shape (2 squares one direction, 1 square perpendicular). Jumps over other pieces.
> - **D&D Action:** _Fey Step_. Instead of a chess capture, the Blink Dog teleports to _any_ unoccupied square within 2 squares of it and makes a Bite attack against an adjacent piece.

> [!info]+ Bishops: Priests
> 
> - **Chess Move:** Any number of squares diagonally.
> - **D&D Action:** _Guiding Bolt._ Range: 6 squares (120 ft). Bounces to one chosen adjacent piece

> [!info]+ Rooks: Stone Golem (Rolling Boulders)
> 
> - **Chess Move:** Any number of squares horizontally or vertically.
> - **D&D Action:** _Rolling Charge._ The Rook moves in a straight line up to 3 squares destroying any piece in the way (including allies!)

> [!info]+ Queen: Hag
> 
> - **Chess Move:** Any number of squares in any direction.
> - **D&D Action:** _Lightning Bolt._ Range: 6 squares (100 ft) in a straight line. Destroys all pieces (including allies)

> [!info]+ King: Commander
> 
> - **Appearance:** A heavily armored, regal statue with a massive crown.
> - **Chess Move:** 1 square in any direction.
> - **D&D Action:**: Is immune to D&D actions from all other pieces
