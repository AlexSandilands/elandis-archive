### Scene 1: The Guardian Chamber

*Continues from cliffhanger — the stone construct's red eyes have just ignited.*

**WoW-style raid boss. Immune by default; players must sacrifice HP to open damage windows.**

> [!cite]+ Read Aloud
> *The construct rises to its full height — fifteen feet of ancient stone, fists larger than your torso. Its eyes burn a deep, furious red. The four pillars around the room hum, and a beam of searing light crackles to life between two of them.*

**Phase 1 Mechanics:**

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| Pylons | 4 in room. Laser fires between 2 at top of each round, rotating counter-clockwise |
| Golem Fixate | Roll **d10** each round — see fixate table below. Moves toward target, attacks, casts **Slow** |
| Immunity | Golem is **immune to all damage and effects** by default |
| Laser hit | Creature hit is **stunned for the round** — but Golem becomes **vulnerable to all damage/effects** that round |
| Strategy | Someone must "soak" the laser to open the damage window |

**Fixate Table (d10):**

| Roll | Target |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | [[Ryo]] |
| 3–4 | [[Dondar]] |
| 5–6 | [[Berberis]] |
| 7–8 | [[Eryndor]] |
| 9–10 | [[Kai]] |

> [!danger] Phase 2 — 50% HP (adjust on the fly)
> Floor collapses. Party falls to a lower chamber — no damage, just narrative drama.
>
> - Same core mechanics, more pylons
> - Golem gets a shield, must be hit by two lasers before being stunned
> - **Mini golems** spawn each round — roll **d4** for number spawned
> - Mini golems: **20 HP**, resistant to non-magical physical damage, **cannot attack**, **grapple only** (DC 13)
> - Purpose: prevent kiting, force the party to split attention between adds and boss

### Scene 2: The Crossroads

*After the Guardian falls, the party enters a chamber with two sealed doors. The same cipher script from the previous session is carved above each.*

> [!cite]+ Read Aloud
> *The corridor opens into a hexagonal antechamber. Two doors face you — heavy stone, sealed. Above each, the familiar cipher alphabet glows faintly. The left door reads a single phrase. The right door reads another.*

- **Left door:** ᚠᚨᛚᛊᛖ ᛗᛖᛗᛟᚱᛁᛖᛊ → **"FALSE MEMORIES"**
- **Right door:** ᚺᚨᚹᛖ ᚠᚨᛁᛏᚺ → **"HAVE FAITH"**
- Both doors must be completed before the final passage opens
- Order is the party's choice

> [!info]- Cipher Note
> Players already cracked the alphabet last session — this should be quick. If they've forgotten, let them work it out again briefly. The cipher key was a runic substitution; [[Berberis]] and [[Kai]] decoded it collaboratively.

### Scene 3: Have Faith — The Bridge of Trust

*A test of trust and communication. One player is blinded; the others must guide them across an invisible bridge over a void.*

> [!cite]+ Read Aloud
> *A vast, dark chamber. The floor drops away into nothing — an endless void, no bottom visible. On the far wall, two stone levers jut from the rock, thirty feet apart. A narrow platform extends from the doorway to your left.*

**Mechanics:**

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| Trigger | When a player steps onto the side platform, they become **blinded** |
| Bridge | The rest of the party sees a narrow bridge form across the void — the blinded player cannot |
| Movement | Player must drag their token at least **5ft per move** (no micro-stepping to cheese it) |
| Edge | If token touches the bridge edge → **DEX save** (DC scales with how far over) or fall |
| Minions | Partway across, minions appear ahead or behind — they chase and try to **grapple the player off the edge** |
| Falling | Player falls into void — narratively unconscious, no mechanical consequences. Someone else must go |
| Goal | **Two players** must make it across and pull both levers simultaneously |
| Attempts | Unlimited — keeps going until two make it |

> [!question] Mid-Bridge Trigger
> When the blinded player reaches roughly the halfway point:
> > [!danger]- Action
> > Minions spawn either in front of or behind the player on the bridge. They move toward the player and attempt to grapple them off the edge. The rest of the party can see this and must shout warnings/directions.

### Scene 4: False Memories — Spot the Error

*The Vault replays scenes from the party's journey — but each contains one deliberate error. Players must identify it aloud before the scene progresses.*

> [!cite]+ Read Aloud
> *The door opens onto a shimmering corridor. As you step through, the stone walls dissolve and you are somewhere else entirely — somewhere familiar. The air smells like salt and rain. You've been here before.*

**Rules:**
- Players call out the error verbally — no check required
- No time limit; scene stays frozen until they spot it
- No punishment for wrong guesses — the Vault simply doesn't react
- Play as many or as few as time allows — these are ordered roughly by campaign chronology

> [!info]- DM Pacing Note
> You have 16 scenes prepared. You will NOT get through all of them — pick the ones most memorable to your table and skip freely. Aim for 6-8 depending on pace. Save the Ayana ring scene for last if possible — it's the most recent and dramatic.

### Scene 5: The Dragon's Eye

*Final cliffhanger. After both chambers are complete, the passage forward opens into darkness.*

> [!cite]+ Read Aloud
> *The final passage descends. The air changes — it is ancient here, heavy with the weight of millennia. The tunnel opens into a cavern so vast you cannot see the walls. Enormous stone statues line the darkness — kings and queens of the Silmara line, their faces worn smooth by time. Silence. And then — one of the statues blinks.*

- A single massive eye opens — not stone, but alive, glowing with silver-white light
- **An ancient Silver Dragon**, coiled among the statues, has been waiting here for centuries
- The Vault of Memories stub says: *"The dragon chose the Vault because only those of true Silmara blood could reach it, and waited. For what, it has not said."*
- **End session here.** No dialogue, no interaction — just the reveal
