#### Lilliana's Tale

>[!cite]+ Read
> As the evening descends, and the caravan forms a ring around the flickering fire, Lilliana Featherfoot steps into the soft firelight, her colourful garments dancing with each sway. Her emerald eyes glimmer with an unspoken mischief as she strums her lute, capturing the attention of every onlooker.
> 
> "Dear travellers," she begins, her voice carrying the honeyed lilt of a seasoned storyteller, "I have a tale for you. A tale from the time before time, from the realm of forgotten ages. The tale of the how world itself was split."
> 
> She strums a minor chord, casting a suspenseful hush over the crowd.
> 
> "Many moons ago, our world was bereft of magic," she continues, her fingers dancing on the lute's strings. "In those days, we weren't the children of fantasy, but of science. Massive cities filled the landscape, as tall as mountains, shining in the sun. No monstrous creatures scurried in the dark or flew in the skies, and no spells were cast but for the few crazy men and women, shunned to live on the outskirts of society. Instead, there were machines of iron and steam, and the art of the gods was mere whispering amongst few."
> 
> She strikes another chord, and the fire seems to crackle in tune.
> 
> "Then, one day, the divine dragon queen, enamoured by our world, descended upon us from the realm above," Lilliana's voice rises with the strum of the lute. "She sought to bless us with the gift of power. In her wake, she brought dragons of every hue, creatures of grandeur, and magic - a divine blessing that flowed into our world, as potent as a river in flood."
> 
> Her fingers start speeding more quickly over the lute, matching the rhythm of the story.
> 
> "From this divine infusion, people were reborn. No longer one in ten thousand, but one in ten carried magic in their veins. A gift, a curse, who can say? Chaos unfolded, as the queen's children, all manner of creatures and dragons, roamed free, their fires ravaging our shining cities."
> 
> She switches the melody, adding a touch of melancholy. "For seven centuries, wars raged across the world. The very land itself breaking, whole cities raised to ash."
> 
> *She goes on to describe great battles, the to and fro of war where one side seems to be getting the edge, then the other manages to pushes back.*
> 
> "It seemed we were to be locked in an eternal conflict, but one day, just as suddenly as the chaos began, it stopped. No one knows how it happened. Some say all the people of the land gathered together for a singular final push to drive the dragon queen from our world. Some say the Seven Divines themselves came down and banished her with holy magics."
> 
> Lilliana's lute goes silent, her story hanging heavy in the air.
> 
> "All we know is that peace settled on the world again, now changed irrevocably. Ancient ruined scars are all that remind us of a time before. Creatures hunt us in the dark and haunt our dreams. And no one has ever seen a dragon, but some say they are out there still, biding their time, waiting for the right opportunity to strike back."

- **Reaction:** A bit of wood pops in the fire, startling [[Harland]]. He starts clapping and mentions he prefers the version where the Titans come down and sunder the world.
