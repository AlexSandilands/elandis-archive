## Scenario 1: The Private Meeting

> [!summary]- Details
> - Where: [[Illythia Voss]]'s private quarters in the Locus Arcanum, Etherean Canton
> - When: Immediately at session open — late evening, pre-Confluence
> - Key NPCs: [[Illythia Voss]], [[Ayana Syndrosa]]

### Scene 1: The Reunion

- Ayana leads the party cloaked, hood up — she doesn't want to be spotted before the Confluence
- Illythia dismisses her arcanists, brings them inside, closes the door

**[[Illythia Voss]]** — Meridian of the Weave; tall, precise and composed, warmth underneath; tonight wound tight

> [!danger]+ The Reunion — 40 Years
> Illythia stops walking. A long beat. Then:
>
> *"40 years... and not one message."*
>
> Ayana begins: *"Illythia, it was better this way, there was no way of knowing if..."*
>
> Illythia slaps her. Not hard enough to injure — hard enough to mean it.
>
> A beat. Then Illythia pulls her in and kisses her.
>
> *"You left me in charge of the Circle. I have been furious, terrified, proud and lonely every single day."*
>
> Ayana: *"I know."* — *"Never again."* — *"Okay."*

- Ayana asks for Weave robes — her current clothes aren't fit for a state appearance
- Illythia smooths her robes, looks at the party: *"Right. Shall we go over the council?"*

> [!question] Trigger
> Ayana turns to Ryo — "There's something she should know before tonight."
> > [!info]- Action
> > - Ryo can choose to share his heritage; Ayana will not force it
> > - If Ryo shares: Illythia's composure slips briefly. *"...The Silmara line."* Then back to tactical mode.

> [!question] Trigger
> Ayana mentions Eirieneth — she glances at Eryndor.
> > [!info]- Action
> > - Eryndor can choose to open up; Ayana does not push
> > - Eirieneth's survival may surface at the Confluence if anyone has pieced it together

- Party explains the evidence — the Empire, the red priests, the pattern
- Illythia listens, asks tight practical questions: *numbers, locations, timeline*

> [!info]+ Illythia's Briefing
> - Kaelen Vayne has the support of Naivara Siannodel (Scales) and Elrohir Gemflower (Scroll)
> - Uncertain: Elder Silaqui Amastacia (Dawn), Master Artisan Fenian (Loom), Warden Sylvaris (Root)
> - *"Kaelen is not evil. He's a man who decided the best way to protect people is to control everything. His son was lost to the Vale of Eternal Night. He's been different ever since."*
> - *"Naivara will likely come up with some convoluted legal argument"*
> - *"Article Twelve of the accords names the unlocking of the Vault as a binding verification mechanism."*
> - Illythia must appear neutral during the Confluence; she cannot openly advocate

## Scenario 2: The Confluence of the Seven

> [!summary]- Details
> - Where: Crystalline platform atop the Royal Citadel — seven Nexus Citadels docked, bridges of pale light between them
> - When: Moonrise — the whole city watches from below
> - Key NPCs: All seven Meridians + [[Ayana Syndrosa]]
> - Shape: Formal debate — Ayana presents, council challenges, players jump in with skill checks

### Scene 2: The Approach

> [!cite]+ Read Aloud
> *The citadels drift in, slowly, as though pulled by invisible tides. One by one, bridges of pale light extend from each to the central platform — a wide circle of open crystal above the city, roof open to the sky. You can hear the crowd below, thousands of voices going quiet as the last bridge clicks into place. Seven chairs. Seven Meridians. And you.*

- Party enters behind Ayana; they stand to the side — guests, not peers
- Let players take in the view before Ayana speaks

### Scene 3: The Confluence

**Opening — Ayana's case:**
- Removes her hood. Let the room react.
- Presents: evidence of Empire agents, pattern of chaos (monsters leaving dens, citizens turning on each other), red priests, Kharazoth
- Then: Ryo. His bloodline. The Vault as proof that cannot be forged.

**The pushback — Kaelen's bloc:**

**[[Kaelen Vayne]]** — Meridian of the Blade; heavily scarred, watchful; dangerous when quiet; believes the royal line was a relic and the city is better without it

- Kaelen: the elves have survived 100 years without kings; Ryo is a stranger who walked in through a Faewild tear; the Empire is a Material Plane problem
- Naivara (Scales): legal argument — succession was never formally dissolved, but Ryo has no documentation, no witnesses, no standing before the law
- Elrohir (Scroll): historical precedent — every time elves intervened in Material Plane affairs, it cost them dearly

**Skill checks — use when players try to argue or persuade:**

| Situation | Check | DC |
|---|---|---|
| Persuade Silaqui (Dawn) with compassion | Persuasion | 12 |
| Persuade Fenian (Loom) with practical benefit | Persuasion | 14 |
| Persuade Sylvaris (Root) with Empire-as-existential-threat | Persuasion | 16 |
| Counter Kaelen's political argument | Persuasion / History | 18 |
| Spot that Elrohir isn't fully convinced by Kaelen | Insight | 14 |
| Catch Naivara in a legal inconsistency | History / Persuasion | 16 |
| Reframe a weakness via Deception | Deception | 14 |

---

**Direct challenges — read these aloud to pull players in:**

> [!danger]+ Kaelen Vayne (Blade) — DC 18 Persuasion or History · *Directed at anyone*
> *"We built this council to protect the elven people. We needed no king for a hundred years — no king while we rebuilt, no king while we survived, no king while we kept this city breathing. This stranger walked in through a Faewild tear. We do not know what he's already traded away to the forces that helped him get here. And the woman vouching for him abandoned this city forty years ago."*
>
> He lets that land. Then, quieter:
>
> *"I don't have a question. I have an objection. The floor is welcome to answer it."*
>
> **To crack him:** Logic won't do it — he's already decided. Two angles: *(1)* His son was lost to the Vale of Eternal Night — the Vale is a direct consequence of Empire-driven Faewild destabilisation. If the Empire grows, so does the Vale. He has a personal stake in stopping it. Use this carefully; it will make him dangerous if it lands wrong. *(2)* Trap him legally — if he forces Naivara to concede the Vault test, he can't block it. Let Naivara's inconsistency do the work.

> [!warning]+ Naivara Siannodel (Scales) — DC 16 History / Persuasion · *Directed at anyone*
> *"The Accords of the Exile govern succession. Specifically, Article Seven: any claim to the Silmara line requires either a living witness of the prior crown, or invocation of a verification mechanism recognised within the Accords. You have no witness. You have no documentation. You have a story, a woman who has been absent for forty years, and a young man who appeared through a planar tear. On what legal basis do you ask this council to act today?"*
>
> **The inconsistency:** Article Twelve of the same Accords names the Vault as a recognised verification mechanism. She cited Seven without Twelve. If a player identifies this — *"The Vault test is Article Twelve, Justiciar"* — she cannot oppose it without contradicting herself. She will go cold and quiet and concede. History check DC 14 to know the Accords exist; then Persuasion DC 16 to press the point cleanly.

> [!warning]+ Elrohir Gemflower (Scroll) — Insight DC 14, then Persuasion DC 14 · *Directed at anyone*
> *"Every time the elven people have acted as saviours of the Material Plane, we have been diminished for it. The Chaos Wars. The campaign against the Dread Kings. Each time, we bled for a world that forgot us within a generation. I do not dispute the young man's lineage. I dispute the idea that this time will be different."*
>
> **After Insight DC 14:** He's citing this from habit, not conviction. He sounds like he's reading from a book he's not sure he still believes. If pressed to name the specific precedents, one of them breaks down — the Dread Kings campaign was requested by the elves themselves, not the Material Plane. The counter isn't "history is wrong" — it's *"the precedent doesn't hold; those were interventions. This is defence."* He can be shifted by someone who actually engages with him as a historian rather than dismissing him.

> [!tip]+ Silaqui Amastacia (Dawn) — DC 12 Persuasion · *Directed at the party, not Ryo*
> Silaqui has been watching Ryo the whole time. She raises her hand gently, and speaks past him to the others:
>
> *"Forgive me — I don't wish to speak around you as though you aren't in the room. But your companions have walked with you across worlds, and I find I want to hear it from them. Would one of you tell me — not his bloodline, not his claim — what kind of person is he?"*
>
> **Any player can take this.** She just needs something real and specific — a moment, a decision, something Ryo did that wasn't for his own benefit. Sincerity matters more than eloquence. She's already half there; she just wants to feel sure.

> [!tip]+ Fenian (Loom) — DC 14 Persuasion · *Directed at the party's most tactical mind*
> Fenian leans forward slightly, hands clasped. He's been watching the room, not the speakers.
>
> *"Many of us have not forgotten why the Emerald Ceremony was sanctioned. We did not seal the gates because we stopped caring about the world outside. We sealed them because we had no safe way back. But the question I cannot answer — and I have tried — is this: even if we vote yes today, even if we accept the claim and open our arms, how do we act on it? Planar travel is restricted. The Liar's River is there for a reason. A vote of intent is not a bridge."*
>
> **The answer:** Ryo. The Silmara bloodline built Ithilmara — the gateway used at the Exile — into itself. It's not a Circle of the Weave ritual; it predates the Circles. If the Vault confirms his lineage, Ryo can open and close sanctioned passages between the Faewild and the Material Plane without tearing the barrier. That's the bridge Fenian is asking for. **Direct this at whoever's been paying attention to Ryo's bloodline lore** — Ayana can back it up if players are uncertain.

> [!tip]+ Sylvaris (Root) — DC 16 Persuasion · *Directed at nature-aligned players — Berberis, Eryndor*
> Sylvaris hasn't spoken yet. When he does, it's slow, like he's been listening to something else at the same time.
>
> *"The Faewild is already fraying. I feel it through the roots — things pressing against the mountain passes that don't belong here. Whatever your Empire is doing in the Material Plane, it is not staying there."*
>
> A pause.
>
> *"I'm not asking whether his claim is real. I'm asking whether opening our gates is the cure — or whether it lets whatever is spreading reach us faster."*
>
> **Best handled by Berberis or Eryndor** — someone who's seen the chaos in the wild or has a druidic connection. The winning argument isn't "we should help." It's *"the wall is already breached — staying sealed is the illusion of safety, not the reality."* He already suspects this. He needs someone to say it plainly.

## Scenario 3: The Vault of Memories

> [!summary]- Details
> - Where: Beneath the Royal Citadel — sealed stone doors carved with the World Tree
> - When: Immediately after the Confluence verdict
> - Key NPCs: None initially. The vault is semi-sentient.

> [!info]+ Lore: What This Place Is
> The pillar of stone the Royal Citadel stands on is the heartstone — the physical remnant of a petrified World Tree root. The Vault was carved down into this root over millennia. The walls are not stone — they are compressed root-memory, ancient organic material retaining the World Tree's primordial properties. Sealed for 100 years; no one has entered since the Exile.

### Scene 4: Opening the Doors

> [!cite]+ Read Aloud
> *The stone is warm. Not stone-warm — alive-warm. There's a sound, low and resonant, not heard so much as felt in the chest. The carvings begin to glow — not bright, not dramatic, just present, like something waking. The doors don't swing. They retract — pulling back and down, as though the stone is breathing in. Beyond: darkness, and air that smells of old wood and rain and something that has no name.*

- The council watches. Kaelen says nothing. Silaqui makes a small sound.

### Scene 5: The Visions

- Spiral staircase descending into a tall cylindrical chamber; center is open void
- Visions appear in the center — the vault is *reading* the party, not directing them
- Each vision is personal. Let players react; they can share or not.

> [!info]+ The Visions
> - **Ryo:** Two cloaked figures hand a small bundle to an old gnome (Jek, Lighthaven). Then: a male elf cradling a wounded female elf — General Blackmarsh in black armor drives a sword through both in one stroke.
> - **Dondar:** A dozen dwarves in an underground hall, wearing extra padding (concealed devices). Grim, resolved. A leader moves down the line. In the back, a hooded dwarf storms out, slams the door. *(Dondar leaving the resistance.)*
> - **Berberis:** A frail figure in a ruined castle, night sky through crumbling ceiling, finishing a ritual — then shuffling, moaning, the dead clawing upward. *(The necromancer who started the Val Aran death plague.)*
> - **Eryndor:** A woman with long black hair, sitting in a stone cell, wrists cuffed, looking through barred window at a city below. A river outside. A tower being built on a river island. *(Eirieneth — Eryndor's mother. Alive. Held in the Empress's tower above Val Luminor.)*
> - **Kai:** A figure cross-legged in a cave before a statue of a woman, something placed on the ground, head bowed. The figure lights with celestial moonlight. *(Elaris Moonsong, Selune's chosen — and a possible future for Kai.)*

- Visions stop when they reach the bottom landing. Silence. A corridor ahead.

### Scene 6: The Cipher

- Circular chamber; walls covered in runes; flagstones each bearing a single rune
- A sentence on the walls in cipher — answer is the last word; players stand on flagstones spelling it in order

**The sentence (written in cipher on the walls):**

> ᛏᚺᛖ ᚹᚨᚢᛚᛏ ᚣᛁᛖᛚᛞᛋ ᛏᛟ ᚾᛟ ᚲᚱᛟᚹᚾ ᚨᚾᛞ ᚾᛟ ᚹᛁᛚᛚ
> ᚾᚨᛗᛖ ᚹᚺᚨᛏ ᚣᛟᚢ ᚲᚨᚱᚱᚣ ᛏᚺᚨᛏ ᛏᚺᛖ ᚲᚱᛟᚹᚾ ᚲᚨᚾᚾᛟᛏ ᚷᚱᚨᚾᛏ
> ᛏᚺᛖ ᚹᚨᚢᛚᛏ ᚺᚨᛋ ᚹᚨᛁᛏᛖᛞ ᛏᛟ ᚺᛖᚨᚱ ᛏᚺᛖ ᚨᚾᛋᚹᛖᚱ
> ᚹᚺᚨᛏ ᛏᚺᛖ ᚲᚱᛟᚹᚾ ᚲᚨᚾᚾᛟᛏ ᚷᚱᚨᚾᛏ ᛁᛋ ᛒᛚᛟᛟᛞ

Translation:
*The vault yields to no crown and no will.*
*Name what you carry that the crown cannot grant.*
*The vault has waited to hear the answer.*
*What the crown cannot grant is blood.*

Answer word: **BLOOD** — runes: ᛒ ᛚ ᛟ ᛟ ᛞ

Flagstones available: only ᛒ, ᛚ, ᛟ, ᛞ (the letters of BLOOD)

> [!danger] Wrong Order
> Standing on the runes in the wrong sequence triggers a Glyph of Warding (4th level) — 5d8 lightning damage, DC 14 Dexterity save for half.
