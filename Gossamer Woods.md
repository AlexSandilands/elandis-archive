**Ruling Court:** The Court of Morning Dew (Seelie / Summer Allied) 
**Alignment:** Chaotic Good / Chaotic Neutral

## Overview

Often serving as the landing point for portals connecting to the material plane, The Gossamer Woods present a vibrant, disarming introduction to the Fae Realm. Deliberately structured to lull planar travellers into a false sense of security, the region is governed by the Court of Morning Dew, a lesser fae court that values elaborate pranks, fleeting beauty, and intense curiosity over malice. It is a place of overwhelming sensory input and deceptive safety.

## Atmosphere & Terrain

- **Lighting:** The woods are bathed in a perpetual, warm "golden hour" sunlight. Shadows are soft and long, regardless of the actual time of day, creating an eternal mid-summer evening aesthetic.
- **Flora & Geography:** The vegetation is massively oversized and intensely colored. Towering trees possess canopies of leaves that resemble spun sugar or candy-floss, blooming in vibrant shades of bright pink and glowing orange. The undergrowth is dotted with massive, softly luminescent mushrooms that pulse with the rhythm of the ambient magic.
- **Sensory Details:** The air is heavy and smells strongly of sweet vanilla mixed with a sharp, metallic hint of ozone—reminiscent of the atmosphere immediately following a lightning strike. The ambient soundscape is a constant, chiming rustle akin to glass wind-chimes, punctuated by distant, echoing giggles.
- **The Ground:** The soil and moss of the woods are unnervingly soft. Walking through the forest feels akin to stepping on a springy mattress. While it does not impede movement, the unnatural buoyancy is a constant physical reminder of the realm's chaotic nature.

## Inhabitants

The woods are heavily populated, though much of the native life remains magically hidden or invisible until a traveler does something deemed "interesting."

- **Creatures:** The canopy and underbrush are teeming with Pixies, Sprites, Blink Dogs, and Faerie Dragons.
- **Demeanor:** The denizens of the Gossamer Woods are wildly curious but entirely lack a basic understanding of mortal limits, physical boundaries, or personal space. While they are not inherently malicious or evil, their concept of a "harmless prank" can be devastating, confusing, or highly disruptive to unprepared mortals.

## Magical Anomalies: Gossamer Pollen

The most notable hazard within the woods is not predatory, but rather a chaotic byproduct of the local fey's affection. The air in certain glades is thick with a shimmering, iridescent dust often cultivated by local pixies.

- **Fey "Blessings":** When the local fey become fascinated by a traveler—particularly those carrying powerful magic, ancient bloodlines, or interesting secrets—they will often attempt to "bless" or "gift" the traveler by dousing them in concentrated handfuls of this gossamer pollen.
- **Chaotic Effects:** The pollen acts as a highly concentrated, chaotic mixture of transmutation and enchantment magic. Exposure to heavy doses can cause sudden physical age regression, temporarily reverting adults into children while leaving their adult memories and personalities intact.
- **Magical Scrambling:** The dust also severely disrupts high-level arcane weaves. Highly educated or powerful spellcasters affected by the pollen often find their magic scrambled; attempts to cast devastating or complex spells will instead misfire into harmless, spectacular displays—such as showers of confetti, bursts of soap bubbles, or turning stones into teacups. These effects are tied deeply to the ambient magic of the Faewild and are notoriously difficult to dispel through standard abjuration.
