The preeminent anti-Empire criminal and intelligence network in Voltarra. Faction, major — load-bearing across the campaign and directly entangled with Eryndor's personal arc, Lia's story, the Finegold operation, and the broader rebellion.

## Established
- Founded after Korvin Blackfeather's legendary heist from a tyrant king, known as the "Raven's Flight" — this event gave scattered thieves, spies, and mercenaries a symbol to unite under (Order of Ravens.md)
- Originally politically neutral; shifted to active anti-Empire resistance when the Empire rose and began hunting nests (Order of Ravens.md)
- Ebon, the Grand Raven ~50 years ago, directed the Order's anti-Empire turn and was instrumental in instigating the Dwarven rebellions (Order of Ravens.md)
- Members typically stay until death or retirement to protect the Order's secrets; execution is permitted for operatives who become a danger to the network (Session 26–27 prep)
- Eryndor's father Jarek was a veteran Raven operative (Sessions 21, 23)
- Darmouth Nest active — Ella Sable and Uric Graad operating there; mission to undermine Skyreach construction (Sessions 3–4)
- Val Miriel Nest headquartered beneath The Gilded Crow, The Artisan Way; entrance concealed behind a keg; the Nest is built into the Underrun; passphrase "I'll have one of your sharpest drinks" (Sessions 23, 27–28)
- Thaddeus "Ravenwing" Karr is Nest Father of Val Miriel (Sessions 23, 27)
- Val Miriel operatives include Trak "Shadow" Aeris and Gideon "Whisper" Blackthorn; Lia joined as a Fledgling after Session 17 (Sessions 17–28)
- Val Aran Nest exists — Thaddeus sent word ahead for the party to expect a contact there (Session 23)
- Val Noren Nest: abandoned, discovered by Eryndor in Session 44
- Val Solis: a hidden Nest operates as a refuge and hub for Ravens who need to disappear (Order of Ravens.md)
- Camaar Nest exists but the party never visited it; several operatives referenced — Sera "Nightshade" Velora (Talon), Valerius "Blackclaw" Draven (Talon), Leona "Sparrow" Voss (Fledgling) (Val Miriel.md, old notes)
- Finegolds have worked with the Order for some time — Gemma's mother was killed by the Empire, giving her a personal vendetta; Finegold caravans provide smuggling routes and information movement across the continent (Gemma Codex entry, Session 3)
- The Order maintains no formal alliances; steers clear of the Green Gryphons; offers a quiet nod of respect to the Veiled Cubs within Camaar (DM decision)
- Secret symbols — representations of ravens — are hidden throughout cities: marking safe houses, meeting points, and danger warnings (Order of Ravens.md)
- Field codenames are earned naturally as operatives prove themselves, bestowed informally (DM decision)

## Structure
- **Grand Raven** — supreme leader; identity concealed from virtually all members, including Nest Fathers; appears obscured even in rare direct meetings. The fewer points of failure, the harder the network is to dismantle.
- **Nest** — local chapter in a city where the Order has a presence
- **Nest Mother / Nest Father** — leader of the local Nest
- **Talon** — elite operative; inner circle of the local Nest
- **Raven** — full member
- **Fledgling** — new recruit

## History
Korvin Blackfeather, the first Grand Raven, unified disparate criminals under a single symbol after his daring "Raven's Flight" heist. The Order operated for centuries as a politically neutral power — manipulating societal dynamics for its own interests, never taking sides in the broader political struggles of Voltarra.

When the Valtorran Empire rose, the calculus changed. The Hands of the Empire declared open season on the Ravens; nests were driven underground or wiped out. Under Ebon's direction, the Order shed its neutrality and became an active resistance — running agents into the Empire's inner circles, instigating rebellions, and working to prevent the Empire from consolidating unchecked power. This was never a comfortable ideological shift; the Order's founding principle was neutrality, and there is still cultural tension within it between the old guard who want to return to that stance and those who believe the Empire has made neutrality impossible.

- **Finegolds**: Long-term working relationship; Finegold caravans function as a smuggling and information network. The Skyreach intelligence mission from Darmouth to Camaar was the Finegolds' first contracted job for the Order.

## Restricted

Gemma Finegold is the Grand Raven.

She was elevated to the role by Ebon — the previous Grand Raven, a dwarf now retired and living quietly in [[Silverdeep]] — shortly before the campaign began. Ebon has held the role for roughly fifty years; his dwarven lifespan made his long tenure possible, and his personal connection to the Dwarven peoples made him the architect of those rebellions half a century ago.

Ebon found Gemma in Silverdeep not long after her mother's public execution by the Empire. She was a young woman acting independently — she had already coordinated a clandestine rescue of several Raven operatives who had been compromised, smuggling them out of the city and preventing sensitive intelligence (possibly intelligence that would have led the Empire directly to Ebon) from falling into Imperial hands. She had no idea who Ebon was. He had every idea who she was.

He began grooming her for the role over the years that followed: folding her gradually into the Order's work, giving her increasing responsibility, watching how she thought. He saw in her the same qualities he'd had — a merchant's ability to move freely, a personal wound deep enough to sustain a lifetime of commitment, and a mind that worked in networks and leverage rather than blades and confrontation. When he judged the time right, and his own age meant the mantle needed to pass, he gave it to her.

Her first act as Grand Raven was the Skyreach intelligence mission in Darmouth — and the recruitment of the Bloody Nails, which was her own initiative. She identified them as assets worth cultivating from their first meeting. They have been working for the Grand Raven from the start without ever knowing it.

**Barak Finegold** is not related to Gemma. He is the captain of the Albatros and a Mawbreaker. Gemma deliberately targeted him — she "bumped into" him in a tavern in Camaar. The friendship is genuine on his end; the introduction was engineered on hers. She needed a reliable Mawbreaker connection, and Barak was the right person. He does not know she is the Grand Raven.

**Communication**: The Grand Raven's identity is never exposed, even to Nest Fathers. Orders reach Nests via fixed drop points in each city — the specific location known only to the Nest Father. Gemma also delivers messages personally through her caravan routes: a Raven receives a trade letter or a spoken word from a passing merchant and has no idea the merchant is the one issuing the order. The system is designed so that no single point of failure can unravel the whole network. No Nest Father has ever seen the Grand Raven's face unobscured.

**Ebon's current status**: Retired in Silverdeep. He is old even for a dwarf, but alive. He watches from a distance and trusts Gemma completely. He would only surface if the Order faced something existential — and even then, only to her.
