### The Imperial Siege

#### Initial Force

- **Initial Estimate**: ~10,000 soldiers

#### Losses and Attrition

- **Frostway Trap (Wolf Pack)**: ~1,000 soldiers lost.
- **Siege Losses**: Attacking a fortified city can result in significant casualties. Historically, a siege assault often leads to around **20-40% casualties** of attacking forces, especially against strong resistance. Conservatively, estimate **~2,500 soldiers** lost or incapacitated during the siege.
- **Insurgent Battle (Urban Combat)**: Urban insurgency warfare is notoriously costly for occupying forces. Over several weeks, it's reasonable to assume continuous attrition through guerrilla tactics, ambushes, and sabotage. A further **1,000-1,500 soldiers** could easily be lost or rendered ineffective.

#### Realistic Remaining Estimate

- **Initial**: 10,000
- **Minus Frostway Trap**: 9,000 remaining
- **Minus Siege Losses (~2,500)**: ~6,500 remaining
- **Minus Insurgency (~1,500)**: ~5,000 remaining

#### Adjusted Lower-End Final Estimate

For a lower-end, yet still realistic scenario, you might consider:

- Additional desertions or incapacitations due to harsh conditions, injuries, morale, lack of supplies, disease, or disorganization: roughly **500-1,000 additional losses**.

Thus, a conservative, realistic remaining number would be around:

**~4,000 soldiers remaining.**

This estimate is grounded in historical parallels and would present a formidable, but believably depleted, force for your players to encounter, creating tension without overwhelming odds.

### The People

#### Military Commanders

- [[Captain Erendir Thalos]] – Commander of the city guard. Loyal to the king, but pragmatic. His men are overstretched maintaining order in the city and manning the walls.
- **Marshal Kaelen Dravorn** – Veteran rebel leader who commanded much of the uprising. Fiercely opposed to the Empire, quick to push for aggressive action, but sometimes disregards logistics.
- [[Ayana Wynne]] – Young but respected lead advisor of the King. Was there since the beginning and help encourage Marius to start the rebellion. (secretly an Elf in disguise)

#### Nobility and Loyal Houses

- **Lady Elira Norven** – A cousin of the king, one of the few nobles with legitimate claim to bloodline loyalty. She feels entitled to lead in his absence and may push her own agenda.
- **Lord Veyric Halstrom** – A wealthy noble who turned against the Empire only when the rebellion began to succeed. Seen as opportunistic, but his coin and men are crucial.
- **Baroness Maedra Sornel** – An older noble who sheltered rebels during the occupation. Respected, but cautious, urging diplomacy over rash strikes.

#### Civic and Civil Voices

- **High Priest Orlen of Torgar** – Voice of the temple, representing faith and tradition. Argues for resilience and unity, sees the kidnapping as a test from the gods.
- **Mistress Aeva Durn** – Matron of the refugee council. Speaks for the displaced and poor of the city, reminding the council of the human cost of war.

## Locations

### 1. Guildhall of Chandlers _(“Ember Hall”)_

**Description**: A broad timber-roofed guildhouse once used by candle and lamp-makers. The chandlers are long gone, but the rebels fill it with maps, stolen weapons, and flickering candlelight — a symbol of stubborn resistance.  
**NPCs**:

- **Serra Duskvale** – veteran rebel coordinator, pragmatic, quick to cut through arguments.
- **Bran “One-Eye” Korrin** – scarred messenger who risks his life slipping through enemy lines.


### 2. Saint Aveline’s Cloister

**Description**: A centuries-old monastery dedicated to a healer-saint. Half-ruined by bombardment, its stone cloister and chapel now hold rows of cots and pallets. Refugees and wounded rebels crowd the aisles while incense struggles to mask the smell of blood.  
**NPCs**:

- **Brother Calem** – weary priest, selfless but nearly spent of both supplies and faith.
- **Dara Ironleaf** – druid and herbalist, runs makeshift gardens on the roof for medicines.


### 3. North Market Hall _(“Shattered Market”)_

**Description**: A great covered market for cloth and dye merchants. Now, a ramshackle sprawl of refugee tents, barter stalls, and stolen imperial goods. The air stinks of sweat, smoke, and desperation.  
**NPCs**:

- **Garrick Thane** – opportunistic trader who deals in smuggled food and arrows.
- **Toma Redhand** – rebel quartermaster, constantly haggling to keep supplies fair.


### 4. Bell Tower of Calmor Street _(“Broken Bells”)_

**Description**: A civic tower that once rang out the hours, its bells shattered by imperial stone-throwers. Rebels have converted the ruined belfry into a sniper’s nest and signal post. From its height, they can track imperial patrols.  
**NPCs**:

- **Salen the Hawk** – keen-eyed archer, commands the lookouts.
- **Verris Toll** – ex-bellringer, agile climber who relays signals and scouts rooftops.


### 5. Eastgate Granaries

**Description**: Massive silos and warehouses built a century ago to store harvests. Now they’re crammed with hundreds of refugees. The smell of old grain, sweat, and damp straw hangs heavy.  
**NPCs**:

- **Marra Finn** – refugee leader, fiercely protective of “her” displaced families.
- **Hob “Pitchfork” Renn** – farmer turned militia guard, armed with nothing but a hayfork and grim resolve.


### 6. The Nail & Tankard Inn _(now painted with a red nail symbol)_

**Description**: A once-popular inn with a battered signboard. Rebels drink, plot, and swap news here, while its cellars hide stashes of weapons. Its common room is always crowded and loud.  
**NPCs**:

- **Jareth Holloway** – stout innkeeper, a fiery speaker who turns every toast into defiance.
- **Selka Morrin** – sharp-eyed courier, flits in and out with urgent news.


### 7. Chandlers’ Wharf

**Description**: A row of river warehouses once used for wax, fish oil, and salt. Now used to smuggle supplies into rebel territory by boat at night. Always smells of tallow and stagnant water.  
**NPCs**:

- **Ferren Latch** – wharfmaster turned smuggler, missing three fingers from a “deal gone wrong.”
- **Ina Brook** – young boatwoman, fearless on the water, runs messages along the river.


### 8. Widow’s Fountain

**Description**: A civic fountain in a once-pleasant square, its statues chipped and dry. Now a gathering place for widows, orphans, and displaced families. Washing lines and campfires surround it.  
**NPCs**:

- **Erya Talven** – charismatic widow who keeps morale alive with stories and folk songs.
- **Kerric the Quiet** – old soldier missing a leg, trains children with sticks for self-defense.


### 9. The Ashen Forge _(Old Droskan Smithy)_

**Description**: An ancient smithy, its roof blackened from siege fires but its forges still hot. Rebel smiths hammer scavenged iron into spearheads, nails, and makeshift armor. Sparks and hammer-strikes echo day and night.  
**NPCs**:

- **Helda Coalbraid** – tireless dwarf smith, blistered hands but unstoppable will.
- **Milo Drass** – teenage apprentice desperate to prove himself, usually in the way.


### 10. The Ratways

**Description**: The old storm drains and cisterns beneath Val Norren, reeking of mildew and torch smoke. Used by smugglers for generations, now critical for rebel movement under the city.  
**NPCs**:

- **“Rook” Lerris** – wiry guide who knows every grate and manhole cover.
- **Imka Vey** – saboteur who fills tunnels with traps and deadfalls for imperials who follow.


### 11. The Stoneheart

**Description**: Primary place of worship within the city. Huge structure in the centre, dedicated to Torgar Earthshaker, but other worshippers are welcome.

Set in the centre of a large stone courtyard, in which also stands a huge statue of the god Torgar Earthshaker, in a pose looking up, one first raise with a heavy looking stone gaunlet on the one hand.

The building has a large central structure. The main doors open to a long hall leading to an octagonal main chamber. On both sides is a separate wing, each with their own entries
