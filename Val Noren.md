The ancient capital of the north and the seat of House Noren, Val Noren is the oldest surviving city-state on the island chain northeast of Valtorra — a walled, mountain-backed city whose history, economy, and faith are all built around a single resource: [[Renite Steel]]. Major location; the most pivotal city in the northern arc of the campaign.

## Established

- Northern island city, separated from the mainland by the jagged island chain that freezes into the [[Frostway]] each winter; [[Point Blackrock]] is the nearest mainland transit point (Session 1, Session 33, Session 35)
- Population: 50,000
- The [[Rennite River]] flows down from the mountain range behind the city, passes east to west through the city, and exits to the sea; the water glitters with tiny specks of Renite ore (Val Noren stub)
- The river is 100 feet wide and was the tactical dividing line during the Imperial siege — Loyalists held the northern half, Imperials the southern (Session 41 Prep, Session 42, sketch)
- Primary deity: [[Torgar Earthshaker]]; [[The Stoneheart]] temple dominates the city centre (Sessions 37–43)
- Ruled by [[King Marius Noren]], whose royal claim stretches back centuries before Imperial occupation (King Marius Noren entry)
- Under Imperial occupation for ~100 years; liberated on the 6th of Water, 3rd, 2507 — Marius declared it "Shield of the North" (Session 42)
- Claim to fame: [[Renite Steel]] — the city's primary export, weapons made from it crit on 19–20 (Session 43)
- [[Dondar]] holds the city's Teleportation Circle sigil (Session 43)
- Defining trait: City of Old — ancient construction, massive stone landmarks, pre-Imperial architecture still in use

## Geography

Island off the northeastern coast of Valtorra, separated from the mainland by the broken island chain that freezes into the [[Frostway]] each winter. Mountain ranges occupy the northern interior of the island; the city is built at their southern base where the land levels toward the coast.

The [[Rennite River]] descends from the mountains and bisects the city east to west before reaching the sea. During the Imperial siege, the river served as the strategic dividing line: Loyalists held the northern half (the Keep, [[The Stoneheart]], and the rebel barricade line) while Imperial forces held the southern half and maintained their main encampment outside the south wall. Two river gate towers — one on each bank — controlled access to the river crossings within the walls. The wharf district on the eastern side (Chandlers' Wharf) handles river and coastal trade.

Access to the mainland is by sea in summer and via the Frostway ice crossing in winter. Both routes carry risk; open water is difficult to navigate against ice and storms, and the Frostway has its own dangers. This near-isolation shaped the city's character as much as its geography.

## History

Val Noren is over a thousand years old. It was not planned or built by a conquering power — it grew. The Noren family held the original claim to the Renite ore mines in the mountains above the site, and the settlement that formed around them attracted miners, smiths, and traders drawn to the rare metal. Over generations the settlement became a town, the town became a city, and the Norens formalized their rule by building walls and establishing governance around their dynasty. They gave the city its name.

For centuries Val Noren was an independent city-state, trading [[Renite Steel]] south to the rest of Valtorra. Its isolation and high walls kept it out of most continental conflicts. When [[Valtorran Empire|The Crimson Empire]] rose under [[Empress Morganna Eventide]] approximately a hundred years ago, Val Noren fell with the rest of the continent — not through any particular weakness, but because nothing survived the conquest. The Noren line was preserved under occupation, repurposed as governors rather than kings.

The rebellion began when [[King Marius Noren]], guided over years by his advisor [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana Wynne]], expelled the Imperial garrison and declared independence. The Empire responded with [[Dread General Gerard Blackmarsh]] and an army of ~10,000 soldiers. The siege lasted the duration of the campaign's northern arc before being broken in Session 42.

## The People

### Military Commanders

- [[Captain Erendir Thalos]] — Commander of the city guard. Loyal to the king, pragmatic. His men were overstretched maintaining order in the city and manning the walls during the siege.
- Marshal Kaelen Dravorn — Veteran rebel leader who commanded much of the uprising. Fiercely opposed to the Empire, quick to push for aggressive action, sometimes disregards logistics.
- [[Ayana Syndrosa|Ayana Wynne]] — Young but respected lead advisor of the king. Present since the beginning of the rebellion and instrumental in encouraging Marius to act.

### Nobility and Loyal Houses

- Lady Elira Norven — Cousin of the king, one of the few nobles with a legitimate bloodline claim. Felt entitled to lead in his absence and pushed her own agenda during the siege.
- Lord Veyric Halstrom — Wealthy noble who turned against the Empire only when the rebellion began to succeed. Seen as opportunistic, but his coin and men were crucial.
- Baroness Maedra Sornel — Older noble who sheltered rebels during the occupation. Respected and cautious; urged diplomacy over rash action.

### Civic and Civil Voices

- High Priest Orlen of Torgar — Voice of [[The Stoneheart]] temple. Argued for resilience and unity during the siege; sees hardship as Torgar's test.
- Mistress Aeva Durn — Matron of the refugee council. Spoke for the displaced and poor throughout the occupation, reminding the war council of the human cost of every decision.

## Culture and Beliefs

Val Noren's character was shaped by isolation. Cut off from the mainland by open sea in summer and treacherous ice in winter, the city developed a self-sufficiency that runs deep. Its people are reserved with outsiders, fiercely proud, and not easily rattled — qualities earned through centuries of surviving on their own.

[[Torgar Earthshaker]] is the dominant god, and his worship here takes a form distinct from elsewhere in Valtorra. Faith and craft are the same thing. Temple walls are hung with weapons and armor — not as trophies but as offerings and expressions of devotion. The forge is a sacred space. The finest smiths are considered touched by Torgar, and the act of working metal carries a reverence that outsiders sometimes find strange.

This tradition found its highest institutional expression in the Stonesworn — a guild of master smiths who are simultaneously devotees of [[Torgar Earthshaker]]. They control the most prestigious [[Renite Steel]] commissions, train apprentices in both metallurgy and religious practice, and maintain the armory of [[The Stoneheart]] temple. A blade bearing the Stonesworn mark is among the most coveted weapons in Valtorra.

## Renite Steel

The ore comes from the mountain range directly above and behind the city. Active mines operate in the mountains, historically controlled by the Noren family and seized by the Empire during occupation. The [[Rennite River]] carries tiny glittering specks of ore dust down from the mountains, giving the water its distinctive shimmer.

Renite Steel can be forged to an edge sharper and stronger than conventional steel while remaining notably lighter. In skilled hands it holds an edge that makes critical strikes more likely (weapons crit on 19–20). Val Noren's economy has been built around the ore for over a thousand years — it is the reason the city was founded, the reason the Empire wanted it, and the reason it survived long enough to matter.

## The Imperial Siege

When [[King Marius Noren]] declared rebellion and expelled the Imperial garrison, the Empire sent [[Dread General Gerard Blackmarsh]] with an initial force of ~10,000 soldiers. The army crossed via the Frostway but lost approximately 1,000 to a trap laid by the Frostwardens — the Wolf Crew led a portion of the Imperial advance off the safe path into Remorhaz territory (Session 35).

The remaining force surrounded and partially occupied the city. The [[Rennite River]] divided it: Loyalists held the northern half behind a secondary barricade line, Imperials held the southern half with their main camp outside the south wall. During the occupation, [[The Red Priests]] used an undead assault as cover to breach the Keep and kidnap [[King Marius Noren]], holding him in the crypts of [[The Stoneheart]] (Sessions 37–40).

Casualties by liberation reduced the Imperial force to approximately 4,000 soldiers (from the initial 10,000, accounting for Frostway losses, siege assault attrition, and insurgency).

The liberation was executed by [[The Bloody Nails]] in Session 42: they infiltrated and secured both river gate towers, the [[Mawbreakers]] under [[Barak Stormrider]] constructed a pontoon bridge under cover of fog, and the full Loyalist army crossed to flank the Imperial force. The remaining [[Hand of The Empire|Hands of the Empire]] took their own lives rather than surrender; the Imperial soldiers broke and yielded.

## Locations

### The Stoneheart

Primary place of worship within the city. An enormous ancient structure at the city's centre, dedicated to [[Torgar Earthshaker]] but welcoming of other worshippers. Set within a large stone courtyard that also holds a massive statue of Torgar — depicted looking upward, one fist raised, wearing a heavy stone gauntlet. The main doors open to a long hall leading to an octagonal central chamber, with separate wings on either side. During the Imperial siege, the temple was seized by [[The Red Priests]], who held [[King Marius Noren]] in the royal crypts beneath it.

### The Rennite River

100 feet wide. The water glitters from Renite ore dust carried down from the mountains. During the siege, the river divided Loyalist and Imperial territory within the city walls. The two river gate towers — west and east — controlled access across it; their capture by the party in Session 42 allowed the pontoon bridge to be constructed and the liberation to begin.

### 1. Guildhall of Chandlers ("Ember Hall")

A broad timber-roofed guildhouse once used by candle and lamp-makers. The chandlers are long gone; during the siege, rebels used it for maps, stolen weapons, and planning — a symbol of stubborn resistance. NPCs: Serra Duskvale (veteran rebel coordinator, pragmatic); Bran "One-Eye" Korrin (scarred messenger who slipped through enemy lines).

### 2. Saint Aveline's Cloister

A centuries-old monastery dedicated to a healer-saint. Half-ruined by bombardment during the siege; its stone cloister and chapel held rows of wounded rebels and refugees. The purification ritual that transformed the Dread General's armour into the Moonlit Aegis was performed here (Session 43). NPCs: Brother Calem (weary priest, nearly spent of both supplies and faith); Dara Ironleaf (druid and herbalist, ran makeshift roof gardens for medicines).

### 3. North Market Hall ("Shattered Market")

A great covered market for cloth and dye merchants, converted during the siege into a sprawling refugee camp of tents, barter stalls, and stolen Imperial goods. NPCs: Garrick Thane (opportunistic trader dealing in smuggled food and arrows); Toma Redhand (rebel quartermaster, constantly haggling to keep supplies fair).

### 4. Bell Tower of Calmor Street ("Broken Bells")

A civic tower whose bells were shattered by Imperial stone-throwers. Rebels converted the belfry into a sniper's nest and signal post. NPCs: Salen the Hawk (keen-eyed archer, commands the lookouts); Verris Toll (ex-bellringer, agile climber who relays signals and scouts rooftops).

### 5. Eastgate Granaries

Massive silos and warehouses crammed with hundreds of refugees during the siege. NPCs: Marra Finn (refugee leader, fiercely protective of displaced families); Hob "Pitchfork" Renn (farmer turned militia guard, armed with a hayfork and grim resolve).

### 6. The Nail & Tankard Inn

A once-popular inn, its signboard repainted with a red nail symbol during the rebellion. Rebels drink, plot, and swap news here; its cellars hide stashes of weapons. NPCs: Jareth Holloway (stout innkeeper, a fiery speaker who turns every toast into defiance); Selka Morrin (sharp-eyed courier, flits in and out with urgent news).

### 7. Chandlers' Wharf

A row of river warehouses on the eastern side of the city, once used for wax, fish oil, and salt. During the siege, used to smuggle supplies into rebel territory by boat at night. NPCs: Ferren Latch (wharfmaster turned smuggler, missing three fingers); Ina Brook (young boatwoman, fearless on the water, runs messages along the river).

### 8. Widow's Fountain

A civic fountain in a once-pleasant square, its statues chipped and dry. A gathering place for widows, orphans, and displaced families during the siege. NPCs: Erya Talven (charismatic widow who kept morale alive with stories and folk songs); Kerric the Quiet (old soldier missing a leg, trains children with sticks for self-defense).

### 9. The Ashen Forge (Old Droskan Smithy)

An ancient smithy, its roof blackened from siege fires but its forges still burning. Rebel smiths hammered scavenged iron into weapons and makeshift armor throughout the occupation. After liberation, Helda Coalbraid forged the Moonlit Aegis here in a divine fugue state (Session 43). NPCs: Helda Coalbraid (tireless dwarf smith, Stonesworn member); Milo Drass (teenage apprentice).

### 10. The Ratways

The old storm drains and cisterns beneath Val Noren, reeking of mildew and torch smoke. Used by smugglers for generations; critical for rebel movement under the city during the siege. NPCs: "Rook" Lerris (wiry guide who knows every grate and manhole cover); Imka Vey (saboteur who fills tunnels with traps and deadfalls).

## Restricted

She has been guiding Marius for years before the rebellion, shaping him from a reluctant governor into the king the resistance needed. She knows about the Emerald Ceremony and has been protecting her identity aggressively — she cast Modify Memory on Ryo after he accidentally discovered her pointed ears (Session 43). The players do not know this.
- Open thread: Berberis has a personal quest goal to "end the dead walking the north — the necromancer in the ruins of Val Noren" (Session 49). A dark presence may still be operating in the siege-damaged parts of the city even after liberation — possibly connected to the Red Priests or their master.
